Making Your Vacation Rental Pet-Friendly

Pet-friendly vacation rentals represent one of the most underserved and high-demand niches in the short-term rental market. According to APPA industry research, nearly 70% of US households own at least one pet — and those pet owners face significant challenges finding quality accommodation that welcomes their animals.

The result: pet-friendly properties earn premium nightly rates (typically 10-25% more), command higher occupancy, and build intensely loyal repeat guest bases. Guests traveling with pets are often willing to pay more and book further in advance to secure a confirmed pet-friendly property.

This complete guide covers how to make your vacation rental pet-friendly in a way that maximizes revenue while protecting your property.

Should You Accept Pets? The Business Case

Many hosts default to "no pets" out of concern about damage and allergies. It's worth examining whether that default is leaving significant money on the table.

The case for accepting pets:

  • Higher nightly rates: Pet-friendly listings command a measurable premium in most markets
  • Reduced vacancy: Pet owners often struggle to find options, filling your calendar during slower periods
  • Guest loyalty: Pet owners who find a host who genuinely welcomes their animals return again and again
  • Competitive differentiation: In markets where most hosts don't accept pets, being pet-friendly immediately stands out

The case for caution:

  • Pets do cause more wear and tear (primarily: pet hair, odors, scratches)
  • Some guests have pet allergies (your property can't be marketed to both pet-allergic and pet-friendly guests effectively)
  • Noise (barking) can create neighbor issues in certain property types

General recommendation: Most hosts with single-family homes or vacation properties in non-attached settings benefit significantly from going pet-friendly. Hosts in attached buildings (apartments, condos) face more constraints.

Setting Up Your Pet Policy

A clear, fair pet policy protects your property and sets appropriate expectations with guests.

Key Policy Decisions

Pet types:

  • Dogs only (most common)
  • Cats allowed
  • Small pets only (under 25 lbs is a common threshold)
  • No weight limit
  • Limited to 1-2 pets

Pet fee structure:

  • Per-stay fee: Most common; typically $25-75 per stay for 1-2 pets
  • Per-night fee: Better for longer stays; $10-20/night
  • Per-pet fee: Useful when allowing multiple pets
  • Refundable pet deposit: Less common but option for higher-end properties

Pet-specific rules:

  • Pets must not be left unattended at the property (or define conditions under which they can be)
  • Pets must not be on the furniture (or explicitly state which furniture is OK)
  • Pet owners must clean up after their pets outdoors
  • Pets must be crated at night if they have a history of destructive behavior
  • Any pet damage must be disclosed at checkout

Sample Pet Policy Language (for Your Listing)

"We love dogs! We welcome up to [2] dogs of any size for a per-stay pet fee of $[X]. We ask that:

  • Pets not be left unattended inside the property for more than [4] hours
  • Pets not be allowed on upholstered furniture (we provide a washable pet blanket)
  • All outdoor pet waste be cleaned up and disposed of properly
  • Any pet damage be disclosed at checkout

We keep a pet kit stocked with food/water bowls, waste bags, and a pet towel for post-walk paw cleaning. We want this to be a great stay for the whole family!"

Protecting Your Property from Pet Damage

Going pet-friendly doesn't mean accepting unlimited damage. Smart property management significantly limits the risk.

Flooring

Carpet and pets are a problematic combination. Pet-friendly properties should prioritize:

  • Luxury vinyl plank (LVP): Scratch-resistant, waterproof, easy to clean — the gold standard for pet-friendly STRs
  • Tile: Durable but cold; pair with washable area rugs
  • Hardwood: Use a matte or satin finish that hides scratches better; avoid glossy finishes

For area rugs, choose washable options (RUGGABLE is popular for exactly this use case) in patterns that don't show hair and minor staining.

See our damage prevention guide for comprehensive property protection strategies.

Furniture

  • Use performance fabrics (Crypton, Sunbrella) that resist pet hair, scratches, and odors
  • Place a washable slipcover or designated pet blanket on sofas and chairs where pets are permitted
  • Avoid upholstered pieces with texture that traps pet hair (boucle, velvet loops)
  • Consider a dog crate or bed station in a visible corner as an invitation for pets to have their own space

Bedding and Linens

  • Use waterproof mattress protectors (critical for pet-friendly properties)
  • Keep a separate set of designated "pet bedding" (a dog bed, old blanket, or washable cover) for guests to use instead of your nice linens
  • Explicitly note in your pet policy (and welcome book) that pets are not permitted on the main beds, unless you've decided to allow it

Odor Management

Pet odor is the primary concern after cleaning. Build odor management into your standard cleaning protocol:

  • Use enzyme-based cleaners on pet accidents (simple neutralizing sprays don't work on pet odors)
  • Wash all soft furnishings after every pet stay (throws, decorative pillows, pet blankets)
  • Air the property before new guests arrive (open windows during cleaning)
  • Use air purifiers in primary living areas
  • Consider pet-safe scent diffusers as a backup (ensure they're non-toxic to animals for the pet guests, too)

Pet-Friendly Amenities to Include

The hosts who earn the best reviews from pet-owning guests are those who genuinely think through what makes the stay easier for pet owners.

The Pet Welcome Kit

Create a small basket or kit with:

  • Food and water bowls (stainless steel, easy to clean)
  • Dog towel or chamois for paw cleaning at the door
  • Pet waste bags (stock generously)
  • List of nearby dog-friendly parks, trails, and beaches
  • Dog bed or washable floor pad in a designated pet sleeping area
  • Treats (optional but memorable — guests love this)
  • Local vet and emergency vet information (for anxious pet parents)

Add a note: "Welcome, [Dog's Name]!" — guests almost always mention this in reviews.

Outdoor Pet Amenities

  • A fenced yard or outdoor space is the single biggest upgrade for pet-friendly properties; market it prominently
  • A hose or outdoor shower for washing muddy paws
  • Waste bag dispenser at the back door or yard entrance
  • A dog tie-out stake or cable in the yard for guests who want to let their dog be outside supervised

Property Features to Highlight in Your Listing

If your property has any of these features, lead with them in your marketing:

  • Fenced yard ("fully fenced yard — safe for dogs to run")
  • Doggy door (if applicable)
  • Proximity to dog-friendly parks, beaches, or trails
  • First-floor or step-free entry (easier for large dogs)
  • No stairs required for any bedroom (accessibility for senior dogs)

Marketing Your Pet-Friendly Property

Going pet-friendly only pays off if pet-owner guests can find you. Use these tactics to maximize visibility.

Optimize Your Listing on Platforms

On Airbnb:

  • Enable the "Pets Allowed" amenity toggle (this adds you to pet filter searches)
  • Add "pet-friendly," "dog-friendly," and "fenced yard" to your title or listing description where appropriate
  • Include photos of your pet amenities and yard/outdoor space

On VRBO:

  • VRBO has dedicated pet-friendly filter options; ensure all relevant boxes are checked
  • VRBO's pet-owner audience is especially active, as the platform is popular with family and group travelers who often include dogs

On your direct booking site:

  • Create a dedicated landing page or section for pet-owner guests
  • Include photos of the yard, the pet kit, and nearby dog-friendly spots

According to VRBO's host resources, pet-friendly listings receive significantly more searches and higher average booking values than non-pet listings in most leisure destinations.

Write a Pet-Specific Listing Description

Include a paragraph specifically addressing pet-owner guests:

"Traveling with your four-legged family member? [Property Name] is a true dog-friendly home. We have a fully fenced half-acre yard, a pet welcome kit with bowls and waste bags, and a list of the best local dog parks and pet-friendly trails. The neighborhood is quiet and walkable, and [Coffee Shop] on the corner has a dog-friendly patio. We love dogs and it shows."

Leverage Pet-Focused Marketing Channels

  • BringFido.com: A dedicated pet travel platform where you can list your property
  • GoPetFriendly.com: Another pet travel directory
  • Petswelcome.com: Long-established pet travel resource
  • Pet owner Facebook groups in your target market (families traveling with pets)

For broader listing marketing strategies, see our rental property marketing strategies guide.

Cleaning Protocol for Pet Stays

Your standard turnover protocol needs adjustments for pet stays:

Additional cleaning steps:

  1. Vacuum all upholstered surfaces, rugs, and floors with a pet hair attachment
  2. Inspect all mattress protectors and pillow protectors for any pet contact
  3. Wash any designated pet blankets and throws
  4. Use an enzyme cleaner on any identified accident spots (whether visible or not — pets sometimes have accidents in non-obvious spots; inspect systematically)
  5. Check under furniture for pet hair accumulation
  6. Inspect the yard for pet waste
  7. Air the property with open windows during cleaning

Communicate pet stay additions to your cleaner: Your cleaning service should know when a pet stay has occurred and understand the additional protocol. Build a modest additional fee into your pet charge to cover the extra cleaning time.

Handling Pet Damage

Despite best precautions, damage from pets will occasionally occur. Your approach:

  1. Document at checkout — photograph any damage during your standard post-checkout inspection
  2. Communicate with the guest — most pet-owning guests are mortified by damage and respond well to direct, calm communication
  3. File a claim — both Airbnb's AirCover and VRBO's damage protection cover documented pet damage
  4. Keep receipts — for carpet cleaning, furniture repair, or replacement

Being transparent about your pet damage policy in your listing (and your welcome book) sets expectations and makes it easier to have these conversations when necessary.

Conclusion

Going pet-friendly is one of the most effective niche strategies in short-term rental hosting. The combination of higher rates, improved occupancy, and loyal repeat guests makes it a compelling choice for most property types.

The key is doing it thoughtfully: a clear pet policy, a durable and pet-tolerant property setup, a genuine pet welcome kit, and a cleaning protocol that maintains your property's condition across pet stays.

Guests who travel with pets are some of the most appreciative and loyal guests in the STR market. Earn their trust, and they'll come back every year.
