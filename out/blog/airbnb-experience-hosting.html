Most people think of Airbnb as a platform for renting rooms and homes. But since 2016, Airbnb has operated a second marketplace, Airbnb Experiences, that lets locals and hosts offer curated activities to travelers.

Cooking classes, photography walks, surf lessons, wine tastings, guided hikes, cocktail-making workshops: the range of Experiences on the platform is enormous.

The SBA offers guidance on permits for new ventures. For STR hosts, Airbnb Experiences represent a compelling revenue diversification opportunity.

You do not need to own property to host an Experience, and if you already have guests staying in your listing, Experiences give you a way to earn more from the same customer relationship. This guide covers everything you need to know to launch a successful Airbnb Experience, from ideation and application to pricing and promotion.

What Is an Airbnb Experience?

An Airbnb Experience is a bookable activity hosted by a local expert. Unlike accommodations, Experiences are about skills, knowledge, and access, things the host offers that travelers cannot get from a hotel or a Google search.

Experiences fall into two broad categories:

In-Person Experiences

Activities hosted in a physical location, typically limited to small groups (4, 12 guests). Examples:

  • Guided food tours through a local neighborhood
  • Cooking classes teaching a regional cuisine
  • Photography sessions at hidden local spots
  • Kayaking or surfing lessons
  • Wine or spirits tasting experiences
  • Cultural workshops (pottery, painting, traditional craft)
  • Fitness and wellness sessions (yoga, meditation retreats)
  • History and architecture walking tours

Online Experiences

Launched during the pandemic, Online Experiences allow hosts to connect with guests globally via video. They have lower overhead (no venue required) and can reach international audiences.

Examples:

  • Virtual cooking classes
  • Live music sessions
  • Online language lessons
  • Virtual fitness and yoga classes
  • Digital art workshops
  • Storytelling and cultural exchange

Why STR Hosts Should Consider Airbnb Experiences

Revenue Without Additional Property

Experiences generate income entirely from your time and skills. There is no property to furnish, no cleaning to manage, no maintenance to handle.

For hosts who already have operational capacity, this is pure incremental revenue.

Deepen Relationships with Existing Guests

If your STR guests attend your Experience, they are more likely to:

  • Leave detailed, glowing reviews mentioning the host personally
  • Return for future stays
  • Recommend both your listing and your Experience to friends

This creates a flywheel effect: great accommodations + great Experience = stronger reviews + stronger bookings for both offerings.

Market Differentiation

A host who offers both a place to stay and a memorable local experience is categorically different from the average STR host. Many guests choose accommodation based on who the host is, and an active Experience profile signals engagement, expertise, and local knowledge.

Diversification Against STR Regulatory Risk

STR regulations are tightening in many cities. Experiences face far fewer regulatory hurdles than overnight rentals.

If your STR business becomes restricted, an Experience income stream provides continuity and keeps you in the Airbnb ecosystem. For more on the regulatory landscape, see our short-term rental regulations guide.

How to Become an Airbnb Experience Host

Step 1: Identify Your Experience Concept

The best Experiences are rooted in genuine expertise, local knowledge, or a skill that guests cannot easily replicate on their own. Ask yourself:

  • What do I know about this city that most visitors never discover?
  • What skills do I have that would be valuable or fun to share?
  • What experiences do my STR guests ask me about most frequently?
  • What do I do that people consistently find interesting or impressive?

The Experience does not need to be exotic. A pasta-making class taught by an Italian grandmother consistently outperforms a generic cooking class.

A photography tour led by a working photographer beats a generic walking tour with a camera. Authenticity and expertise are the differentiators.

Step 2: Research the Market

Before applying, research existing Experiences in your city to understand:

  • What categories are over-saturated (generic city tours, basic cooking classes)
  • What is missing from the local market
  • What price points similar Experiences charge
  • What review scores top Experiences maintain

Aim for a niche where you can genuinely compete on quality, not just availability.

Step 3: Prepare Your Application

Airbnb reviews all Experience applications and rejects those that do not meet their standards. To improve your approval odds:

Required elements of a strong application:

  • Clear, compelling concept description that explains your unique angle
  • High-quality photos of the experience in action (or equivalent if brand new)
  • A defined group size (typically 1, 12 guests)
  • A realistic duration (1, 4 hours is most common)
  • A professional, complete host profile with photo

Airbnb's standards include:

  • The experience must be hosted by you (not a third party)
  • You must have genuine expertise or local insight in the area
  • The experience must be unique and offer access guests cannot get on their own
  • Safety requirements must be addressed for any physical activities

Step 4: Set Your Pricing

Pricing Airbnb Experiences requires thinking about:

Your time cost: What is an hour of your time worth?

Your cost of goods: Ingredients for a cooking class, equipment rentals, venue fees, transportation

Market comparables: What are similar Experiences in your city charging?

Group size economics: A per-guest price of $75 with a maximum of 8 guests generates $600 for a 3-hour experience. At 2 experiences per week, that is $4,800/month in additional revenue.

Typical Experience pricing by category:

Category Typical Per-Guest Rate
Cooking/food class $60, 120 per person
Guided walking tour $40, 80 per person
Adventure/outdoor activity $80, 150 per person
Arts and craft workshop $50, 100 per person
Tasting experience (wine, spirits) $60, 120 per person
Online Experience $15, 40 per person

Airbnb takes a 20% commission on all Experience bookings. The IRS requires reporting this income on your tax return.

What Makes an Airbnb Experience Successful

The difference between a thriving Experience with hundreds of bookings and one that sits idle is usually a combination of four factors:

1. A Distinct Point of View

Generic tours and classes are forgettable. The most successful Experiences have a clear, specific angle:

  • Not "Rome food tour" but "Rome food tour with a third-generation pasta maker from Trastevere"
  • Not "photography walk" but "Golden hour rooftop photography in Barcelona's hidden terraces"
  • Not "sushi class" but "Sushi secrets from a former Tokyo restaurant chef"

The more specific and credible your angle, the more your listing stands out, and the higher a price you can justify.

2. Exceptional Photos

Experience photos are critical because guests are buying a vision of what the experience will feel like. Invest in professional or semi-professional photography of your Experience in action.

Show:

  • Guests engaged and smiling
  • The environment at its most attractive
  • The final product (a plated dish, a finished painting, a stunning view)
  • You as the host in an authoritative, warm role

3. Fast Response and Easy Booking

Guests browsing Experiences are in a spontaneous, exploratory mindset. If they have to wait hours for your approval, they book someone else.

Enable Instant Book for Experiences where safety allows, and respond to all inquiries within 30, 60 minutes.

4. Review Management

Just like accommodations listings, Experience rankings are driven by review volume and quality. For your first few months:

  • Invite friends and willing community members to try your Experience and leave honest reviews
  • Over-deliver on early sessions, arrive early, prepare thoroughly, personalize the experience
  • Ask every guest directly (verbally and via the post-experience message) to leave a review

Promoting Your Experience Beyond Airbnb

Airbnb's internal search drives most Experience bookings, but you can accelerate your growth with additional promotion:

Use Your Existing STR Guest Network

Include a reference to your Experience in your welcome message to STR guests:

"While you're in town, I also host a [pasta-making class / photography tour / wine tasting] that many of my guests have loved, check it out at [Airbnb Experience link] if you're interested!"

This warm audience already trusts you and is already in the city.

Social Media

Instagram and TikTok are natural fits for Experience content. Short videos of your cooking class, walking tour, or art workshop can build an audience that converts into bookings.

Tag your city and use relevant hashtags.

Partner with Local Hotels and Concierges

Hotel concierges are always looking for high-quality local activities to recommend. Reach out to boutique hotels in your area and offer a referral arrangement.

Many are happy to recommend Airbnb Experiences if the quality is exceptional.

Google Business Profile

Create a Google Business Profile for your Experience. Guests searching "cooking class [city]" or "things to do in [city]" may find you through Google before they ever open Airbnb.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Underpricing: Many new hosts price too low hoping to generate bookings. Low prices attract bargain-hunters and signal low quality. Price at market rate from day one and earn your premium through exceptional reviews.

Overpromising in the listing: Setting guest expectations too high leads to disappointed guests even when your experience is objectively good. Be honest and specific about what guests will do and what the outcome will be.

Neglecting the pre-experience communication: Guests often have questions or logistics needs before arriving. A clear, warm pre-experience message reduces no-shows and sets a positive tone.

Ignoring cancellation policy: Set a cancellation policy that protects your time. A strict 48-hour cancellation policy is reasonable for most Experiences.

Airbnb Experiences as Part of Your STR Revenue Strategy

For property investors thinking holistically about rental income, Airbnb Experiences fit naturally alongside other revenue diversification strategies. They require no capital investment, generate income from skills rather than assets, and deepen your relationship with the platform and its community of travelers.

Combined with a well-improved STR listing, competitive pricing via tools covered in our short-term rental pricing strategy guide, and smart revenue management, Experiences can add a meaningful additional income stream with relatively low operational burden.

For the complete guide to building a high-performing STR business, visit our short-term rental management guide. And for ideas on generating additional streams beyond Airbnb Experiences, see our guide on maximizing rental income strategies.

Summary

Airbnb Experiences offer STR hosts and property investors a low-capital, skills-based revenue stream that complements and strengthens their existing rental business. Key takeaways:

  1. Find your genuine niche, Authenticity and specific expertise outperform generic offerings
  2. Invest in great photos, Your Experience photos are your primary sales tool
  3. Price competitively from day one, Do not underprice to generate bookings
  4. Generate early reviews aggressively, Volume and quality of reviews drive your ranking
  5. Promote to your existing STR guests, They are your warmest possible audience
  6. Respond fast and enable Instant Book, Spontaneous guests will not wait

Whether you are looking to diversify your income, differentiate your host brand, or simply share your local expertise with travelers, Airbnb Experiences is one of the most accessible new revenue channels available to property hosts today.
