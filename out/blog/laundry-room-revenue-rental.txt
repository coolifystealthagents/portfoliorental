24:T322a,<p>Shared laundry facilities are one of the most consistently profitable amenity investments a multifamily landlord can make. Tenants will pay for the convenience of on-site laundry—and unlike most property improvements, a laundry room generates income from the first week of operation with minimal ongoing management.</p>
<p>This guide covers everything from estimating your revenue potential to choosing the right equipment model, so you can decide whether adding laundry room revenue makes sense for your property.</p>
<h2 id="why-shared-laundry-still-makes-sense">Why Shared Laundry Still Makes Sense</h2>
<p>With in-unit laundry increasingly common, you might wonder if shared laundry is worth the investment. Here's why it often is:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>In-unit laundry isn't always feasible:</strong> Older buildings, small units, and buildings without sufficient plumbing or electrical capacity often can't support in-unit hookups</li>
<li><strong>Cost savings vs. in-unit:</strong> Shared laundry avoids the cost and maintenance of individual washers/dryers in every unit</li>
<li><strong>Revenue generation:</strong> Unlike in-unit laundry (which tenants use at no extra charge), shared laundry generates ongoing income</li>
<li><strong>Tenant amenity value:</strong> Properties with on-site laundry command higher rents than those without—you get the premium AND the laundry revenue</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="how-much-laundry-room-revenue-can-you-earn">How Much Laundry Room Revenue Can You Earn?</h2>
<p>Laundry revenue depends on the number of units, equipment cycles, and pricing:</p>
<p><strong>Revenue estimation formula:</strong></p>
<pre><code>Monthly Revenue = (# of machines) × (average cycles/day) × (days in month) × (price per cycle)
</code></pre>
<p><strong>Example: 12-unit apartment building</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>2 washers, 2 dryers</li>
<li>Average utilization: 3 cycles/machine/day (conservative)</li>
<li>Price: $2.50/wash, $2.00/dry</li>
<li>Washer revenue: 2 × 3 × 30 × $2.50 = $450/month</li>
<li>Dryer revenue: 2 × 3 × 30 × $2.00 = $360/month</li>
<li><strong>Gross monthly laundry revenue: $810</strong></li>
<li><strong>Annual gross: $9,720</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>Industry benchmarks suggest <strong>$15–$25/unit/month</strong> in laundry revenue for buildings with well-maintained equipment and competitive pricing. For a 12-unit building: $180–$300/month, or $2,160–$3,600/year.</p>
<p>Larger buildings with more tenants per machine yield lower per-machine utilization due to queuing constraints; smaller buildings with high occupancy often exceed the per-unit benchmarks.</p>
<h2 id="three-laundry-room-business-models">Three Laundry Room Business Models</h2>
<h3 id="model-1-coin-operated-traditional">Model 1: Coin-Operated (Traditional)</h3>
<p>The classic approach: coin-operated washers and dryers where tenants pay per use.</p>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Simple, reliable technology</li>
<li>No subscription or app required for tenants</li>
<li>Easy to manage</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Cash handling and theft risk</li>
<li>Regular coin collection required</li>
<li>Increasingly uncommon (tenants may not carry coins)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Older buildings with existing coin infrastructure; markets where tenants prefer cash payments</p>
<h3 id="model-2-smart-card-or-token-system">Model 2: Smart Card or Token System</h3>
<p>Proprietary card or token systems where tenants add value to a card and swipe to pay.</p>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Eliminates coin handling</li>
<li>Tracks usage data</li>
<li>Reduces theft</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Tenants must maintain card balance</li>
<li>Higher equipment cost than basic coin</li>
<li>Card management overhead</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-size multifamily (20–100 units) where a semi-centralized payment system makes sense</p>
<h3 id="model-3-app-based-payment-modern-standard">Model 3: App-Based Payment (Modern Standard)</h3>
<p>Machines equipped with QR codes or NFC readers connected to a mobile app (e.g., LaundryConnect, Laundrylux, Speed Queen Connect). Tenants pay via credit card or digital wallet.</p>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>No cash handling</li>
<li>Real-time usage monitoring and alerts</li>
<li>Revenue reporting and machine diagnostics remotely</li>
<li>Most appealing to modern renters</li>
<li>Can implement dynamic pricing (lower prices during off-peak hours)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Higher equipment cost</li>
<li>Technology-dependent (requires good WiFi in laundry room)</li>
<li>Less accessible for tenants without smartphones</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> New installations in Class B/A properties; tech-forward markets</p>
<h3 id="model-4-laundry-service-partnership">Model 4: Laundry Service Partnership</h3>
<p>Partner with a commercial laundry company that installs and maintains machines at no cost to you in exchange for a revenue share (typically 50–70% to the landlord).</p>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Zero capital investment</li>
<li>Zero equipment maintenance responsibility</li>
<li>Immediate income</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Lower revenue share vs. owning equipment outright</li>
<li>Less control over pricing and equipment quality</li>
<li>Typical contract terms: 5–10 years</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords who want laundry income without capital outlay; larger buildings (20+ units) that are attractive to laundry service companies</p>
<h2 id="equipment-costs-and-roi-calculation">Equipment Costs and ROI Calculation</h2>
<p>If you're purchasing equipment outright:</p>
<h3 id="equipment-cost">Equipment Cost</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Commercial washer (front-load):</strong> $1,200–$2,500 (new), $400–$1,200 (refurbished)</li>
<li><strong>Commercial dryer:</strong> $1,000–$2,000 (new), $350–$1,000 (refurbished)</li>
<li><strong>App payment hardware/software:</strong> $300–$800 per machine</li>
<li><strong>Plumbing and electrical installation:</strong> $2,000–$6,000 (varies by existing infrastructure)</li>
<li><strong>Laundry room construction/renovation (if needed):</strong> $5,000–$20,000</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Total typical investment (2 washers + 2 dryers, new equipment, existing space):</strong>
$8,000–$15,000</p>
<h3 id="roi-example">ROI Example</h3>
<p>Using the 12-unit example above:</p>
<ul>
<li>Total investment: $12,000</li>
<li>Annual revenue: $9,720</li>
<li>Annual maintenance/utilities (water, electricity, maintenance): $2,400</li>
<li><strong>Net annual laundry income: $7,320</strong></li>
<li><strong>Cash-on-cash return: 61%</strong></li>
<li><strong>Payback period: 1.6 years</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>Even at more conservative utilization (50% of the example):</p>
<ul>
<li>Net annual income: $3,060</li>
<li>Cash-on-cash return: 25.5%</li>
<li>Payback period: 3.9 years</li>
</ul>
<p>This is among the highest-returning capital improvements available in multifamily real estate.</p>
<p><strong>Property value impact:</strong> At a 6.5% cap rate, $7,320 in annual NOI from laundry adds <strong>$112,615 in property value</strong>—from a $12,000 investment.</p>
<p>For context on how this fits into your overall NOI strategy, see our guide on <a href="/blog/net-operating-income-rental">net operating income for rental properties</a>.</p>
<h2 id="setting-laundry-pricing">Setting Laundry Pricing</h2>
<p>Pricing has a significant impact on both revenue and tenant satisfaction:</p>
<p><strong>Current market rates (2026):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Washer: $2.25–$4.00/cycle (varies by market and machine size)</li>
<li>Dryer: $1.75–$3.50/cycle (or $0.25–$0.50 per 10 minutes)</li>
<li>Premium (large capacity): Add $0.50–$1.00 per cycle</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pricing strategy tips:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Research local laundromats to understand what the market supports</li>
<li>Price slightly below the nearest laundromat to emphasize on-site convenience</li>
<li>Consider a lower introductory rate to establish usage habits</li>
<li>Review and adjust pricing annually—most tenants accept modest increases</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="laundry-revenue-for-single-family-rentals">Laundry Revenue for Single-Family Rentals</h2>
<p>Can single-family landlords benefit from laundry revenue? Sometimes, yes:</p>
<ul>
<li>If the house has laundry hookups, offering the machines themselves (as included appliances) allows you to charge higher rent vs. requiring tenants to bring their own</li>
<li>Some landlords include coin-op laundry in single-family rentals, but this rarely pencils out—the revenue rarely justifies the equipment cost for one household</li>
<li>The better play for single-family: use <strong>washer/dryer inclusion</strong> as a rent justification rather than a coin-op income stream</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="maintaining-laundry-equipment-for-maximum-revenue">Maintaining Laundry Equipment for Maximum Revenue</h2>
<p>Equipment downtime is your biggest enemy. A broken washer for 2 weeks = lost revenue + frustrated tenants. Maintenance best practices:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Service contract:</strong> Commercial equipment companies offer annual service contracts ($200–$600/year) that cover regular maintenance and emergency repairs</li>
<li><strong>Regular lint trap cleaning:</strong> Weekly or bi-weekly to prevent fire hazards and maintain efficiency</li>
<li><strong>Ventilation maintenance:</strong> Clogged dryer vents are a fire risk; inspect quarterly</li>
<li><strong>Prompt repairs:</strong> Keep a relationship with a local appliance repair company for fast response</li>
<li><strong>Machine monitoring:</strong> App-based systems alert you to malfunctions in real time</li>
</ul>
<p>Budget 2–3% of equipment replacement cost annually for maintenance. Well-maintained commercial laundry equipment lasts 10–15 years.</p>
<h2 id="legal-and-tax-considerations">Legal and Tax Considerations</h2>
<p><strong>Lease clauses:</strong> If your lease charges separately for laundry, ensure your lease or building rules clearly describe the laundry policy—availability, pricing, rules, and landlord's right to change pricing.</p>
<p><strong>Utilities:</strong> Who pays the water and electricity for the laundry room? This is typically the landlord. Track these separately to calculate your true net income.</p>
<p><strong>Tax deductions:</strong> Laundry equipment is depreciable personal property (typically 5-year MACRS schedule). Under Section 179 and bonus depreciation provisions, you may be able to deduct the full equipment cost in Year 1. Consult our <a href="/blog/rental-property-depreciation-guide">rental property depreciation guide</a> for details.</p>
<p><strong>Revenue reporting:</strong> Laundry income is rental income for tax purposes. Report it on Schedule E as part of your rental property income.</p>
<h2 id="laundry-room-revenue-as-part-of-your-ancillary-income-strategy">Laundry Room Revenue as Part of Your Ancillary Income Strategy</h2>
<p>Laundry revenue is one component of a broader ancillary income strategy that can add 5–15% to your annual rental income. Other high-ROI ancillary income streams include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/blog/garage-rental-income">Garage and parking rental income</a></li>
<li><a href="/blog/storage-unit-rental-income">Storage unit rental income</a></li>
<li><a href="/blog/pet-rent-fees-landlord">Pet rent and fees</a></li>
<li>Vending machines (snacks, beverages) in common areas</li>
<li>Package lockers (growing demand post-COVID)</li>
</ul>
<p>Combined, these streams can meaningfully increase your <a href="/blog/net-operating-income-rental">net operating income</a> and property value without acquiring additional properties.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.coinlaundry.org/resources/research" rel="noopener" target="_blank">The Coin Laundry Association's industry research</a> provides detailed data on laundry revenue benchmarks and equipment ROI for various building sizes.</p>
<h2 id="summary">Summary</h2>
<p>Adding shared laundry to your rental property is one of the highest-ROI improvements available in multifamily real estate. The combination of immediate income generation, strong payback periods (often under 4 years), and meaningful property value increase makes it compelling for most buildings lacking existing laundry facilities.</p>
<p>For maximum return, choose modern app-based payment systems, price competitively with local laundromats, maintain equipment proactively, and track your laundry revenue separately to monitor performance.</p>
