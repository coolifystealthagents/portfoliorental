Adding Floor Plans to Rental Listings for More Interest

When prospective tenants browse rental listings, they're trying to answer one fundamental question: can I picture myself living here? Photos help, but they tell an incomplete story. A floor plan answers the spatial questions that photos never can — how do the rooms connect? Does the bedroom have privacy from the living area? Is there a logical place for my dining table?

Adding a floor plan to your rental listing is one of the most underutilized tactics available to landlords. NAR's home buyer research shows that listings with floor plans receive significantly more engagement than those without. Yet the majority of rental listings — especially for individual units and single-family homes — don't include one.

This guide explains why floor plans matter, how to create them efficiently, and how to use them effectively across your rental marketing channels.

Why Floor Plans Drive More Serious Inquiries

The case for including a floor plan in your rental listing isn't complicated. It boils down to one insight: the more confidently a tenant can picture themselves in your property, the more likely they are to inquire, schedule a showing, and ultimately apply.

Specific benefits of floor plan rental listings:

  • Reduces spatial uncertainty — Tenants can evaluate room sizes, layout flow, and furniture placement before visiting
  • Filters for better-fit tenants — A tenant who sees the layout and still inquires is far more likely to apply after the showing
  • Saves time for both parties — Fewer showings with people who realized the layout didn't work for them
  • Supports remote decision-making — Out-of-state and international tenants often make decisions without an in-person visit. HUD's renter resources help tenants searching remotely.
  • Differentiates your listing — Most competitors don't include floor plans, making yours stand out

When combined with professional photography and a compelling video walkthrough, a floor plan creates the most complete remote property experience available. This multi-asset approach is central to effective rental property marketing strategies.

What Makes a Good Rental Listing Floor Plan

Not all floor plans are equally useful. A blurry photo of a hand-drawn sketch doesn't inspire confidence. A clear, dimensioned, professionally presented floor plan does.

Elements of an effective rental floor plan:

  • Room labels — Each space clearly identified (Primary Bedroom, Kitchen, Living Room, etc.)
  • Dimensions — Room sizes in feet and/or square footage
  • Doorways and windows — Showing placement helps tenants plan furniture
  • North arrow — Helps tenants assess natural light and orientation
  • Total square footage — Prominently displayed
  • Unit number or address — Especially useful for multi-unit properties
  • Clean, readable design — Not hand-drawn; professional-looking and easy to interpret

Optional but valuable additions:

  • Furniture placement overlays (showing where a bed, sofa, and dining table fit naturally)
  • Compass direction overlay
  • Outdoor space dimensions (balcony, yard)
  • Storage locations (closets, pantry, utility room)

How to Create a Floor Plan for Your Rental Property

You don't need to hire an architect to create a usable floor plan. Several tools exist specifically for landlords and property managers.

Option 1: DIY Apps and Software (Free to Low Cost)

Floorplanner.com A browser-based tool that lets you drag and drop walls, doors, windows, and furniture. Produces clean, professional-looking floor plans in 2D and 3D. Free tier available with paid upgrades for HD exports.

RoomSketcher Similar to Floorplanner, RoomSketcher is intuitive and produces polished floor plans. Offers a 3D view that can be particularly compelling in listings. Subscription-based with a free tier for basic plans.

Magicplan (Mobile App) Uses your smartphone camera and AR to automatically measure and draft a floor plan as you walk through the space. Highly accurate and fast. Available on iOS and Android with a free tier for basic exports.

SmartDraw Desktop-based floor plan software with a rental property template. More technical than the above options but produces highly detailed results.

Option 2: Professional Floor Plan Services

If you prefer to outsource, several services specialize in creating floor plans from measurements or photos:

  • HomeByMe — Submit measurements and receive a rendered floor plan
  • BoxBrownie — Professional floor plan service used widely in real estate; typically $20-40 per floor plan
  • Local architects or interior designers — More expensive but capable of the highest quality output

Option 3: Measure and Sketch, Then Digitize

If you prefer a hands-on approach:

  1. Use a measuring tape or laser measure (a $25-50 investment that pays off quickly) to measure each room
  2. Sketch the layout on graph paper with measurements noted
  3. Enter the sketch into a digital tool like Floorplanner or RoomSketcher for a clean final product

This approach takes 60-90 minutes for a typical unit but produces a permanent asset you can reuse every time the property turns over.

Measuring Your Property for a Floor Plan

Accurate measurements are the foundation of a useful floor plan. Follow this process for consistent, reliable results:

Tools You'll Need

  • Laser measure (recommended) or tape measure
  • Notepad and pen
  • Smartphone for photos of each wall
  • Blank paper for sketching the rough layout

Measurement Process

  1. Start with a rough sketch — Walk through the property and draw the overall shape of each room, noting how they connect
  2. Measure each room — Measure length and width at the longest and widest points; note any alcoves, bump-outs, or irregular shapes
  3. Measure door and window locations — Note the distance from the nearest corner to each door and window opening, plus the opening width
  4. Measure ceiling height — Record in at least one room; note if there are vaulted or tray ceilings
  5. Photograph each wall — These reference photos help when you're inputting measurements later

Pro tip: For irregular-shaped rooms (L-shaped, rooms with closets that project into the space, etc.), break them into rectangles for easier measurement. Measure each rectangle separately.

How to Present Your Floor Plan in the Listing

Creating the floor plan is only half the job. Presenting it effectively in your listing is equally important.

As a Photo in Your Gallery

Upload the floor plan as one of the photos in your listing gallery — typically near the end, after the interior and exterior photos. Label it clearly ("Floor Plan — Unit 2B") so tenants know what they're looking at.

As a Standalone Feature (Where Supported)

Some platforms — including Zillow, Apartments.com, and Realtor.com — have dedicated floor plan fields separate from the photo gallery. Always use these when available, as they're often displayed more prominently and appear in relevant filtered searches.

In Virtual Tour Integration

If you've created a 3D virtual tour using Matterport or a similar tool, most platforms generate an automatic floor plan "dollhouse view" from the scan data. This is arguably the most compelling presentation format — interactive, accurate, and generated automatically. See our guide on 3D virtual tours for rental properties for details.

In Listing Descriptions

Reference the floor plan in your description text to draw tenants' attention to it:

"The open floor plan connects the living and dining areas seamlessly — see the attached floor plan for dimensions and room layout."

Floor Plans and Virtual Tours: A Powerful Combination

Floor plans and virtual tours serve complementary purposes and are most powerful when used together:

  • Floor plan — Communicates spatial logic, room flow, and dimensions in a static, easy-to-reference format
  • Video walkthrough — Conveys the emotional experience of moving through the space
  • 3D virtual tour — Allows the tenant to explore the property interactively on their own terms

A tenant who can view a floor plan, watch a video walkthrough, and explore a 3D tour before ever contacting you is essentially a pre-qualified showing. They've seen the property in detail and still want to inquire — that's a serious lead.

Floor Plans for Different Property Types

Studios and Junior 1-Bedrooms

These are the hardest sell in rental listings because of their compact size. A floor plan that shows creative furniture placement — a Murphy bed, a dedicated workspace, built-in storage — can transform a tenant's perception from "that's too small" to "I can see how this works."

Multi-Bedroom Apartments and Houses

Tenants in multi-bedroom properties care deeply about bedroom privacy, bathroom access, and whether the primary bedroom has its own ensuite. A floor plan answers these questions immediately and filters for tenants whose needs align with your layout.

Multi-Unit Buildings

For property managers with multiple units per building, floor plans help tenants compare options (e.g., 2nd floor corner unit vs. 4th floor center unit) without requiring individual showings of each. Provide floor plans for each unit type and note which specific units are available.

Luxury and High-End Rentals

Luxury tenants expect floor plans. Omitting them signals a lower level of professionalism. For luxury listings, consider commissioning a rendered floor plan with high-end presentation — compass orientation, room labels in elegant typography, building orientation overlaid.

The ROI of Adding Floor Plans

Let's put the numbers in perspective. If your unit rents for $1,800/month and a floor plan reduces your vacancy by just one week, that's $450 in saved vacancy cost. A professional floor plan service charges $20-40. A DIY floor plan costs you 60-90 minutes of time.

The math is clear. Forbes explains vacancy cost impact on rental property ROI. The floor plan pays for itself in the first tenant inquiry it accelerates.

For additional strategies to reduce vacancy and fill your units faster, see our guides on reducing vacancy rate for rental properties and reducing days on market.

Conclusion

Floor plans are one of the most underused and highest-impact tools in rental property marketing. They reduce tenant uncertainty, filter for serious applicants, support remote decision-making, and differentiate your listing in a crowded marketplace — all for a minimal time and money investment.

Whether you create a floor plan yourself using a free app or outsource it to a professional service, adding one to your listing is a straightforward upgrade that can meaningfully improve your inquiry rate and reduce time to lease.
