Breaking Into the Corporate Housing Market

Corporate housing is one of the most lucrative — and least understood — segments of the rental market. While traditional landlords compete on price in a crowded unfurnished market, corporate housing operators often achieve 1.5–2.5x the equivalent unfurnished monthly rent by serving a market that values quality, convenience, and flexibility over everything else.

Breaking into the corporate housing market requires a specific type of property preparation, a distinct approach to marketing, and a management model calibrated to the needs of business tenants. But for landlords with the right assets and market position, the financial rewards are substantial.

What Is Corporate Housing?

Corporate housing occupies the space between hotels and traditional long-term rentals. It offers:

  • Fully furnished units: Everything from furniture and linens to kitchenware and small appliances is included.
  • Flexible lease terms: Stays typically run 30 days to 12 months, far shorter than traditional 12-month leases but longer than hotel or short-term rental stays.
  • All utilities included: In most corporate housing arrangements, rent includes electricity, water, gas, high-speed internet, and cable.
  • Move-in ready condition: Business clients (and the companies paying for their housing) expect hotel-like quality on arrival.

The primary tenant pool includes:

  • Corporate relocatees whose permanent housing is not yet ready
  • Project-based workers deployed for 2–9 month engagements (consultants, construction project managers, IT specialists)
  • Traveling healthcare professionals (travel nurses, locum physicians, allied health workers)
  • Insurance displacement clients (families temporarily displaced by home damage)
  • Government and military personnel on temporary duty assignments. GSA per diem rates set government housing budgets.
  • International business executives and expats on assignment

The Financial Case: Why Corporate Housing Commands Premium Rates

The revenue premium in corporate housing is not accidental — it reflects genuine value delivered to the client:

  • Convenience: A fully furnished, move-in-ready unit is worth significant time savings to a relocating employee or traveling professional.
  • Predictable cost: A single all-inclusive monthly rate simplifies budgeting for corporate HR and finance departments.
  • Flexibility: Companies pay a premium for the ability to extend or shorten stays as project timelines shift.

Revenue illustration: A 2-bedroom apartment that rents for $2,200/month unfurnished might achieve $3,500–$4,500/month as corporate housing — a premium of $1,300–$2,300/month. Against furnishing costs of $8,000–$15,000 (which depreciate over years of use), the payback period is short.

However, costs are also higher: utilities, internet, cable, furnishing maintenance, more frequent cleaning, higher management intensity, and furnishing replacement. Investopedia covers rental ROI calculations in depth. Model these carefully before committing. Is Your Property Suited for Corporate Housing?

Not every property is a good fit for the corporate housing market. Strong candidates share these characteristics:

Location

Corporate housing demand concentrates near:

  • Major corporate campuses (tech companies, headquarters, regional offices)
  • Hospitals and medical centers (the travel nurse and locum physician market is massive and growing)
  • Universities and research institutions (visiting faculty, research fellows, medical residents)
  • Government facilities and military bases (TDY housing demand is consistent and often institutionally funded)
  • Major construction or infrastructure projects (project-based workers need medium-term housing)
  • Business districts (relocating executives often need temporary housing near downtown offices)

A property within 15–20 minutes of one or more of these demand generators has natural corporate housing potential.

Property Characteristics

Ideal corporate housing properties feature:

  • Private entry (business clients value privacy)
  • Secure parking (often included in the package)
  • In-unit washer/dryer (essential — a shared laundry is a dealbreaker for many corporate clients)
  • Home office setup or dedicated workspace
  • Quality kitchen (business clients often cook and entertain)
  • Bedroom separation for multi-person occupancy

Studio and one-bedroom units work for solo travelers. Two- and three-bedroom units serve couples, families, or multi-person teams and command higher per-night equivalents.

Furnishing Your Corporate Housing Unit

The furnishing standard is the most important differentiator between a successful corporate housing property and one that fails to attract premium clients.

Furniture

Invest in real furniture — not cheap flat-pack units. Corporate clients expect quality that signals professionalism:

  • Living room: Sofa and loveseat or sectional (performance fabric, easy to clean), coffee table, side tables, lamps, TV (65"+ for primary living areas)
  • Dining area: Table and 4–6 chairs
  • Bedroom(s): Quality bed frames, nightstands, lamps, dresser or chest of drawers
  • Mattresses: This is not the place to cut corners. A quality mattress (memory foam or innerspring hybrid) signals care and generates positive reviews.
  • Home office: Desk, ergonomic chair, proper task lighting

Kitchen

Supply the kitchen as if you were moving in yourself:

  • Full cookware set (pots, pans, baking sheets)
  • Dinnerware and glassware for 4–6 persons
  • Full utensil set
  • Small appliances: coffee maker, toaster, microwave, blender
  • Adequate pantry staples at move-in (oil, salt, pepper, coffee, tea)

Linens and Towels

Provide hotel-quality linens:

  • Two sets of sheets per bed (allows for laundry turnover)
  • Quality pillows and blankets
  • Two sets of bath towels, hand towels, and washcloths per bathroom

Technology

  • Gigabit fiber internet is a non-negotiable for corporate tenants who work from home or need reliable connectivity for video calls. The FCC broadband speed guide helps determine adequate service.
  • Smart TV with streaming app access
  • Smart lock for self-check-in (essential for efficient operation)

Total furnishing budgets for a well-done 2-bedroom corporate unit typically run $10,000–$20,000 for quality that will last several years.

Building Your Corporate Housing Client Network

Unlike traditional rentals — where you list on Zillow and Apartments.com and wait for applicants — corporate housing often requires proactive relationship-building with the organizations that generate tenant demand.

Direct Corporate Client Outreach

Target the HR, relocation, and facilities management departments of major employers near your property:

  • Email or call directly: introduce your property, provide a one-page brochure with photos, pricing, and availability.
  • Offer corporate accounts with consistent pricing, easy booking, and reliable service standards.
  • Ask to be included in their preferred housing vendor list.

Companies that regularly bring in employees for projects or relocate staff frequently need a reliable housing partner — and will return repeatedly if you deliver.

Relocation Management Companies (RMCs)

Relocation management companies coordinate housing for corporate relocatees on behalf of employer clients. Becoming an approved vendor with one or more RMCs can generate consistent, institutionally backed bookings:

  • Research RMCs operating in your market (Cartus, BGRS, Aires, and Graebel are among the largest).
  • Apply to be a preferred housing provider.
  • Understand their booking and billing processes, which typically involve corporate direct billing.

Travel Nurse and Healthcare Staffing Agencies

The travel healthcare market is one of the most reliable demand sources for corporate housing. Travel nurse agencies — which place registered nurses on 13-week contracts at hospitals across the country — are always sourcing housing for their workers.

  • Contact staffing agencies directly (AMN Healthcare, Travel Nurse Across America, Cross Country Healthcare, and many others) about housing for their placed workers.
  • Hospitals often have housing boards or coordinators who maintain a vetted list of short-term housing options.

Online Corporate Housing Platforms

List your property on platforms specifically serving the corporate and extended-stay market:

  • Furnished Finder (dominant in the travel nurse and corporate market)
  • Corporate Housing by Owner (CHBO)
  • Oakwood (if you qualify for their network)
  • Airbnb for Business (the extended-stay version of the Airbnb platform)
  • Blueground

Managing Corporate Housing Effectively

Corporate housing requires a different operational model than traditional long-term rentals:

Turnover management: Higher turnover frequency means cleaning and inspection between each guest. Professional cleaning services are essential — budget $100–$250 per turnover depending on unit size.

Maintenance responsiveness: Business clients have zero tolerance for unresolved maintenance issues. A broken appliance or internet outage in a traditional rental is an inconvenience; in corporate housing, it is a professional crisis. Commit to 2-hour response and same-day resolution for critical issues.

Inventory management: Track furnishing condition and replace worn or damaged items proactively. Fraying towels, worn mattresses, and scratched furniture signal declining quality to discerning business clients.

Communication: Corporate clients and the companies booking on their behalf expect professional communication — quick responses, clear invoices, formal documentation.

Pricing strategy: Corporate housing is typically priced per day (with a monthly total), allowing you to flex rates for shorter stays (which should command higher daily rates) versus longer engagements.

Corporate Housing and Your Broader Portfolio Strategy

Corporate housing works best as part of a deliberate portfolio strategy. Properties in corporate markets that can be repositioned as furnished executive rentals can significantly increase portfolio income — but require more active management than a standard unfurnished portfolio.

Many landlords operate a hybrid model: some properties as traditional long-term rentals for stable baseline income, others as corporate housing for income maximization. This approach balances revenue upside with operational demands.

For guidance on building a portfolio strategy that incorporates corporate housing, see our build rental property portfolio guide and our overview of best rental property types.

Conclusion

The corporate housing market offers landlords a compelling path to premium rents and creditworthy institutional clients — but it requires deliberate preparation, proactive marketing, and an operational commitment to quality and responsiveness that exceeds what traditional rentals demand.

The landlords who succeed in this niche treat it as a hospitality business as much as a real estate business. They invest in furnishings, build corporate relationships, and deliver consistently on the professional standard their clients expect. The financial rewards — 50–150% above comparable unfurnished rents — justify the investment for those willing to make the commitment.
