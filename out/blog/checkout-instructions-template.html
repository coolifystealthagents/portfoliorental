Checkout Instructions Template for STR Guests

Checkout is the last chapter of every guest's stay — and for hosts, it sets up the next one. Clear, friendly checkout instructions accomplish two things simultaneously: they give guests a positive final experience that contributes to a strong review, and they ensure the property is ready for an efficient turnover.

Too many hosts either skip checkout instructions entirely (leaving guests guessing), or write them in a tone that reads more like a legal warning than a farewell. This guide gives you copy-paste checkout instruction templates for every delivery format, along with best practices for keeping checkout smooth and stress-free.

Why Checkout Instructions Matter

Well-designed checkout instructions directly impact:

Guest experience: Guests who know exactly what to do at checkout feel calm and confident. Those who don't feel anxious about whether they've done enough — or worse, do nothing and feel guilty.

Review scores: Checkout instructions contribute to the "communication" and "value" review categories. NerdWallet explains rental hosting costs and returns. Clear instructions signal that you're a thoughtful, professional host.

Turnover efficiency: When guests follow consistent checkout procedures, your cleaning team can work faster and more efficiently between bookings.

Damage documentation: A standard checkout procedure (photos of key areas before departure) creates a baseline for any damage claims.

For the full operational framework, see our short-term rental management guide.

The Checkout Instructions Philosophy

Before writing your checkout instructions, decide on your philosophy:

Option A: Minimal checkout tasks You charge a higher cleaning fee and ask guests to do very little — just remove trash and leave. This maximizes the guest experience and reviews but requires more cleaner time and cost.

Option B: Moderate checkout tasks Guests handle a handful of tasks (start dishwasher, strip beds, take out trash) that meaningfully reduce the cleaner's workload. Most common approach.

Option C: Extensive checkout tasks Guests handle significant cleaning tasks in exchange for a lower or no cleaning fee. Works with budget travelers but can feel onerous and affect reviews.

For most STR hosts, Option B strikes the right balance. The tasks are simple and reasonable, guests generally comply without complaint, and the cleaner can work faster.

Template 1: In-Property Checkout Card (Print and Display)

This is a laminated card or framed print to display visibly in the property — ideal on the kitchen counter, by the front door, or on the back of the door.

Checkout is at [TIME] on [Date]

Thank you so much for staying with us! Before you head out:

5 Easy Steps:

  1. Dishes — Load and start the dishwasher (or wash and dry if no dishwasher)
  2. Trash — Empty all trash bins into the large can [location — e.g., "by the garage"] and replace liners
  3. Towels — Leave used towels in the bathtub or shower
  4. Beds — Strip the sheets and leave them on the mattress (or leave beds as-is — your preference)
  5. Lock up — Close and lock all windows and doors; leave the key [on the counter / in the lockbox]

Please take all personal items — we can't be responsible for items left behind.

Wi-Fi, streaming services, and all other access will deactivate at checkout time.

Safe travels! We'd love to host you again. 🏡

— [Your Name / Property Name]

Print this card on cardstock, laminate it, and display it prominently. Update the checkout time and key return instructions for your property.

Template 2: Pre-Checkout Message (Night Before)

Send this via Airbnb messaging (or your PMS) the evening before checkout.

Hi [Guest Name],

Hope you've had a wonderful time at [Property Name]! Just a quick reminder that checkout is tomorrow by [TIME].

To make sure everything is set: ✓ Load and start the dishwasher ✓ Leave used towels in the shower/bathtub ✓ Take out any trash ✓ Lock the door and leave the key [location]

That's it — the rest is on us!

Safe travels to wherever you're headed next. It's been a pleasure hosting you.

[Your Name]

Why the night before? Guests who read checkout instructions the morning of checkout often feel rushed. A night-before reminder gives them time to feel prepared and is less likely to feel like a demand.

Template 3: Morning-of Checkout Reminder

For guests who haven't messaged back or for stays where you want an extra touchpoint:

Good morning, [Guest Name]!

Just a quick note — checkout is today by [TIME]. The instructions are on the kitchen counter if you need them.

I hope you had a fantastic stay. If you have a moment, a review means the world to us. I'll be leaving you a great one as well!

Have a safe trip home.

[Your Name]

Template 4: Welcome Book Checkout Section

Include a dedicated checkout section in your welcome book:

Checkout Instructions

Checkout is by [TIME] on your departure day.

We've tried to keep this simple — just a few steps:

Before you leave:

  • Run the dishwasher (pods are under the sink)
  • Leave used towels in the shower or bathtub
  • Strip the beds and leave linens on the mattress
  • Take trash to the outdoor bin [location description]
  • Check all rooms for personal belongings
  • Lock all windows and exterior doors
  • Return the key to [location]

Optional but appreciated:

  • Start the washing machine if you've used beach/pool towels
  • Wipe down kitchen counters after your last use

If something was damaged or broken during your stay: Please let us know before you leave — we always appreciate honesty and handle these situations fairly.

Forgot something? Email us at [address] — we hold found items for 30 days and can ship them back to you.

Template 5: Digital Checkout Instructions (for Touch Stay / Hostfully / Lodgify)

Digital welcome books allow you to include rich formatting. Structure your digital checkout section this way:

Header: Checkout Day — Everything You Need to Know

Section 1: Checkout Time Your checkout time is [TIME]. Late checkout is available upon request and subject to availability — just message us the day before and we'll do our best.

Section 2: Your Checkout Checklist [Interactive checklist with checkboxes]

  • Dishwasher loaded and running
  • Towels left in bath/shower
  • Beds stripped (linens left on mattress)
  • Trash in outdoor bin
  • Personal items collected
  • Windows and doors locked
  • Key returned to [location]

Section 3: What Happens Next Our cleaning team arrives after you leave to get everything sparkling for the next guests. This is why your checkout time matters — thank you for helping us keep everything on schedule!

Section 4: Before You Go

  • Did we earn a 5-star review? Leave one at [link]
  • Book your next stay: [direct booking link or Airbnb listing]
  • Follow us for special offers: [social media or email signup]

What Not to Include in Checkout Instructions

Just as important as what you include is what you don't.

Avoid:

  • Long lists of rules that read like consequences ("failure to comply will result in...")
  • Excessive tasks (mopping floors, cleaning the oven, doing all the laundry)
  • Confusing or location-ambiguous instructions ("put the key where you found it" — tell them exactly where)
  • Multiple contradictory checkout times across different documents
  • Tiny font or hard-to-read formatting

Keep it under 8-10 bullet points. Anything longer starts feeling onerous and affects how guests experience their departure.

Handling Late Checkouts

Late checkout requests are common and handling them graciously (when possible) generates goodwill.

If same-day turnover permits a late checkout:

"Absolutely! As long as our next guests aren't checking in until [time], you're welcome to stay until [late time]. Happy to make that work for you."

If same-day turnover doesn't allow it:

"I wish I could — we actually have guests arriving today and the cleaning team needs the full window. I hope you understand! If you need to store luggage while you explore, [nearby hotel / luggage storage option] is a great option."

Checkout Key Return Options

Choose the method that fits your property and security setup:

Option Best For Notes
Smart lock (self-latching) Most properties Guest simply leaves; lock self-secures
Key in lockbox Properties with physical keys Guest returns key, you can confirm remotely
Key on kitchen counter Low-security needs Easy for guest, less secure
Key with neighbor or keyholder Rural properties Requires neighbor coordination

Recommendation: Smart locks are the gold standard — no key handoffs, automatic security, and you receive an alert when the guest has left. The FTC covers smart home device security best practices. See damage prevention for more on smart lock setup.

Post-Checkout Workflow for Hosts

Once your guests check out, your job isn't done:

  1. Confirm checkout — check your smart lock log or send a quick message to confirm departure
  2. Notify your cleaner — trigger the turnover (many PMS tools do this automatically via Airbnb automation tools)
  3. Review cleaner photos — confirm property condition before releasing the security deposit
  4. Leave a guest review — do this within 24-48 hours while the stay is fresh
  5. Send a review request message — a warm, brief post-checkout message inviting them to review

For the complete turnover checklist your cleaning team should use, see our vacation rental turnover checklist.

Conclusion

Checkout instructions are a small but meaningful part of the guest experience. They signal that you're organized, thoughtful, and respectful of the guest's time — and they set your cleaning team up for success.

Use the templates in this guide to create checkout instructions that are warm, clear, and appropriately brief. Display them prominently in the property, send a reminder the night before, and follow up with a gracious review request the morning of checkout.

Together, these habits create a professional, memorable final impression that contributes directly to the five-star reviews that drive your STR's long-term success. For the complete guide to managing your short-term rental, visit our short-term rental management guide.
