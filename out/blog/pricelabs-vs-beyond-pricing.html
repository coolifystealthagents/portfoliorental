If you manage a short-term rental and still set prices manually, you are almost certainly leaving money on the table. Dynamic pricing software analyzes market supply and demand in real time and automatically adjusts your nightly rates — filling gaps in your calendar at competitive prices while capturing premium revenue during peak demand.

Two platforms dominate this space: PriceLabs and Beyond Pricing (now rebranded simply as Beyond). Both are trusted by tens of thousands of hosts worldwide, but they take meaningfully different approaches to automation, customization, and pricing philosophy. This deep-dive comparison will help you choose the right tool for your portfolio.

Why Dynamic Pricing Matters for STR Hosts

Static pricing — picking a flat rate and sticking with it — worked when Airbnb's marketplace was less crowded. Today, your competitors are already using algorithms to undercut you on slow nights and maximize revenue on sold-out weekends. Without dynamic pricing you are either turning away guests with rates that are too high, or losing revenue with rates that are too low.

According to data published by AirDNA market research, hosts who switch to data-driven dynamic pricing typically see revenue increases of 10–40% in their first year, depending on market conditions and how aggressively they were under-pricing before.

A solid short-term rental pricing strategy combines dynamic software with intelligent manual overrides — and understanding which tool enables that workflow is exactly what this article covers.

PriceLabs: Overview

PriceLabs was founded in 2014 and has grown into one of the most customizable dynamic pricing platforms available. It connects directly with Airbnb, Vrbo, Booking.com, and more than 100 property management systems (PMS).

How PriceLabs Works

PriceLabs uses a base-price model. You set a base price that reflects your property's normal value, and the algorithm applies percentage-based adjustments above or below that price depending on:

  • Local market demand and occupancy trends
  • Day of week and seasonal patterns
  • Lead time (how far in advance a booking is made)
  • Your own historical booking data
  • Competitor listing rates in your area

The platform's Market Dashboard gives you a live view of local market occupancy and pricing, letting you benchmark your performance against comparable listings.

PriceLabs Key Features

  • Hyper-local demand data pulled from millions of active listings
  • Customization rules engine — set minimum prices, maximum prices, orphan-day logic, last-minute discounts, and far-future premiums
  • Neighborhood data so you can see how your submarket differs from the broader city
  • Portfolio analytics with revenue, occupancy, and RevPAR tracking
  • Multi-calendar view for hosts managing several properties
  • Automations including auto-apply and scheduled syncs with your PMS

PriceLabs Pricing

PriceLabs charges a flat monthly fee per listing rather than a percentage of revenue. As of 2026, fees start around $19.99/month for a single listing and decrease on a per-unit basis as your portfolio grows. For large operators this model becomes significantly cheaper than percentage-based competitors.

Beyond Pricing: Overview

Beyond (formerly Beyond Pricing) launched in 2013 with a mission to bring hotel revenue management principles to the short-term rental world. It has since evolved into a broader revenue management platform and is particularly popular among professional property managers.

How Beyond Works

Beyond uses what it calls a Smart Rates algorithm. Rather than asking you to set a base price, Beyond analyzes your market and recommends a starting point automatically. It then applies dynamic adjustments based on:

  • Real-time supply and demand signals
  • Forward-looking booking pace in your market
  • Seasonal trends and local events
  • Your listing's historical conversion rate
  • Cancellation patterns across your market

Beyond's pricing engine is intentionally less hands-on — it is designed to be set-and-forget, making it attractive for hosts who want automation without deep configuration.

Beyond Key Features

  • Signal market intelligence dashboard with forward-looking demand data
  • Automated minimum stays that adjust based on demand
  • Revenue estimates before you even list a new property
  • Insights reporting with comp-set benchmarking
  • Direct booking support and website tools for property managers
  • Integrations with Airbnb, Vrbo, Guesty, Hostaway, and dozens of other PMS platforms

Beyond Pricing Model

Beyond charges a percentage of revenue — typically around 1% of booking revenue. For a host generating $60,000/year, that equals roughly $600/year. For high-revenue portfolios this model becomes more expensive than PriceLabs's flat fee.

PriceLabs vs Beyond Pricing: Head-to-Head Comparison

Customization and Control

PriceLabs wins here. Its rules engine is the most granular on the market. You can define custom date ranges, override specific nights, set dynamic minimum stays, and create complex logic around orphan days (single open nights between bookings that are hard to fill). If you enjoy dialing in your pricing strategy, PriceLabs gives you the toolbox.

Beyond is cleaner and simpler. You get fewer levers but a faster setup. For hosts who find PriceLabs overwhelming, Beyond's guided approach removes decision fatigue.

Data Quality and Market Intelligence

Both platforms source data from major OTAs and listing sites, but they present it differently. PriceLabs's Market Dashboard provides granular neighborhood-level occupancy data and is widely praised for its transparency. Beyond's Signal dashboard emphasizes forward-looking booking pace, which can be valuable for anticipating demand shifts before they show up in occupancy numbers.

For advanced data, both integrate with AirDNA's datasets — though AirDNA sells its own analytics product separately if you want the deepest possible market insight.

Ease of Use

Beyond wins for simplicity. Onboarding takes minutes, the interface is clean, and you can have your listings priced within an hour. PriceLabs has improved its UI considerably, but its depth of options means a steeper learning curve — especially for hosts new to revenue management.

Integration and PMS Compatibility

Both platforms integrate with all major OTAs and PMS tools. PriceLabs edges ahead with 100+ native integrations; Beyond is close behind and adds value through its own property management tools for professional operators.

Which Tool Is Right for You?

Choose PriceLabs If:

  • You want maximum control over pricing rules
  • You manage 3+ listings and want to minimize software costs
  • You enjoy data analysis and want to understand your market deeply
  • You use a PMS and need broad integration support
  • You want to implement sophisticated orphan-day and minimum-stay logic

Choose Beyond If:

  • You manage 1–2 listings and want a simple set-and-forget solution
  • You are new to dynamic pricing and want guided automation
  • You prefer percentage-based pricing tied to your revenue performance
  • You value clean UX and fast onboarding over deep customization
  • You want integrated direct booking tools within one platform

Tips for Getting the Most from Either Platform

Regardless of which tool you choose, dynamic pricing software works best when paired with strong listing fundamentals. A poorly photographed listing with a weak title will convert badly even at the perfect price point.

Read our guide to Airbnb listing optimization to ensure your listing quality supports the revenue gains that dynamic pricing can unlock.

Also review your short-term rental management guide — pricing is just one lever in a broader revenue management system that includes reviews, response rates, and operational efficiency.

Other tactics to layer on top of dynamic pricing:

  • Set a firm price floor — Investopedia explains break-even to help set your minimum
  • Block or adjust far-future dates — demand data for dates 9–12 months out is thin; consider manual overrides
  • Monitor weekly during the first 60 days — both platforms have a learning curve and early calibration matters
  • Use minimum stay rules strategically — read our minimum stay strategy guide for detail
  • Plan for last-minute inventory — both tools handle last-minute discounts, but you should understand your own threshold

Can You Use Both?

Technically, yes — but it creates sync conflicts and is not recommended. Each platform writes prices to your calendar, and having two tools compete for control leads to unpredictable results. Pick one, commit to it for 60–90 days, track your RevPAR (revenue per available room), and then evaluate.

If you decide to switch platforms, export your booking history first. PriceLabs can use historical data to calibrate its base price more accurately.

Summary

Both PriceLabs and Beyond Pricing are excellent dynamic pricing tools that will outperform manual rate-setting for nearly every host. The decision comes down to your priorities:

  • PriceLabs is the power user's choice — maximum control, best value at scale, steeper learning curve.
  • Beyond is the streamlined choice — fast setup, clean UX, slightly higher cost as a percentage of revenue.

For most hosts managing two or more listings who want to build a professional STR operation, PriceLabs offers the combination of data depth and cost efficiency that makes it the preferred tool. For brand-new hosts who want to get pricing automated quickly without complexity, Beyond is a strong starting point.

Either way, enabling dynamic pricing is one of the highest-ROI decisions per SBA small business guidance. Combine it with an optimized listing, smart minimum-stay rules, and a solid short-term rental management guide framework, and you will be well ahead of the average host in your market.
