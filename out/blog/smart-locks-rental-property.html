Smart locks have become one of the most practical smart home technology investments a rental property owner can make. They eliminate the expense and inconvenience of rekeying between tenants, enable remote access management, simplify entry for vendors and contractors, and are a premium amenity that many tenants now expect — particularly in the short-term rental market.

This guide covers the best smart lock options for rental properties, what features matter most for landlord use cases, installation considerations, and the legal and privacy issues landlords need to understand before deploying smart locks.

Why Smart Locks Make Sense for Rental Properties

Eliminate Rekeying Costs

Traditional lock rekeying costs $50-$150 per lock per tenant turnover. For a portfolio of 10 properties, that is $500-$1,500 per turnover cycle. Smart locks eliminate this cost entirely — a code change takes 30 seconds and can be done remotely.

Remote Access Management

Smart locks allow you to:

  • Grant access to contractors and vendors without being present. See building a vendor network for tips
  • Revoke access immediately when a vendor's work is complete
  • Provide showing access to prospective tenants during vacancy
  • Monitor entry logs to confirm when vendors accessed the property

Tenant Convenience

No lost key issues. Tenants can enter with a code, phone, or fob — and many tenants, particularly younger renters, prefer keyless entry. Some landlords charge a small premium for units with smart lock access.

Short-Term Rental Management

For short-term or vacation rentals, smart locks are nearly essential — they allow automated code generation for guests, eliminate the need for key handoffs, and integrate with platforms like Airbnb and VRBO through property management software.

Key Features to Evaluate

When selecting smart locks for rental properties, prioritize:

Access Methods

Most smart locks offer multiple access methods:

  • Keypad code — Most reliable for rental use; no phone or app needed
  • Mobile app — Convenient but requires smartphone and battery
  • Physical key backup — Essential for rental applications; always ensure a physical key backup exists
  • Key fob — Good for multi-family common areas

For rental properties, a keypad with backup physical key is the most reliable and practical configuration.

Power Source and Battery Life

Smart locks run on batteries (typically AA or specialized lithium). Battery life varies from 3 months to 12+ months depending on use. Choose locks with:

  • 12-month battery life or longer
  • Low battery warning (in-app notification or beep)
  • Emergency backup power option (9V battery contact for keypad models)

Schlage Encode and Yale Assure consistently receive strong reviews for battery life in high-use rental scenarios.

Remote Management Capability

For landlords, remote access management is the most valuable feature:

  • Lock/unlock remotely via app
  • Generate and revoke entry codes remotely
  • View entry logs with timestamps
  • Receive notifications when the lock is used

This requires either a Wi-Fi enabled lock or a Z-Wave/Zigbee lock paired with a compatible smart home hub.

Integration with Property Management Software

For multi-unit portfolios, choose locks that integrate with your property management platform:

  • Schlage Encode — Integrates with many PM platforms including Buildium and AppFolio
  • Yale Access — Works with August system; integrates with various platforms
  • Kwikset Halo — Wi-Fi enabled; compatible with several PM software platforms

Top Smart Lock Recommendations for Rental Properties

Best Overall: Schlage Encode Plus

The Schlage Encode Plus combines robust construction (Schlage is known for commercial-grade durability), built-in Wi-Fi (no hub required), and excellent app functionality. Features include:

  • Up to 100 access codes
  • Access scheduling (codes can be valid only during certain hours)
  • Apple Home Key support
  • ANSI Grade 1 security rating (highest for residential locks)
  • Strong battery life (6+ months in typical rental use)

Best for: Single-family homes and apartment doors where durability and direct Wi-Fi connectivity are priorities.

Price: $250-$300 retail.

Best for Multi-Family: Schlage B60N + Z-Wave Module

For multi-family properties using a Zwave hub or property management system, the Schlage commercial deadbolt with a Z-Wave module offers:

  • Extremely durable construction for high-use applications
  • Integration with SmartThings, Hubitat, and commercial property management systems
  • ANSI Grade 1 deadbolt strength

Best Budget Option: Kwikset SmartCode 916

A well-regarded budget option for landlords who want basic smart keypad functionality without the premium price:

  • Z-Wave integration available
  • Easy code management
  • Physical key backup
  • 8-month battery life

Price: $100-$150 retail.

Best for Short-Term Rentals: August WiFi Smart Lock

The August WiFi Smart Lock is widely integrated with Airbnb, VRBO, and short-term rental property management platforms:

  • Integrates directly with major STR platforms for automated guest code generation
  • Installs on existing deadbolt (does not replace exterior hardware — good for tenant-facing aesthetics)
  • DoorSense feature confirms door is closed and locked

Price: $200-$250 retail.

Best for Multi-Family Common Areas: Latch M, R, or C Series

Latch products are designed specifically for multi-family residential applications:

  • Designed for heavy commercial use in apartment buildings
  • Full property management platform with detailed access logs
  • Supports multiple access methods (app, code, card)
  • Audit trail for every entry

Price: Commercial/enterprise pricing; contact Latch for volume pricing.

Installation Considerations

Deadbolt Standards

Most smart locks replace the thumb turn on a standard single-cylinder deadbolt. They work with standard door prep (2-1/8" hole for lock body). Verify your door prep before ordering.

Door Thickness and Handedness

  • Standard door thickness: 1-3/8" to 1-3/4"; confirm your door is within range
  • Most locks are adjustable for both left- and right-handed doors
  • Verify backset (distance from door edge to center of hole): typically 2-3/8" or 2-3/4"

Professional vs DIY Installation

Smart lock installation is typically DIY-friendly:

  • Most installs complete in 20-30 minutes with a screwdriver
  • Basic door alignment and strike plate adjustment may be needed
  • For commercial or high-security applications, professional installation ensures proper alignment and security

Existing Lock Compatibility

Some smart locks (like August) install over existing deadbolts, leaving exterior hardware unchanged. Others replace the entire lock assembly. The replacement approach provides the most complete smart functionality; the overlay approach is faster to install and removes no existing hardware.

Managing Codes for Rental Properties

Code Security Best Practices

  • Change all codes at every tenant turnover — Even if a prior tenant was cooperative, security requires a full code reset
  • Use unique codes for each access user — Never share a master code with multiple people; create individual codes for each contractor, cleaner, and vendor
  • Set code expiration — Many smart locks allow time-limited codes; use this feature for one-time contractor access
  • Review access logs — Periodically review entry logs for unexpected access patterns

Master Code Security

Some landlords maintain a permanent master code for emergency access. This code should:

  • Be different from the tenant's code
  • Be known only to the property owner and designated property manager
  • Be changed if there is any possibility of compromise

Long-Term Tenants

Provide tenants with one or two permanent codes for household members. Tenants should be able to add additional codes (for family members, dog walkers, etc.) within a set limit. Clarify this in your lease addendum.

Legal and Privacy Considerations

Disclosure

The FTC's privacy guidance requires transparency. Always disclose that the property uses smart lock technology. Tenants have a reasonable expectation of privacy, and undisclosed monitoring technology — including access logs — can raise legal issues.

Entry Logs and Privacy

Smart locks generate entry logs. Use these logs only for legitimate property management purposes (security, access management, investigating reported incidents). Accessing entry logs for non-legitimate purposes (monitoring tenant's daily schedule without justification) raises serious privacy concerns.

Tenant Rights

The HUD tenant rights page confirms tenants have the right to control access to their unit. Ensure that:

  • Tenants can add their own codes within agreed limits
  • The landlord does not use smart lock access to enter without proper notice. Review landlord right of entry rules for your state
  • The lease clearly addresses smart lock terms

Smart Locks and Lease Provisions

Your lease or a technology addendum should address:

  • That the property uses smart lock technology
  • How many access codes the tenant may create
  • That all codes must be changed at lease termination
  • That the landlord retains a master access code for emergency and authorized entry
  • Privacy terms for access log use

Conclusion

Smart locks are one of the most cost-effective technology investments in rental property management. For landlords with multiple units, the elimination of rekeying costs alone can generate a positive ROI within 2-3 tenant turnovers per lock. Combined with remote access management and integration with property management software, smart locks streamline operations significantly.
