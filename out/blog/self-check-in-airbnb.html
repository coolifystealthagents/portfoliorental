Today's travelers expect flexibility. Many guests arrive late at night, change their plans last minute, or simply prefer not to coordinate with a host for arrival. Self check-in for Airbnb properties addresses all of these expectations — and it benefits hosts just as much as guests.

According to NerdWallet's hosting tips, self check-in eliminates the need to be present for every arrival. Guests can arrive on their own schedule, enter securely, and start enjoying their stay immediately. Your response to the question "Do you offer self check-in?" can be the deciding factor for many bookings.

This guide covers everything you need to know: the best self check-in methods, how to set them up, what to include in your check-in instructions, and how to use self check-in to improve your listing performance.

Why Self Check-In Matters for Airbnb Hosts

It Is What Guests Expect

Airbnb data consistently shows that self check-in is among the most searched amenities on the platform. Guests filtering by this feature are actively excluding properties that require host-assisted check-in. Without self check-in, you are invisible to a significant portion of the booking market.

It Enables Remote Management

If you are not local to your property, or if you manage multiple properties, being physically present for every arrival is not realistic. Self check-in is the foundation of a scalable, remotely manageable STR operation.

It Improves Your Check-In Rating

Airbnb's check-in sub-rating reflects how smoothly guests were able to access the property. Hosts with well-designed self check-in systems consistently receive higher scores in this category.

It Supports Flexible Arrival Times

Standard check-in windows create friction. With self check-in, guests can arrive at any time — early morning, late night, or during a gap between cleanings — as long as the property is ready. This is particularly valuable for business travelers.

Self Check-In Methods: Comparing Your Options

There are three primary self check-in methods available to Airbnb hosts. Each has trade-offs in cost, security, and guest experience.

Option 1: Smart Lock (Best Experience)

A smart lock replaces or augments your existing door lock and allows you to create and delete unique access codes remotely. Each guest gets a code that works only during their reservation window.

How it works:

  • Guest receives a unique access code in their pre-arrival message
  • Code activates at check-in time and expires automatically at checkout
  • You receive notifications when the door is unlocked
  • No physical key exchange required

Popular smart lock brands for Airbnb:

  • August Smart Lock — Fits over most existing deadbolts; works with Airbnb's smart lock integration
  • Schlage Encode — Standalone unit; built-in WiFi without a hub requirement
  • Yale Assure Lock — Reliable, widely compatible, integrates with many PMS platforms
  • Nuki Smart Lock — Popular in European markets
  • Igloohome — Works offline; codes generated algorithmically, no WiFi required at the door

Pros: Best guest experience, maximum security, remote management, integration with property management software Cons: Upfront cost ($100–$300+), requires installation, may need professional setup

Airbnb Integration: Airbnb has partnered with select smart lock manufacturers to allow direct code generation through the Airbnb app. When connected, Airbnb automatically sends unique codes to guests without host action. Check the Airbnb Help Center for a current list of compatible devices.

Option 2: Lockbox (Most Affordable)

A lockbox is a small secure box mounted near your door that holds physical keys. Guests access it by entering a combination you provide in advance.

How it works:

  • You set a combination (usually 4–6 digits) before each stay
  • Guests enter the code, retrieve the key, and let themselves in
  • After checkout, guests return the key and you reset the code

Types of lockboxes:

  • Wall-mounted combination lockboxes — Fixed to the wall beside your door or on a gate post
  • Portable/shackle lockboxes — Loop around a door handle or railing (less secure)
  • Electronic keypad lockboxes — No combination dial; guests enter a code on a keypad

Pros: Low cost ($20–$60), no WiFi or technology required, simple setup Cons: Physical key can be copied, less secure than smart locks, requires resetting between guests, no activity notifications

Option 3: Keypad Lock (Middle Ground)

A keypad lock is a door lock with a built-in numerical keypad. Unlike smart locks, most keypad locks are not WiFi-connected — they require physical programming to change codes. Some models, however, do support remote programming via Bluetooth or WiFi.

How it works:

  • You program a unique code for each guest directly on the lock or through an app
  • Guest enters their code to unlock the door
  • Code is deleted after checkout

Pros: More secure than a lockbox, no physical key, moderate cost ($80–$200) Cons: Some models require physical access to reprogram, no remote monitoring unless WiFi-enabled

How to Set Up Self Check-In on Airbnb

Step 1: Choose and Install Your Access Method

Select the method that best fits your budget and technical comfort level. For most hosts, a smart lock delivers the best return on investment when factoring in time savings and guest satisfaction.

If you are installing a smart lock yourself, most models come with step-by-step instructions. For locks requiring drilling or wiring, consider professional installation.

Step 2: Enable Self Check-In on Your Airbnb Listing

Once your access system is in place:

  1. Go to your Airbnb listing settings
  2. Navigate to Amenities
  3. Select Self check-in as an available feature
  4. Choose your check-in method (smart lock, lockbox, keypad, or building staff)

This adds the self check-in badge to your listing and makes it searchable in the self check-in filter.

Step 3: Write Clear Check-In Instructions

Airbnb has a dedicated Check-in Instructions section in your listing. Use it to provide step-by-step directions. Keep it detailed but scannable:

Example Check-In Instructions:

  1. Park in the driveway or on the street in front of the house
  2. Walk to the front door — it has a black keypad lock
  3. Enter your unique code: [provided 48 hours before arrival]
  4. Push the door handle down while turning the knob to open
  5. Your code works from [check-in time] to [checkout time]
  6. Find your welcome packet on the kitchen counter

Step 4: Automate Your Pre-Arrival Messaging

Do not rely on guests to read check-in instructions in the listing. Use your property management system or Airbnb's scheduled messages to send:

  • Booking confirmation message: Acknowledge the booking, set expectations for future communication
  • 48-hour pre-arrival message: Send the specific access code, parking details, WiFi password, and a link to your house rules
  • Day-of-arrival message: A short welcome message with your contact number for any issues

If you want to automate this process, see our guide on airbnb automation tools.

What to Include in Self Check-In Instructions

Great check-in instructions anticipate every question a guest might have on arrival. Include:

Access Information

  • Exact address and any distinguishing property features (color, unit number)
  • Specific access code and where to enter it
  • Which door to use (front, side, garage)
  • Whether the code is the same for all entry points or different for each

Parking Directions

  • Where to park (driveway, street, dedicated spot)
  • Any parking restrictions (street cleaning days, permit zones)

Property Orientation

  • Where to find the WiFi password
  • Location of towels, extra linens, and toiletries
  • How to operate the thermostat, TV, and any smart home devices
  • Location of trash and recycling bins

Emergency Information

  • Your phone number (or co-host/property manager contact)
  • Nearest hospital or urgent care
  • Utility shutoffs (water, gas, electricity) — especially important for longer stays

House Rules Summary

  • A brief reminder of key rules: no parties, quiet hours, pet policy
  • Your full house rules are covered separately — link here for reference

Smart Lock Best Practices for Airbnb Hosts

Generate Unique Codes Per Guest

Never reuse codes between guests. Always assign a fresh, unique code for each reservation. Most property management systems can automate this if integrated with a compatible smart lock.

Time-Restricted Access

Program codes to activate no more than 30 minutes before check-in and expire 30 minutes after checkout. This prevents guests from accessing the property during the cleaning window between reservations.

Backup Access Plan

The CPSC's home safety tips remind hosts that even smart locks fail. Always have a backup plan:

  • A lockbox with a master key hidden on the property
  • A spare key with a trusted neighbor or co-host
  • 24/7 contact number guests can call if they are locked out

Regular Battery Replacement

Smart locks run on batteries. Low batteries cause lock malfunctions that strand guests — a quick way to earn a bad review. Check battery levels monthly and replace proactively (not when the low battery warning appears).

Monitor Entry Logs

Most smart locks provide an activity log. Review it after each checkout to confirm the guest departed. If unexpected entries appear during non-reservation periods, investigate promptly.

Self Check-In and Airbnb's Check-In Sub-Rating

Airbnb asks guests to rate their check-in experience specifically. This sub-rating contributes to your overall score and appears on your listing profile.

To maximize your check-in sub-rating:

  • Ensure your instructions are accurate and easy to follow (test them yourself)
  • Send access codes in advance — never make guests ask for them
  • Make sure the lock or lockbox is working perfectly before every arrival
  • Have a rapid response plan for access failures

Hosts who nail self check-in consistently report higher overall ratings because the first experience in the property sets the emotional tone for the entire stay.

Self Check-In for Multi-Unit and Apartment Properties

If your property is in a multi-unit building, self check-in has additional complexity:

Building Access: You may need a solution for both the building entrance and the unit door. Some smart lock systems integrate with building intercom or access control systems.

HOA and Building Rules: The FTC's HOA guidance notes many buildings restrict smart lock installations. Check your lease or HOA rules before installing any hardware.

Elevator and Common Areas: Make sure your check-in instructions cover the full path from the street to the unit — including which elevator to use and what floor.

Integrating Self Check-In with Your Overall Operation

Self check-in is most powerful when integrated into a full operational system:

  • Cleaning notifications: Your cleaner should receive automatic notifications when a guest checks out, triggered by the smart lock or your PMS
  • Maintenance alerts: Use smart lock activity logs to confirm cleaners have accessed the property between stays
  • Welcome home automation: Smart home integrations can turn on lights, set the thermostat to a welcome temperature, and play music when a guest's code is entered

For hosts building a fully automated operation, our airbnb property management tips guide covers these integrations in detail.

Conclusion

Self check-in is one of the most impactful upgrades you can make to your Airbnb operation. It eliminates friction for guests, enables remote property management, improves your listing's amenity profile, and supports a consistently high check-in rating.

Start with a smart lock if your budget allows — it offers the best combination of security, convenience, and automation capability. Pair it with clear, proactive communication, and your guests will arrive ready for a great stay before they even open the door.

Self Check-In Setup Checklist:

  • Choose your access method: smart lock, lockbox, or keypad
  • Install and test the system before your next booking
  • Enable self check-in in your Airbnb listing amenities
  • Write detailed step-by-step check-in instructions in your listing
  • Automate your 48-hour pre-arrival message with the access code
  • Create a backup access plan for technology failures
  • Monitor battery levels and access logs regularly
