A well-crafted welcome book is one of the most valuable tools in a vacation rental host's arsenal — and one of the most overlooked. While some hosts leave nothing at all, and others leave a single laminated sheet, the best hosts create a thoughtful, complete guest guide that makes every stay feel effortless and personal.

This guide walks you through everything you need to know to create a welcome book for your vacation rental: what to include, how to organize it, how to make it look great, and how to keep it updated over time.

What Is a Vacation Rental Welcome Book?

A welcome book (also called a house manual, guest guide, or welcome binder) is a physical or digital document left at your property that gives guests everything they need to enjoy their stay. It covers:

  • How the property works (appliances, systems, quirks)
  • House rules and important policies
  • Check-in and checkout procedures
  • Local recommendations (restaurants, activities, transportation)
  • Emergency contacts and nearby services

A great welcome book reduces guest questions by 50-70% — meaning fewer messages during their stay, fewer negative reviews about confusion, and more time for guests to actually enjoy themselves. It's a cornerstone of excellent guest communication for short-term rentals.

Physical vs. Digital Welcome Books: Which Is Better?

Both formats have their place, and many hosts use both.

Physical Welcome Book

Pros:

  • Available immediately when guests arrive (no phone needed)
  • Works during internet outages
  • Feels warm and personal
  • Guests actually read it

Cons:

  • Can get damaged, lost, or defaced
  • Requires manual updates when info changes
  • Can look low-quality if not done well

Best for: Primary living spaces, older guest demographics, properties in areas with unreliable cell service

Digital Welcome Book

Pros:

  • Easy to update instantly
  • Can include clickable links, maps, and videos
  • Can be shared before arrival via message
  • No physical maintenance required

Cons:

  • Requires guest to have their phone handy
  • Less tactile/personal feeling
  • Tech issues can make it inaccessible

Best for: Tech-forward guests, urban properties, portfolio hosts managing many properties

Tools for digital welcome books: Hostfully, Touch Stay, Lodgify, and Notion (for budget-conscious hosts)

Recommendation: Create a digital version first, then print a condensed physical version for in-property use.

The 8 Essential Sections of a Vacation Rental Welcome Book

Section 1: Welcome Message

Start with a warm, personal greeting. This sets the tone for the entire guest experience.

Template:

Welcome to [Property Name]!

We're so glad you're here. This home has been a labor of love for us, and we genuinely hope it becomes your favorite place to stay in [City/Area].

This guide has everything you need — from how to work the coffee maker to where to find the best tacos in town. Read through it when you get a moment, but don't stress about memorizing anything. We're just a text away if you need us.

Enjoy every minute.

— [Your Name(s)]

Include your contact information here and reiterate that guests should reach out if anything is unclear.

Section 2: Property Essentials

This is the "how things work" section — the practical information guests need first.

Must include:

  • Wi-Fi: Network name and password (make this large and easy to find)
  • Parking: Exact instructions with a photo or diagram if needed
  • Check-in/Checkout times and procedure
  • Heating and cooling: How to use the thermostat, acceptable temperature range
  • Washer/dryer: Controls, detergent location, any special instructions
  • Trash and recycling: Bin locations, pickup schedule, sorting rules
  • TV and streaming: How to access streaming apps, smart TV instructions
  • Smart home devices: How to use any Alexa/Google devices, smart locks, etc.

For each item, add a one-sentence description of any quirks: "The back right burner on the stove runs a bit hot — adjust accordingly."

Section 3: Kitchen Guide

Guests often struggle with unfamiliar appliances. A brief kitchen guide prevents both frustration and appliance misuse.

Cover:

  • Coffee maker (type, where pods/grounds are, how to clean)
  • Oven and stove (any quirks or digital controls)
  • Dishwasher (settings, detergent pods location)
  • Garbage disposal (if applicable — note if there isn't one)
  • Location of: utensils, pots, cutting boards, spices, cleaning supplies
  • What's available to cook with vs. what should not be used

Cross-reference this with your amenities checklist to make sure everything listed is actually stocked.

Section 4: House Rules

Be clear and specific, but not punitive in tone. State rules as information, not warnings.

Common rules to include:

  • No-smoking policy (inside and potentially on property) -- see EPA indoor air quality guidance
  • Pet policy (allowed/not allowed, any fees)
  • Guest limits (maximum number of overnight guests)
  • Noise/quiet hours (especially important for multi-unit buildings or neighborhoods)
  • Parking rules (number of vehicles, where to park)
  • Pool/hot tub rules (if applicable)
  • Checkout expectations (dishes, trash, towels)
  • Event policy (parties allowed or not)

For your full checkout expectations, see our checkout instructions template — you can summarize the key points in the welcome book and reference the full instructions on a separate card.

Section 5: Local Area Guide

This is where your welcome book goes from useful to unforgettable. Guests want the insider knowledge that only a local can provide — not Google results, but real recommendations.

Organize by category:

Restaurants & Cafes List 5-10 favorites with a sentence about each:

  • [Restaurant Name] — Best breakfast burritos in the city; get there before 9am or expect a wait. 0.3 miles away.
  • [Restaurant Name] — A hidden gem for date night; make a reservation. Great wine list.

Activities & Attractions

  • [Park/Trail] — Easy 3-mile loop with a waterfall at the end; parking fills up by 9am on weekends
  • [Museum/Gallery] — Free on Thursday evenings; skip the main exhibits and go straight to [specific wing]

Practical Information

  • Nearest grocery store (with hours)
  • Nearest pharmacy
  • Gas stations
  • Urgent care clinic
  • Hospital (with address)
  • ATM locations
  • Public transit options

Getting Around

  • Ride-share availability in the area
  • Bike rental locations
  • Parking tips for downtown/popular areas

This section is worth the investment of time. Guests who follow your local recommendations consistently mention them in reviews. For a more detailed approach, see our local area guide for vacation rental guests.

Section 6: Emergency Information

Place this section clearly — guests should be able to find it fast in a stressful moment.

Include:

  • Your phone number (and backup contact if you're unavailable)
  • Local emergency number (911 in the US)
  • Non-emergency police line
  • Nearest urgent care clinic and hospital
  • Poison control center (1-800-222-1222)
  • Gas company (for suspected leaks)
  • Electrician emergency contact
  • Property management contact (if different from you)
  • Location of: fire extinguisher, first aid kit, circuit breaker, water shutoff valve. The CPSC home safety checklist covers essential safety items.

Consider placing the emergency section as a laminated card on the fridge in addition to the welcome book.

Section 7: Checkout Instructions

Keep this section brief in the welcome book — a friendly reminder of what's expected.

Template:

Checkout by [Time]

To help us prepare for the next guests:

  • Leave used towels in the bathtub or shower
  • Strip the beds and leave linens on the mattress
  • Load and start the dishwasher
  • Take out any trash and recyclables
  • Return furniture to its original position
  • Lock all doors and windows before leaving
  • Leave the key [in lockbox / on kitchen counter / other]

That's it! Thank you for being a wonderful guest.

Section 8: FAQ Page

Anticipate the top 10 questions you get from guests and answer them here.

Common FAQs:

  • "Where is the [nearest] grocery store?" — [Answer]
  • "Can I check in early / check out late?" — [Answer]
  • "The [appliance] isn't working — what do I do?" — [Contact info + basic troubleshooting]
  • "Where can I find extra [towels/blankets/pillows]?" — [Location]
  • "Is the neighborhood safe to walk at night?" — [Honest answer]
  • "Where should I watch the sunset?" — [Local insider tip]

Designing Your Physical Welcome Book

Presentation matters. A professional-looking welcome book signals that you take hosting seriously.

Options by budget:

  • Budget: Print at home in color, put in a clear binder with tabbed dividers
  • Mid-range: Design in Canva, print at a local print shop, bind at Staples
  • Premium: Hire a designer on Fiverr or use a service like Blurb to create a professionally printed booklet

Design tips:

  • Use your property's color palette
  • Include photos of the property, nearby attractions, and recommended restaurants
  • Use icons to make sections scannable
  • Keep fonts readable (minimum 12pt for body text)
  • Include a table of contents

Keeping Your Welcome Book Updated

An outdated welcome book is worse than none — it sends guests to closed restaurants and gives them wrong codes. Set a calendar reminder to review and update your welcome book:

  • After every third booking: Check that all codes, passwords, and appliance instructions are current
  • Seasonally: Update local recommendations (hours change, restaurants close)
  • After any property changes: New appliances, updated checkout procedures, new rules

Conclusion

A great welcome book is an investment that pays dividends in reduced guest questions, higher review scores, and a reputation for exceptional hospitality. The hosts who take the time to create a thoughtful, personalized guest guide consistently outperform those who don't — on every metric that matters.

Start with the eight sections in this guide, add your personal voice and local knowledge, and update it regularly. Combined with proactive guest communication templates and a well-stocked property, your welcome book becomes a signature element of a guest experience worth raving about.
