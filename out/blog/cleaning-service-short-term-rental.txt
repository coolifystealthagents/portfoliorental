28:T35ae,<p>Cleanliness is the single most cited factor in short-term rental reviews — for better or worse. A sparkling clean property gets mentioned in five-star reviews. A missed toilet or dusty ceiling fan shows up in three-star ones. For STR hosts, finding a reliable, professional cleaning service isn't optional — it's foundational.</p>
<p>This guide walks you through the complete process of finding, vetting, hiring, and managing a <strong>cleaning service for your short-term rental</strong>: from knowing what to look for in a cleaner to setting up a system that runs smoothly whether you're on-site or across the country.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="why-str-cleaning-is-different-from-regular-house-cleaning">Why STR Cleaning Is Different from Regular House Cleaning</h2>
<p>Cleaning a vacation rental between guests is fundamentally different from weekly house cleaning. Understanding those differences helps you find the right service.</p>
<p><strong>Key differences:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Turnover speed</strong> — cleaners often have 2-4 hour windows between checkout and check-in</li>
<li><strong>Hotel-level standards</strong> — guests expect pristine conditions, not just "tidied up"</li>
<li><strong>Restocking duties</strong> — cleaners often also restock consumables (toilet paper, shampoo, coffee)</li>
<li><strong>Linen management</strong> — stripping, laundering, and remaking beds is standard</li>
<li><strong>Inspection role</strong> — a great STR cleaner also checks for damage and missing items</li>
<li><strong>Variable timing</strong> — stays vary in length and guest count, affecting workload each time</li>
</ul>
<p>Regular residential cleaners may not be equipped for this — which is why finding cleaners experienced in <strong>STR or vacation rental turnover</strong> is so important.</p>
<p>For the full framework of managing your STR operations, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="where-to-find-str-cleaning-services">Where to Find STR Cleaning Services</h2>
<h3 id="1-str-specific-platforms">1. STR-Specific Platforms</h3>
<p>Several platforms specialize in connecting hosts with vacation rental cleaning services:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>TurnoverBnB</strong> — purpose-built marketplace connecting hosts with vetted STR cleaners; handles scheduling, payment, and syncs with your calendar</li>
<li><strong>Properly</strong> — photo-based task management and cleaner coordination; strong for portfolio hosts</li>
<li><strong>Handy for Pros</strong> — general cleaning platform with STR experience in major markets</li>
<li><strong>TaskRabbit</strong> — flexible for individual cleaners, though STR experience varies</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="2-local-referrals">2. Local Referrals</h3>
<p>Ask in local STR host communities:</p>
<ul>
<li>Airbnb host Facebook groups for your city</li>
<li>Nextdoor neighborhood groups</li>
<li>Local real estate investor meetups</li>
<li>Your property manager (if you have one) — they often have preferred cleaner contacts</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="3-cleaning-company-directories">3. Cleaning Company Directories</h3>
<p>Search for local companies with vacation rental experience:</p>
<ul>
<li>Google: "[Your City] vacation rental cleaning service"</li>
<li>Yelp reviews filtered by commercial cleaning</li>
<li>HomeAdvisor and Thumbtack for vetted local services</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="4-direct-outreach">4. Direct Outreach</h3>
<p>If there's a hospitality professional in your area whose work you admire — a hotel housekeeper, a resort staffer — they may be available for freelance STR cleaning on their days off.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-to-look-for-in-an-str-cleaner">What to Look for in an STR Cleaner</h2>
<p>Not all cleaners are equal. Here are the qualities that separate a great STR cleaner from an average one:</p>
<p><strong>Non-negotiables:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Experience with vacation rental or hotel turnover</li>
<li>Reliable transportation and punctuality</li>
<li>Strong attention to detail (test with a trial clean)</li>
<li>Willingness to follow a checklist</li>
<li>Ability to communicate about issues (damage, missing items, low supplies)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Strong positives:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Brings their own supplies and equipment</li>
<li>Comfortable with smart lock access</li>
<li>Has backup support if they're sick or unavailable</li>
<li>Can handle laundry on-site or coordinates linen service</li>
<li>Available on short notice for last-minute bookings</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="interview-questions-for-prospective-cleaners">Interview Questions for Prospective Cleaners</h2>
<p>Before hiring anyone, conduct a brief phone or in-person interview. Key questions:</p>
<ol>
<li>"Do you have experience cleaning Airbnb or vacation rental properties specifically?"</li>
<li>"How do you handle a situation where you find damage or a missing item?"</li>
<li>"What is your process if you're sick and can't make a scheduled turnover?"</li>
<li>"Do you bring your own supplies, or do we provide them?"</li>
<li>"Are you comfortable using a checklist and sending photo confirmation when done?"</li>
<li>"What is your pricing structure — by the hour, or flat per turnover?"</li>
<li>"How do you handle linen changes — do you wash on-site, or use a linen service?"</li>
<li>"Are you available for same-day bookings or last-minute scheduling changes?"</li>
</ol>
<p>A cleaner who has clear, confident answers to all of these is far more likely to perform consistently.</p>

<h2 id="setting-up-your-cleaning-system">Setting Up Your Cleaning System</h2>
<h3 id="create-a-master-turnover-checklist">Create a Master Turnover Checklist</h3>
<p>Your cleaner should follow a detailed checklist for every turnover. This removes ambiguity and ensures consistency regardless of who cleans. See our <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-turnover-checklist">vacation rental turnover checklist</a> for a complete room-by-room template you can adapt.</p>
<p><strong>At minimum, the checklist should cover:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Bathroom deep clean (toilet, shower, sink, mirror, floor)</li>
<li>Kitchen clean (appliances, counters, inside microwave, stovetop)</li>
<li>Bedroom strip, launder, and remake (military-tight or hotel-style)</li>
<li>Vacuuming/mopping all floors</li>
<li>Dusting surfaces, fans, baseboards</li>
<li>Wiping down door handles, light switches, remotes. The <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/when-and-how-to-clean-surfaces.html">CDC recommends disinfecting</a> high-touch surfaces regularly.</li>
<li>Taking out all trash</li>
<li>Restocking consumables to defined minimums</li>
<li>Checking for guest left items and damage</li>
<li>Locking up and reporting status</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="set-up-photo-documentation">Set Up Photo Documentation</h3>
<p>Require your cleaner to send timestamped photos of key areas after each clean. This serves two purposes:</p>
<ol>
<li>It confirms the property is ready for the next guest</li>
<li>It creates a record showing condition before each new guest checks in (useful for damage claims)</li>
</ol>
<p>Apps like Properly and TurnoverBnB have built-in photo workflows. For independent cleaners, a simple WhatsApp or text photo will do.</p>
<h3 id="sync-scheduling-with-your-booking-calendar">Sync Scheduling with Your Booking Calendar</h3>
<p>Manual scheduling is a recipe for missed cleans. Use a tool that syncs your cleaner's schedule with your booking calendar automatically. Options:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>TurnoverBnB</strong> — syncs directly with Airbnb, VRBO, and most major OTAs</li>
<li><strong>Your PMS calendar</strong> — if you use Hostaway, Guesty, or similar, set up cleaner access</li>
<li><strong>Google Calendar sharing</strong> — low-tech option for single-property hosts with one cleaner</li>
</ul>
<p>For broader automation tools that can integrate with cleaning scheduling, see our <a href="/blog/airbnb-automation-tools">Airbnb automation tools guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="managing-linen-in-unit-vs-linen-service">Managing Linen: In-Unit vs. Linen Service</h2>
<p>One of the biggest operational decisions for STR cleaning is how to handle linens.</p>
<h3 id="option-a-in-unit-laundry">Option A: In-Unit Laundry</h3>
<p>The cleaner strips and washes linens on-site using the property's washer/dryer.</p>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong> Lower cost, no logistics
<strong>Cons:</strong> Adds 1-2 hours to turnover time, limits back-to-back same-day bookings</p>
<h3 id="option-b-linen-service">Option B: Linen Service</h3>
<p>A commercial laundry service collects used linens and delivers fresh ones, hotel-style.</p>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong> Faster turnovers, professional results, backup supply available
<strong>Cons:</strong> Additional cost ($20-60 per set), requires coordination and logistics</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Hosts with frequent bookings, same-day turnovers, or large properties with many beds</p>
<h3 id="option-c-par-level-system">Option C: Par Level System</h3>
<p>Stock enough linens for 2-3 complete sets per bed. Cleaners use clean sets from storage, send used ones to the laundry, and the cycle rotates.</p>
<p>This is the most professional and scalable approach for growing STR portfolios.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="handling-issues-when-a-clean-doesn-t-meet-standards">Handling Issues: When a Clean Doesn't Meet Standards</h2>
<p>Even great cleaners have off days. Build a feedback system that makes it easy to address quality issues without damaging the relationship.</p>
<p><strong>Best practices:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Review photos before approving payment when possible</li>
<li>Communicate issues within 24 hours of the clean</li>
<li>Be specific ("the stovetop burners weren't wiped down") not general ("the kitchen wasn't clean")</li>
<li>Have the cleaner return to fix major issues rather than letting them slide</li>
<li>Track patterns — occasional misses are human; recurring issues signal a systemic problem</li>
</ul>
<p>Document recurring issues and set clear expectations about what constitutes an unacceptable clean. After two or three unaddressed quality issues, it's time to find a backup.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="building-your-cleaner-relationship-for-long-term-success">Building Your Cleaner Relationship for Long-Term Success</h2>
<p>Your cleaner is one of the most important people in your STR business. Treat the relationship accordingly.</p>
<p><strong>Invest in the relationship:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Pay promptly and fairly (tip for excellent work or especially difficult cleans)</li>
<li>Give advance notice of schedule changes whenever possible</li>
<li>Communicate guest counts so they know what to expect</li>
<li>Ask for their input — experienced STR cleaners often notice issues before anyone else</li>
<li>Send a thank-you or bonus during busy seasons</li>
</ul>
<p>A great, reliable cleaner who knows your property is worth far more than constantly searching for someone cheaper. The <a href="https://www.sba.gov/business-guide/manage-your-business/hire-manage-employees">SBA offers hiring guidance</a> for small business contractors.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Finding the right cleaning service for your short-term rental is one of the highest-impact operational decisions you'll make. Cleanliness drives reviews, reviews drive bookings, and bookings drive revenue. The investment in finding a reliable, experienced STR cleaner — and building a system that supports them — pays off immediately in guest satisfaction and long-term in your property's rating.</p>
<p>Start with a referral from your local STR community, conduct a thorough interview, run a test clean with your checklist, and build from there. Combined with a solid <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-turnover-checklist">vacation rental turnover checklist</a> and smart <a href="/blog/airbnb-automation-tools">Airbnb automation</a>, your cleaning operation can run like clockwork.</p>
<p>For the complete guide to running a successful short-term rental, visit our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
