28:T37cd,<p>Pet-friendly vacation rentals represent one of the most underserved and high-demand niches in the short-term rental market. According to <a href="https://www.americanpetproducts.org/pubs_survey.asp">APPA industry research</a>, nearly 70% of US households own at least one pet — and those pet owners face significant challenges finding quality accommodation that welcomes their animals.</p>
<p>The result: pet-friendly properties earn premium nightly rates (typically 10-25% more), command higher occupancy, and build intensely loyal repeat guest bases. Guests traveling with pets are often willing to pay more and book further in advance to secure a confirmed pet-friendly property.</p>
<p>This complete guide covers how to make your <strong>vacation rental pet-friendly</strong> in a way that maximizes revenue while protecting your property.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="should-you-accept-pets-the-business-case">Should You Accept Pets? The Business Case</h2>
<p>Many hosts default to "no pets" out of concern about damage and allergies. It's worth examining whether that default is leaving significant money on the table.</p>
<p><strong>The case for accepting pets:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Higher nightly rates:</strong> Pet-friendly listings command a measurable premium in most markets</li>
<li><strong>Reduced vacancy:</strong> Pet owners often struggle to find options, filling your calendar during slower periods</li>
<li><strong>Guest loyalty:</strong> Pet owners who find a host who genuinely welcomes their animals return again and again</li>
<li><strong>Competitive differentiation:</strong> In markets where most hosts don't accept pets, being pet-friendly immediately stands out</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>The case for caution:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Pets do cause more wear and tear (primarily: pet hair, odors, scratches)</li>
<li>Some guests have pet allergies (your property can't be marketed to both pet-allergic and pet-friendly guests effectively)</li>
<li>Noise (barking) can create neighbor issues in certain property types</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>General recommendation:</strong> Most hosts with single-family homes or vacation properties in non-attached settings benefit significantly from going pet-friendly. Hosts in attached buildings (apartments, condos) face more constraints.</p>
<p>For the complete framework on optimizing your STR, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="setting-up-your-pet-policy">Setting Up Your Pet Policy</h2>
<p>A clear, fair pet policy protects your property and sets appropriate expectations with guests.</p>
<h3 id="key-policy-decisions">Key Policy Decisions</h3>
<p><strong>Pet types:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Dogs only (most common)</li>
<li>Cats allowed</li>
<li>Small pets only (under 25 lbs is a common threshold)</li>
<li>No weight limit</li>
<li>Limited to 1-2 pets</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pet fee structure:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Per-stay fee:</strong> Most common; typically $25-75 per stay for 1-2 pets</li>
<li><strong>Per-night fee:</strong> Better for longer stays; $10-20/night</li>
<li><strong>Per-pet fee:</strong> Useful when allowing multiple pets</li>
<li><strong>Refundable pet deposit:</strong> Less common but option for higher-end properties</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Pet-specific rules:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Pets must not be left unattended at the property (or define conditions under which they can be)</li>
<li>Pets must not be on the furniture (or explicitly state which furniture is OK)</li>
<li>Pet owners must clean up after their pets outdoors</li>
<li>Pets must be crated at night if they have a history of destructive behavior</li>
<li>Any pet damage must be disclosed at checkout</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="sample-pet-policy-language-for-your-listing">Sample Pet Policy Language (for Your Listing)</h3>
<blockquote>
<p>"We love dogs! We welcome up to [2] dogs of any size for a per-stay pet fee of $[X]. We ask that:</p>
<ul>
<li>Pets not be left unattended inside the property for more than [4] hours</li>
<li>Pets not be allowed on upholstered furniture (we provide a washable pet blanket)</li>
<li>All outdoor pet waste be cleaned up and disposed of properly</li>
<li>Any pet damage be disclosed at checkout</li>
</ul>
<p>We keep a pet kit stocked with food/water bowls, waste bags, and a pet towel for post-walk paw cleaning. We want this to be a great stay for the whole family!"</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h2 id="protecting-your-property-from-pet-damage">Protecting Your Property from Pet Damage</h2>
<p>Going pet-friendly doesn't mean accepting unlimited damage. Smart property management significantly limits the risk.</p>
<h3 id="flooring">Flooring</h3>
<p>Carpet and pets are a problematic combination. Pet-friendly properties should prioritize:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Luxury vinyl plank (LVP):</strong> Scratch-resistant, waterproof, easy to clean — the gold standard for pet-friendly STRs</li>
<li><strong>Tile:</strong> Durable but cold; pair with washable area rugs</li>
<li><strong>Hardwood:</strong> Use a matte or satin finish that hides scratches better; avoid glossy finishes</li>
</ul>
<p>For area rugs, choose washable options (RUGGABLE is popular for exactly this use case) in patterns that don't show hair and minor staining.</p>
<p>See our <a href="/blog/damage-prevention-short-term-rental">damage prevention guide</a> for comprehensive property protection strategies.</p>
<h3 id="furniture">Furniture</h3>
<ul>
<li>Use performance fabrics (Crypton, Sunbrella) that resist pet hair, scratches, and odors</li>
<li>Place a washable slipcover or designated pet blanket on sofas and chairs where pets are permitted</li>
<li>Avoid upholstered pieces with texture that traps pet hair (boucle, velvet loops)</li>
<li>Consider a dog crate or bed station in a visible corner as an invitation for pets to have their own space</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="bedding-and-linens">Bedding and Linens</h3>
<ul>
<li>Use waterproof mattress protectors (critical for pet-friendly properties)</li>
<li>Keep a separate set of designated "pet bedding" (a dog bed, old blanket, or washable cover) for guests to use instead of your nice linens</li>
<li>Explicitly note in your pet policy (and welcome book) that pets are not permitted on the main beds, unless you've decided to allow it</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="odor-management">Odor Management</h3>
<p>Pet odor is the primary concern after cleaning. Build odor management into your standard cleaning protocol:</p>
<ul>
<li>Use <a href="https://www.epa.gov/saferchoice">enzyme-based cleaners</a> on pet accidents (simple neutralizing sprays don't work on pet odors)</li>
<li>Wash all soft furnishings after every pet stay (throws, decorative pillows, pet blankets)</li>
<li>Air the property before new guests arrive (open windows during cleaning)</li>
<li>Use air purifiers in primary living areas</li>
<li>Consider pet-safe scent diffusers as a backup (ensure they're non-toxic to animals for the pet guests, too)</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="pet-friendly-amenities-to-include">Pet-Friendly Amenities to Include</h2>
<p>The hosts who earn the best reviews from pet-owning guests are those who genuinely think through what makes the stay easier for pet owners.</p>
<h3 id="the-pet-welcome-kit">The Pet Welcome Kit</h3>
<p>Create a small basket or kit with:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Food and water bowls</strong> (stainless steel, easy to clean)</li>
<li><strong>Dog towel</strong> or chamois for paw cleaning at the door</li>
<li><strong>Pet waste bags</strong> (stock generously)</li>
<li><strong>List of nearby dog-friendly parks, trails, and beaches</strong></li>
<li><strong>Dog bed or washable floor pad</strong> in a designated pet sleeping area</li>
<li><strong>Treats</strong> (optional but memorable — guests love this)</li>
<li><strong>Local vet and <a href="https://www.avma.org/resources/pet-owners/emergencycare">emergency vet information</a></strong> (for anxious pet parents)</li>
</ul>
<p>Add a note: "Welcome, [Dog's Name]!" — guests almost always mention this in reviews.</p>
<h3 id="outdoor-pet-amenities">Outdoor Pet Amenities</h3>
<ul>
<li>A <strong>fenced yard or outdoor space</strong> is the single biggest upgrade for pet-friendly properties; market it prominently</li>
<li>A <strong>hose or outdoor shower</strong> for washing muddy paws</li>
<li><strong>Waste bag dispenser</strong> at the back door or yard entrance</li>
<li>A <strong>dog tie-out stake or cable</strong> in the yard for guests who want to let their dog be outside supervised</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="property-features-to-highlight-in-your-listing">Property Features to Highlight in Your Listing</h3>
<p>If your property has any of these features, lead with them in your marketing:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fenced yard ("fully fenced yard — safe for dogs to run")</li>
<li>Doggy door (if applicable)</li>
<li>Proximity to dog-friendly parks, beaches, or trails</li>
<li>First-floor or step-free entry (easier for large dogs)</li>
<li>No stairs required for any bedroom (accessibility for senior dogs)</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="marketing-your-pet-friendly-property">Marketing Your Pet-Friendly Property</h2>
<p>Going pet-friendly only pays off if pet-owner guests can find you. Use these tactics to maximize visibility.</p>
<h3 id="optimize-your-listing-on-platforms">Optimize Your Listing on Platforms</h3>
<p><strong>On Airbnb:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Enable the "Pets Allowed" amenity toggle (this adds you to pet filter searches)</li>
<li>Add "pet-friendly," "dog-friendly," and "fenced yard" to your title or listing description where appropriate</li>
<li>Include photos of your pet amenities and yard/outdoor space</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>On VRBO:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>VRBO has dedicated pet-friendly filter options; ensure all relevant boxes are checked</li>
<li>VRBO's pet-owner audience is especially active, as the platform is popular with family and group travelers who often include dogs</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>On your direct booking site:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Create a dedicated landing page or section for pet-owner guests</li>
<li>Include photos of the yard, the pet kit, and nearby dog-friendly spots</li>
</ul>
<p>According to VRBO's host resources, pet-friendly listings receive significantly more searches and higher average booking values than non-pet listings in most leisure destinations.</p>
<h3 id="write-a-pet-specific-listing-description">Write a Pet-Specific Listing Description</h3>
<p>Include a paragraph specifically addressing pet-owner guests:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Traveling with your four-legged family member? [Property Name] is a true dog-friendly home. We have a fully fenced half-acre yard, a pet welcome kit with bowls and waste bags, and a list of the best local dog parks and pet-friendly trails. The neighborhood is quiet and walkable, and [Coffee Shop] on the corner has a dog-friendly patio. We love dogs and it shows."</p>
</blockquote>
<h3 id="leverage-pet-focused-marketing-channels">Leverage Pet-Focused Marketing Channels</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>BringFido.com:</strong> A dedicated pet travel platform where you can list your property</li>
<li><strong>GoPetFriendly.com:</strong> Another pet travel directory</li>
<li><strong>Petswelcome.com:</strong> Long-established pet travel resource</li>
<li><strong>Pet owner Facebook groups</strong> in your target market (families traveling with pets)</li>
</ul>
<p>For broader listing marketing strategies, see our <a href="/blog/rental-property-marketing-strategies">rental property marketing strategies guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="cleaning-protocol-for-pet-stays">Cleaning Protocol for Pet Stays</h2>
<p>Your standard turnover protocol needs adjustments for pet stays:</p>
<p><strong>Additional cleaning steps:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Vacuum all upholstered surfaces, rugs, and floors with a pet hair attachment</li>
<li>Inspect all mattress protectors and pillow protectors for any pet contact</li>
<li>Wash any designated pet blankets and throws</li>
<li>Use an enzyme cleaner on any identified accident spots (whether visible or not — pets sometimes have accidents in non-obvious spots; inspect systematically)</li>
<li>Check under furniture for pet hair accumulation</li>
<li>Inspect the yard for pet waste</li>
<li>Air the property with open windows during cleaning</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Communicate pet stay additions to your cleaner:</strong> Your <a href="/blog/cleaning-service-short-term-rental">cleaning service</a> should know when a pet stay has occurred and understand the additional protocol. Build a modest additional fee into your pet charge to cover the extra cleaning time.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="handling-pet-damage">Handling Pet Damage</h2>
<p>Despite best precautions, damage from pets will occasionally occur. Your approach:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Document at checkout</strong> — photograph any damage during your standard post-checkout inspection</li>
<li><strong>Communicate with the guest</strong> — most pet-owning guests are mortified by damage and respond well to direct, calm communication</li>
<li><strong>File a claim</strong> — both Airbnb's AirCover and VRBO's damage protection cover documented pet damage</li>
<li><strong>Keep receipts</strong> — for carpet cleaning, furniture repair, or replacement</li>
</ol>
<p>Being transparent about your pet damage policy in your listing (and your welcome book) sets expectations and makes it easier to have these conversations when necessary.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Going pet-friendly is one of the most effective niche strategies in short-term rental hosting. The combination of higher rates, improved occupancy, and loyal repeat guests makes it a compelling choice for most property types.</p>
<p>The key is doing it thoughtfully: a clear pet policy, a durable and pet-tolerant property setup, a genuine pet welcome kit, and a cleaning protocol that maintains your property's condition across pet stays.</p>
<p>Guests who travel with pets are some of the most appreciative and loyal guests in the STR market. Earn their trust, and they'll come back every year.</p>
