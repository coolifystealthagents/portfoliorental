2b:T3a20,<p>Running a short-term rental can be incredibly rewarding — but it is also time-intensive. Between managing guest communications, coordinating cleaners, handling check-ins, and keeping your listing optimized, many hosts find themselves overwhelmed. That is where <strong>Airbnb co-hosting</strong> comes in.</p>
<p>Co-hosting is a flexible arrangement that allows property owners to bring on a trusted partner — often a local property manager, friend, or professional co-host — to share operational responsibilities. It is one of the most practical tools available to hosts who want to scale without burning out.</p>
<p>This guide covers everything you need to know: how Airbnb co-hosting works, what co-hosts actually do, how fees are structured, and how to find or become a co-host.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-is-airbnb-co-hosting">What Is Airbnb Co-Hosting?</h2>
<p>Airbnb co-hosting is an official feature on the Airbnb platform that lets a primary host add one or more co-hosts to help manage their listing. Co-hosts get access to the listing dashboard and can perform tasks like responding to guests, updating calendars, managing pricing, and coordinating housekeeping.</p>
<p>Unlike a traditional property management company, co-hosting keeps the listing under the primary host's account. The original host retains ownership and control of the listing, while the co-host operates with a defined set of permissions.</p>
<h3 id="who-uses-co-hosting">Who Uses Co-Hosting?</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Property owners</strong> who travel frequently or have day jobs and cannot manage day-to-day operations</li>
<li><strong>Investors</strong> who own multiple properties and need operational support</li>
<li><strong>Remote hosts</strong> managing listings in cities they don't live in</li>
<li><strong>New hosts</strong> who want an experienced partner to guide their early days</li>
</ul>
<p>For a broader view of how property management fits into STR investing, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-does-an-airbnb-co-host-do">What Does an Airbnb Co-Host Do?</h2>
<p>Co-hosts can take on as many or as few responsibilities as the primary host delegates. Airbnb's platform gives primary hosts granular control over what each co-host can access and manage.</p>
<h3 id="common-co-host-responsibilities">Common Co-Host Responsibilities</h3>
<p><strong>Guest Communication</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Responding to inquiries and booking requests</li>
<li>Sending pre-arrival messages with check-in instructions</li>
<li>Handling mid-stay issues and guest complaints</li>
<li>Coordinating post-checkout reviews</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Listing Management</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Updating availability calendars</li>
<li>Adjusting pricing (with or without pricing tools)</li>
<li>Editing listing descriptions, photos, and amenities</li>
<li>Managing minimum stay requirements and booking settings</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Operations Coordination</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Scheduling and confirming cleaning crews</li>
<li>Overseeing maintenance requests</li>
<li>Conducting property inspections between stays</li>
<li>Restocking consumables and supplies</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Financial Reporting</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Tracking income and expenses</li>
<li>Communicating earnings to the primary host</li>
<li>Assisting with documentation for taxes</li>
</ul>
<p>To understand how strong operational systems underpin STR success, explore our guide on <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-property-management">vacation rental property management</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="airbnb-co-hosting-permissions-what-hosts-can-control">Airbnb Co-Hosting Permissions: What Hosts Can Control</h2>
<p>When you add a co-host on Airbnb, you choose their permission level. As of 2026, Airbnb allows primary hosts to grant co-hosts access to specific features:</p>
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Permission Level</th>
<th>What Co-Host Can Do</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Full Access</td>
<td>Manage everything including payouts</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Limited Access</td>
<td>Manage bookings and communications, no financials</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Custom</td>
<td>Pick and choose individual permissions</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>This flexibility is important. If you are bringing on a co-host for the first time, starting with limited access and expanding permissions as trust builds is a smart approach.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="how-much-do-airbnb-co-hosts-earn">How Much Do Airbnb Co-Hosts Earn?</h2>
<p>Co-host compensation varies widely depending on the scope of work, local market, and the agreement between parties. There is no fixed Airbnb co-hosting fee set by the platform — it is negotiated between the primary host and co-host.</p>
<h3 id="typical-co-hosting-fee-structures">Typical Co-Hosting Fee Structures</h3>
<p><strong>Percentage of Revenue (Most Common)</strong>
Most co-hosts charge between <strong>10% and 30% of gross rental revenue</strong>, depending on responsibilities:</p>
<ul>
<li>10–15%: Light duties (guest messaging, calendar management only)</li>
<li>15–25%: Full operations including cleaning coordination and inspections</li>
<li>25–30%+: Fully managed, including furnishing and setup services</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Flat Monthly Fee</strong>
Some co-hosts charge a flat rate regardless of occupancy. This works well for hosts in high-season markets where the property is almost always booked.</p>
<p><strong>Per-Task Pricing</strong>
Less common but used for specific one-off services like a deep clean after a long-term stay or a seasonal property refresh.</p>
<h3 id="who-pays-the-cleaning-fee">Who Pays the Cleaning Fee?</h3>
<p>In most arrangements, the cleaning fee collected from guests goes directly to cover cleaning costs. If the co-host manages the cleaning crew, they may handle payment and simply reconcile costs with the primary host monthly.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="how-to-find-an-airbnb-co-host">How to Find an Airbnb Co-Host</h2>
<p>Finding the right co-host is critical. A bad fit can lead to poor guest experiences, damaged reviews, and financial disputes.</p>
<h3 id="where-to-look">Where to Look</h3>
<p><strong>Airbnb's Co-Host Network</strong>
Airbnb has introduced tools to connect hosts with experienced co-hosts in their area. Check your Airbnb hosting dashboard for the co-host marketplace or network feature.</p>
<p><strong>Local STR Communities</strong>
Facebook groups, Reddit communities (r/airbnb, r/airbnbhosts), and local real estate investor meetups are excellent places to find vetted co-hosts.</p>
<p><strong>Professional Property Managers</strong>
Many traditional property management companies also offer co-hosting services. Compare what they offer versus standalone co-hosts — a <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-property-management">vacation rental property management</a> company may offer more robust systems but at higher cost.</p>
<p><strong>Personal Network</strong>
Trusted friends or family members who are local to your property can make excellent co-hosts, especially if they already have hospitality or real estate experience.</p>
<h3 id="what-to-look-for-in-a-co-host">What to Look for in a Co-Host</h3>
<ul>
<li>Strong communication skills and fast response times</li>
<li>Experience managing short-term rentals (check their own host profile and reviews)</li>
<li>Knowledge of local regulations and STR compliance requirements</li>
<li>Reliability — especially for last-minute issues like broken appliances or early arrivals</li>
<li>Alignment on hosting style and guest experience standards</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="how-to-become-an-airbnb-co-host">How to Become an Airbnb Co-Host</h2>
<p>If you are on the other side of this equation — interested in becoming a co-host as a business — here is how to build that career.</p>
<h3 id="step-1-build-your-own-hosting-experience">Step 1: Build Your Own Hosting Experience</h3>
<p>The best co-hosts have firsthand experience running their own Airbnb listing. Start by hosting a room or a small property to understand the workflow from the inside.</p>
<h3 id="step-2-get-to-know-your-local-market">Step 2: Get to Know Your Local Market</h3>
<p>Understand average daily rates, occupancy trends, local regulations, and what guests in your area expect. Tools like AirDNA can help you benchmark performance across your market.</p>
<h3 id="step-3-develop-your-systems">Step 3: Develop Your Systems</h3>
<p>Successful co-hosts run tight operational systems. This means:</p>
<ul>
<li>Standardized guest messaging templates</li>
<li>Reliable cleaning and maintenance teams</li>
<li>A process for handling emergencies at any hour</li>
<li>Familiarity with <a href="/blog/airbnb-automation-tools">airbnb automation tools</a> that save time</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="step-4-set-your-rate-and-services">Step 4: Set Your Rate and Services</h3>
<p>Define your service packages clearly:</p>
<ul>
<li>What tasks are included in your base fee?</li>
<li>What is billed additionally (deep cleans, emergency callouts, restocking runs)?</li>
<li>How will you report earnings and expenses to property owners?</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="step-5-market-yourself">Step 5: Market Yourself</h3>
<p>Create a professional profile, gather testimonials from hosts you have worked with, and list yourself on co-host marketplaces. Word of mouth is powerful in this industry — delivering exceptional results for one host often leads to referrals.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="co-hosting-vs-property-management-what-s-the-difference">Co-Hosting vs. Property Management: What's the Difference?</h2>
<p>Many hosts confuse co-hosting with hiring a full property management company. There are meaningful differences:</p>

<td>Stays with primary host</td>
<td>May be transferred to PM account</td>
</tr>

<td>Platform</td>
<td>Airbnb native feature</td>
<td>Often third-party tools</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Flexibility</td>
<td>Highly customizable</td>
<td>Standard service packages</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Cost</td>
<td>Typically 10–25%</td>
<td>Often 20–40%</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Scale</td>

</tr>

<p>For hosts managing a growing portfolio, a channel manager may also become important. See our guide on <a href="/blog/airbnb-property-management-tips">airbnb property management tips</a> to understand how professional systems support scale.</p>

<h2 id="legal-and-tax-considerations-for-co-hosts">Legal and Tax Considerations for Co-Hosts</h2>
<h3 id="written-agreements-are-essential">Written Agreements Are Essential</h3>
<p>Even if you co-host with a friend, always document the arrangement in writing, a practice the <a href="https://www.sba.gov/business-guide/launch-your-business/get-federal-and-state-tax-id-numbers" rel="noopener" target="_blank">SBA recommends for all businesses</a>. Your agreement should cover:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fee structure and payment schedule</li>
<li>Scope of responsibilities</li>
<li>How liability is handled for property damage or guest incidents</li>
<li>Termination conditions and notice periods</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="tax-treatment-of-co-host-income">Tax Treatment of Co-Host Income</h3>
<p>Co-hosts who earn income from hosting services are generally classified as <a href="https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/self-employed-individuals-tax-center" rel="noopener" target="_blank">self-employed by the IRS</a>. This means:</p>
<ul>
<li>Co-hosting income is reportable to tax authorities</li>
<li>Business expenses related to co-hosting may be deductible</li>
<li>Depending on volume, co-hosts may need to register as a business entity</li>
</ul>
<p>Consult a tax professional familiar with short-term rental income. Our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-tax-guide">short-term rental tax guide</a> covers the basics for STR operators.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="common-co-hosting-mistakes-to-avoid">Common Co-Hosting Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
<p><strong>1. Not Setting Clear Expectations</strong>
Ambiguity breeds conflict. Define who handles what before the first guest arrives.</p>
<p><strong>2. Skipping the Written Agreement</strong>
Handshake deals go wrong. A simple one-page agreement protects both parties.</p>
<p><strong>3. Choosing the Wrong Person</strong>
A co-host who is slow to respond or unreliable will tank your reviews. Treat the selection process like hiring an employee.</p>
<p><strong>4. Micromanaging</strong>
If you hired a co-host to take things off your plate, let them. Trust your systems and check in on performance metrics rather than every decision.</p>
<p><strong>5. Ignoring Performance Data</strong>
Track your response rate, review scores, occupancy, and revenue before and after bringing on a co-host. Data tells you whether the arrangement is working.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="is-co-hosting-right-for-you">Is Co-Hosting Right for You?</h2>
<p>Co-hosting is an excellent option if:</p>
<ul>
<li>You own a property but lack time for daily management</li>
<li>You want to scale your portfolio without becoming a full-time operator</li>
<li>You are entering a new market and want local expertise</li>
<li>You are burning out from self-managing</li>
</ul>
<p>It may not be the right fit if:</p>
<ul>
<li>You are highly hands-on and have difficulty delegating</li>
<li>Your property generates modest income and a co-host fee would significantly cut margins</li>
<li>You cannot find a trustworthy co-host in your area</li>
</ul>
<p>For more strategies on running a profitable short-term rental operation, review our comprehensive <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Airbnb co-hosting is one of the most underutilized strategies in short-term rental investing. Whether you are a property owner looking for operational relief or an experienced host ready to monetize your skills, co-hosting opens up a scalable, flexible path forward.</p>
<p>The keys to success are clear communication, a well-defined agreement, and the right systems in place. With those foundations, co-hosting can transform how you manage — and grow — your short-term rental portfolio.</p>
<p><strong>Quick Reference: Airbnb Co-Hosting Essentials</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Co-hosts earn 10–30% of revenue depending on scope</li>
<li>Primary hosts control all permission levels on the platform</li>
<li>Always use a written co-hosting agreement</li>
<li>Look for co-hosts with verified STR experience and strong communication skills</li>
<li>Track performance data to measure the value of your co-hosting arrangement</li>
</ul>
