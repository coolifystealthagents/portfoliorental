The way people search for and evaluate rental properties has fundamentally shifted. Prospective tenants now expect to do much of their decision-making before they ever set foot inside a property. Static photos satisfy part of that need. A 3D virtual tour satisfies it completely.

A 3D virtual tour allows a tenant to "walk through" your property on their own schedule, from any device, at their own pace. They can rotate in any direction, zoom in on details, revisit rooms, check measurements, and get a realistic spatial sense of the unit — all without a showing. For out-of-state movers, busy professionals, and anyone who's been burned by misleading listing photos, a 3D tour is the confidence builder that drives them to apply.

This guide covers the leading 3D virtual tour tools for rental properties, how they compare in cost and capability, how to get the most out of each, and where to publish your tours to maximize exposure.

Why 3D Virtual Tours Belong in Your Rental Marketing Stack

Before exploring the tools, it's worth understanding the case for 3D virtual tours in concrete terms.

They attract more serious applicants. A tenant who has explored a 3D tour before inquiring has already decided the layout and space work for them. They're not curious — they're interested. This self-selection dramatically improves the quality of leads you receive.

They reduce wasted showings. Every landlord has experienced the showing that ends in "it's not quite what I pictured." Virtual tours eliminate that outcome. When tenants can preview the property accurately, only those who genuinely want it will book a showing.

They support remote and out-of-state tenants. Corporate relocations, military moves, and graduate student rentals often involve tenants who can't visit in person before signing, a trend confirmed by NAR research and statistics. A 3D tour can make the difference between landing that tenant and losing them to a competitor who has one.

They improve listing performance on platforms. Major rental platforms including Zillow Rental Manager and Apartments.com give preferred placement and additional search visibility to listings with 3D tours. They're not just a tenant experience feature — they're a ranking signal.

They're reusable. Unlike a showing, a 3D tour scan never wears out. Scan once, use the tour every time the property turns over. The cost per vacancy drops with each use.

3D virtual tours are one component of a complete rental property marketing strategies approach, alongside professional photography, video walkthroughs, floor plans, and multi-platform listing distribution.

The Leading 3D Virtual Tour Tools for Landlords

1. Matterport

Best for: Multi-unit portfolios, luxury properties, property managers who want the industry-standard tool

Matterport is widely considered the gold standard in 3D property scanning. It uses a specialized 360-degree camera to create an immersive, interactive "digital twin" of your property. Tenants can navigate room to room, zoom in on details, switch to dollhouse view (a 3D overhead model), and view a generated floor plan — all from the same experience.

How it works:

  • Rent or purchase a Matterport Pro camera (Pro2 or Pro3), or hire a Matterport Service Partner to scan for you
  • The camera automatically captures and stitches together 360-degree images as you move through the space
  • Upload to Matterport's cloud platform; the tour is hosted on their servers
  • Share the link or embed it on your listing

Key features:

  • Interactive navigation (click to move, look in all directions)
  • Auto-generated floor plan with accurate measurements
  • Dollhouse view (3D aerial overview of the entire space)
  • Highlight tags (add info hotspots to features)
  • MLS and major platform integration
  • 4K image quality with Pro3 camera

Pricing:

  • Matterport camera rental: available through their partner network
  • Matterport Pro camera purchase: $4,000-$6,000+
  • Service partner scans: $100-$400 depending on property size and location
  • Hosting subscription: Free tier (1 active space), paid plans from ~$10/month for multiple active tours

Platform compatibility: Embeds on Zillow, Apartments.com, Realtor.com, and most property management websites

2. Zillow 3D Home

Best for: Individual landlords on Zillow, low-budget option, quick setup

Zillow's free 3D Home app lets landlords create 360-degree virtual tours using a compatible smartphone or 360-degree camera. The result is embedded directly in your Zillow listing, with priority placement over standard photo listings.

How it works:

  • Download the free Zillow 3D Home app
  • Walk through the property capturing 360-degree panoramas in each room
  • The app stitches them together and creates an interactive tour
  • The tour publishes directly to your Zillow listing

Key features:

  • Free to use
  • Direct Zillow integration with priority listing placement
  • Floor plan overlay (in compatible models)
  • Works with Ricoh Theta, Insta360, and supported Samsung phones
  • Guided in-app instructions

Pricing: Free

Limitations:

  • Tour hosted on Zillow only (not portable to other platforms)
  • Lower image quality than Matterport
  • Limited interactivity compared to full Matterport experience

3. iGUIDE

Best for: Property managers who need accurate floor plans alongside 3D tours

iGUIDE is a strong alternative to Matterport, with a particular emphasis on measurement accuracy. The system uses a specialized camera that generates both a 3D tour and a precisely measured floor plan simultaneously. For landlords who need to advertise accurate square footage and room dimensions, iGUIDE is particularly valuable.

Key features:

  • Highly accurate floor plans with room-level measurements
  • Interactive 3D walkthrough
  • MLS integration
  • Photo integration with the tour
  • Canadian-developed platform with strong North American adoption

Pricing:

  • Camera purchase: ~$3,500
  • Service provider scans: $100-$250 per property
  • Hosting included in scan cost for 12 months

4. Kuula

Best for: Budget-conscious landlords creating 360-degree tours from smartphone photos

Kuula is a cloud-based virtual tour platform that creates interactive 360-degree tours from standard 360-degree photos taken with any compatible camera or supported smartphone. It's not a scanning device — it's a hosting and presentation platform.

Key features:

  • Upload 360-degree photos from any camera
  • Add hotspots, labels, and navigation
  • Embed on any website
  • Branded tours for property management companies
  • Social sharing tools

Pricing: Free tier (limited tours), paid from $16/month for unlimited active tours

Best paired with: A Ricoh Theta camera ($300-500), which makes shooting 360-degree photos simple and fast

5. Matterport Axis (formerly Leica RTC360 equivalent)

For larger property management companies scanning dozens of units monthly, enterprise-grade solutions offer the highest efficiency. These systems typically combine hardware investments with high-volume pricing structures and dedicated technical support.

If you're scanning 20+ units per month, the ROI calculation for a full Matterport Pro3 system or equivalent changes significantly — contact Matterport directly for enterprise pricing.

How to Create a 3D Virtual Tour: Step-by-Step

Whether you use Matterport, iGUIDE, or a DIY option, the preparation process is the same.

Step 1: Prepare the Property

A 3D scan captures everything — including imperfections. Before you scan:

  • Deep clean every room
  • Stage with simple, neutral decor
  • Remove personal items, clutter, and excess furniture
  • Open all blinds for natural light
  • Turn on all interior lights
  • Check that all fixtures, faucets, and hardware look presentable

For a complete pre-scan staging checklist, see our guide on rental property staging tips.

Step 2: Plan Your Scan Path

Map out the rooms in order and plan where you'll position the camera in each. For Matterport, you'll want scans positioned:

  • At the center of each room
  • At the entrance of each room
  • At corners or areas with unique features

More scan positions create smoother navigation for the viewer.

Step 3: Scan and Upload

Follow the specific instructions for your chosen tool. Matterport guides you through the process via its app. Zillow 3D Home provides in-app guidance for each room. Allow the upload and processing time (typically 15-60 minutes after the scan, depending on size).

Step 4: Review and Edit

Before publishing, review the tour for:

  • Gaps in navigation (areas the tenant can't reach)
  • Overexposed or dark sections
  • Anything that shouldn't be in the tour (an embarrassing magnet on the fridge, a visible lease document)

Add highlight tags for key features: "Quartz countertops," "In-unit washer/dryer," "Custom built-in shelving."

Step 5: Publish and Distribute

Where to Publish Your 3D Virtual Tour

Major Rental Platforms

  • Zillow — Use Zillow 3D Home for native integration, or embed a Matterport link
  • Apartments.com — Supports embedded 3D tour links on Gold and Platinum listings
  • Realtor.com — Supports 3D tour integration for verified listings
  • Facebook Marketplace — Share the tour link in the description

Your Property Management Website

Embed the 3D tour directly on your individual property listing pages. This is a major benefit for rental listing SEO — it increases time on page and signals content quality to Google.

Email and Social Media

When you share the listing, include the 3D tour link directly. For social media, a screen recording of the 3D tour exploring the space makes an engaging video clip. See our guide on social media marketing for rental properties for platform-specific content tips.

3D Tours vs. Video Walkthroughs: Which Do You Need?

Both formats have a role in a complete rental marketing strategy:

3D Virtual Tour Video Walkthrough
Viewer control Full (interactive) None (passive)
Production time 1-2 hours 30-90 min + editing
Rewatch value High Medium
Platform integration Wide Wide
Emotional impact Moderate High
Best for Remote tenants, layout evaluation Initial property impression

Ideally, use both. The video walkthrough creates an emotional connection to the space. The 3D tour lets tenants explore logistically. Together, they create the most complete remote property experience available.

For detailed guidance on video production, see video walkthroughs for rental properties. For adding floor plans to complete the picture, see floor plans for rental listings.

The ROI of 3D Virtual Tours

Consider a rental property at $2,000/month. If a 3D tour reduces vacancy by just one week, that's $500 in recovered revenue. A professional Matterport scan costs $100-$300. The tour can be reused every lease cycle — often 5-10 times before the property requires another scan.

Over a three-year period, the cost per use drops to $20-$60. The ROI is unambiguous, particularly in competitive markets where being different drives inquiries.

Conclusion

3D virtual tours are no longer a luxury feature for luxury properties. They've become a meaningful competitive advantage for any landlord willing to invest the time and modest cost to implement them. The benefits — higher-quality leads, fewer wasted showings, support for remote tenants, and improved platform rankings — compound over time and across every property in your portfolio.

Start with the free Zillow 3D Home app if you're new to virtual tours, then consider upgrading to Matterport or iGUIDE as your portfolio grows. Pair your 3D tours with professional photos, a compelling video walkthrough, and a complete listing presence across major platforms as part of your full rental property marketing strategies approach.
