Managing one Airbnb listing by hand is doable. Managing five, ten, or twenty listings without automation is a full-time headache.

The most successful short-term rental hosts do not work harder. They use the right tools to automate the repetitive tasks that eat up their time.

Airbnb automation tools handle everything from guest communication to pricing adjustments to cleaning schedules. They free you up to focus on growing your portfolio and improving the guest experience.

In this guide, we cover the ten categories of automation tools every serious host needs and the top products in each category.

For a complete overview of running short-term rentals, start with our short-term rental management guide.

1. Channel Managers

If you list your property on more than one platform, a channel manager is the first tool you need. Without one, you risk double bookings, calendar conflicts, and hours spent updating multiple listings manually.

What Channel Managers Do

A channel manager syncs your calendars, rates, and availability across Airbnb, VRBO, Booking.com, and other platforms in real time. When a guest books on Airbnb, the channel manager blocks those dates on VRBO and every other connected platform instantly.

Top Channel Manager Options

Guesty is built for professional property managers. It handles channel management, guest communication, payment processing, and reporting all in one platform. Guesty works best for hosts with ten or more listings and teams that need multi-user access. Pricing is custom based on your portfolio size.

Hospitable (formerly Smartbnb) focuses on automation-first management. It combines channel management with powerful automated messaging and task management. Hospitable is popular with hosts who manage five to twenty listings and want to automate as much as possible. Plans start around $40 per month for two listings.

iGMS provides channel management along with a unified inbox, automated messaging, and cleaning management. It is a good mid-range option for hosts who want solid features without enterprise-level complexity. iGMS offers both per-listing and flat-rate pricing.

Choosing Your Channel Manager

Consider these factors when picking a channel manager:

  • How many listings you manage now and plan to manage in the future
  • Which platforms you list on
  • Whether you need team management features
  • Your budget and preferred pricing model
  • Integration with your other tools like pricing and cleaning software

2. Automated Messaging

Guest communication takes more time than most hosts expect. Between booking confirmations, check-in instructions, mid-stay check-ins, checkout reminders, and review requests, you could easily send ten messages per guest.

Multiply that by twenty bookings a month and you are drowning in messages.

How Automated Messaging Works

Automated messaging tools send pre-written messages at specific trigger points. Common triggers include:

  • Booking confirmed: Send a welcome message and ask about arrival time
  • One day before check-in: Send detailed check-in instructions with door codes and directions
  • Check-in day: Send a welcome note and house rules reminder
  • Mid-stay: Ask if everything is going well and if the guest needs anything
  • Checkout day: Send checkout instructions and thank the guest
  • After checkout: Request a review

Personalization That Feels Real

The best messaging tools insert guest names, check-in dates, door codes, and property details into templates automatically. A well-crafted template feels personal even though it is sent automatically.

Tips for effective automated messages:

  • Keep messages short and friendly
  • Use the guest's first name
  • Include only the information they need at that moment
  • Add a personal touch like a local restaurant recommendation
  • Always invite them to reach out with questions

Most channel managers include automated messaging. Standalone options like Hospitable and Host Tools offer dedicated messaging features if you need more flexibility.

3. Smart Locks and Keyless Entry

Physical keys are one of the biggest headaches in short-term rental management. Guests lose them.

You have to coordinate handoffs. Lockouts happen at the worst times.

Smart locks solve all of these problems.

Benefits of Smart Locks

  • Unique codes for every guest. Generate a new code for each booking and delete it after checkout.
  • Remote access. Lock and unlock doors from your phone anywhere in the world.
  • Access logs. See exactly when someone enters and exits your property.
  • Cleaner access. Give your cleaning team their own code that works only during turnover windows.
  • No lockouts. Guests can always get in with their code. No key exchanges needed.

Popular Smart Lock Brands

  • Schlage Encode offers reliable Wi-Fi-connected locks that work without a hub
  • Yale Assure integrates with most smart home systems and offers multiple access methods
  • August Smart Lock retrofits onto existing deadbolts for easy installation
  • RemoteLock is designed for property managers with multiple units and offers centralized management

Integration with Your PMS

Most channel managers and property management systems can automatically generate and send door codes to guests. When a booking comes in, the system creates a unique code, sets it to activate at check-in time, and sends it to the guest.

At checkout, the code is automatically deactivated.

4. Pricing Automation

Setting prices manually for every night across multiple listings is not sustainable. Pricing automation tools analyze market data and adjust your rates in real time to maximize revenue.

How Pricing Automation Works

These tools pull data from thousands of comparable listings, local events, hotel rates, and historical booking patterns. They use algorithms to recommend or automatically set optimal prices for each night on your calendar.

Leading Pricing Tools

  • PriceLabs offers deep customization with market-specific data and custom rules
  • Beyond (formerly Beyond Pricing) provides a simple, automated approach with minimal setup
  • Wheelhouse gives you transparent data and the choice between automated and recommendation modes

What to Automate in Your Pricing

At minimum, automate these pricing elements:

  • Daily rate adjustments based on demand
  • Seasonal multipliers for peak and off-peak periods
  • Last-minute discounts for upcoming vacant nights
  • Minimum stay requirements that change by season
  • Event-based pricing surges

For a deeper look at pricing strategies, check our guide on Airbnb property management tips.

5. Cleaning Scheduling and Management

Coordinating cleaning between guests is one of the most operationally complex tasks in short-term rental management. Automation makes it manageable.

How Cleaning Automation Works

When a guest checks out, the system automatically notifies your cleaning team and assigns the turnover. The cleaner receives the property address, check-in time for the next guest, and a checklist of tasks.

When cleaning is complete, they mark it done in the app and you get a notification.

Top Cleaning Management Tools

  • TurnoverBnB connects hosts with local cleaners and automates scheduling based on your booking calendar
  • Breezeway handles cleaning, inspections, and maintenance tasks with detailed checklists and photo verification
  • Properly provides visual checklists and quality verification for cleaning teams

Key Features to Look For

  • Automatic scheduling based on checkout and check-in times
  • Customizable cleaning checklists
  • Photo verification of completed tasks
  • Cleaner ratings and performance tracking
  • Integration with your channel manager or PMS

6. Automated Review Requests

Reviews are the lifeblood of your Airbnb listing. More five-star reviews mean higher search rankings and more bookings.

But many hosts forget to leave reviews for guests, which means guests are less likely to leave reviews in return.

How Review Automation Works

Review automation tools send a review for every guest automatically after checkout. Some tools write custom reviews based on whether there were any issues during the stay.

They also send a gentle reminder to the guest encouraging them to leave a review for you.

Best Practices for Review Automation

  • Leave honest, positive reviews for guests who followed your house rules
  • Time your review request to arrive shortly after checkout while the experience is fresh
  • Include a personal note thanking the guest
  • If there were issues, handle them privately before the review period ends

Most channel managers include review automation. Hospitable is particularly strong in this area.

7. Noise Monitoring

Party complaints from neighbors can lead to fines, HOA violations, and even loss of your rental permit. Noise monitoring devices alert you to potential problems before they escalate.

How Noise Monitors Work

These devices measure noise levels inside your property without recording conversations. When noise exceeds a threshold you set, you receive an alert on your phone.

You can then message the guest and ask them to keep it down before neighbors complain.

Popular Noise Monitoring Devices

  • Minut monitors noise, occupancy, and cigarette smoke. It also tracks temperature and humidity.
  • NoiseAware is designed specifically for short-term rentals and provides real-time alerts and historical data.
  • Alertify detects noise, smoking, and vaping with a single device.

Privacy Considerations

Noise monitors do not record audio or video. They only measure decibel levels.

This makes them legal in most places and acceptable to guests. Always disclose the presence of noise monitoring devices in your listing description and house rules.

8. Guest Screening

Not every booking request is a good fit for your property. Guest screening tools help you identify potential problems before they arrive.

What Screening Tools Check

  • Guest identity verification
  • Past booking reviews and ratings
  • Social media and public records
  • Known bad-guest databases
  • Booking patterns that suggest parties (same-city bookings, last-minute weekend reservations, young guest with large group)

Top Guest Screening Services

  • Autohost provides automated screening that assigns risk scores to each booking
  • Superhog offers identity verification and damage protection in one package
  • Guest Ranger screens guests and provides insurance coverage for approved bookings

Balancing Security and Hospitality

Screening should reduce risk without making honest guests feel unwelcome. Use screening as a behind-the-scenes tool.

Only decline bookings when there are clear red flags. Most guests will pass screening without ever knowing they were checked.

9. Accounting Integration

Tracking income and expenses across multiple properties and platforms gets complicated fast. Accounting automation keeps your finances organized and makes tax time much easier.

What to Automate in Your Finances

  • Income tracking from all platforms and direct bookings
  • Expense categorization for cleaning, supplies, maintenance, and repairs
  • Mileage tracking for property visits
  • Receipt capture and storage
  • Profit and loss reports by property
  • Tax report generation

Popular Accounting Tools for Hosts

  • QuickBooks is the industry standard for small business accounting and integrates with most STR tools
  • Stessa is designed specifically for rental property owners and offers free basic features
  • Baselane combines banking and accounting for landlords and STR hosts

Integration Tips

Connect your accounting software to your channel manager and bank accounts for automatic transaction imports. Set up rules to categorize common expenses automatically.

Review your books monthly to catch errors early.

10. ROI of Automation: Is It Worth the Cost?

Automation tools cost money. A typical host might spend $200 to $500 per month on software.

Is it worth it?

Time Savings

Consider how much time you spend on manual tasks each month:

  • Guest messaging: 5 to 10 hours
  • Calendar management: 3 to 5 hours
  • Pricing adjustments: 2 to 4 hours
  • Cleaning coordination: 3 to 5 hours
  • Financial tracking: 2 to 3 hours
  • Review management: 1 to 2 hours

That is 16 to 29 hours per month for a small portfolio. At even a modest hourly value of $30, that is $480 to $870 per month in time savings.

The tools pay for themselves in time alone.

Revenue Impact

Automated pricing tools alone can increase revenue by 10% to 20% compared to static pricing. For a property earning $3,000 per month, that is $300 to $600 in additional monthly revenue.

Automated messaging improves guest satisfaction, leading to better reviews, which leads to higher search rankings, which leads to more bookings. The compounding effect is significant.

Error Reduction

Manual processes create errors. Double bookings cost you refunds and bad reviews.

Missed messages lead to frustrated guests. Forgotten cleaning assignments lead to disasters.

Automation eliminates these human errors and protects your reputation.

When to Start Automating

Start with the tools that address your biggest pain points. For most hosts, that means:

  1. First: Channel manager and automated messaging
  2. Second: Pricing automation and smart locks
  3. Third: Cleaning management and noise monitoring
  4. Fourth: Guest screening and accounting integration

You do not need to adopt everything at once. Add tools as your portfolio grows and your needs become clearer.

Putting Your Automation Stack Together

The best automation setup is one where all your tools talk to each other. Here is a sample stack for a host with five to ten listings:

  • Channel Manager: Hospitable (handles messaging, calendar sync, and task management)
  • Pricing: PriceLabs (dynamic pricing connected to your channel manager)
  • Smart Locks: Schlage Encode or Yale Assure (codes generated and sent automatically)
  • Cleaning: TurnoverBnB (auto-scheduled from your booking calendar)
  • Noise Monitoring: Minut (alerts to your phone)
  • Accounting: QuickBooks or Stessa (connected to your bank and channel manager)

This stack automates the vast majority of daily operations. You still need to handle maintenance issues, respond to non-standard guest requests, and make strategic decisions.

But the routine work runs on autopilot. If you manage both short-term and long-term rentals, consider a full platform that handles both; our guide to the best property management software for portfolios covers multi-property solutions in detail.

Final Thoughts

Airbnb automation tools are not a luxury. They are a necessity for any host who wants to scale beyond a handful of listings without burning out.

The right tools save you time, increase your revenue, reduce errors, and improve the guest experience.

Start with the tools that solve your biggest problems. Build your stack over time.

And enjoy the freedom that comes from running a short-term rental business that does not require you to be available around the clock.
