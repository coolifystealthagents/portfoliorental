32:T43d2,<p>Running a short-term rental without the right insurance is like driving without a seatbelt. Things might go fine for a while, but when something goes wrong, the consequences can be devastating. A guest slips on your stairs. A burst pipe floods the unit. A party destroys your furniture. Without proper short-term rental insurance, you could be on the hook for tens of thousands of dollars.</p>
<p>This guide explains why standard homeowner policies fall short, what STR-specific insurance covers, how platform protection programs work, and how to choose the right policy for your situation.</p>
<h2 id="why-standard-homeowner-insurance-falls-short">Why Standard Homeowner Insurance Falls Short</h2>
<p>If you own a property and list it on Airbnb or VRBO, your regular homeowner insurance policy probably does not cover you. Most standard policies are designed for owner-occupied homes. They exclude commercial activity, and renting your home to paying guests is commercial activity.</p>
<p>Here is what can go wrong if you rely on a standard policy:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Denied claims.</strong> If your insurer discovers you were renting the property when the damage occurred, they can deny your claim entirely.</li>
<li><strong>Policy cancellation.</strong> Many insurers will cancel your entire policy if they learn you are running a short-term rental without proper coverage.</li>
<li><strong>Liability gaps.</strong> Standard policies may not cover injuries to paying guests, leaving you personally liable for medical bills and lawsuits.</li>
<li><strong>No lost income coverage.</strong> If your property is damaged and you cannot rent it, a standard policy will not reimburse your lost rental income.</li>
</ul>
<p>The bottom line is clear. If you are hosting guests for money, you need insurance that is designed for that purpose. For more on insurance requirements for rental properties, see our guide on <a href="/blog/landlord-insurance-requirements">landlord insurance requirements</a>.</p>
<h2 id="str-specific-insurance-coverage">STR-Specific Insurance Coverage</h2>
<p>Short-term rental insurance is built for the unique risks that come with hosting strangers in your property. These policies bridge the gaps that standard homeowner or landlord policies leave open.</p>
<h3 id="what-str-insurance-typically-covers">What STR Insurance Typically Covers</h3>
<p>A good STR insurance policy should include:</p>
<p><strong>Property damage protection.</strong> This covers damage to your building and its contents caused by guests, weather, fire, vandalism, or other covered events. STR policies are designed to cover damage from paying guests, which standard policies exclude.</p>
<p><strong>Liability coverage.</strong> If a guest or visitor is injured on your property, liability coverage pays for medical bills, legal fees, and settlements. Most policies offer $1 million or more in liability protection.</p>
<p><strong>Contents and furnishing coverage.</strong> Your furniture, appliances, electronics, and decor are all at risk when guests stay in your home. STR policies cover theft and damage to these items.</p>
<p><strong>Lost income protection.</strong> If your property is damaged and you cannot rent it during repairs, lost income coverage reimburses you for the bookings you miss. This is crucial for hosts who depend on rental income to cover their mortgage.</p>
<p><strong>Amenity liability.</strong> If you have a pool, hot tub, fire pit, or other amenity, STR insurance covers injuries related to these features. Standard policies often exclude or limit coverage for these high-risk amenities.</p>
<h3 id="how-str-insurance-differs-from-landlord-insurance">How STR Insurance Differs from Landlord Insurance</h3>
<p>Landlord insurance covers long-term rental properties. It assumes a tenant lives there for months or years. STR insurance accounts for the higher turnover, increased wear and tear, and unique risks that come with short-term guests.</p>
<p>Key differences include:</p>
<ul>
<li>STR policies cover damage caused by guests, while landlord policies typically do not</li>
<li>STR policies offer per-incident coverage rather than just annual limits</li>
<li>STR policies can cover gaps between guests when the property sits empty</li>
<li>STR policies often include coverage for guest belongings, which landlord policies do not</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="platform-protection-programs">Platform Protection Programs</h2>
<p>Airbnb, VRBO, and other booking platforms offer their own protection programs. These can provide a safety net, but they have important limitations.</p>
<h3 id="airbnb-aircover-for-hosts">Airbnb AirCover for Hosts</h3>
<p>Airbnb's AirCover program provides:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>$3 million in host damage protection</strong> for damage to your property and belongings</li>
<li><strong>$1 million in liability insurance</strong> for guest injuries</li>
<li><strong>Pet damage protection</strong> for damage caused by guests' pets</li>
<li><strong>Deep cleaning protection</strong> for excessive cleaning needs</li>
<li><strong>Income loss protection</strong> for verified income losses</li>
</ul>
<p>AirCover sounds comprehensive, but experienced hosts report several challenges:</p>
<ul>
<li>Claims can take weeks or months to process</li>
<li>Airbnb may dispute the value of damaged items</li>
<li>Coverage only applies to bookings made through Airbnb</li>
<li>Some types of damage may not be covered</li>
<li>You must file claims within 14 days of guest checkout</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="vrbo-property-protection">VRBO Property Protection</h3>
<p>VRBO offers a property damage protection program that guests can purchase at booking. This program covers accidental damage up to a certain amount, typically $1,500 to $5,000.</p>
<p>The VRBO program is more limited than AirCover. It is opt-in for guests, covers a smaller dollar amount, and does not include liability protection for hosts. You should not rely on it as your primary insurance.</p>
<h3 id="why-platform-protection-is-not-enough">Why Platform Protection Is Not Enough</h3>
<p>Platform protection programs are a nice bonus, but they should not be your only insurance. Here is why:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>They only cover platform bookings.</strong> If you book guests directly or through other channels, platform protection does not apply.</li>
<li><strong>You cannot control the process.</strong> The platform decides whether to approve your claim and how much to pay.</li>
<li><strong>There are coverage gaps.</strong> Not all types of damage or liability are covered.</li>
<li><strong>They can change the terms.</strong> Platforms can modify their protection programs at any time.</li>
</ol>
<p>Think of platform protection as a first layer of defense. Your own insurance policy is the foundation.</p>
<h2 id="commercial-insurance-policies">Commercial Insurance Policies</h2>
<p>For hosts who manage multiple properties or earn significant income from short-term rentals, a commercial insurance policy may be the best option.</p>
<h3 id="what-commercial-policies-offer">What Commercial Policies Offer</h3>
<p>Commercial STR insurance provides higher coverage limits and broader protection. These policies typically include:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Higher liability limits</strong> of $2 million or more</li>
<li><strong>Business personal property coverage</strong> for all furnishings and equipment</li>
<li><strong>Business interruption coverage</strong> for lost income during covered events</li>
<li><strong>Workers compensation</strong> if you have employees</li>
<li><strong>Commercial auto coverage</strong> if you use a vehicle for your rental business</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="when-to-consider-commercial-insurance">When to Consider Commercial Insurance</h3>
<p>You should look into commercial insurance if:</p>
<ul>
<li>You manage three or more short-term rental properties</li>
<li>Your annual STR income exceeds $50,000</li>
<li>You have employees or independent contractors</li>
<li>You offer high-risk amenities like pools or hot tubs</li>
<li>You want the highest possible coverage limits</li>
</ul>
<p>Commercial policies cost more than personal STR policies, but they provide much stronger protection for serious rental businesses.</p>
<h2 id="liability-coverage-deep-dive">Liability Coverage Deep Dive</h2>
<p>Liability is often the biggest risk for short-term rental hosts. A single lawsuit from a guest injury can cost hundreds of thousands of dollars.</p>
<h3 id="common-liability-scenarios">Common Liability Scenarios</h3>
<p>Here are real-world situations where liability coverage protects you:</p>
<ul>
<li>A guest trips on a loose step and breaks their arm</li>
<li>A child is injured in the pool area</li>
<li>A guest gets food poisoning from contaminated water</li>
<li>Carbon monoxide from a faulty heater makes a guest sick</li>
<li>A balcony railing fails and a guest falls</li>
<li>A guest's dog bites another guest at your property</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="how-much-liability-coverage-do-you-need">How Much Liability Coverage Do You Need?</h3>
<p>Most insurance professionals recommend at least $1 million in liability coverage for short-term rental hosts. If you have significant personal assets to protect, consider $2 million or more.</p>
<p>The cost difference between $500,000 and $1 million in liability coverage is usually small, often just $100 to $200 per year. The extra protection is well worth it.</p>
<h2 id="damage-protection-strategies">Damage Protection Strategies</h2>
<p>Insurance is your last resort for handling damage. Smart hosts also use other strategies to prevent and manage damage.</p>
<h3 id="security-deposits">Security Deposits</h3>
<p>Many platforms allow you to set a security deposit. This gives you a buffer for minor damage without filing an insurance claim. Common deposit amounts range from $250 to $1,000 depending on the property value and amenities.</p>
<h3 id="guest-screening">Guest Screening</h3>
<p>Screening guests before they book can reduce damage risk. Look at their review history, verify their identity, and communicate your house rules clearly. Some hosts use third-party screening services for additional protection.</p>
<h3 id="house-rules-and-agreements">House Rules and Agreements</h3>
<p>Clear house rules set expectations and protect you legally. Your rules should cover:</p>
<ul>
<li>Maximum occupancy</li>
<li>No smoking policy</li>
<li>No parties or events</li>
<li>Quiet hours</li>
<li>Pet policy</li>
<li>Parking rules</li>
<li>Pool and hot tub rules</li>
</ul>
<p>Have guests acknowledge these rules before check-in. This creates a record that can support damage or liability claims.</p>
<p>For more on short-term rental regulations and compliance, read our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-regulations">short-term rental regulations guide</a>.</p>
<h2 id="lost-income-coverage">Lost Income Coverage</h2>
<p>Lost income coverage, also called business interruption insurance, reimburses you for rental income lost when your property is uninhabitable due to a covered event.</p>
<h3 id="how-lost-income-coverage-works">How Lost Income Coverage Works</h3>
<p>If a fire, flood, or major damage forces you to cancel bookings, lost income coverage pays you what you would have earned. The payout is based on your booking history and market rates.</p>
<p>Most policies cover lost income for a set period, typically 12 to 24 months. Some policies have a waiting period of 24 to 72 hours before coverage kicks in.</p>
<h3 id="calculating-your-lost-income-needs">Calculating Your Lost Income Needs</h3>
<p>To determine how much lost income coverage you need, calculate your average monthly rental income over the past 12 months. Multiply that by the number of months it could take to fully repair your property after a major event. For most hosts, six to twelve months of coverage is sufficient.</p>
<h2 id="umbrella-policies">Umbrella Policies</h2>
<p>An umbrella policy provides extra liability coverage beyond what your STR insurance offers. It sits on top of your existing policies and kicks in when those limits are exhausted.</p>
<h3 id="why-hosts-need-umbrella-coverage">Why Hosts Need Umbrella Coverage</h3>
<p>Umbrella policies are affordable and provide significant protection. A $1 million umbrella policy typically costs $200 to $400 per year. If a guest sues you and the judgment exceeds your STR policy limits, the umbrella policy covers the difference.</p>
<h3 id="how-umbrella-policies-work-with-str-insurance">How Umbrella Policies Work with STR Insurance</h3>
<p>Your umbrella policy extends over all your underlying insurance policies, including your STR policy, auto insurance, and personal liability coverage. It creates a consistent layer of extra protection across your entire financial life.</p>
<p>Most umbrella policies require you to carry minimum coverage limits on your underlying policies. Check with your umbrella provider to make sure your STR policy meets their requirements. For more on structuring your overall liability protection, see our guide on <a href="/blog/landlord-liability-protection">landlord liability protection</a>.</p>
<h2 id="choosing-an-insurance-provider">Choosing an Insurance Provider</h2>
<p>With many insurance companies now offering STR-specific products, choosing the right provider takes some research.</p>
<h3 id="top-str-insurance-providers">Top STR Insurance Providers</h3>
<p>Several companies specialize in short-term rental insurance:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Proper Insurance</strong> is one of the most well-known STR insurers. They offer comprehensive policies designed specifically for vacation rental hosts.</li>
<li><strong>CBIZ</strong> provides commercial-grade coverage for professional STR operators.</li>
<li><strong>Safely</strong> offers per-booking coverage that can supplement your annual policy.</li>
<li><strong>Steadily</strong> focuses on landlord and STR insurance with a simple online application process.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="what-to-look-for-in-a-provider">What to Look for in a Provider</h3>
<p>When comparing providers, evaluate:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Coverage scope.</strong> Make sure the policy covers guest damage, liability, lost income, and contents.</li>
<li><strong>Coverage limits.</strong> Check the maximum payout for each category of coverage.</li>
<li><strong>Deductibles.</strong> Lower deductibles mean higher premiums, but less out-of-pocket cost per claim.</li>
<li><strong>Claims process.</strong> Read reviews about how the company handles claims. Fast, fair claims processing matters most when you actually need your insurance.</li>
<li><strong>Platform compatibility.</strong> Ensure the policy works with all the platforms you list on, not just one.</li>
<li><strong>Price.</strong> Get quotes from at least three providers. STR insurance typically costs $1,500 to $3,500 per year per property, depending on location, value, and coverage limits.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="questions-to-ask-before-you-buy">Questions to Ask Before You Buy</h3>
<p>Before purchasing a policy, ask these questions:</p>
<ol>
<li>Does this policy cover damage caused by short-term rental guests?</li>
<li>What is the liability coverage limit?</li>
<li>Does it cover lost rental income during repairs?</li>
<li>Are there exclusions for specific amenities like pools or hot tubs?</li>
<li>How do I file a claim and what is the typical processing time?</li>
<li>Does the policy cover bookings from all platforms and direct bookings?</li>
<li>Can I add additional properties to the same policy?</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="building-your-insurance-stack">Building Your Insurance Stack</h2>
<p>The most protected hosts use a layered approach to insurance. Here is a recommended stack:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>STR-specific insurance policy</strong> as your foundation, covering property damage, liability, contents, and lost income</li>
<li><strong>Platform protection programs</strong> like AirCover as a supplementary layer</li>
<li><strong>Umbrella policy</strong> for extra liability protection beyond your STR policy limits</li>
<li><strong>Security deposits</strong> on each booking for minor damage</li>
<li><strong>Guest screening</strong> and clear house rules to reduce risk</li>
</ol>
<p>This multi-layer approach ensures you are covered no matter what happens. No single layer is perfect, but together they create strong protection.</p>

<p>Short-term rental insurance is not optional. It is a core business expense that protects your investment, your income, and your personal assets. Take the time to find the right policy, review it annually, and keep your coverage current as your portfolio grows.</p>
