29:T3a26,<p>The Airbnb Superhost badge is one of the most visible trust signals on the platform. For guests, it signals a reliably great experience. For hosts, it translates into better search visibility, higher booking rates, and stronger long-term income.</p>
<p>But earning — and keeping — Superhost status requires consistent performance across several metrics. Many hosts fall short not because they are bad hosts, but because they do not fully understand how Airbnb evaluates their account or what triggers a status review.</p>
<p>This guide covers the exact <strong>Airbnb Superhost requirements</strong>, how the assessment works, what benefits you gain, and the specific strategies experienced hosts use to maintain elite status year after year.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-is-airbnb-superhost-status">What Is Airbnb Superhost Status?</h2>
<p>Superhost is a designation Airbnb awards to its highest-performing hosts. It is reassessed quarterly — every January 1, April 1, July 1, and October 1 — based on your hosting activity over the past 12 months.</p>
<p>Superhost status is not a permanent achievement. It must be re-earned at each quarterly review. Hosts who fall below any of the criteria lose the badge until they meet the thresholds again.</p>
<p>The Superhost badge appears on your profile, your listings, and in search results. It is one of Airbnb's most effective trust indicators for prospective guests choosing between comparable properties.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-4-official-airbnb-superhost-requirements">The 4 Official Airbnb Superhost Requirements</h2>
<p>To earn or retain Superhost status, you must meet all four criteria simultaneously:</p>
<h3 id="1-complete-at-least-10-stays-or-100-nights-on-3-reservations-in-the-past-year">1. Complete at Least 10 Stays (or 100 Nights on 3+ Reservations) in the Past Year</h3>
<p>You need a minimum hosting volume to be evaluated. Specifically:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>10 or more completed stays</strong>, OR</li>
<li><strong>100 nights across at least 3 reservations</strong> (for hosts with longer-stay properties where individual bookings are fewer but longer)</li>
</ul>
<p>This threshold ensures Superhost status reflects a meaningful sample of hosting activity, not a handful of lucky stays.</p>
<h3 id="2-maintain-a-90-or-higher-response-rate">2. Maintain a 90% or Higher Response Rate</h3>
<p>Your response rate measures how often you respond to new messages and booking requests within 24 hours. Airbnb calculates this over the past 365 days.</p>
<p>Important nuances:</p>
<ul>
<li>You must respond to the first message in each new conversation thread</li>
<li>If you decline or pre-approve a request within 24 hours, that counts as responding</li>
<li>Using Instant Book does not exempt you from the response rate requirement for inquiries</li>
</ul>
<p>A 90% response rate means you can miss no more than 1 in 10 initial messages. For most active hosts, maintaining 100% is the realistic goal.</p>
<h3 id="3-maintain-a-1-or-lower-cancellation-rate">3. Maintain a 1% or Lower Cancellation Rate</h3>
<p>Airbnb calculates your cancellation rate as the number of reservations you cancelled divided by the total number of reservations you received. The threshold is <strong>1% or less</strong>, which for most hosts means zero cancellations.</p>
<p>For context: if you complete 50 stays per year, even a single host-initiated cancellation puts you at a 2% rate — enough to lose Superhost status.</p>
<p>Exceptions: Airbnb does allow certain extenuating circumstance cancellations that do not count against your rate. Natural disasters, government travel restrictions, and similar events may qualify.</p>
<h3 id="4-maintain-a-4-8-or-higher-overall-rating">4. Maintain a 4.8 or Higher Overall Rating</h3>
<p>Your overall rating must average at least <strong>4.8 stars</strong> across all reviews from the past 365 days. This is calculated from the overall star rating guests leave — not the category-specific sub-ratings (cleanliness, accuracy, communication, etc.).</p>
<p>A 4.8 average sounds high, but it is achievable with consistent effort. A single 3-star review from 20 stays can drop your average to 4.85. Two bad reviews can push it to 4.7 — below the threshold.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="how-airbnb-evaluates-superhost-status">How Airbnb Evaluates Superhost Status</h2>
<p>Airbnb assesses Superhost status <strong>four times per year</strong>, looking back at a rolling 12-month window:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>January 1</strong> — Reviews activity from January 1 to December 31 of the previous year</li>
<li><strong>April 1</strong> — Reviews activity from April 1 to March 31</li>
<li><strong>July 1</strong> — Reviews activity from July 1 to June 30</li>
<li><strong>October 1</strong> — Reviews activity from October 1 to September 30</li>
</ul>
<p>You do not need to apply for Superhost. If you meet all four criteria at the time of a quarterly assessment, Airbnb automatically awards you the badge. If you fall below any criteria, the badge is removed — often without individual notification.</p>
<p>Check your progress at any time in the Airbnb app under <strong>Profile > Superhost status</strong>.</p>

<h2 id="benefits-of-airbnb-superhost-status">Benefits of Airbnb Superhost Status</h2>
<p>Superhost is not just a vanity badge. It delivers measurable business benefits:</p>
<h3 id="improved-search-visibility">Improved Search Visibility</h3>
<p>Airbnb confirms that Superhosts receive a boost in search ranking. In competitive markets, this can be the difference between appearing on page one or being buried.</p>
<h3 id="guest-trust-and-higher-conversion">Guest Trust and Higher Conversion</h3>
<p><a href="https://www.investopedia.com/terms/r/rentalreal-estate-loss-allowance.asp">Investopedia covers rental income</a> tax implications worth understanding. Many guests filter search results to show only Superhosts. Without the badge, you are invisible to this segment of the market. Guests who see the Superhost badge also convert at higher rates — they book faster and inquire less.</p>
<h3 id="exclusive-superhost-rewards">Exclusive Superhost Rewards</h3>
<p>Airbnb periodically offers Superhost-exclusive rewards including:</p>
<ul>
<li>$100 USD travel coupon after each year of Superhost status</li>
<li>Priority access to new Airbnb features</li>
<li>Dedicated Superhost support line (faster response times from Airbnb customer service)</li>
<li>Invitation to Superhost ambassador programs in some markets</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="competitive-advantage-during-disputes">Competitive Advantage During Disputes</h3>
<p>While not official policy, hosts in the Superhost community report that Airbnb customer service tends to side with Superhosts during resolution disputes, given the track record of quality hosting.</p>

<h2 id="strategies-to-earn-airbnb-superhost-status">Strategies to Earn Airbnb Superhost Status</h2>
<h3 id="master-your-response-rate">Master Your Response Rate</h3>
<p><strong>Set up instant replies.</strong> Airbnb lets you configure an auto-reply message that goes out immediately to every new inquiry. Even a simple acknowledgment ("Thanks for reaching out — I'll respond with full details within a few hours") counts as a response for rate calculation purposes.</p>
<p><strong>Use the Airbnb app.</strong> Enable push notifications for every new message. Commit to responding within one hour for all inquiries, giving you buffer on your 24-hour window.</p>
<p><strong>Use a co-host or messaging tool.</strong> If you are managing multiple properties or cannot always be available, a co-host or <a href="/blog/airbnb-automation-tools">airbnb automation tools</a> can handle initial responses automatically.</p>
<h3 id="protect-your-cancellation-rate">Protect Your Cancellation Rate</h3>
<p><strong>Never cancel unless absolutely necessary.</strong> Even one host-initiated cancellation per year can cost you Superhost status on smaller portfolios.</p>
<p><strong>Screen guests carefully before confirming.</strong> If you use Instant Book, review your Instant Book settings to require government ID verification and a track record of positive reviews. Screening before acceptance reduces the need to cancel after the fact.</p>
<p><strong>Understand what counts as a host cancellation.</strong> Cancellations initiated by guests do not count against your rate. If a guest asks to cancel, let them initiate the process through Airbnb rather than cancelling on their behalf.</p>
<h3 id="build-and-maintain-a-4-8-rating">Build and Maintain a 4.8+ Rating</h3>
<p><strong>Set accurate expectations.</strong> The <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftcs-endorsement-guides-what-people-are-asking">FTC endorsement guidelines</a> require honest marketing claims. The leading cause of disappointing reviews is the property not matching the listing. Use honest photos, accurately list all amenities, and describe any quirks (stairs, street noise, shared laundry) upfront.</p>
<p><strong>Deliver exceptional cleanliness.</strong> Cleanliness is the sub-rating that guests weigh most heavily when leaving overall ratings. A professional-grade cleaning process is non-negotiable. Our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-cleaning-schedule">short-term rental cleaning schedule</a> guide provides a full framework.</p>
<p><strong>Communicate proactively.</strong> Send a pre-arrival message with detailed check-in instructions, local recommendations, and your contact information. Check in mid-stay with a brief message. Guests who feel cared for rate higher.</p>
<p><strong>Respond gracefully to issues.</strong> When something goes wrong (it will), your response speed and professionalism determine whether a guest leaves a 5-star or a 3-star review. A rapid, solution-focused response can turn a problem into a positive story guests share in their review.</p>
<p><strong>Leave reviews first.</strong> Research consistently shows that when hosts leave reviews first, guests are more likely to leave reviews in return — and often with a positive sentiment influenced by seeing a thoughtful host review.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="common-mistakes-that-cost-hosts-superhost-status">Common Mistakes That Cost Hosts Superhost Status</h2>
<p><strong>Missing a single inquiry message.</strong> Response rate drops fast if you are traveling, dealing with an emergency, or simply distracted. Automation and co-hosting are essential safeguards.</p>
<p><strong>Cancelling a problem booking.</strong> Hosts sometimes cancel to avoid a guest they are uncomfortable with. A better approach is to use Airbnb's resolution center or contact Airbnb support for help managing the situation without initiating a host cancellation.</p>
<p><strong>Underestimating the impact of one bad review.</strong> On a small review sample (fewer than 25 stays), a single 3-star review can push your average below 4.8. Focus intensely on each guest experience, especially in your early hosting months.</p>
<p><strong>Ignoring cleanliness standards.</strong> Many hosts discover their rating drops are almost entirely tied to cleanliness scores. Invest in the cleaning process before anything else.</p>
<p><strong>Not checking your Superhost dashboard.</strong> Airbnb provides a real-time tracker in your account. Check it monthly to catch any issues before a quarterly assessment.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-happens-if-you-lose-superhost-status">What Happens If You Lose Superhost Status?</h2>
<p>Losing Superhost status is common — even experienced hosts go through periods where a rough stay or an unusual circumstance drops them below the threshold.</p>
<p>If you lose the badge:</p>
<ol>
<li>Review your Superhost dashboard to identify which metric dropped below the threshold</li>
<li>Focus specifically on improving that area</li>
<li>Continue hosting consistently — status can be recovered at the next quarterly review</li>
</ol>
<p>Superhost status lost is not permanent. Many hosts lose and regain it multiple times. The key is understanding why it happened and making targeted improvements.</p>

</tbody>
</table>
<p>It is possible to be a Superhost without having a Guest Favorite listing, and to have a Guest Favorite listing without being a Superhost. Pursuing both provides the strongest trust signal for prospective guests.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="superhost-status-for-multi-property-hosts">Superhost Status for Multi-Property Hosts</h2>
<p>If you manage multiple Airbnb listings, your Superhost status is evaluated at the account level — all properties combined. This means:</p>
<ul>
<li>One poorly performing property can drag down your overall metrics</li>
<li>A single cancellation or missed response affects the entire portfolio</li>
<li>Your 4.8 rating average is calculated across all your completed stays</li>
</ul>
<p>Multi-property hosts should establish strong systems for each property. Our <a href="/blog/airbnb-property-management-tips">airbnb property management tips</a> guide covers the operational frameworks that keep multi-property performance consistent.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Airbnb Superhost status is one of the most valuable credentials an STR host can hold. The requirements — 10+ stays, 90%+ response rate, 1% or lower cancellation rate, and 4.8+ overall rating — are achievable with the right systems and mindset.</p>
<p>The hosts who maintain Superhost status year after year are not the ones with the most luxurious properties. They are the ones with the most consistent processes: automated responses, professional cleaning, accurate listings, and proactive guest communication.</p>
<p>Build those systems now, and Superhost status will follow — and stay.</p>
<p><strong>Superhost Requirements at a Glance:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>10+ stays completed (or 100 nights across 3+ stays) in the past 12 months</li>
<li>90% or higher response rate</li>
<li>1% or lower cancellation rate</li>
<li>4.8 or higher overall rating</li>
<li>Assessed quarterly: January 1, April 1, July 1, October 1</li>
</ul>
