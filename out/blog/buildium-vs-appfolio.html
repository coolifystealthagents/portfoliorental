Buildium and AppFolio are two of the most widely used property management platforms in the industry. Both serve professional landlords and property management companies, both handle the full operational workflow, and both carry serious price tags compared to entry-level tools.

So how do you choose between them?

The answer depends on your portfolio type, team size, and what features matter most to your operation. This comparison breaks down both platforms across every major dimension, pricing, features, ease of use, reporting, integrations, and customer support, so you can make an informed decision.

Pricing Comparison

Buildium Pricing

Buildium uses a tiered pricing model:

  • Essential: ~$58/month for up to 150 units, includes basic property management, online payments, maintenance requests, and reporting
  • Growth: ~$183/month, adds unlimited e-leasing, property inspection tools, and priority support
  • Premium: ~$375/month, includes full accounting, open API access, and dedicated account management

Additional per-transaction fees apply for payments and tenant screening. The total monthly cost for a 100-unit portfolio on the Essential plan, including transaction fees, typically runs $100, $150/month.

Buildium's entry-level pricing is accessible for landlords starting to professionalize their operations. The 14-day free trial lets you test the platform before committing.

AppFolio Pricing

AppFolio charges per unit per month, with a minimum monthly spend:

  • Core: ~$1.40/unit/month (minimum $298/month), covers core residential management
  • Plus: ~$3.00/unit/month, adds AI leasing tools, advanced analytics, and revenue management
  • Max: Custom pricing, designed for enterprise portfolios with advanced customization needs

AppFolio's minimum spend makes it impractical for portfolios under 50 units. At 100 units on the Core plan, you're looking at roughly $140, $200/month in base fees plus transaction costs.

For portfolios of 200+ units, AppFolio's per-unit pricing often compares favorably to Buildium's tier-based structure.

Feature-by-Feature Comparison

Accounting and Financial Reporting

Both platforms include full property management accounting, general ledger, accounts payable and receivable, bank reconciliation, and financial statement generation.

Buildium offers strong accounting tools, including owner and tenant ledgers, budget management, and automated bank reconciliation. The financial reports cover standard needs: profit and loss, balance sheet, cash flow, and trust accounting. The interface is clear enough that non-accountants can use it effectively.

AppFolio has a more sophisticated accounting engine, particularly for commercial leases with percentage rent, CAM reconciliation, and custom lease terms. If you manage commercial or mixed-use properties alongside residential, AppFolio handles the complexity better.

Winner: AppFolio for complex portfolios. Buildium for standard residential.

Leasing and Tenant Screening

Buildium integrates with TransUnion for tenant screening, credit reports, background checks, and eviction history. The leasing workflow covers vacancy advertising, online applications, screening, lease generation, and e-signatures. Listings sync to Zillow, Trulia, Hotpads, and other sites.

AppFolio also handles the full leasing workflow and includes its AI Leasing Assistant, which responds to prospect inquiries 24/7, schedules showings, and moves applicants through the funnel with minimal manual involvement. For property managers handling high vacancy volume, this feature delivers measurable time savings.

Winner: AppFolio on leasing automation. Buildium is competitive for landlords who prefer more manual control.

Maintenance Management

Both platforms handle maintenance requests through a tenant-facing portal, with work order creation, vendor assignment, and status tracking.

Buildium supports maintenance request intake, work order management, and vendor payments through the platform. The inspection tool (available on Growth and above) allows mobile-based property inspections with photos and condition ratings.

AppFolio offers similar capabilities plus a maintenance contact center add-on, a 24/7 maintenance call service that AppFolio staff can handle on your behalf. This is particularly useful for property managers who don't want to field 2 a.m. emergency calls.

Winner: AppFolio if 24/7 maintenance coverage matters. Buildium for standard DIY maintenance management.

Reporting and Analytics

Buildium covers standard property management reports well, vacancy rates, rent roll, delinquency, owner statements, and financial summaries. The report library is full enough for most residential landlords and their accountants.

AppFolio Plus introduces AI-driven insights, customizable dashboards, and revenue management tools that analyze market data to recommend rental pricing. For operators running large portfolios where improving rents matters, this data layer is genuinely useful.

Winner: AppFolio Plus for analytics-driven operators. Buildium for standard reporting needs.

Website and Marketing Tools

Buildium includes a basic website builder that creates a branded property management site, handles tenant and owner portals, and publishes vacancy listings. It's functional but not sophisticated.

AppFolio provides a more robust marketing site option and tighter integration with listing platforms. The AI leasing tools extend to marketing by managing initial prospect communication at scale.

Winner: AppFolio for marketing-focused operations. Buildium for basic online presence.

Mobile App

Both platforms have capable mobile apps. AppFolio's app is generally considered the more polished experience, with a faster interface and more features available on mobile.

Buildium's app handles maintenance, communications, and basic reporting on mobile. Full accounting and reporting workflows often push you back to the browser.

Winner: AppFolio on mobile experience.

Ease of Use

Buildium has a reputation for being easier to onboard. The interface is well-organized, the setup wizard walks you through initial configuration, and the learning curve is manageable for landlords who have not used property management software before.

AppFolio has more depth, which means more complexity. New users often need dedicated onboarding time, and the platform rewards users who invest in learning it.

AppFolio provides onboarding support, but the tool is built for professional operators who will use it intensively, not occasional users.

Winner: Buildium for ease of use, particularly for first-time software adopters.

Customer Support

Buildium offers phone, chat, and email support on business hours. Premium plan subscribers get dedicated account management. Support quality is generally well-rated.

AppFolio provides phone and email support, with an online help center and community forum. Enterprise clients get dedicated support resources. Response times and quality are solid, but support is less accessible on lower-tier plans.

Winner: Tie. Both are competitive, with advantage to Buildium for phone accessibility and AppFolio for enterprise-level support.

Integrations

Both platforms integrate with major third-party tools:

Buildium connects with TransUnion (screening), PayNearMe (in-person payments), and a range of accounting tools via its open API on the Premium plan.

AppFolio integrates with a wider range of tools including revenue management platforms, insurance providers, and utility billing services. The API is available on Plus and Max plans.

For a broader overview of how these tools fit into a full software stack, see our property management software comparison and the complete guide to property management services.

Who Should Choose Buildium

Buildium is the better choice if:

  • Your portfolio is under 200 units and primarily residential
  • You are new to property management software and want a manageable learning curve
  • You manage associations (HOAs, condos) alongside residential units, Buildium has strong association management tools
  • Budget predictability matters, Buildium's flat-tier pricing is easier to plan around

Who Should Choose AppFolio

AppFolio is the better choice if:

  • Your portfolio is 50+ units, particularly if it includes commercial or mixed-use
  • High vacancy turnover makes automated leasing tools a priority
  • You want AI-driven pricing and analytics to improve portfolio performance
  • You manage multiple property types with complex lease structures

Alternatives Worth Considering

Neither platform is the right fit for every operation. Smaller landlords may find better value in tools like TurboTenant, Avail, or TenantCloud, see our free property management software overview for zero-cost options.

Larger enterprise operations may want to evaluate Yardi or RealPage.

Summary

Buildium and AppFolio are both serious platforms built for professional property management. Buildium wins on accessibility, pricing transparency, and association management.

AppFolio wins on AI-powered leasing, mobile experience, and analytics depth.

For a 50, 150 unit residential portfolio where ease of use and predictable pricing matter, Buildium is the stronger starting point. For a 200+ unit operation, especially one with commercial assets or high vacancy volume, AppFolio's additional capabilities justify the higher cost.

Take advantage of Buildium's free trial before committing. AppFolio typically offers demo calls with their sales team that provide a clear picture of the platform's capabilities before any purchase decision.
