28:T329f,<p>TenantCloud is one of the more comprehensive platforms in the independent landlord market. It handles the full property management workflow—from vacancy listings to lease signing, rent collection, maintenance requests, and accounting—under one roof and at a price point that competes with both free alternatives and premium platforms.</p>
<p>This review examines what TenantCloud actually delivers, where it performs well, where it falls short, and how it compares to other options available to small and mid-size landlords.</p>
<h2 id="tenantcloud-at-a-glance">TenantCloud at a Glance</h2>
<p>TenantCloud was founded in 2014 and has grown to serve hundreds of thousands of landlords. It targets independent landlords and small property management companies—the market of operators managing between 1 and 200 units who need real functionality without enterprise pricing.</p>
<p>The platform covers:</p>
<ul>
<li>Vacancy marketing and listing syndication</li>
<li>Online rental applications</li>
<li>Tenant screening</li>
<li>Lease management and e-signatures</li>
<li>Online rent collection</li>
<li>Maintenance request management</li>
<li>Accounting and financial reporting</li>
<li>Owner and tenant portals</li>
</ul>
<p>This breadth makes TenantCloud unusual in the free-and-low-cost segment. Most free platforms sacrifice operational features for accounting, or accounting for operations. TenantCloud attempts both.</p>
<h2 id="tenantcloud-pricing">TenantCloud Pricing</h2>
<p>TenantCloud uses a tiered pricing model with a free entry point:</p>
<h3 id="free-plan">Free Plan</h3>
<ul>
<li>Up to 75 units</li>
<li>Basic rent collection</li>
<li>Tenant portal</li>
<li>Maintenance requests</li>
<li>Listings on TenantCloud's marketplace</li>
</ul>
<p>The free plan works for landlords who want the basic infrastructure without paying for features they don't use yet. However, several key features—e-signatures, listing syndication to major sites, and advanced accounting—require a paid upgrade.</p>
<h3 id="starter-plan-17-month">Starter Plan (~$17/month)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Up to 150 units</li>
<li>Listing syndication to Zillow, Apartment List, and others</li>
<li>E-signatures on leases</li>
<li>Maintenance scheduling</li>
<li>Basic accounting</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="growth-plan-40-month">Growth Plan (~$40/month)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Up to 500 units</li>
<li>Advanced accounting features</li>
<li>Property performance reports</li>
<li>Priority support</li>
<li>Custom lease templates</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="business-plan-65-month">Business Plan (~$65/month)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Unlimited units</li>
<li>White-label tenant and owner portals</li>
<li>Business analytics</li>
<li>Dedicated account support</li>
</ul>
<p>Pricing is subject to change—always verify current rates on TenantCloud's website. Per-transaction fees apply for tenant screening reports (typically $35–$45 per applicant for full credit, background, and eviction reports).</p>
<p>For landlords comparing TenantCloud to free alternatives, see our overview of <a href="/blog/free-property-management-software">free property management software</a>. For a broader market comparison, see the <a href="/blog/property-management-software-comparison">property management software comparison</a>.</p>
<h2 id="feature-review">Feature Review</h2>
<h3 id="vacancy-listings-and-marketing">Vacancy Listings and Marketing</h3>
<p>TenantCloud's free plan lists vacancies on its own marketplace. The Starter plan and above syndicate to Zillow, Trulia, Apartment List, Rentable, and other platforms.</p>
<p>The listing creation tool is straightforward—add property details, upload photos, set availability, and publish. Listings are kept current across synced platforms when you update them in TenantCloud.</p>
<p>One limitation: TenantCloud does not currently syndicate to Apartments.com (which requires its own landlord account through Cozy/Realtor.com). For landlords who want maximum listing distribution, running TenantCloud alongside an Apartments.com account may be necessary.</p>
<h3 id="online-applications-and-tenant-screening">Online Applications and Tenant Screening</h3>
<p>Applications are customizable. You can add questions beyond the standard form—employment verification, pet ownership, move-in timeline—and configure which documents applicants must upload.</p>
<p>Tenant screening is paid per report and runs through TransUnion. Standard reports include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Credit report with score</li>
<li>Criminal background check</li>
<li>Eviction history</li>
</ul>
<p>Reports are typically returned within minutes. The screening workflow is embedded in the application process, which keeps the flow clean for applicants.</p>
<h3 id="lease-management-and-e-signatures">Lease Management and E-Signatures</h3>
<p>TenantCloud includes lease templates for all 50 states on paid plans. Templates are editable, allowing you to add custom clauses, addenda, and property-specific terms.</p>
<p>E-signatures are fully supported on Starter and above. Tenants sign digitally, signed documents are stored in the platform, and both parties receive copies. For landlords who still email PDF leases and wait for wet signatures, upgrading to e-signatures alone often justifies the Starter plan cost.</p>
<h3 id="rent-collection">Rent Collection</h3>
<p>Rent collection is a core feature across all plans. Tenants pay through the tenant portal via ACH (bank transfer) or credit card.</p>
<p>ACH payments: Free for tenants on paid landlord plans; fees may apply on the free tier.
Credit card payments: Tenants typically pay a 3.5% processing fee.</p>
<p>Autopay is supported—tenants can set up recurring payments, which significantly reduces late payments. Late fees can be configured to apply automatically after a grace period.</p>
<p>Payment history is tracked in the tenant ledger, making it easy to document on-time payment history for lease renewals or references.</p>
<h3 id="maintenance-requests">Maintenance Requests</h3>
<p>Tenants submit maintenance requests through the portal with descriptions and photos. Landlords receive notifications, can assign requests to vendors, and track status through completion.</p>
<p>The maintenance workflow is functional but not deeply developed. There is no built-in vendor marketplace or purchasing system. Vendor management is essentially a contact list—you assign a request to a vendor you've already added, then communicate outside the platform.</p>
<p>For landlords with established vendor relationships, this is fine. For those building out a maintenance network, the limitations are noticeable compared to platforms like Rent Manager or AppFolio that have more developed vendor management tools.</p>
<h3 id="accounting-and-financial-reporting">Accounting and Financial Reporting</h3>
<p>TenantCloud's accounting module covers income and expense tracking, owner statements, and basic financial reports. On the Growth and Business plans, reporting includes property performance analytics and comparison across multiple units.</p>
<p>What TenantCloud accounting does well:</p>
<ul>
<li>Tracking rent collected and outstanding balances</li>
<li>Logging maintenance and other expenses</li>
<li>Generating basic profit and loss summaries</li>
<li>Producing owner statements for investors</li>
</ul>
<p>What it does less well:</p>
<ul>
<li>Full general ledger accounting with accounts payable/receivable detail</li>
<li>Bank account reconciliation (present but not as smooth as dedicated tools)</li>
<li>Complex multi-entity accounting</li>
</ul>
<p>Landlords who need robust accounting often supplement TenantCloud with a dedicated tool like Stessa for financial tracking, or QuickBooks for full accounting. See the <a href="/blog/best-property-management-software-for-portfolios">best property management software for portfolios</a> guide for how these tools can work together.</p>
<h3 id="owner-portal">Owner Portal</h3>
<p>For landlords managing properties on behalf of investors or co-owners, TenantCloud's owner portal (available on Growth and above) allows owners to log in and view statements, financial performance, and property details.</p>
<p>This feature is more relevant to property managers than to individual landlords, but it is a meaningful differentiator for anyone starting to manage properties for others.</p>
<h2 id="mobile-app">Mobile App</h2>
<p>TenantCloud's mobile app covers core functions—rent tracking, maintenance requests, tenant communications, and payment notifications. The app is available for iOS and Android.</p>
<p>The mobile experience is functional but not leading the category. Complex tasks like accounting review or lease generation push you toward the desktop interface. For day-to-day monitoring and maintenance management, the app handles the basics adequately.</p>
<h2 id="ease-of-use">Ease of Use</h2>
<p>TenantCloud's interface is organized and reasonably intuitive for new users. The dashboard gives a clean summary of current occupancy, pending maintenance requests, upcoming rent due dates, and recent payments.</p>
<p>Setup takes an afternoon for a small portfolio—add properties, set up units, invite tenants, configure rent collection. The platform guides you through the process with in-app prompts.</p>
<p>The biggest friction point is the pricing gate on certain features. The free plan is functional, but landlords frequently hit limits that push toward paid plans—particularly around e-signatures and listing syndication. This is by design, but it can feel like a feature that was bundled into free and then pulled into paid without clear upfront communication.</p>
<h2 id="customer-support">Customer Support</h2>
<p>TenantCloud offers support via email, live chat (on paid plans), and an online help center with documentation and video tutorials.</p>
<p>Phone support is not available, which is a notable gap compared to platforms like Buildium and Rent Manager. For landlords who prefer talking through a problem, the lack of phone support is a real limitation.</p>
<p>Response times for email and chat support are generally 24 hours or less on paid plans, longer on the free tier.</p>
<h2 id="who-tenantcloud-works-best-for">Who TenantCloud Works Best For</h2>
<p>TenantCloud is a strong fit for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Independent landlords managing 1–75 units who want one platform for operations and accounting</li>
<li>Landlords who need listing syndication, applications, screening, and rent collection without paying enterprise prices</li>
<li>Landlords ready to move beyond spreadsheets who want a guided, organized system</li>
<li>Those looking for a solid free starting point with a clear upgrade path</li>
</ul>
<p>TenantCloud is not the best fit for:</p>
<ul>
<li>Landlords who need robust accounting comparable to QuickBooks</li>
<li>Large portfolios that need full vendor management and purchasing systems</li>
<li>Operators who want AI-driven leasing automation</li>
<li>Anyone who requires phone-based customer support</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="verdict">Verdict</h2>
<p>TenantCloud delivers more operational functionality per dollar than most alternatives in its price range. The free plan is a genuine starting point, not a stripped-down trial. The paid plans are priced reasonably for what they include.</p>
<p>The platform's weaknesses—limited phone support, basic vendor management, and accounting depth that requires supplementation for complex portfolios—are real but manageable for its target market.</p>
<p>If you are an independent landlord managing under 100 units and want a single platform that covers listings, applications, leases, rent collection, and basic financials without a large monthly bill, TenantCloud is worth a serious look.</p>

<h2 id="summary">Summary</h2>
<p>TenantCloud is a competent, comprehensive platform for independent landlords. Its breadth—covering the full operational workflow from listing to lease to maintenance—at a low price point makes it one of the better values in the market. The free plan is a workable starting point, and the paid tiers are reasonably priced for the functionality they unlock.</p>
<p>The platform's primary limitations are accounting depth and customer support access. For most independent landlords, these trade-offs are acceptable given the cost.
