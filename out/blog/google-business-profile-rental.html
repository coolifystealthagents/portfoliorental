When a prospective tenant types "apartments for rent near me" into Google, what determines who appears at the top of the results? In most cases, it comes down to one factor: a well-optimized Google Business Profile. Yet most landlords and property managers overlook this completely free, high-impact tool.

A Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) puts your rental business on the map — literally. It populates your listing in Google Search and Google Maps, giving potential tenants a snapshot of your properties, reviews, contact details, and more before they even visit your website.

This guide walks you through every step of setting up and optimizing a Google Business Profile for your rental property business, so you can attract more qualified leads without paying for ads.

Why Google Business Profile Matters for Landlords

Before diving into setup, it is worth understanding why this tool deserves your attention.

Local search dominates rental queries. The vast majority of tenant searches include location-based terms — "two-bedroom apartments in Austin" or "pet-friendly rentals downtown." Google's local search results (the "map pack" that appears at the top of results) are powered almost entirely by Google Business Profiles.

It builds instant trust. A verified Google Business Profile displays your star rating, reviews, photos, and hours of operation. Tenants can see that your business is legitimate before they make any contact.

It is free advertising. Unlike running Google Ads for rental properties, a Google Business Profile costs nothing. The investment is purely your time.

It complements your broader marketing. Whether you are leveraging social media marketing for your rental property or optimizing your listings on the best rental listing sites, a Google Business Profile acts as your anchor in local search and reinforces every other channel you use.

Who Should Create a Google Business Profile

A Google Business Profile works best for:

  • Property management companies with a physical office or service area
  • Independent landlords who manage multiple properties and treat rental as a business
  • Real estate investors who want to establish a local brand presence

If you own a single property and rent it casually, a full business profile may be overkill. But if you manage three or more units — or plan to scale — setting up a profile now pays dividends over time.

Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your Google Business Profile

Step 1: Create or Sign In to Your Google Account

Use a dedicated Google account for your rental business rather than a personal Gmail. This keeps your business communications organized and makes it easier to manage access if you add staff or a property manager later.

Step 2: Go to Google Business Profile Manager

Navigate to Google's Business Profile manager (search "Google Business Profile" to find the official Google product page). Click "Manage now" to begin.

Step 3: Enter Your Business Name

Use your official business name — the name you use on leases, signage, and your website. If you operate as "Riverstone Property Management," use that exact name. Avoid keyword stuffing in your business name (for example, "Austin Cheap Apartments Riverstone") as Google may suspend your profile for this.

Step 4: Choose Your Business Category

This is one of the most important decisions in your setup. Your primary category tells Google what type of business you operate.

Recommended categories for rental businesses:

  • Property management company (best for management firms)
  • Real estate rental agency (good for landlords with multiple units)
  • Apartment building or complex (for single-site operators)
  • Apartment rental agency

You can add secondary categories later. For example, a property manager might use "Property management company" as primary and "Real estate agency" as secondary.

Step 5: Add Your Location or Service Area

Google asks whether you have a physical location customers can visit.

  • If you have an office: Enter your street address. Google will show a pin on Maps.
  • If you manage properties but work remotely: Select "I deliver goods and services to customers" and define your service area by city, ZIP code, or radius. You can hide your home address if needed.

Step 6: Add Contact Information

Include:

  • Your business phone number (use a dedicated business line if possible)
  • Your website URL
  • Your business hours

If your availability for showing properties differs from your office hours, use the "More hours" feature to specify showing hours separately.

Step 7: Verify Your Business

Google requires verification to publish your profile. Common methods include:

  • Postcard by mail (most common — Google mails a PIN to your address)
  • Phone or email verification (available for some accounts)
  • Video verification (increasingly common for service-area businesses)

Complete verification promptly. Until verified, your profile will not appear in search results.

Optimizing Your Profile for Maximum Visibility

Setting up a profile is only the beginning. Optimization is what separates landlords who get consistent leads from those who get none.

Write a Compelling Business Description

Your description can be up to 750 characters. Use it to explain what makes your properties and management style stand out. Mention your service area, property types, and any standout features (pet-friendly, new appliances, included utilities, etc.).

Example:

"Riverstone Property Management offers professionally managed apartments and single-family rentals across downtown Austin and the surrounding suburbs. We specialize in modern, pet-friendly units with responsive maintenance and online rent payment. Explore available properties or contact us to schedule a showing."

Upload High-Quality Photos

Profiles with photos receive significantly more clicks than those without. Add:

  • Exterior photos of your properties or office
  • Interior photos of common unit types (living rooms, kitchens, bathrooms)
  • Amenity photos (parking, laundry, pool, gym)
  • Team photos if you have staff (builds trust)

Aim for a minimum of 10 photos to start, and add more as you acquire or renovate properties. This pairs naturally with the approach covered in our guide to virtual tours for rental properties.

Collect and Respond to Reviews

Reviews are one of the top ranking factors for local search. Strategies for earning them:

  • Ask tenants to leave a review after move-in when satisfaction is highest
  • Send a follow-up email after maintenance requests are resolved
  • Include a review link in your welcome packet

Respond to every review — positive and negative. A thoughtful response to a critical review demonstrates professionalism to prospective tenants reading it.

Post Regular Updates

Google Business Profile has a "Posts" feature that works similarly to social media. Use it to:

  • Announce newly available units
  • Highlight property features or recent renovations
  • Share local area news relevant to renters
  • Promote seasonal offers (reduced deposit for quick move-in, etc.)

Posting once or twice a week keeps your profile fresh and signals to Google that your business is active.

Enable Messaging

Turn on the messaging feature so prospective tenants can contact you directly from your Google listing. Respond promptly — Google tracks response rates and may display your typical response time on your profile.

Advanced Tips: Google Business Profile Features for Landlords

Add Products or Services

Use the "Services" section to list what you offer:

  • Residential rentals
  • Property management services
  • Short-term rentals (if applicable)
  • Tenant screening services

Use Q&A Strategically

The Q&A section on your profile allows anyone to ask — and answer — questions. Get ahead of it by posting your own frequently asked questions and answering them yourself. Common ones for landlords:

  • "Do you allow pets?"
  • "What is the minimum lease term?"
  • "Is parking included?"
  • "How do I apply for a unit?"

Monitor Insights

Google Business Profile provides analytics on:

  • How customers found your profile (direct search vs. discovery)
  • What actions they took (website visits, calls, direction requests)
  • Photo views compared to competitors

Use these insights to refine your optimization strategy over time.

Integrating Google Business Profile with Your Broader Marketing Strategy

A Google Business Profile is most powerful when it works in concert with your other channels. Your complete rental property marketing strategies should include this profile as a cornerstone of your local digital presence.

Connect it to:

  • Your website: Link directly to your availability page, not just your homepage
  • Your listing sites: Consistent NAP (name, address, phone) data across rental listing sites strengthens your local SEO
  • Your social channels: Cross-promote content between Google posts and your social platforms

Google Business Profile help provides detailed guidance on profile features and best practices for local search.

Common Mistakes Landlords Make with Google Business Profile

  1. Inconsistent business name/address across the web — Google cross-references your information. Forbes explains local SEO ranking factors. Inconsistencies hurt your ranking.
  2. Ignoring reviews — Unanswered negative reviews damage trust and rankings.
  3. No photos — Profiles without photos are passed over by both tenants and Google's algorithm.
  4. Wrong category — A mismatched primary category sends the wrong signals to Google.
  5. Set-and-forget mentality — Profiles that are not updated regularly lose ground to competitors who are active.

Conclusion

A Google Business Profile is the single highest-ROI digital marketing tool available to landlords — and it is completely free. By setting up and consistently optimizing your profile, you establish a credible local presence that captures tenants at the exact moment they are searching for a home.

Start with the basics: claim your profile, verify it, upload photos, and write a strong description. Then layer in reviews, regular posts, and Q&A content to pull ahead of competing landlords in your market. As part of your full suite of rental property marketing strategies, a polished Google Business Profile can meaningfully reduce your vacancy rate and attract higher-quality tenants.
