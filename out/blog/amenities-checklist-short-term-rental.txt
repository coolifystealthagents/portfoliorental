2a:T47d6,<p>Walk into any top-rated vacation rental and you'll notice something: it feels complete. Nothing is missing. Guests aren't hunting through cabinets for a can opener or calling the host because there's no toilet paper. That feeling of completeness — of thoughtfulness — is what drives five-star reviews and repeat bookings.</p>
<p>This comprehensive <strong>amenities checklist for short-term rentals</strong> covers everything from baseline necessities to premium upgrades, organized room by room so you can audit your property systematically.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="why-amenities-directly-impact-your-revenue">Why Amenities Directly Impact Your Revenue</h2>
<p>Amenities aren't just about comfort — they're about competitiveness. On platforms like Airbnb and VRBO, guests filter searches by amenity. A property without Wi-Fi, air conditioning, or a washer/dryer is immediately excluded from a significant portion of search results.</p>
<p>Beyond search filters, amenities drive:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Review scores</strong> — especially the "accuracy" and "value" categories</li>
<li><strong>Booking conversion</strong> — guests comparing listings choose the one that feels more complete</li>
<li><strong>Nightly rate potential</strong> — premium amenities justify higher pricing (see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-pricing-strategy">short-term rental pricing strategy</a>)</li>
<li><strong>Repeat bookings</strong> — guests remember properties that anticipated their needs</li>
</ul>
<p>For the full framework on running a high-performing STR, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="tier-1-absolute-essentials-non-negotiable">Tier 1: Absolute Essentials (Non-Negotiable)</h2>
<p>These are the baseline amenities guests expect at any price point. Missing any of these will almost certainly generate negative reviews.</p>
<h3 id="technology-x26-connectivity">Technology &#x26; Connectivity</h3>
<ul class="contains-task-list">
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> High-speed Wi-Fi (minimum 50 Mbps; 100+ Mbps preferred)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Wi-Fi password clearly displayed (in multiple locations)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Working TV with streaming services (Netflix, Hulu, or similar)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Reliable cell signal or documented dead zone disclosure</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="climate-control">Climate Control</h3>
<ul class="contains-task-list">
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Air conditioning (in warm climates or summer destinations)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Heating (in cool climates or winter destinations)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Ceiling fans or portable fans as backup</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="safety">Safety</h3>
<ul class="contains-task-list">
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Smoke detectors (in every bedroom and common area)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Carbon monoxide detector (<a href="https://www.cpsc.gov/Safety-Education/Safety-Education-Centers/Carbon-Monoxide-Information-Center">CPSC safety guidelines</a>)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Fire extinguisher (accessible and inspected, per <a href="https://www.fema.gov/emergency-managers/risk-management/fire">FEMA fire safety tips</a>)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> First aid kit</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Emergency contact list posted visibly</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="entry-x26-access">Entry &#x26; Access</h3>
<ul class="contains-task-list">
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Keypad/smart lock or clearly explained key system</li>

<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Iron and ironing board</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Drying rack</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3 id="outdoor-spaces-if-applicable">Outdoor Spaces (If Applicable)</h3>
<p><strong>Patio / Deck:</strong></p>
<ul class="contains-task-list">
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Outdoor seating (table + chairs)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Grill (propane or charcoal with fuel/charcoal)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Grill brush and tongs</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> String lights or outdoor lighting</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Yard / Grounds:</strong></p>
<ul class="contains-task-list">
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Hose or watering access</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Outdoor games (cornhole, bocce, ladder toss)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Fire pit with firewood (if safe and legal)</li>
</ul>
<p>For properties with pools, see our detailed <a href="/blog/pool-maintenance-vacation-rental">pool maintenance guide for vacation rentals</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="tier-3-premium-amenities-that-justify-higher-rates">Tier 3: Premium Amenities That Justify Higher Rates</h2>
<p>Once the essentials are covered, these upgrades differentiate your property and support premium pricing.</p>
<h3 id="technology-upgrades">Technology Upgrades</h3>
<ul>
<li>Smart TV (65"+) with universal remote</li>
<li>Smart home devices (Alexa, Google Home for lighting/temperature control)</li>
<li>EV charging outlet in garage or driveway</li>
<li>Noise monitoring device (Minut, NoiseAware) for peace of mind</li>
<li>Ring or Nest doorbell camera (exterior only, disclosed in listing)</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="kitchen-upgrades">Kitchen Upgrades</h3>
<ul>
<li>Espresso machine or high-end coffee maker</li>
<li>Instant Pot or air fryer</li>
<li>Full spice rack</li>
<li>Pour-over or French press for coffee enthusiasts</li>
<li>Wine opener and bottle stopper set</li>
<li>Cocktail kit (shaker, strainer, jigger)</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="bedroom-upgrades">Bedroom Upgrades</h3>
<ul>
<li>Hotel-quality pillows (memory foam or down alternatives)</li>
<li>Premium mattress (guests notice)</li>
<li>White noise machine in each bedroom</li>
<li>Reading lights with USB ports on each bedside table</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="guest-experience-extras">Guest Experience Extras</h3>
<ul>
<li>Welcome basket (local snacks, wine, sparkling water)</li>
<li>Local guidebook with personal recommendations</li>
<li>Umbrella (often forgotten by guests)</li>
<li>Baby gear (crib, high chair, baby bathtub) — great niche differentiator</li>
<li>Pack of playing cards and a few novels</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="consumable-restocking-how-much-is-enough">Consumable Restocking: How Much Is Enough?</h2>

<td>2 full rolls</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Dish soap</td>
<td>One full bottle</td>

<td>2 per bin size</td>

<td>Laundry detergent</td>

<p>For turnover checklists that cover restocking systematically, see our <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-turnover-checklist">vacation rental turnover checklist</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="listing-your-amenities-correctly">Listing Your Amenities Correctly</h2>
<p>Having an amenity is only half the battle — you need to list it accurately to attract the right guests and avoid expectation mismatches.</p>
<p><strong>Best practices for listing amenities:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Mark only what's actually available and functional</li>
<li>Add descriptions for unique or premium items ("Nespresso machine with capsule starter pack")</li>
<li>Upload photos of key amenities — a photo of the well-stocked kitchen is more compelling than a checkbox</li>
<li>Update your listing when you add or remove items</li>
</ul>
<p>Guests booking on Airbnb and VRBO filter heavily by amenities — accurate listings mean you appear in the right searches. According to VRBO's host resources, properties with 10+ amenities listed consistently outperform those with fewer in search ranking. <a href="https://www.epa.gov/indoor-air-quality-iaq">EPA indoor air quality</a> standards also apply to rental properties.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="seasonal-amenity-adjustments">Seasonal Amenity Adjustments</h2>
<p>Don't set your amenity supply once and forget it. Adjust for the season:</p>
<p><strong>Summer additions:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Beach towels (if near water)</li>
<li>Sunscreen (travel size)</li>
<li>Insect repellent</li>
<li>Cooler for day trips</li>
<li>Fan or extra cooling capacity</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Winter additions:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Extra blankets and flannel sheets</li>
<li>Hot cocoa or cider packets</li>
<li>Boot tray near entrance</li>
<li>Space heater (with safety disclosure)</li>
<li>Umbrella and rain gear</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Holiday seasons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Seasonal decorations</li>
<li>Board games for longer stays</li>
<li>Extra pantry items for cooking</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>A fully stocked, thoughtfully equipped short-term rental is one of the most reliable paths to consistent five-star reviews. Guests rarely comment on amenities when they're all there — but they always notice when something is missing.</p>
<p>Use this checklist to audit your property room by room, prioritize the Tier 1 essentials first, then layer in the premium upgrades that fit your property type and target guest. Pair your amenities strategy with strong <a href="/blog/guest-communication-short-term-rental">guest communication</a> and you've built a guest experience that stands out in any market.</p>
<p>For a complete roadmap to running a profitable STR, visit our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
