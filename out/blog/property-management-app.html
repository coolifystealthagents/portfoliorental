Modern landlords do not manage properties from a desk. They handle maintenance calls on the road, review tenant applications between meetings, and check rent payment status from wherever they happen to be. A good property management app makes all of that possible without requiring a laptop.

The property management app market has matured significantly. Several platforms now offer mobile experiences that rival their desktop counterparts—with real-time notifications, photo uploads, digital signatures, and full accounting dashboards available from a phone. This guide covers the top property management apps, what makes each one worth downloading, and how to match the right app to your management style.

What Makes a Property Management App Actually Useful

An app that crashes, loads slowly, or limits what you can do on mobile is worse than no app at all. Before downloading anything, look for these qualities:

  • Full feature parity with desktop: Rent collection, maintenance requests, and tenant communications should all work from the mobile app—not just a watered-down version.
  • Push notifications: Real-time alerts for rent received, maintenance requests submitted, and lease renewals due keep you ahead of problems.
  • Offline functionality: Basic functions like viewing tenant contact info should work without a strong data connection.
  • Document and photo uploads: Tenants and landlords need to submit photos for maintenance requests and lease documents from their phones.
  • Secure payment processing: Any app handling money should use encryption and comply with PCI standards.

Top Property Management Apps Reviewed

1. Buildium

Buildium is built for professional property managers and landlords running portfolios of 50+ units. The mobile app is polished and covers the full range of operations: rent collection, maintenance request management, tenant communications, owner reporting, and inspections.

Strengths of the Buildium app:

  • Full accounting access on mobile
  • Maintenance workflow management with photo uploads
  • Resident portal integration
  • Owner and board communications
  • Inspection checklists

The downside is cost. Buildium's pricing starts at around $58/month for up to 150 units and scales from there. For larger portfolios where professional management efficiency matters, it's competitive. For small landlords with 2–3 units, it's likely overkill.

2. AppFolio

AppFolio's mobile app is consistently rated among the best in the category. The interface is clean, fast, and designed for property managers who need to do real work from a phone—not just check balances.

Mobile features worth noting:

  • AI-powered leasing assistant that handles prospect inquiries
  • Maintenance technician tools with work order management
  • Resident communication hub
  • Real-time financial dashboards
  • Electronic signatures on leases

AppFolio requires a minimum of 50 units to get started, making it a poor fit for small independent landlords. For portfolio landlords and management companies, it competes at the top of the market. For a detailed comparison, see Buildium vs AppFolio.

3. TurboTenant

TurboTenant targets independent landlords and offers a free plan with solid mobile capabilities. The app handles the full leasing workflow—advertising vacancies, processing applications, collecting rent, and managing tenant communications.

What works well on mobile:

  • Listing management and applicant tracking
  • Online rent collection with payment status notifications
  • Lease generation and e-signatures
  • Maintenance request submission and tracking
  • Direct messaging with tenants

TurboTenant's free plan covers core features, with screening reports charged per transaction. The mobile experience is one of the better ones in the free-tier category.

4. Avail

Avail's app is designed for DIY landlords managing a handful of properties. The interface is approachable, and the mobile app handles most tasks without forcing you to switch to a browser.

Mobile highlights:

  • Vacancy listings synced to multiple sites
  • Tenant screening with credit and background reports
  • Online lease signing
  • Rent collection (with ACH fees on the free plan)
  • Maintenance request tracking

Avail works well for landlords with under 10 units who want a clean, guided experience. The app is particularly strong for onboarding new tenants—from application to signed lease.

5. Landlord Studio

Landlord Studio's mobile app is built around accounting and expense management. The standout feature is receipt scanning—point your phone at a receipt, and the app categorizes and logs the expense automatically.

Mobile features:

  • Receipt scanning and expense categorization
  • Mileage tracking for property visits
  • Income and expense reporting
  • Tenant and lease management
  • Rent collection (on paid plans)

For landlords whose biggest pain point is tax time and expense tracking, Landlord Studio's mobile experience is among the most practical available.

6. Cozy (now part of Apartments.com)

Cozy was acquired by Apartments.com and its features have been folded into the Apartments.com landlord platform. The mobile experience includes rent collection, tenant screening, and vacancy listings. Integration with Apartments.com's renter traffic makes it a strong option for landlords prioritizing vacancy fill speed.

Using Property Management Apps Alongside a Full Platform

Most landlords find that the best setup combines a primary management platform with a few specialized tools. For example:

  • Primary platform: Avail or TurboTenant for operations
  • Financial tracking: Stessa for income/expense reporting (see our rental property portfolio management tools guide)
  • Tenant communication: Some landlords prefer a dedicated messaging app for maintenance coordination

The downside of using multiple apps is data fragmentation. If your rent collection lives in one platform and your financial tracking lives in another, you'll spend time reconciling records that a single integrated platform would handle automatically.

For landlords with growing portfolios, our guide to best property management software for portfolios covers platforms designed for scale.

Rent Collection via App: What to Know

Nearly every major property management app now supports online rent collection. Here is what varies between platforms:

  • ACH fees: Some platforms pass ACH fees to tenants; others absorb them. Confirm before setting up.
  • Credit card surcharges: Credit card payments almost always carry a 2.5–3.5% fee, typically paid by the tenant.
  • Payment timing: ACH transfers typically settle in 2–5 business days. Some platforms offer faster settlement for an additional fee.
  • Autopay setup: Tenants enrolling in autopay reduces late payments significantly. Look for apps where autopay enrollment is simple from the tenant side.

For best practices around online rent collection, see our article on rent collection best practices.

Tenant Communication Features to Look For

Keeping tenant communication inside a single app creates a paper trail, reduces miscommunication, and simplifies dispute resolution. Useful communication features include:

  • In-app messaging tied to lease and unit records
  • Automated notifications for rent due dates and lease renewals
  • Maintenance request status updates pushed to tenants
  • Document sharing (inspection reports, notices, lease addenda)

For more on building effective communication systems with tenants, see our tenant communication tips.

Common App Mistakes Landlords Make

Choosing based on price alone: The cheapest or free option is not always the right fit. Time lost to a poor interface or missing features costs money.

Not training tenants on the platform: An app only works if tenants use it. Walk new tenants through the portal at move-in and explain how to submit maintenance requests, pay rent, and access their lease.

Ignoring data security: Any app handling payment information should use encryption and two-factor authentication. Check the platform's security documentation before storing sensitive data.

Switching platforms mid-lease: Migrating tenant data, payment histories, and lease records mid-tenancy creates gaps. Evaluate and commit to a platform before onboarding new tenants when possible.

Summary

The best property management app depends on your portfolio size and management style. For small independent landlords, Avail, TurboTenant, and Landlord Studio offer the best free or low-cost mobile experiences. For mid-to-large portfolios, Buildium and AppFolio deliver full-featured platforms with polished apps designed for professional use.

Prioritize apps that offer full feature parity with their desktop versions, clear push notifications, and a smooth tenant-facing portal. A tool your tenants resist using is a tool that creates more work, not less.

Start with the platform that covers your biggest current pain point—whether that's rent collection, maintenance tracking, or accounting—and expand from there.
