Managing rental properties with spreadsheets and sticky notes works for a while. But as your portfolio grows, you need better tools. Property management software handles rent collection, maintenance tracking, tenant communication, accounting, and reporting all in one place.

The challenge is picking the right platform. There are dozens of options, each with different strengths, pricing models, and target audiences. The wrong choice wastes money and creates frustration. The right choice saves hours every week and helps you run a tighter operation.

This property management software comparison breaks down five of the most popular platforms. We cover their features, pricing, strengths, and ideal use cases so you can make a confident decision. What to Look for in Property Management Software

Before comparing specific platforms, here are the features that matter most.

Core Features

  • Online rent collection with ACH and credit card support
  • Tenant portal for maintenance requests and communication
  • Maintenance tracking with work order management
  • Accounting and bookkeeping with financial reports
  • Lease management with document storage
  • Tenant screening with background and credit checks
  • Owner portal for investors or partners who need access

Nice-to-Have Features

  • Mobile app for on-the-go management
  • Vacancy listing syndication to rental sites
  • E-signature for leases and documents
  • Vendor management
  • Insurance tracking
  • Tax reporting (1099s, Schedule E data)
  • API integrations with other tools

Pricing Considerations

Pricing models vary widely. Some platforms charge per unit per month. Others charge a flat monthly fee based on tier. A few offer free plans with limited features. Consider your portfolio size now and where you plan to be in two to three years.

Buildium

Buildium is one of the most established property management platforms. It serves landlords, property managers, and community associations.

Best For

Mid-size to large portfolios with 50 or more units. It is also strong for property managers who handle third-party portfolios.

Key Features

  • Full accounting suite with general ledger
  • Online rent collection with automatic late fees
  • Tenant and owner portals
  • Maintenance request tracking with vendor assignment
  • Tenant screening through TransUnion
  • Vacancy listing syndication
  • Document storage and e-signatures
  • 1099 e-filing
  • Community association management tools
  • Robust reporting with customizable dashboards

Pricing

Buildium offers three tiers:

  • Essential: Starting around $58 per month for up to 20 units
  • Growth: Starting around $183 per month with additional features
  • Premium: Starting around $375 per month for larger portfolios

Per-unit costs decrease as your portfolio grows. All plans include unlimited users.

Strengths

Buildium's accounting is its biggest strength. It handles complex financial situations including multiple bank accounts, trust accounting, and owner distributions. The reporting engine is powerful and customizable.

The platform also has a strong tenant screening integration. You can run credit checks, background checks, and eviction history searches directly from the application.

Limitations

Buildium can feel complex for landlords with small portfolios. The learning curve is steeper than simpler platforms. Some users report that customer support response times could improve during peak periods. The mobile app, while functional, does not offer every feature available on the desktop version.

AppFolio

AppFolio targets professional property managers and larger portfolio owners. It emphasizes automation and AI-powered features.

Best For

Professional property management companies and landlords with 50 or more units who want advanced automation.

Key Features

  • AI-powered leasing assistant for tenant communication
  • Online rent collection with multiple payment methods
  • Integrated maintenance management with photo uploads
  • Full accounting with bank reconciliation
  • Tenant screening with SmartMove integration
  • Vacancy marketing with listing syndication
  • Inspection tools with mobile photo documentation
  • Owner statements and distributions
  • Insurance tracking
  • Smart maintenance technology for routing requests

Pricing

AppFolio requires a minimum portfolio size and charges per unit per month.

  • Core: Around $1.40 per unit per month (minimum 50 units)
  • Plus: Around $3.00 per unit per month for advanced features
  • Max: Custom pricing for large enterprises

There is also a one-time onboarding fee.

Strengths

AppFolio excels at automation. Its AI leasing assistant handles initial tenant inquiries, schedules tours, and follows up. This saves significant time for busy property managers. The maintenance routing feature automatically assigns work orders to the right vendor based on the request type.

The mobile app is one of the best in the industry. Property managers can handle inspections, approvals, and communication from their phones.

Limitations

The minimum portfolio size requirement excludes small landlords. Pricing adds up for larger portfolios. Some users find that the emphasis on automation means less flexibility for custom workflows. The onboarding process can take several weeks.

Rent Manager

Rent Manager is a feature-rich platform built for serious property managers who need maximum control and customization.

Best For

Large portfolios and property management companies that need advanced customization, especially those managing commercial and residential properties together.

Key Features

  • Highly customizable database structure
  • Full double-entry accounting
  • Work order management with vendor portals
  • Tenant and owner portals
  • Built-in call tracking and communication logging
  • Inspection scheduling and tracking
  • Custom report builder
  • Integration with over 100 third-party services
  • Multi-entity management for portfolios with different ownership structures
  • Marketing and leasing tools

Pricing

Rent Manager uses custom pricing based on portfolio size and feature needs. Contact their sales team for a quote. Plans generally start around $1 per unit per month with minimum fees.

Strengths

Customization is where Rent Manager shines. You can build custom fields, reports, workflows, and automations. For property managers with complex portfolio structures or specific reporting needs, this flexibility is invaluable.

The accounting module is extremely thorough. It handles multi-entity accounting, CAM reconciliation for commercial properties, and complex owner distribution scenarios.

Limitations

The extensive customization options create a steep learning curve. This is not a platform you set up in an afternoon. Training is recommended, and Rent Manager offers onboarding programs. Smaller landlords may find it overkill for their needs.

TenantCloud

TenantCloud positions itself as an affordable, user-friendly option for independent landlords and small portfolio owners.

Best For

Small landlords with 1 to 75 units who want simplicity and low cost.

Key Features

  • Free plan for up to a limited number of units
  • Online rent collection
  • Maintenance request management
  • Basic accounting and reporting
  • Tenant screening
  • Listing syndication
  • Document storage
  • Tenant and owner portals
  • QuickBooks integration
  • Tax-ready reporting

Pricing

TenantCloud offers a tiered pricing model:

  • Free (Starter): Limited features, up to a small number of units
  • Growth: Around $15 per month for expanded features
  • Pro: Around $50 per month for full feature access

These are some of the lowest prices in the industry, making TenantCloud attractive for cost-conscious landlords.

Strengths

TenantCloud's biggest advantage is accessibility. The interface is clean and simple. You can set up an account and start using it in less than an hour. For landlords who are switching from spreadsheets, TenantCloud is an easy transition.

The free plan is genuinely useful. It covers basic rent collection, maintenance tracking, and tenant management. This lets you try the platform before committing money.

Limitations

TenantCloud lacks the depth of platforms like Buildium or Rent Manager. The accounting features are basic. Reporting options are limited. As your portfolio grows past 50 to 75 units, you may outgrow the platform. Customer support is email-only on lower tiers.

Stessa

Stessa takes a different approach. It focuses primarily on financial tracking and portfolio analytics rather than day-to-day property management.

Best For

Landlords who manage properties themselves or use a property manager but want better financial visibility and tax preparation.

Key Features

  • Automatic income and expense tracking
  • Bank account integration
  • Real-time cash flow dashboards
  • Property performance analytics
  • Tax-ready financial reports
  • Document storage
  • Mortgage tracking
  • Portfolio-level metrics
  • Net worth tracking

Pricing

Stessa offers a generous free plan.

  • Free (Essential): Core financial tracking and reporting
  • Pro: Around $20 per month for advanced features and tax tools

Strengths

Stessa excels at financial tracking. It connects to your bank accounts and automatically categorizes transactions. The dashboards give you a clear picture of how each property and your overall portfolio are performing.

Tax time is where Stessa really shines. It generates Schedule E reports, tracks depreciation, and organizes expenses by category. Many landlords use Stessa alongside another property management platform to get the best of both worlds.

Limitations

Stessa is not a full property management platform. It does not handle rent collection, tenant screening, or maintenance management in the same way as Buildium or AppFolio. If you need those features, you will need to pair Stessa with another tool or handle them manually.

How to Choose the Right Platform

Picking the right software depends on your specific situation. Here is a simple decision framework.

Portfolio Size

  • 1-10 units: TenantCloud or Stessa
  • 10-50 units: TenantCloud (Growth/Pro) or Buildium (Essential)
  • 50-200 units: Buildium or AppFolio
  • 200+ units: AppFolio, Rent Manager, or Buildium (Premium)

Management Style

  • Self-managing landlord: TenantCloud + Stessa
  • Hands-on property manager: Buildium or AppFolio
  • Full-service management company: AppFolio or Rent Manager

Budget

  • Minimal budget: TenantCloud (Free) + Stessa (Free)
  • Moderate budget: Buildium Essential or TenantCloud Pro
  • Full investment: AppFolio or Rent Manager

Technical Comfort

  • Prefer simplicity: TenantCloud or Stessa
  • Comfortable with complexity: Buildium or AppFolio
  • Want maximum control: Rent Manager

Making the Switch

Once you pick a platform, plan your transition carefully.

  1. Export your current data. Pull tenant information, lease details, financial records, and maintenance history from your existing systems.
  2. Set up during a slow period. Avoid switching during lease renewal season or tax time.
  3. Start with one property. Test the platform with a single property before migrating everything.
  4. Train your team. Make sure anyone who uses the software knows how it works.
  5. Notify your tenants. Let them know about the new tenant portal and any changes to rent payment methods.
  6. Run parallel systems briefly. Keep your old system running for a month until you are confident in the new one.

The Impact of Good Software

Good property management software does more than save time. It changes how you run your business. You make better decisions because you have better data. You spend less time on paperwork and more time on strategy. You respond faster to tenant needs, which improves retention.
