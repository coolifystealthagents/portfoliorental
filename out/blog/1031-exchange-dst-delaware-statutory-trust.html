Working with Qualified Intermediaries and Tax Professionals

Tenant selection is one of the highest-leverage decisions a landlord makes. A well-qualified tenant who pays on time, maintains the property, and renews their lease is worth far more than the rent they pay—they represent stability, lower maintenance costs, and reduced management burden. By contrast, a poorly screened tenant can cost thousands in lost rent, repairs, and legal fees.

An effective screening process for DST real estate investment starts with consistent, written criteria applied to every applicant. These criteria typically include minimum credit score thresholds, income requirements (usually 2.5-3x monthly rent), rental history verification, and criminal background screening that complies with local fair chance housing ordinances.

The tools available for tenant screening have improved dramatically. Modern screening services integrate with listing platforms and can return comprehensive reports—credit, criminal, eviction history—within minutes. Some platforms also verify income through bank statement analysis, which is more reliable than pay stubs alone in an era of gig economy workers and self-employed applicants.

Beyond the data, trust your process over your instincts. Gut feelings are unreliable predictors of tenant performance and can expose you to fair housing liability. If an applicant meets your written criteria, approve them. If they don't, decline them with a documented reason tied to your criteria. This systematic approach protects you legally and produces better outcomes over time.
