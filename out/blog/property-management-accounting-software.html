Accounting is one of the most consequential—and most commonly neglected—parts of managing rental properties. Landlords who track income and expenses precisely pay less in taxes, make better investment decisions, and avoid the year-end scramble of reconstructing records from bank statements. The right property management accounting software makes this possible without requiring an accounting background.

This guide covers the leading accounting tools available to rental property owners, from property-specific platforms to general accounting software, so you can choose the right fit for your portfolio complexity and reporting needs.

Why Rental Property Accounting Has Unique Requirements

General business accounting and rental property accounting share fundamentals, but property-specific accounting has several unique requirements:

  • Schedule E compliance: Rental income and expenses are reported on IRS Schedule E, with specific categories that differ from standard business expense accounting.
  • Depreciation tracking: Rental properties are depreciated over 27.5 years. Tracking and calculating depreciation correctly is required for accurate tax reporting.
  • Multiple property entities: Many investors hold properties in separate LLCs, requiring accounting that segregates income and expenses by entity.
  • Capital improvements vs. repairs: The distinction between deductible repairs and capitalized improvements has significant tax implications and requires careful categorization.
  • Security deposit accounting: Security deposits are liabilities, not income, until applied. Accounting software needs to handle this correctly.
  • Owner distribution tracking: For properties with multiple investors, tracking distributions and equity calculations adds complexity.

Software that is built specifically for rental properties handles these requirements natively. General accounting software like QuickBooks requires more setup to achieve the same result.

Property-Specific Accounting Software

Stessa

Stessa is the leading free option for rental property financial tracking. It is designed specifically for real estate investors and aligns categories directly with Schedule E requirements.

Core accounting features:

  • Automatic transaction import from bank accounts via Plaid
  • Income and expense categorization by property
  • Depreciation tracking
  • Net operating income and cash flow dashboards
  • Schedule E preparation reports
  • Year-end financial summaries

What Stessa handles well: Stessa is excellent for tracking operating income and expenses, understanding portfolio-level financial performance, and preparing the data needed for Schedule E filing. The automatic bank import and category learning significantly reduces manual bookkeeping time.

What Stessa handles less well: Full general ledger accounting with accounts payable/receivable, trust accounting, and complex multi-entity structures are beyond Stessa's scope. It is an investor-facing tracking tool, not a professional accounting system.

The free plan covers most landlords' needs. Stessa Pro ($20/month) adds document storage, OCR receipt scanning, and advanced reporting. See our full Stessa review for property management for a detailed breakdown.

Best for: Individual investors and landlords who want clean financial visibility and tax-ready reports without paying for software.

Landlord Studio

Landlord Studio is a mobile-first accounting platform designed for independent landlords. Its standout feature is receipt scanning on mobile—photograph a receipt and the app categorizes and logs the expense automatically.

Core accounting features:

  • Receipt scanning with OCR
  • Income and expense tracking
  • Mileage tracking
  • Bank account reconciliation
  • Financial reports including profit and loss, cash flow, and tax summaries
  • Tenant and lease tracking alongside accounting

What Landlord Studio handles well: For landlords who capture expenses on the go—lumber at the hardware store, contractor invoices at a job site, tool purchases—the mobile receipt scanning is practically superior to any desktop-first tool. The integration of accounting with basic property management functions makes it a useful combined tool.

What Landlord Studio handles less well: The free plan is limited to 3 properties. The platform lacks the depth needed for commercial portfolios or complex accounting structures.

Paid plans start at around $12/month for up to 3 properties, scaling with unit count.

Best for: Landlords with a small portfolio who want mobile-first expense capture alongside basic property management.

Rentec Direct

Rentec Direct is a full property management platform with strong integrated accounting. Unlike Stessa (finance-only) or Avail (operations-heavy), Rentec balances both sides of the management equation.

Core accounting features:

  • Full general ledger
  • Accounts payable and receivable
  • Bank reconciliation
  • Trust accounting (for property managers handling client funds)
  • 1099 preparation and filing
  • Owner distribution processing
  • Financial statements: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow

What Rentec handles well: The accounting engine is more professional-grade than most tools in the independent landlord market. Trust accounting support makes Rentec relevant for property managers handling multiple investor-owner accounts. The 1099 preparation and filing built into the platform saves significant administrative work.

Pricing starts around $45/month for up to 10 units.

Best for: Small property management companies or landlords who need professional-grade accounting integrated with property operations.

General Accounting Software Adapted for Rentals

QuickBooks Online

QuickBooks is the dominant small business accounting platform. It can be configured for rental property management with the right setup—class tracking for properties, custom chart of accounts, and memo fields for tracking lease-related transactions.

What works:

  • Full double-entry accounting
  • Payroll integration (if you have employees)
  • Bank reconciliation
  • Accounts payable for vendor management
  • Customizable financial reports
  • Extensive accountant and CPA familiarity

What requires setup:

  • Chart of accounts must be customized for rental property categories
  • Class or location tracking must be enabled and used consistently for per-property reporting
  • Schedule E reports are not built in—your CPA or accountant will generate them from your QuickBooks data
  • Depreciation tracking typically requires journal entries or a separate asset management tool

For landlords working with a CPA, QuickBooks has a significant advantage: nearly every CPA is proficient in it. Your CPA can access your books directly, which saves time and reduces errors in year-end preparation.

QuickBooks Online pricing starts at $30/month for the Simple Start plan, scaling to $60/month for Plus (which includes class tracking needed for multi-property reporting).

Best for: Landlords working closely with a CPA, or those with complex business operations beyond rentals who want one accounting platform for everything.

Wave Accounting

Wave is a free cloud accounting platform that covers the basics well. It is not designed for rental property management but can be used effectively for simple portfolios.

What works:

  • Free core accounting features
  • Income and expense tracking
  • Invoicing (useful for sending rent invoices)
  • Bank account connections
  • Basic financial reports

Limitations: No property-specific features, no Schedule E alignment, no per-property tracking without manual workarounds. Customer support is limited on the free plan.

Best for: Early-stage landlords who want free bookkeeping software and don't mind manual customization. Most landlords outgrow Wave quickly.

FreshBooks

FreshBooks is primarily designed for service businesses and freelancers, but some landlords use it for invoice-based rent collection and expense tracking.

What works:

  • Invoice creation and sending for rent billing
  • Expense tracking with receipt uploads
  • Time tracking (irrelevant for most landlords)
  • Client management

Limitations: Not designed for property management. No Schedule E alignment, no per-property reporting, no depreciation support.

Best for: Not recommended as a primary property management accounting tool.

Accounting Within Full Property Management Platforms

Several full property management platforms include accounting robust enough to eliminate the need for separate accounting software:

AppFolio includes a full general ledger, bank reconciliation, accounts payable, owner statements, and financial reporting. For larger portfolios already using AppFolio, the built-in accounting is sufficient for most operators.

Buildium includes property management accounting covering all standard needs—general ledger, bank reconciliation, 1099 generation, and owner statements.

Rent Manager has one of the deepest accounting engines in the mid-market property management software category, supporting commercial lease structures, multiple entities, and professional trust accounting.

For landlords already investing in a full platform, using its built-in accounting rather than adding a separate tool reduces complexity and eliminates data sync issues.

For a comparison of these platforms, see our guides on best property management software for portfolios and rental property record keeping.

Key Accounting Features to Evaluate

When comparing accounting tools, prioritize these features based on your situation:

  • Bank reconciliation: Can you connect bank accounts and reconcile transactions automatically?
  • Per-property reporting: Can you see income and expenses broken down by individual property?
  • Schedule E alignment: Are expense categories aligned with IRS Schedule E?
  • Depreciation tracking: Does the platform track depreciation schedules for your properties?
  • Trust accounting: Required if you manage properties on behalf of investor clients.
  • 1099 generation: Do you pay contractors enough to require 1099 filing?
  • Multi-entity support: Do you hold properties in multiple LLCs?
  • Accountant access: Can your CPA log in directly to review and adjust records?

For detailed guidance on what to track and how, see our articles on rental property expense tracking and rental property tax deductions.

Building Your Accounting Stack

The right combination of tools depends on your portfolio complexity:

1–5 properties, simple structure: Stessa free plan covers most needs. Add a spreadsheet rent roll. Share Stessa exports with your CPA at year-end.

5–20 properties, moderate complexity: Stessa Pro or Landlord Studio for tracking, combined with an operational platform (Avail, TurboTenant) for leasing and maintenance. Alternatively, a single platform like TenantCloud or Rentec Direct that covers both.

20+ properties or multiple entities: Full property management platform (AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager) with built-in accounting, or QuickBooks Online with a property-management-configured chart of accounts and CPA oversight.

Summary

The best property management accounting software depends on your portfolio size, complexity, and whether you prioritize simplicity or depth.

For most independent landlords, Stessa's free plan delivers the best combination of rental-specific features, ease of use, and cost. Landlord Studio is the better choice for mobile-first expense capture. Rentec Direct or a full platform like Buildium or AppFolio is the right call when you need professional-grade accounting integrated with operations.

Whatever tool you choose, consistency matters more than perfection. A simple system you maintain weekly beats a sophisticated system you update quarterly in a panic before your CPA's deadline.
