The ROI of Adding a Bathroom to Your Co-Living Property

When bathroom ratios are the limiting factor on your co-living property's occupancy or pricing, adding a bathroom may be the highest-ROI renovation available to you. The math is often compelling: a bathroom addition that costs $15,000-$25,000 can allow you to add one or two additional rentable rooms, increase per-room pricing due to improved ratios, or both.

Run the numbers before committing to any renovation. If adding a bathroom converts a 3-bathroom, 7-resident property to 4-bathrooms, allowing you to increase to 8 residents at the same rent, your annual revenue increase is one room's annual rent.

At $800/month, that's $9,600 per year. At a $20,000 bathroom addition cost, payback period is about 25 months, an excellent ROI for a property improvement.

If the bathroom addition allows you to charge $75/month more per room across all existing rooms because the ratio improves to a marketable standard, the math is also strong: $75 × 7 rooms × 12 months = $6,300 per year in additional revenue from the same tenant count, for an even faster payback.

The most cost-effective bathroom additions in co-living properties are typically three-quarter baths (toilet, sink, shower) rather than full baths (adding a bathtub). The practical value difference between a three-quarter and full bath is small for most co-living residents, but the cost difference is significant, $3,000-$6,000 less for a three-quarter bath.

Install shower-only configurations unless your market's tenant profile specifically values bathtubs.

Consult with a licensed contractor who understands permit requirements in your jurisdiction. Bathroom additions require permits in virtually all jurisdictions; unpermitted additions create problems at resale, with insurers, and with building code enforcement.

Budget for permits and inspections as part of your project cost.

According to Forbes, bathroom additions deliver one of the highest returns on investment of any home improvement, particularly in high-demand rental markets where bathroom access directly affects rent pricing and marketability.

Key Takeaways

  • The industry standard for co-living bathroom ratios is one bathroom per 2-3 residents; properties above a 3:1 resident-to-bathroom ratio experience measurably lower satisfaction scores and higher turnover.
  • En-suite bathrooms command a 10-20% room price premium and dramatically improve tenant satisfaction, focus on them for master bedrooms when your property layout allows.
  • Adding a bathroom to a property with a poor ratio often delivers strong ROI: calculate the annual revenue increase (more rooms or higher per-room pricing) against the renovation cost to determine if the investment makes sense for your specific property.
