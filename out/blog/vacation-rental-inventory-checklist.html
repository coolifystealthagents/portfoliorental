Setting up a vacation rental from scratch — or auditing an existing one — requires a systematic approach. Missing a critical item can mean a bad guest review ("no coffee filters!") or a genuine safety issue ("no first aid kit"). Having too many redundant items means unnecessary costs and cluttered spaces.

A vacation rental inventory checklist gives you a structured framework to set up your property correctly from the start, maintain it consistently over time, and quickly identify what needs restocking or replacing.

This guide provides a comprehensive, room-by-room inventory checklist, guidance on what to prioritize, and tips for ongoing inventory management.

Why Inventory Management Matters for STR Hosts

Guest Experience

The items in your vacation rental directly shape guest experience. A well-stocked kitchen, a bathroom with quality toiletries, and a bedroom with the right amenities create the comfort that drives 5-star reviews.

Conversely, guests who cannot find a can opener, discover the WiFi router has been unplugged, or run out of toilet paper on day two of a week-long stay will mention it in their review — often in a way that affects your rating.

Cost Control

Without a systematic inventory approach, hosts tend to over-order some items and under-stock others. A documented inventory with defined par levels (minimum quantities) allows you to order precisely what you need.

Cleaning Efficiency

When your cleaning team knows exactly what should be at the property and where, they can restock and verify inventory during every turnover without calling you for guidance. This makes turnovers faster and more consistent.

Insurance and Tax Documentation

Maintaining an accurate inventory with purchase values provides documentation for insurance claims in the event of damage or theft, and supports depreciation deductions for tax purposes.

How to Use This Checklist

This inventory is organized into three categories:

  1. Furniture and Fixed Items — Things that stay in the property permanently
  2. Supplies and Consumables — Items that are used and restocked
  3. Safety and Compliance Items — Non-negotiable for guest safety

Use this checklist to:

  • Initial Setup: Verify you have everything needed before opening your listing
  • Turnover Audit: Check consumables are stocked to par levels at each clean
  • Quarterly Inspection: Assess condition of furnishings, linens, and equipment
  • Annual Review: Refresh and replace worn or dated items

Bedroom Inventory Checklist

Furniture (Per Bedroom)

  • Bed frame (appropriate size: Twin, Full, Queen, King)
  • Mattress with mattress protector (waterproof, hypoallergenic)
  • Headboard
  • Nightstand(s) with drawer or shelf
  • Dresser or chest of drawers
  • Closet or wardrobe with hangers (minimum 10 per bedroom)
  • Luggage rack or bench at foot of bed
  • Bedside lamps with working bulbs
  • Blackout curtains or blinds
  • Mirror (full-length recommended)
  • Trash bin with liner

Bedding and Linens (Per Bed, Two Full Sets)

  • Fitted sheet
  • Flat sheet
  • Pillowcases (one per pillow; minimum 2)
  • Pillows (2 per bed, hypoallergenic option recommended)
  • Duvet or comforter with duvet cover
  • Decorative throw pillow(s) for staging
  • Extra blanket in closet or chest

Bedroom Supplies

  • Bedside charging station or power outlets accessible from bed
  • Alarm clock or phone charging pad
  • Earplugs (optional, nice touch)
  • Sleep mask (optional, nice touch)
  • Guest information card or QR code to house manual

Bathroom Inventory Checklist

Fixtures and Equipment

  • Toilet with lid and seat
  • Shower and/or bathtub
  • Shower curtain and liner (or glass door)
  • Shower curtain rod and rings
  • Vanity with sink and mirror
  • Medicine cabinet or mirror cabinet (with key if locking)
  • Towel bars (2) and towel hooks (2–4)
  • Toilet paper holder
  • Trash bin with lid and liner

Towels and Linens (Per Bathroom, Two Full Sets)

  • Bath towels (2 per guest + 2 extras)
  • Hand towels (2 per bathroom)
  • Washcloths (2 per guest)
  • Bath mat (non-slip, machine washable)

Toiletries and Supplies

  • Toilet paper (minimum 2–3 rolls visible, 3–6 stored)
  • Hand soap (liquid dispenser or individually wrapped bar)
  • Shampoo
  • Conditioner
  • Body wash or soap
  • Lotion (optional but appreciated)
  • Cotton balls and cotton swabs
  • Hair dryer (mounted or accessible)
  • Shower cap (optional, nice touch)
  • Feminine hygiene products (optional)
  • Plunger (stored discreetly)
  • Toilet brush and holder
  • Cleaning supplies under sink (for guest use or cleaning team)

Kitchen Inventory Checklist

Appliances

  • Refrigerator with freezer
  • Oven and stovetop (gas or electric)
  • Microwave
  • Dishwasher (if available)
  • Coffee maker (drip or capsule-based)
  • Kettle (electric)
  • Toaster or toaster oven
  • Blender (recommended for longer-stay properties)
  • Rice cooker (optional, thoughtful in diverse markets)

Cookware

  • Saucepan (small, 1–2 qt)
  • Saucepan (medium, 3–4 qt)
  • Large stockpot (6–8 qt)
  • Skillet/frying pan (medium)
  • Skillet/frying pan (large)
  • Baking sheet (2)
  • Casserole dish or roasting pan
  • Lids for pots and pans

Bakeware and Servingware

  • Mixing bowls (set of 3)
  • Measuring cups (dry and liquid)
  • Measuring spoons
  • Colander/strainer
  • Cutting boards (2 — one for meat, one for vegetables)
  • Serving bowls (2–3 sizes)
  • Serving platter
  • Salad bowl and tongs

Dinnerware (Per Guest Capacity Plus 2 Extra)

  • Dinner plates
  • Salad/side plates
  • Bowls (soup/cereal)
  • Mugs
  • Glasses (water/juice)
  • Wine glasses
  • Champagne flutes (optional, nice touch)

Utensils and Cutlery (Per Guest Capacity Plus 2 Extra)

  • Knives (dinner)
  • Forks
  • Spoons (dinner)
  • Teaspoons
  • Serving spoons (2–3)
  • Spatulas (2)
  • Ladle
  • Whisk
  • Can opener
  • Bottle opener/corkscrew
  • Vegetable peeler
  • Grater
  • Kitchen tongs
  • Oven mitts/pot holders (2)
  • Dish towels (4)

Kitchen Supplies and Consumables

  • Dish soap
  • Sponges and scrubbers (new for each stay)
  • Dishwasher pods (4–6)
  • Aluminum foil
  • Plastic wrap
  • Resealable plastic bags (zip-lock)
  • Paper towels (2 rolls)
  • Trash bags (matching bin size, 3–4 extra)
  • Recycling bags
  • Kitchen trash bin with lid

Pantry Starter Items (Optional but Appreciated)

  • Salt and pepper
  • Cooking oil (olive oil or vegetable oil)
  • Coffee and filters (or pods if capsule machine)
  • Tea selection
  • Sugar and sweetener packets
  • Creamer (shelf-stable packets)
  • Condiment packets or small bottles (ketchup, mustard, soy sauce)

Note: Clearly label pantry items as "Complimentary — please enjoy." Remaining or personalized food items from guests should be disposed of at turnover.

Living and Common Area Inventory Checklist

Furniture

  • Sofa (seats at least the maximum guest count)
  • Coffee table
  • Side tables (2)
  • Armchairs or additional seating
  • Dining table with chairs (to seat maximum guest count)
  • Bookcase or shelving
  • TV stand or wall mount

Entertainment and Technology

  • Smart TV (minimum 42" in main living area; bedroom TVs 32"+)
  • TV remote with fresh batteries
  • Streaming service access (Netflix, Hulu, or smart TV apps)
  • WiFi router in accessible location
  • WiFi network name and password posted visibly
  • HDMI cable
  • Bluetooth speaker (optional, appreciated)
  • Board games or card games
  • Books and/or magazines
  • Puzzles (optional, great for longer stays)

Décor and Comfort

  • Area rugs (hard floor protection and warmth)
  • Throw blankets (2–3 for living area)
  • Decorative pillows (2–4)
  • Wall art or photos (approx. one piece per major wall)
  • Curtains or blinds for natural light control
  • Lamps (minimum 2 for ambient lighting)
  • Plants (low-maintenance artificial or live)
  • Candles or battery candles (note: open flame candles require consideration)

Laundry Inventory Checklist

  • Washer (in-unit or building access)
  • Dryer (in-unit or building access)
  • Laundry detergent pods (4–6 provided)
  • Dryer sheets
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Drying rack (for delicates)
  • Laundry basket or bag
  • Laundry instructions posted near machines

Outdoor Area Inventory (If Applicable)

Patio and Deck

  • Outdoor dining table and chairs
  • Lounge chairs or outdoor sofa
  • Umbrella or shade structure
  • Outdoor grill (gas or charcoal) with cleaning tools
  • Grill starter/lighter
  • Outdoor trash and recycling bins
  • String lights or outdoor lighting
  • Outdoor rugs or mats

Pool and Hot Tub (If Applicable)

  • Pool noodles and floats
  • Pool thermometer
  • Pool net/skimmer
  • Life ring or rescue device (safety)
  • Towel hooks near pool
  • Outdoor shower (if available)
  • No diving/pool rules signage

Safety and Compliance Inventory

These items are non-negotiable. Many are required by law or by Airbnb and VRBO policies.

  • Smoke detectors (one per floor, one per sleeping area — test at each turnover)
  • Carbon monoxide detectors (especially if gas appliances or attached garage)
  • Fire extinguisher (kitchen and main living area minimum) -- review CPSC fire safety tips
  • First aid kit (fully stocked; check and replenish quarterly)
  • Emergency contact card posted visibly (fire, police, host number, nearest hospital)
  • Flashlights with batteries (2)
  • Utility shutoff labels (water, gas, circuit breaker location labeled)
  • Stair gates (if children guests are expected and stairs are present)
  • Pool fence or safety gate (if pool is present; review CPSC pool safety standards)
  • Anti-scald shower regulators
  • Non-slip bath mats
  • Night lights (hallways and bathrooms, recommended)
  • Pool safety ring with rope

Welcome and Arrival Items

  • Welcome basket (optional but memorable — local snacks, wine, coffee)
  • Printed welcome letter with host introduction
  • House manual (physical or QR code to digital version)
  • Local restaurant and activity recommendations
  • Parking instructions card
  • WiFi credentials posted (in bedroom, kitchen, and living area)
  • Maps of local area (optional, appreciated in destination markets)

Ongoing Inventory Management

Create a Master Inventory Spreadsheet

Document every item in your property with:

  • Item name and description
  • Quantity
  • Purchase price (for insurance and tax records)
  • Date purchased
  • Condition (Good / Fair / Replace Soon)

Update this spreadsheet during every quarterly inspection.

Set Par Levels for Consumables

For every consumable item, define the minimum quantity that should be present when a guest arrives. Share these par levels with your cleaning team so they can restock during every turnover without waiting for instructions.

Schedule Quarterly Audits

Every 90 days, walk through your property with the inventory checklist and assess:

  • What is missing?
  • What is worn and needs replacing?
  • What are guests asking for that you are not currently providing?

Review your guest feedback alongside your inventory audit — common guest requests or complaints often point directly to inventory gaps.

For guidance on incorporating inventory management into your overall cleaning and turnover workflow, see our short-term rental cleaning schedule and vacation rental turnover checklist guides.

Conclusion

A complete, well-maintained inventory is the foundation of a great guest experience. When every item is in place, in good condition, and stocked to appropriate levels, guests feel cared for without ever having to ask.

Use this checklist to build your initial inventory, then maintain it systematically with par levels, quarterly audits, and integration into your turnover process. The result is fewer guest complaints, stronger reviews, and a property that stays in excellent condition for years.

For more on running a professional short-term rental operation, explore our airbnb property management tips and our comprehensive short-term rental management guide.

Inventory Checklist Quick Reference:

  • Cover all rooms: bedrooms, bathrooms, kitchen, living areas, laundry, outdoor
  • Never skip safety items: smoke detectors, CO detectors, fire extinguisher, first aid kit
  • Set par levels for all consumables and include in cleaner's turnover checklist
  • Document inventory with purchase prices for insurance and tax purposes
  • Audit inventory quarterly and update based on guest feedback
