Cleanliness is the single most cited factor in short-term rental reviews — for better or worse. A sparkling clean property gets mentioned in five-star reviews. A missed toilet or dusty ceiling fan shows up in three-star ones. For STR hosts, finding a reliable, professional cleaning service isn't optional — it's foundational.

This guide walks you through the complete process of finding, vetting, hiring, and managing a cleaning service for your short-term rental: from knowing what to look for in a cleaner to setting up a system that runs smoothly whether you're on-site or across the country.

Why STR Cleaning Is Different from Regular House Cleaning

Cleaning a vacation rental between guests is fundamentally different from weekly house cleaning. Understanding those differences helps you find the right service.

Key differences:

  • Turnover speed — cleaners often have 2-4 hour windows between checkout and check-in
  • Hotel-level standards — guests expect pristine conditions, not just "tidied up"
  • Restocking duties — cleaners often also restock consumables (toilet paper, shampoo, coffee)
  • Linen management — stripping, laundering, and remaking beds is standard
  • Inspection role — a great STR cleaner also checks for damage and missing items
  • Variable timing — stays vary in length and guest count, affecting workload each time

Regular residential cleaners may not be equipped for this — which is why finding cleaners experienced in STR or vacation rental turnover is so important.

Where to Find STR Cleaning Services

1. STR-Specific Platforms

Several platforms specialize in connecting hosts with vacation rental cleaning services:

  • TurnoverBnB — purpose-built marketplace connecting hosts with vetted STR cleaners; handles scheduling, payment, and syncs with your calendar
  • Properly — photo-based task management and cleaner coordination; strong for portfolio hosts
  • Handy for Pros — general cleaning platform with STR experience in major markets
  • TaskRabbit — flexible for individual cleaners, though STR experience varies

2. Local Referrals

Ask in local STR host communities:

  • Airbnb host Facebook groups for your city
  • Nextdoor neighborhood groups
  • Local real estate investor meetups
  • Your property manager (if you have one) — they often have preferred cleaner contacts

3. Cleaning Company Directories

Search for local companies with vacation rental experience:

  • Google: "[Your City] vacation rental cleaning service"
  • Yelp reviews filtered by commercial cleaning
  • HomeAdvisor and Thumbtack for vetted local services

4. Direct Outreach

If there's a hospitality professional in your area whose work you admire — a hotel housekeeper, a resort staffer — they may be available for freelance STR cleaning on their days off.

What to Look for in an STR Cleaner

Not all cleaners are equal. Here are the qualities that separate a great STR cleaner from an average one:

Non-negotiables:

  • Experience with vacation rental or hotel turnover
  • Reliable transportation and punctuality
  • Strong attention to detail (test with a trial clean)
  • Willingness to follow a checklist
  • Ability to communicate about issues (damage, missing items, low supplies)

Strong positives:

  • Brings their own supplies and equipment
  • Comfortable with smart lock access
  • Has backup support if they're sick or unavailable
  • Can handle laundry on-site or coordinates linen service
  • Available on short notice for last-minute bookings

Interview Questions for Prospective Cleaners

Before hiring anyone, conduct a brief phone or in-person interview. Key questions:

  1. "Do you have experience cleaning Airbnb or vacation rental properties specifically?"
  2. "How do you handle a situation where you find damage or a missing item?"
  3. "What is your process if you're sick and can't make a scheduled turnover?"
  4. "Do you bring your own supplies, or do we provide them?"
  5. "Are you comfortable using a checklist and sending photo confirmation when done?"
  6. "What is your pricing structure — by the hour, or flat per turnover?"
  7. "How do you handle linen changes — do you wash on-site, or use a linen service?"
  8. "Are you available for same-day bookings or last-minute scheduling changes?"

A cleaner who has clear, confident answers to all of these is far more likely to perform consistently.

Pricing: What to Expect for STR Cleaning

STR cleaning costs vary significantly by market, property size, and service level. General benchmarks:

Property Size Typical Turnover Cost
Studio / 1 BR $60–$100
2 BR / 1,000–1,400 sq ft $100–$160
3 BR / 1,400–2,000 sq ft $150–$250
4+ BR / Large properties $250–$500+

Pricing models:

  • Flat per turnover: Most predictable; best for hosts
  • Hourly: Risk of cost overrun on messier stays
  • Subscription/retainer: Works well for high-frequency hosts with consistent schedules

Who pays the cleaning fee? Most hosts pass the cleaning cost directly to guests via a cleaning fee on the listing. Factor in:

  • Actual cleaning cost
  • Linen service (if outsourced)
  • Consumable restocking (if cleaner handles this)
  • A small buffer for supplies and wear

Cleaning fees may be deductible as business expenses. They are also a component of your overall pricing strategy — see our short-term rental pricing strategy guide for how to structure them competitively.

Setting Up Your Cleaning System

Create a Master Turnover Checklist

Your cleaner should follow a detailed checklist for every turnover. This removes ambiguity and ensures consistency regardless of who cleans. See our vacation rental turnover checklist for a complete room-by-room template you can adapt.

At minimum, the checklist should cover:

  • Bathroom deep clean (toilet, shower, sink, mirror, floor)
  • Kitchen clean (appliances, counters, inside microwave, stovetop)
  • Bedroom strip, launder, and remake (military-tight or hotel-style)
  • Vacuuming/mopping all floors
  • Dusting surfaces, fans, baseboards
  • Wiping down door handles, light switches, remotes. The CDC recommends disinfecting high-touch surfaces regularly.
  • Taking out all trash
  • Restocking consumables to defined minimums
  • Checking for guest left items and damage
  • Locking up and reporting status

Set Up Photo Documentation

Require your cleaner to send timestamped photos of key areas after each clean. This serves two purposes:

  1. It confirms the property is ready for the next guest
  2. It creates a record showing condition before each new guest checks in (useful for damage claims)

Apps like Properly and TurnoverBnB have built-in photo workflows. For independent cleaners, a simple WhatsApp or text photo will do.

Sync Scheduling with Your Booking Calendar

Manual scheduling is a recipe for missed cleans. Use a tool that syncs your cleaner's schedule with your booking calendar automatically. Options:

  • TurnoverBnB — syncs directly with Airbnb, VRBO, and most major OTAs
  • Your PMS calendar — if you use Hostaway, Guesty, or similar, set up cleaner access
  • Google Calendar sharing — low-tech option for single-property hosts with one cleaner

For broader automation tools that can integrate with cleaning scheduling, see our Airbnb automation tools guide.

Managing Linen: In-Unit vs. Linen Service

One of the biggest operational decisions for STR cleaning is how to handle linens.

Option A: In-Unit Laundry

The cleaner strips and washes linens on-site using the property's washer/dryer.

Pros: Lower cost, no logistics Cons: Adds 1-2 hours to turnover time, limits back-to-back same-day bookings

Option B: Linen Service

A commercial laundry service collects used linens and delivers fresh ones, hotel-style.

Pros: Faster turnovers, professional results, backup supply available Cons: Additional cost ($20-60 per set), requires coordination and logistics

Best for: Hosts with frequent bookings, same-day turnovers, or large properties with many beds

Option C: Par Level System

Stock enough linens for 2-3 complete sets per bed. Cleaners use clean sets from storage, send used ones to the laundry, and the cycle rotates.

This is the most professional and scalable approach for growing STR portfolios.

Handling Issues: When a Clean Doesn't Meet Standards

Even great cleaners have off days. Build a feedback system that makes it easy to address quality issues without damaging the relationship.

Best practices:

  • Review photos before approving payment when possible
  • Communicate issues within 24 hours of the clean
  • Be specific ("the stovetop burners weren't wiped down") not general ("the kitchen wasn't clean")
  • Have the cleaner return to fix major issues rather than letting them slide
  • Track patterns — occasional misses are human; recurring issues signal a systemic problem

Document recurring issues and set clear expectations about what constitutes an unacceptable clean. After two or three unaddressed quality issues, it's time to find a backup.

Building Your Cleaner Relationship for Long-Term Success

Your cleaner is one of the most important people in your STR business. Treat the relationship accordingly.

Invest in the relationship:

  • Pay promptly and fairly (tip for excellent work or especially difficult cleans)
  • Give advance notice of schedule changes whenever possible
  • Communicate guest counts so they know what to expect
  • Ask for their input — experienced STR cleaners often notice issues before anyone else
  • Send a thank-you or bonus during busy seasons

A great, reliable cleaner who knows your property is worth far more than constantly searching for someone cheaper. The SBA offers hiring guidance for small business contractors.

Conclusion

Finding the right cleaning service for your short-term rental is one of the highest-impact operational decisions you'll make. Cleanliness drives reviews, reviews drive bookings, and bookings drive revenue. The investment in finding a reliable, experienced STR cleaner — and building a system that supports them — pays off immediately in guest satisfaction and long-term in your property's rating.

Start with a referral from your local STR community, conduct a thorough interview, run a test clean with your checklist, and build from there. Combined with a solid vacation rental turnover checklist and smart Airbnb automation, your cleaning operation can run like clockwork.
