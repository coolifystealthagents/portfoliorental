37:T400a,<p>Your house rules are one of the most important documents in your short-term rental operation. They set guest expectations before arrival, protect your property during stays, and give you a documented reference point if something goes wrong.</p>
<p>Well-crafted house rules prevent misunderstandings, reduce damage, and help you maintain a 5-star review profile. Vague or missing rules, on the other hand, lead to noise complaints from neighbors, unexpected guests, property damage, and difficult review situations.</p>
<p>This guide provides a complete <strong>house rules template for short-term rentals</strong> that you can adapt for your property, along with guidance on how to communicate your rules effectively and enforce them professionally.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="why-house-rules-matter-for-str-hosts">Why House Rules Matter for STR Hosts</h2>
<h3 id="legal-and-platform-protection">Legal and Platform Protection</h3>
<p>On Airbnb, your house rules are part of the booking agreement. The <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance">FTC advises consumers</a> to review terms before purchasing. When a guest books, they agree to follow your rules. If a guest violates your rules and causes damage or creates problems, having documented rules strengthens any claim you make through Airbnb's resolution process or your insurance provider.</p>
<h3 id="guest-experience-management">Guest Experience Management</h3>
<p>Rules are not just about restriction — they are about setting the right expectations. A guest who knows exactly what to expect arrives in the right mindset. Clear rules about check-in time, parking, and WiFi usage eliminate the most common guest questions.</p>
<h3 id="neighbor-and-community-relations">Neighbor and Community Relations</h3>
<p>Many STR conflicts with neighbors and local governments stem from guest behavior — noise, parking, and parties. Strong house rules protect your relationships with the surrounding community.</p>
<p>For broader context on managing your STR professionally, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="how-to-display-your-house-rules">How to Display Your House Rules</h2>
<h3 id="airbnb-s-house-rules-section">Airbnb's House Rules Section</h3>
<p>Airbnb provides a dedicated house rules section in your listing settings. Use this to enter key rules that guests must accept before booking. Keep this list to your most essential rules — a wall of text discourages guests from reading anything.</p>
<h3 id="physical-guest-welcome-book">Physical Guest Welcome Book</h3>
<p>Place a printed or digital welcome book (using tools like Hostfully or Touch Stay) at the property. This can include your full rules plus property instructions, local recommendations, and emergency information.</p>
<h3 id="pre-arrival-message">Pre-Arrival Message</h3>
<p>Include a brief rules summary in your automated pre-arrival message. Focus on the rules most relevant to arrival day: check-in time, parking, noise hours, and checkout procedures.</p>
<h3 id="qr-code-or-digital-house-manual">QR Code or Digital House Manual</h3>
<p>Some hosts use a QR code displayed at the property that links to a digital version of their house rules and property manual. This keeps information accessible without printing.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="complete-house-rules-template-for-short-term-rentals">Complete House Rules Template for Short-Term Rentals</h2>
<p>Below is a comprehensive template. Customize sections for your property type, local context, and personal preferences. Items in brackets [ ] indicate where you should fill in your specific details.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="welcome-and-introduction">Welcome and Introduction</h3>
<p>Welcome to [Property Name]! We are thrilled to have you as our guest. To ensure a comfortable stay for you and a respectful experience for our neighbors, please review and follow these house rules. By booking, you agree to these terms.</p>
<p>For any questions or issues during your stay, contact us at [Phone/Email].</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="check-in-and-check-out">Check-In and Check-Out</h3>
<p><strong>Check-in time:</strong> [3:00 PM] or after
<strong>Check-out time:</strong> By [11:00 AM]</p>
<p>Early check-in and late check-out are available when possible. Please message us at least 24 hours in advance to request — we will confirm based on the cleaning schedule.</p>
<p>Self check-in instructions will be sent to you [48 hours] before arrival. Please follow the instructions carefully to access the property.</p>
<p>Upon check-out, please:</p>
<ul>
<li>Strip beds and leave linens in a pile (or in the location specified)</li>
<li>Load and start the dishwasher</li>
<li>Dispose of all trash in the [location of trash/recycling bins]</li>
<li>Return all keys to the lockbox (if applicable)</li>
<li>Close and lock all windows and doors</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3 id="maximum-occupancy">Maximum Occupancy</h3>
<p>This property accommodates a maximum of <strong>[X] guests</strong>. The number of guests staying overnight must not exceed this limit. Day visitors are permitted up to [X] additional people with prior approval.</p>
<p>Exceeding the maximum occupancy without approval may result in immediate termination of the reservation without refund.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="noise-and-quiet-hours">Noise and Quiet Hours</h3>
<p><strong>Quiet hours:</strong> [10:00 PM to 8:00 AM]</p>
<p>During quiet hours, please keep noise to a level that cannot be heard outside the property. This property is located in a [residential neighborhood / building with shared walls] and we ask all guests to be respectful of neighbors.</p>
<p>At all times, guests are expected to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Keep outdoor noise at a moderate level</li>
<li>Avoid slamming doors</li>
<li>Use headphones for music and videos after quiet hours</li>
<li>Limit outdoor gatherings to [X] people</li>
</ul>
<p>Noise violations reported by neighbors may result in immediate termination of the reservation.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="no-parties-or-events">No Parties or Events</h3>
<p>Events, parties, and large gatherings are <strong>not permitted</strong> at this property. This includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Birthday or graduation parties</li>
<li>Wedding or rehearsal events</li>
<li>Corporate events</li>
<li>Any gathering with guests not registered in the booking</li>
</ul>
<p>If you are looking for a space for an event, please inquire separately so we can assess whether we can accommodate your needs.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="pets">Pets</h3>
<p><strong>This property [is / is not] pet-friendly.</strong></p>
<p>[If pet-friendly:]</p>
<ul>
<li>Dogs are permitted (maximum [X] dogs, [weight limit if applicable])</li>
<li>Cats are [permitted / not permitted]</li>
<li>A pet fee of $[XX] per stay applies (charged separately)</li>
<li>Pets must not be left unattended in the property for more than [X] hours</li>
<li>Please clean up after pets in all outdoor areas</li>
<li>Pets are not permitted on furniture or beds unless specifically approved</li>
<li>Any pet damage will be charged to the guest</li>
</ul>
<p>[If not pet-friendly:]
No pets are permitted at this property, including emotional support animals that are not certified service animals. Review <a href="https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/fair_housing_equal_opp/assistance_animals">HUD's pet policy guidance</a> carefully. Undisclosed pets may result in a fee and/or reservation cancellation.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="smoking">Smoking</h3>
<p><strong>Smoking of any kind is strictly prohibited</strong> inside this property, including cigarettes, cigars, marijuana, and vaping.</p>
<p>Designated smoking areas (if applicable): [outdoor area description, e.g., "back patio only, dispose of butts in the ashtray provided"]</p>
<p>A cleaning fee of $[XXX] will be assessed if smoking inside the property is detected.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="parking">Parking</h3>
<p>[Number] parking space(s) are available for guests:</p>
<ul>
<li>[Description: driveway, dedicated lot, street parking]</li>
<li>[Any restrictions: no vehicles over certain height, no commercial vehicles]</li>
</ul>
<p>Street parking rules: [note any permit requirements, street cleaning days, time restrictions]</p>
<p>Please do not block [neighbor's driveway / fire hydrant / other].</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="trash-and-recycling">Trash and Recycling</h3>
<p>Trash pickup is on [day(s) of the week]. Please put bins at the curb by [time] the night before.</p>
<p>Trash bins are located [location]. Recycling bins are located [location].</p>
<p>Please separate: [recycling categories — paper, glass, cans, plastics per local rules].</p>
<p>Do not leave trash bags outside the designated bins or in the yard.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="utilities-and-appliances">Utilities and Appliances</h3>
<p>To keep costs reasonable and the property well-maintained:</p>
<ul>
<li>Turn off lights, fans, and TVs when leaving the property</li>
<li>Keep the thermostat set between [65°F and 75°F / 18°C and 24°C]</li>
<li>Do not open windows and run the AC/heat simultaneously</li>
<li>Report any appliance issues promptly rather than attempting repairs</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Appliance instructions:</strong> [Specify any non-obvious appliances — how to use the TV inputs, fireplace safety, outdoor grill lighting procedure, etc.]</p>
<hr>

<h2 id="tips-for-writing-effective-house-rules">Tips for Writing Effective House Rules</h2>
<h3 id="keep-rules-firm-but-friendly">Keep Rules Firm but Friendly</h3>
<p>Your tone matters. Rules written as commands ("Do not do X or you will be charged") feel adversarial. Rules framed with context ("We ask guests to keep noise down after 10pm to respect our neighbors") set the same expectation more warmly.</p>
<h3 id="explain-the-why">Explain the Why</h3>
<p>Guests are more likely to follow rules when they understand the reason. "No smoking inside" is a rule. "No smoking inside — our bedding and furnishings absorb odors quickly and affect other guests' experiences" is a reason that builds understanding.</p>
<h3 id="do-not-overwhelm-with-length">Do Not Overwhelm With Length</h3>
<p>A 30-item list of rules discourages guests from reading any of them. Focus on the rules that actually matter for your property and market. Remove generic filler.</p>
<h3 id="revisit-after-each-problem">Revisit After Each Problem</h3>
<p>Every time something goes wrong, ask whether a house rule could have prevented it. Add or refine rules based on real experience with your specific property and guest mix.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="house-rules-and-guest-screening">House Rules and Guest Screening</h2>
<p>Your house rules work together with your guest screening process. Guests who do not fit your rules profile can be screened out before booking. See our guide on <a href="/blog/guest-screening-short-term-rental">guest screening for short-term rentals</a> for a full approach to identifying and accepting the right guests.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="enforcing-your-house-rules">Enforcing Your House Rules</h2>
<p>Having house rules is only half the equation. Enforcement — when necessary — requires:</p>
<p><strong>Noise Monitoring Technology:</strong> Devices like Minut or NoiseAware detect decibel levels without recording audio. They alert you when noise exceeds a threshold so you can contact guests before a neighbor complaint is filed.</p>
<p><strong>Security Deposits:</strong> While Airbnb has shifted away from traditional security deposits, you can use damage protection add-ons or require deposits through direct booking platforms.</p>
<p><strong>Clear Communication:</strong> If a guest violates a rule, address it promptly but professionally. Message first, then call if necessary. Keep all communication within the Airbnb platform for documentation.</p>
<p><strong>Airbnb's Resolution Center:</strong> For confirmed rule violations that result in damage or additional cleaning, submit a claim through Airbnb's resolution center promptly after checkout.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Your house rules are not bureaucratic fine print — they are a core tool for protecting your property, managing guest expectations, and maintaining your hosting reputation. A well-crafted set of rules reduces friction before it starts.</p>
<p>Use the template above as a starting point, customize it for your property, and integrate your rules into every touchpoint of the guest journey: the listing, the booking confirmation, the pre-arrival message, and the welcome book at the property.</p>

<p><strong>House Rules Quick Reference:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Cover check-in/out, occupancy, noise, pets, smoking, parking, and utilities</li>
<li>Keep rules friendly in tone but firm in expectation</li>
<li>Display rules in the listing, pre-arrival message, and on-property welcome book</li>
<li>Revisit and update rules based on actual guest incidents</li>
<li>Pair rules with noise monitoring and screening for full protection</li>
</ul>
