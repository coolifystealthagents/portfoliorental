2a:T44b1,<p>Virtual tours for rental property listings are no longer a luxury. They are an expectation. A recent Zillow survey found that 75% of renters consider virtual tours important when searching for a new home. Properties with virtual tours get 87% more views than those without, and they rent faster.</p>
<p>The shift started during the pandemic, but it stuck because it works. Tenants want to see a property before they drive across town for a showing. Landlords want to attract serious, pre-qualified prospects instead of tire-kickers. Virtual tours solve both problems.</p>
<p>This guide covers everything you need to know about virtual tours for rental property listings. You will learn the different types of tours, the best platforms to use, what equipment you need, how much it costs, and how to create tours that actually convert viewers into tenants.</p>
<h2 id="types-of-virtual-tours">Types of Virtual Tours</h2>
<p>Not all virtual tours are the same. The right type depends on your budget, your property, and your target tenant. Here are the three main types.</p>
<h3 id="360-degree-photo-tours">360-Degree Photo Tours</h3>
<p>A 360-degree photo tour is the simplest type of virtual tour. You take panoramic photos of each room using a 360-degree camera or a smartphone app. The viewer can look around each room in all directions, but they cannot move freely between rooms.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Budget-conscious landlords, smaller units, and landlords who want to create tours quickly. A 360-degree tour takes about 30 minutes to shoot for a typical apartment.</p>
<p><strong>Limitations:</strong> The viewer jumps between rooms rather than walking through the space. The experience is less immersive than a full 3D tour.</p>
<h3 id="video-walkthroughs">Video Walkthroughs</h3>
<p>A video walkthrough is exactly what it sounds like. You record a video as you walk through the property, narrating key features along the way. The viewer watches the video like a YouTube clip.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Properties with unique features you want to highlight, landlords who are comfortable on camera, and social media marketing. Video tours work great on Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook.</p>
<p><strong>Limitations:</strong> The viewer cannot control the pace or angle. They see what you choose to show them. Video tours also feel dated faster than other options and may need to be reshot if you make changes to the unit.</p>
<h3 id="3d-virtual-tours">3D Virtual Tours</h3>
<p>A 3D virtual tour is the gold standard. It creates a complete digital model of your property that viewers can navigate at their own pace. They click to move from room to room, look up and down, zoom in on details, and even take measurements. Some platforms also generate a dollhouse view that shows the entire floor plan from above.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Higher-end rentals, large properties, and landlords who want the most immersive experience. 3D tours have the highest engagement rates and longest viewing times.</p>
<p><strong>Limitations:</strong> They require specialized equipment or a professional service. They cost more and take longer to create than other options.</p>
<h2 id="best-platforms-for-virtual-tours">Best Platforms for Virtual Tours</h2>
<p>Several platforms make it easy to create and host virtual tours for rental properties. Here are the top options.</p>
<h3 id="matterport">Matterport</h3>
<p>Matterport is the industry leader in 3D virtual tours. Their platform creates highly detailed, immersive tours that let viewers walk through your property room by room. Matterport tours include a dollhouse view, floor plans, and measurement tools.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> The Matterport Pro2 camera costs about $3,600. Their newer Pro3 camera runs about $5,000. However, you can also create Matterport tours using a compatible 360-degree camera ($300 to $500) or even an iPhone with LiDAR. Hosting plans start at $9.99 per month for five spaces.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords with multiple properties who plan to create tours regularly. The upfront camera cost is high, but per-tour costs drop fast once you own the equipment.</p>
<h3 id="zillow-3d-home">Zillow 3D Home</h3>
<p>Zillow offers a free 3D tour tool through their Zillow 3D Home app. You can create tours using your smartphone. The app guides you through the process and stitches the photos together automatically.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> Free to create and host. This is the most budget-friendly option by far.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords listing on Zillow who want to add a virtual tour without any extra cost. The quality is lower than Matterport, but the price is unbeatable.</p>
<h3 id="kuula">Kuula</h3>
<p>Kuula is a popular platform for 360-degree photo tours. You upload your 360-degree photos, and Kuula creates an interactive tour with hotspots, labels, and floor plans. The interface is simple and the results look polished.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> Plans start at $12 per month. A free tier is available with limited features.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords who already have a 360-degree camera and want an affordable, professional-looking tour platform.</p>
<h3 id="cloudpano">CloudPano</h3>
<p>CloudPano is another 360-degree tour platform aimed at real estate professionals. It offers branding options, lead capture forms, and integration with listing sites.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> Plans start at $19 per month.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Landlords who want lead capture and branding built into their virtual tours.</p>
<h3 id="iguide">iGuide</h3>
<p>iGuide creates floor plans and virtual tours simultaneously using their proprietary camera system. The tours include accurate room measurements and square footage calculations.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> iGuide is typically offered through certified photographers. Tours cost $200 to $400 per property.</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Properties where accurate floor plans and measurements matter, such as larger units or commercial rentals.</p>
<h2 id="equipment-you-need">Equipment You Need</h2>
<p>The equipment you need depends on the type of tour you want to create.</p>
<h3 id="for-smartphone-tours">For Smartphone Tours</h3>
<p>If you are using Zillow 3D Home or a basic 360-degree app, all you need is a recent smartphone. An iPhone 12 or newer with LiDAR is ideal, but any modern smartphone with a good camera works. Add a smartphone tripod ($15 to $30) to keep your shots stable.</p>
<h3 id="for-360-degree-photo-tours">For 360-Degree Photo Tours</h3>
<p>A dedicated 360-degree camera produces better results than a smartphone. Popular options include:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ricoh Theta X</strong> ($500): High-quality 360-degree photos and video. Works with most tour platforms.</li>
<li><strong>Insta360 X4</strong> ($500): Great image quality with easy-to-use app. Good for both tours and video content.</li>
<li><strong>GoPro Max</strong> ($400): Rugged and versatile. Good if you also want action video capabilities.</li>
</ul>
<p>You will also need a tripod with a 360-degree camera mount ($30 to $80) and a tablet or phone to control the camera remotely.</p>
<h3 id="for-matterport-3d-tours">For Matterport 3D Tours</h3>
<p>If you go with Matterport, you can use their dedicated cameras (Pro2 at $3,600 or Pro3 at $5,000) or a compatible third-party camera. The Matterport-compatible Ricoh Theta X produces good results at a much lower price point.</p>
<h3 id="lighting-equipment">Lighting Equipment</h3>
<p>Natural light works best for virtual tours, but you may need supplemental lighting for darker rooms. A pair of portable LED panels ($50 to $100 for a set) can brighten bathrooms, closets, and basement areas.</p>
<h2 id="how-to-create-a-diy-virtual-tour">How to Create a DIY Virtual Tour</h2>
<p>You do not need to hire a professional to create a solid virtual tour. Follow these steps to do it yourself.</p>
<p><strong>Step 1: Prepare the property.</strong> Clean every room thoroughly. Declutter surfaces, make beds, and open curtains. Turn on all lights. Remove personal items and anything that distracts from the space. For more tips, read our guide on <a href="/blog/rental-property-photography-tips">rental property photography tips</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Step 2: Plan your route.</strong> Walk through the property and decide the order you will capture each room. Start at the front door and move logically through the space. Note any tricky areas with poor lighting or tight spaces.</p>
<p><strong>Step 3: Set up your camera.</strong> Mount your camera on a tripod at chest height (about 5 feet). This height gives the most natural perspective. Place the tripod in the center of each room, away from walls and furniture.</p>
<p><strong>Step 4: Capture each room.</strong> Take your 360-degree photos or scans in each room. Make sure to capture hallways and transitions between rooms. For Matterport, follow their app's on-screen instructions. For 360-degree cameras, take one shot per room and one in each hallway.</p>
<p><strong>Step 5: Upload and process.</strong> Upload your photos or scans to your chosen platform. Most platforms process and stitch the tour automatically. Add labels, hotspots, and a floor plan if your platform supports them.</p>
<p><strong>Step 6: Review and publish.</strong> Walk through the finished tour on both desktop and mobile. Check for blurry spots, stitching errors, and missing rooms. Once you are satisfied, embed the tour in your listing.</p>
<p>The whole process takes about one to two hours per property, including prep time.</p>
<h2 id="cost-analysis-virtual-tours-vs-traditional-showings">Cost Analysis: Virtual Tours vs. Traditional Showings</h2>
<p>Let us break down the real cost of virtual tours compared to traditional in-person showings.</p>
<p><strong>Traditional showings cost more than you think.</strong> Each in-person showing takes 30 to 60 minutes of your time, plus travel. If you show a property to 10 prospects, that is 5 to 10 hours of work. At an average landlord's hourly value of $50, that is $250 to $500 in time alone. Add gas, wear and tear, and coordination hassle.</p>

<p><strong>The ROI is strong.</strong> If a virtual tour reduces your showings from 10 to 3 by pre-qualifying tenants online, you save hours of time per vacancy. If it reduces your vacancy period by even a few days, the savings in lost rent far exceed the tour cost.</p>
<p>For a rental at $1,500 per month, each vacant day costs $50. A $200 virtual tour that fills the unit three days faster saves you $150 in vacancy costs, essentially paying for itself. Over a year with multiple turnovers, the savings add up fast.</p>
<h2 id="what-tenants-actually-want">What Tenants Actually Want</h2>
<p>Understanding what tenants look for in a virtual tour helps you create better content.</p>
<p><strong>Accurate representation.</strong> Tenants' top complaint about virtual tours is that the property looks different in person. Use natural lighting, avoid wide-angle distortion, and never use filters. Show the property as it actually is. Pair your tour with a compelling <a href="/blog/writing-rental-listing-descriptions">rental listing description</a> that matches the accuracy of your visuals.</p>
<p><strong>Kitchen and bathroom detail.</strong> These are the rooms tenants zoom into most. Make sure your tour captures countertops, cabinets, appliances, and fixtures clearly. Get close-up shots of any upgraded features.</p>
<p><strong>Storage spaces.</strong> Closets, pantries, and garage spaces matter to renters. Include them in your tour even if they are small. Tenants want to know where their stuff will go.</p>
<p><strong>Natural light.</strong> Tenants pay attention to how much light a unit gets. Shoot your tour during the brightest part of the day with curtains open. Show windows prominently.</p>
<p><strong>Neighborhood context.</strong> Some tour platforms let you add exterior views and neighborhood photos. Show the view from the balcony, the parking area, the pool, or nearby parks. These details help out-of-town tenants especially.</p>
<h2 id="conversion-rates-and-results">Conversion Rates and Results</h2>
<p>Virtual tours do not just attract more views. They attract better leads. Here is what the data shows.</p>
<p>Properties with virtual tours receive 87% more views than listings with photos only. The average viewing time on a listing with a virtual tour is 5 to 10 times longer than a listing with just photos. Longer engagement means the tenant is more interested and more likely to apply.</p>
<p>Landlords who use virtual tours report 40% fewer unnecessary in-person showings. The people who do show up have already explored the property online and are seriously interested. This saves you time and leads to faster lease signings.</p>
<p>Virtual tours also expand your reach to out-of-town tenants. Relocating renters cannot always visit in person before signing a lease. A high-quality virtual tour gives them the confidence to apply remotely, which is especially valuable in competitive markets.</p>

<h2 id="integrating-virtual-tours-with-your-listings">Integrating Virtual Tours With Your Listings</h2>
<p>A virtual tour only works if people can find it. Here is how to integrate your tour with major listing platforms.</p>
<p><strong>Zillow and Trulia.</strong> If you created your tour with Zillow 3D Home, it automatically appears on your Zillow listing. For other tour platforms, paste the tour URL into the virtual tour field when creating your listing.</p>
<p><strong>Apartments.com.</strong> You can upload Matterport tours directly. For other tour types, add the URL in the virtual tour section of your listing.</p>
<p><strong>Facebook Marketplace.</strong> Embed the tour link in your listing description. You can also share the tour directly to your property's Facebook page.</p>
<p><strong>Your own website.</strong> Most tour platforms provide an embed code. Copy and paste it into your property listing page. Make the tour prominent, not buried at the bottom.</p>
<p><strong>Email marketing.</strong> Include the virtual tour link in any emails to prospective tenants. A subject line like "Take a virtual tour of your next home" gets strong open rates.</p>
<p>For a complete guide to listing your property, see our <a href="/blog/rental-property-marketing-strategies">rental property marketing strategies</a>.</p>
<h2 id="accessibility-considerations">Accessibility Considerations</h2>
<p>Virtual tours should be accessible to all prospective tenants. Here are some accessibility tips.</p>
<p><strong>Keyboard navigation.</strong> Make sure your tour platform supports keyboard navigation for people who cannot use a mouse. Matterport and most major platforms support this.</p>
<p><strong>Screen reader compatibility.</strong> Add descriptive text to each room in your tour. Instead of "Room 1," label it "Primary bedroom with two windows facing south." This helps visually impaired users understand the space.</p>
<p><strong>Mobile compatibility.</strong> Most renters browse on their phones. Test your tour on both iOS and Android devices. Make sure it loads quickly and responds to touch controls smoothly.</p>
<p><strong>Bandwidth considerations.</strong> 3D tours can be data-heavy. If your target market includes tenants with limited internet access, also provide a video walkthrough option that uses less bandwidth.</p>
<p><strong>Language.</strong> If you rent in an area with a large non-English-speaking population, consider adding labels in multiple languages.</p>
<h2 id="the-bottom-line">The Bottom Line</h2>
<p>Virtual tours for rental property listings are one of the most effective tools for filling vacancies faster. They attract more views, pre-qualify tenants before in-person showings, and help out-of-town renters apply with confidence.</p>
<p>You do not need expensive equipment to get started. A smartphone and a free platform like Zillow 3D Home can produce a solid tour. As you grow your portfolio, investing in a dedicated 360-degree camera and a platform like Matterport will pay dividends across multiple properties.</p>
<p>The key is to start. Even a simple video walkthrough is better than no tour at all. Your next tenant is probably searching online right now. Give them a reason to choose your property over the competition. The <a href="https://www.nar.realtor" rel="noopener" target="_blank">National Association of Realtors</a> offers additional marketing resources for landlords, and you can enhance your listing's visibility by claiming and optimizing your <a href="https://support.google.com/business" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Google Business Profile</a> to showcase your virtual tours directly in search results.</p>
