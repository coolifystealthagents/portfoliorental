26:T3a96,<p>When you host on Airbnb, the platform includes something called AirCover for Hosts — a suite of protections that includes liability coverage up to $1 million per occurrence. At first glance, this sounds comprehensive. Many hosts assume it means they don't need to purchase their own STR insurance.</p>
<p>That assumption can be costly.</p>
<p>Airbnb's host liability coverage is a supplemental protection program, not a full-replacement insurance policy. It has significant coverage gaps, is controlled entirely by Airbnb, and only applies to bookings made through the Airbnb platform. Understanding exactly what it covers — and what it doesn't — is essential for every host.</p>
<p>This guide breaks down AirCover for Hosts in detail, explains the coverage gaps, and shows you how to build a complete protection strategy. For the broader liability picture, see our <a href="/blog/liability-protection-short-term-rental">liability protection short term rental</a> guide.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-is-aircover-for-hosts">What Is AirCover for Hosts?</h2>
<p>AirCover for Hosts is Airbnb's umbrella protection program, launched in 2022 and updated periodically since. It includes two primary components:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Host Damage Protection:</strong> Covers up to $3 million in property damage caused by guests, including damage to your home, belongings, vehicles, and watercraft/boating equipment</li>
<li><strong>Host Liability Insurance:</strong> Provides up to $1 million in liability coverage if a guest or third party is injured during an Airbnb stay and brings a claim against you as a host</li>
</ol>
<p>Both protections are included automatically for all Airbnb hosts at no additional cost. You don't need to apply or pay a premium — they activate for each booking through the platform.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-airbnb-s-host-liability-coverage-actually-covers">What Airbnb's Host Liability Coverage Actually Covers</h2>
<p>According to Airbnb's AirCover terms, the liability protection component covers:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bodily injury claims:</strong> A guest or third party who is physically injured during an Airbnb stay and sues you for damages</li>
<li><strong>Property damage to third-party property:</strong> If your guest damages a neighbor's property, for example</li>
<li><strong>Coverage limit:</strong> Up to $1 million per occurrence</li>
</ul>
<p>The coverage is administered through a program that Airbnb controls, underwritten by a third-party insurer.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-critical-gaps-in-airbnb-s-liability-coverage">The Critical Gaps in Airbnb's Liability Coverage</h2>
<p>Understanding what the coverage does not include is just as important as understanding what it does.</p>
<h3 id="gap-1-it-only-covers-airbnb-bookings">Gap 1: It Only Covers Airbnb Bookings</h3>
<p>AirCover applies exclusively to guests who booked through the Airbnb platform. If you also list on Vrbo, Booking.com, or accept direct bookings:</p>
<ul>
<li>Any claim arising from a non-Airbnb booking is completely uncovered by AirCover</li>
<li>You'd be relying on your homeowner's insurance (which may deny the claim as a business activity) or no coverage at all</li>
</ul>
<p>For hosts who multi-list across platforms — a common and recommended strategy — AirCover leaves significant gaps.</p>
<h3 id="gap-2-airbnb-controls-the-claims-process">Gap 2: Airbnb Controls the Claims Process</h3>
<p>Unlike a traditional insurance policy where an independent insurer evaluates and processes claims, AirCover claims are decided by Airbnb. This means:</p>
<ul>
<li>Airbnb investigates and determines whether a claim is covered</li>
<li>Airbnb decides what amount to pay</li>
<li>There is no independent arbiter — you cannot appeal to your state's insurance commissioner</li>
<li>The terms of coverage can change at any time with notice in Airbnb's terms of service</li>
</ul>
<p>Hosts have reported frustrating experiences with AirCover claims being denied or significantly reduced, with limited recourse.</p>
<h3 id="gap-3-significant-coverage-exclusions">Gap 3: Significant Coverage Exclusions</h3>
<p>AirCover liability coverage excludes a wide range of situations, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Intentional acts:</strong> Injuries caused intentionally are not covered (reasonable, but worth noting)</li>
<li><strong>Business activities:</strong> If you're conducting any business other than STR hosting on the premises, claims arising from those activities may be excluded</li>
<li><strong>Vehicle-related claims:</strong> Most AirCover terms exclude claims arising from vehicle accidents, even on your property</li>
<li><strong>Certain property types:</strong> Coverage terms may vary for boats, co-hosted properties, or certain commercial spaces</li>
<li><strong>Punitive damages:</strong> Punitive damage awards may be excluded</li>
<li><strong>Employment-related claims:</strong> If you employ cleaners or property managers and they're injured, AirCover doesn't cover workers' compensation claims</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="gap-4-it-s-not-a-real-insurance-policy">Gap 4: It's Not a Real Insurance Policy</h3>
<p>This is perhaps the most important distinction. AirCover is a contractual protection program, not an insurance policy. The differences are significant:</p>

<h3 id="gap-5-interaction-with-your-homeowner-s-insurance">Gap 5: Interaction With Your Homeowner's Insurance</h3>
<p>Airbnb's terms state that AirCover is designed to be primary coverage that pays before your homeowner's insurance. However, if your homeowner's insurer learns you're using the property for commercial STR activity, they may:</p>
<ul>
<li>Deny unrelated claims (your pipes freeze and flood the property — an unrelated incident, but now the insurer knows you're running an STR)</li>
<li>Cancel your policy at renewal</li>
<li>Dispute coverage in litigation</li>
</ul>
<p>AirCover was not designed to fill the gap left by a homeowner's policy that excludes STR activity.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="scenarios-where-aircover-leaves-you-exposed">Scenarios Where AirCover Leaves You Exposed</h2>
<h3 id="scenario-1-multi-platform-hosting">Scenario 1: Multi-Platform Hosting</h3>
<p>You accept 60% of your bookings through Airbnb and 40% through Vrbo. A guest booked through Vrbo slips on your pool deck and suffers a broken hip. The injury leads to a $350,000 lawsuit. AirCover doesn't apply. Your homeowner's insurance denies the claim as a business activity. You're personally responsible for the judgment.</p>
<h3 id="scenario-2-disputed-aircover-claim">Scenario 2: Disputed AirCover Claim</h3>
<p>A guest claims your staircase handrail was loose and they injured their wrist. They demand $75,000. Airbnb investigates, finds inconsistencies in the guest's story, and denies the AirCover claim. You hire an attorney and eventually settle for $30,000 — out of pocket.</p>
<h3 id="scenario-3-neighbor-property-damage">Scenario 3: Neighbor Property Damage</h3>
<p>Your guests throw a party (you weren't aware) and damage a neighbor's fence and landscaping. The neighbor sues you for $15,000 in property damage. The neighbor's claim is against you as the property owner, not against the guests. This may or may not fall within AirCover's property damage scope depending on the specific facts and Airbnb's determination.</p>
<h3 id="scenario-4-cleaning-staff-injury">Scenario 4: Cleaning Staff Injury</h3>
<p>Your cleaning contractor slips and falls in your rental and files a workers' compensation or premises liability claim against you. AirCover does not cover claims by third-party service providers.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-proper-str-insurance-provides">What Proper STR Insurance Provides</h2>
<p>A purpose-built short-term rental insurance policy addresses the gaps that AirCover leaves. Key differences:</p>
<p><strong>Multi-platform coverage:</strong> Your policy protects you regardless of which platform the booking came from or whether the guest was a direct booking.</p>
<p><strong>Independent claims handling:</strong> A licensed insurer with regulatory oversight processes your claim. You can file a complaint with your state's insurance commissioner if you believe a claim was wrongly denied.</p>
<p><strong>Consistent, guaranteed coverage:</strong> Policy terms are locked for the coverage period and cannot be unilaterally changed by the insurer mid-term.</p>
<p><strong>Comprehensive property coverage:</strong> STR-specific policies cover the structure, contents, and loss of rental income — going beyond what AirCover's damage protection offers.</p>
<p><strong>Liability sublimits for specific risks:</strong> Good STR policies include coverage for liquor liability, pool liability, and other specific risks relevant to vacation rental hosting.</p>
<p><strong>Workers' compensation or employer's liability:</strong> Some STR policies can be paired with these coverages to protect against claims by your cleaning and maintenance staff.</p>
<p>For a full comparison of STR insurance products, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-insurance">short-term rental insurance</a> guide.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="how-to-build-a-complete-protection-strategy">How to Build a Complete Protection Strategy</h2>
<p>The right approach treats AirCover as a secondary, supplemental benefit — not as your primary protection. Here's the recommended layered approach:</p>
<p><strong>Layer 1: Purpose-built STR insurance policy</strong>
Your primary and most important protection. Look for commercial general liability coverage of at least $1 million per occurrence, property damage coverage at full replacement value, and loss of income coverage.</p>
<p><strong>Layer 2: AirCover for Hosts</strong>
Continue accepting Airbnb bookings and let AirCover act as a secondary resource. If a claim arises from an Airbnb booking and AirCover provides a quick resolution, that's a positive outcome. But don't rely on it.</p>
<p><strong>Layer 3: LLC or other legal structure</strong>
An LLC separates your personal assets from business liability. <a href="https://www.forbes.com/advisor/business/how-to-start-an-llc/">Forbes covers LLC formation</a> in detail. See our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-llc-setup">short term rental llc setup</a> guide for setup guidance.</p>
<p><strong>Layer 4: Umbrella policy</strong>
For hosts with significant personal assets or high-revenue operations, a commercial umbrella policy provides an additional layer of coverage above primary policy limits.</p>
<p><strong>Layer 5: Operational risk management</strong>
Regular safety inspections, guest screening, clear house rules, and security systems reduce the likelihood of incidents in the first place.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-to-look-for-in-an-str-insurance-policy">What to Look for in an STR Insurance Policy</h2>
<p>When shopping for STR insurance to supplement AirCover, prioritize:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Commercial general liability:</strong> At least $1 million per occurrence, $2 million aggregate</li>
<li><strong>Property damage:</strong> Full replacement cost, not actual cash value</li>
<li><strong>Loss of rental income:</strong> Covers income lost while property is uninhabitable due to a covered loss</li>
<li><strong>Multi-platform coverage:</strong> Explicitly covers all booking sources, including direct bookings</li>
<li><strong>No exclusion for short-term rental use:</strong> The policy must explicitly cover STR activity</li>
<li><strong>Medical payments coverage:</strong> Covers minor injuries without requiring proof of negligence</li>
<li><strong>Claims service reputation:</strong> Research the insurer's claims handling reputation</li>
</ul>
<p>Learn about <a href="https://www.investopedia.com/terms/l/liability_insurance.asp">liability insurance basics</a> before comparing quotes. Insurers with STR-specific products include Proper Insurance, Steadily, Slice, Obie, and others. Rates vary by property type, location, and coverage limits. Always compare multiple quotes.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="frequently-asked-questions-about-aircover">Frequently Asked Questions About AirCover</h2>
<p><strong>Does AirCover cover me if a guest injures a third party (like a neighbor)?</strong>
Potentially, but only for bookings made through Airbnb. The specific facts of the claim determine coverage, and Airbnb makes the determination.</p>
<p><strong>Does AirCover replace homeowner's insurance?</strong>
No. Airbnb explicitly states that AirCover does not replace homeowner's, renter's, or other insurance. It's designed as supplemental protection.</p>
<p><strong>Can I use AirCover if I also rent on Vrbo?</strong>
AirCover only applies to Airbnb bookings. Claims arising from Vrbo or direct bookings are not covered.</p>
<p><strong>What happens if AirCover denies my claim?</strong>
You have limited recourse. AirCover is not insurance, so state insurance regulations don't apply. You can escalate within Airbnb's support system, but you cannot file a state insurance complaint.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="summary-and-key-takeaways">Summary and Key Takeaways</h2>
<p>Airbnb's host liability coverage through AirCover provides valuable but limited protection. Every host should understand:</p>
<ul>
<li>AirCover covers bodily injury and property damage claims up to $1 million — for Airbnb bookings only</li>
<li>It is not a real insurance policy and lacks the regulatory protections insurance provides</li>
<li>Significant gaps include: non-Airbnb bookings, coverage exclusions, no independent claims process</li>
<li>Every serious STR host should carry a dedicated STR insurance policy as their primary protection</li>
<li>AirCover is best treated as a supplemental benefit, not your primary safety net</li>
<li>An LLC, proper insurance, and operational risk management together form a complete protection strategy</li>
</ul>
<p>For a full guide to STR compliance, operations, and profitability, visit our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
