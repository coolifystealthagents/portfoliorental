26:T38f2,<p>Corporate housing is one of the most lucrative — and least understood — segments of the rental market. While traditional landlords compete on price in a crowded unfurnished market, corporate housing operators often achieve 1.5–2.5x the equivalent unfurnished monthly rent by serving a market that values quality, convenience, and flexibility over everything else.</p>
<p>Breaking into the corporate housing market requires a specific type of property preparation, a distinct approach to marketing, and a management model calibrated to the needs of business tenants. But for landlords with the right assets and market position, the financial rewards are substantial.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-is-corporate-housing">What Is Corporate Housing?</h2>
<p>Corporate housing occupies the space between hotels and traditional long-term rentals. It offers:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Fully furnished units</strong>: Everything from furniture and linens to kitchenware and small appliances is included.</li>
<li><strong>Flexible lease terms</strong>: Stays typically run 30 days to 12 months, far shorter than traditional 12-month leases but longer than hotel or short-term rental stays.</li>
<li><strong>All utilities included</strong>: In most corporate housing arrangements, rent includes electricity, water, gas, high-speed internet, and cable.</li>
<li><strong>Move-in ready condition</strong>: Business clients (and the companies paying for their housing) expect hotel-like quality on arrival.</li>
</ul>
<p>The primary tenant pool includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Corporate relocatees whose permanent housing is not yet ready</li>
<li>Project-based workers deployed for 2–9 month engagements (consultants, construction project managers, IT specialists)</li>
<li>Traveling healthcare professionals (travel nurses, locum physicians, allied health workers)</li>
<li>Insurance displacement clients (families temporarily displaced by home damage)</li>
<li>Government and military personnel on temporary duty assignments. <a href="https://www.gsa.gov/travel/plan-book/per-diem-rates">GSA per diem rates</a> set government housing budgets.</li>
<li>International business executives and expats on assignment</li>
</ul>
<p>For context on how corporate housing fits into broader niche positioning strategies, see our guide to <a href="/blog/rental-property-niche-strategy">rental property niche strategies</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-financial-case-why-corporate-housing-commands-premium-rates">The Financial Case: Why Corporate Housing Commands Premium Rates</h2>
<p>The revenue premium in corporate housing is not accidental — it reflects genuine value delivered to the client:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Convenience</strong>: A fully furnished, move-in-ready unit is worth significant time savings to a relocating employee or traveling professional.</li>
<li><strong>Predictable cost</strong>: A single all-inclusive monthly rate simplifies budgeting for corporate HR and finance departments.</li>
<li><strong>Flexibility</strong>: Companies pay a premium for the ability to extend or shorten stays as project timelines shift.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Revenue illustration</strong>: A 2-bedroom apartment that rents for $2,200/month unfurnished might achieve $3,500–$4,500/month as corporate housing — a premium of $1,300–$2,300/month. Against furnishing costs of $8,000–$15,000 (which depreciate over years of use), the payback period is short.</p>
<p>However, costs are also higher: utilities, internet, cable, furnishing maintenance, more frequent cleaning, higher management intensity, and furnishing replacement. <a href="https://www.investopedia.com/articles/investing/062215/how-calculate-roi-rental-property.asp">Investopedia covers rental ROI</a> calculations in depth. Model these carefully before committing. Our <a href="/blog/cap-rate-real-estate">cap rate guide</a> provides the framework for evaluating how corporate housing income and costs translate to property value.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="is-your-property-suited-for-corporate-housing">Is Your Property Suited for Corporate Housing?</h2>
<p>Not every property is a good fit for the corporate housing market. Strong candidates share these characteristics:</p>
<h3 id="location">Location</h3>
<p>Corporate housing demand concentrates near:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Major corporate campuses</strong> (tech companies, headquarters, regional offices)</li>
<li><strong>Hospitals and medical centers</strong> (the travel nurse and locum physician market is massive and growing)</li>
<li><strong>Universities and research institutions</strong> (visiting faculty, research fellows, medical residents)</li>
<li><strong>Government facilities and military bases</strong> (TDY housing demand is consistent and often institutionally funded)</li>
<li><strong>Major construction or infrastructure projects</strong> (project-based workers need medium-term housing)</li>
<li><strong>Business districts</strong> (relocating executives often need temporary housing near downtown offices)</li>
</ul>
<p>A property within 15–20 minutes of one or more of these demand generators has natural corporate housing potential.</p>
<h3 id="property-characteristics">Property Characteristics</h3>
<p>Ideal corporate housing properties feature:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Private entry</strong> (business clients value privacy)</li>
<li><strong>Secure parking</strong> (often included in the package)</li>
<li><strong>In-unit washer/dryer</strong> (essential — a shared laundry is a dealbreaker for many corporate clients)</li>
<li><strong>Home office setup or dedicated workspace</strong></li>
<li><strong>Quality kitchen</strong> (business clients often cook and entertain)</li>
<li><strong>Bedroom separation</strong> for multi-person occupancy</li>
</ul>
<p>Studio and one-bedroom units work for solo travelers. Two- and three-bedroom units serve couples, families, or multi-person teams and command higher per-night equivalents.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="furnishing-your-corporate-housing-unit">Furnishing Your Corporate Housing Unit</h2>
<p>The furnishing standard is the most important differentiator between a successful corporate housing property and one that fails to attract premium clients.</p>
<h3 id="furniture">Furniture</h3>
<p>Invest in real furniture — not cheap flat-pack units. Corporate clients expect quality that signals professionalism:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Living room</strong>: Sofa and loveseat or sectional (performance fabric, easy to clean), coffee table, side tables, lamps, TV (65"+ for primary living areas)</li>
<li><strong>Dining area</strong>: Table and 4–6 chairs</li>
<li><strong>Bedroom(s)</strong>: Quality bed frames, nightstands, lamps, dresser or chest of drawers</li>
<li><strong>Mattresses</strong>: This is not the place to cut corners. A quality mattress (memory foam or innerspring hybrid) signals care and generates positive reviews.</li>
<li><strong>Home office</strong>: Desk, ergonomic chair, proper task lighting</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="kitchen">Kitchen</h3>
<p>Supply the kitchen as if you were moving in yourself:</p>
<ul>
<li>Full cookware set (pots, pans, baking sheets)</li>
<li>Dinnerware and glassware for 4–6 persons</li>
<li>Full utensil set</li>
<li>Small appliances: coffee maker, toaster, microwave, blender</li>
<li>Adequate pantry staples at move-in (oil, salt, pepper, coffee, tea)</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="linens-and-towels">Linens and Towels</h3>
<p>Provide hotel-quality linens:</p>
<ul>
<li>Two sets of sheets per bed (allows for laundry turnover)</li>
<li>Quality pillows and blankets</li>
<li>Two sets of bath towels, hand towels, and washcloths per bathroom</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="technology">Technology</h3>
<ul>
<li>Gigabit fiber internet is a non-negotiable for corporate tenants who work from home or need reliable connectivity for video calls. The <a href="https://www.fcc.gov/consumers/guides/broadband-speed-guide">FCC broadband speed guide</a> helps determine adequate service.</li>
<li>Smart TV with streaming app access</li>
<li>Smart lock for self-check-in (essential for efficient operation)</li>
</ul>
<p>Total furnishing budgets for a well-done 2-bedroom corporate unit typically run $10,000–$20,000 for quality that will last several years.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="building-your-corporate-housing-client-network">Building Your Corporate Housing Client Network</h2>
<p>Unlike traditional rentals — where you list on Zillow and Apartments.com and wait for applicants — corporate housing often requires proactive relationship-building with the organizations that generate tenant demand.</p>
<h3 id="direct-corporate-client-outreach">Direct Corporate Client Outreach</h3>
<p>Target the HR, relocation, and facilities management departments of major employers near your property:</p>
<ul>
<li>Email or call directly: introduce your property, provide a one-page brochure with photos, pricing, and availability.</li>
<li>Offer corporate accounts with consistent pricing, easy booking, and reliable service standards.</li>
<li>Ask to be included in their preferred housing vendor list.</li>
</ul>
<p>Companies that regularly bring in employees for projects or relocate staff frequently need a reliable housing partner — and will return repeatedly if you deliver.</p>
<h3 id="relocation-management-companies-rmcs">Relocation Management Companies (RMCs)</h3>
<p>Relocation management companies coordinate housing for corporate relocatees on behalf of employer clients. Becoming an approved vendor with one or more RMCs can generate consistent, institutionally backed bookings:</p>
<ul>
<li>Research RMCs operating in your market (Cartus, BGRS, Aires, and Graebel are among the largest).</li>
<li>Apply to be a preferred housing provider.</li>
<li>Understand their booking and billing processes, which typically involve corporate direct billing.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="travel-nurse-and-healthcare-staffing-agencies">Travel Nurse and Healthcare Staffing Agencies</h3>
<p>The travel healthcare market is one of the most reliable demand sources for corporate housing. Travel nurse agencies — which place registered nurses on 13-week contracts at hospitals across the country — are always sourcing housing for their workers.</p>
<ul>
<li>Contact staffing agencies directly (AMN Healthcare, Travel Nurse Across America, Cross Country Healthcare, and many others) about housing for their placed workers.</li>
<li>Hospitals often have housing boards or coordinators who maintain a vetted list of short-term housing options.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="online-corporate-housing-platforms">Online Corporate Housing Platforms</h3>
<p>List your property on platforms specifically serving the corporate and extended-stay market:</p>
<ul>
<li>Furnished Finder (dominant in the travel nurse and corporate market)</li>
<li>Corporate Housing by Owner (CHBO)</li>
<li>Oakwood (if you qualify for their network)</li>
<li>Airbnb for Business (the extended-stay version of the Airbnb platform)</li>
<li>Blueground</li>
</ul>
<p>For the full landscape of listing platforms, see our <a href="/blog/best-rental-listing-sites">best rental listing sites</a> guide.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="managing-corporate-housing-effectively">Managing Corporate Housing Effectively</h2>
<p>Corporate housing requires a different operational model than traditional long-term rentals:</p>
<p><strong>Turnover management</strong>: Higher turnover frequency means cleaning and inspection between each guest. Professional cleaning services are essential — budget $100–$250 per turnover depending on unit size.</p>
<p><strong>Maintenance responsiveness</strong>: Business clients have zero tolerance for unresolved maintenance issues. A broken appliance or internet outage in a traditional rental is an inconvenience; in corporate housing, it is a professional crisis. Commit to 2-hour response and same-day resolution for critical issues.</p>
<p><strong>Inventory management</strong>: Track furnishing condition and replace worn or damaged items proactively. Fraying towels, worn mattresses, and scratched furniture signal declining quality to discerning business clients.</p>
<p><strong>Communication</strong>: Corporate clients and the companies booking on their behalf expect professional communication — quick responses, clear invoices, formal documentation.</p>
<p><strong>Pricing strategy</strong>: Corporate housing is typically priced per day (with a monthly total), allowing you to flex rates for shorter stays (which should command higher daily rates) versus longer engagements.</p>
<p>Also see our guide on <a href="/blog/tenant-management-best-practices">tenant management best practices</a> for foundational principles that apply across all rental types.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="corporate-housing-and-your-broader-portfolio-strategy">Corporate Housing and Your Broader Portfolio Strategy</h2>
<p>Corporate housing works best as part of a deliberate portfolio strategy. Properties in corporate markets that can be repositioned as furnished executive rentals can significantly increase portfolio income — but require more active management than a standard unfurnished portfolio.</p>
<p>Many landlords operate a hybrid model: some properties as traditional long-term rentals for stable baseline income, others as corporate housing for income maximization. This approach balances revenue upside with operational demands.</p>
<p>For guidance on building a portfolio strategy that incorporates corporate housing, see our <a href="/blog/build-rental-property-portfolio">build rental property portfolio</a> guide and our overview of <a href="/blog/best-rental-property-types">best rental property types</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>The corporate housing market offers landlords a compelling path to premium rents and creditworthy institutional clients — but it requires deliberate preparation, proactive marketing, and an operational commitment to quality and responsiveness that exceeds what traditional rentals demand.</p>
<p>The landlords who succeed in this niche treat it as a hospitality business as much as a real estate business. They invest in furnishings, build corporate relationships, and deliver consistently on the professional standard their clients expect. The financial rewards — 50–150% above comparable unfurnished rents — justify the investment for those willing to make the commitment.</p>
