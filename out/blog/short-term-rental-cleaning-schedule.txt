27:T4489,<p>Cleanliness is the single most important factor in short-term rental reviews. Guests forgive small inconveniences — a noisy street, a compact layout, average furnishings — but they rarely forgive a dirty property. A single low score in cleanliness drags your overall rating down and costs you future bookings.</p>
<p>A well-designed <strong>short-term rental cleaning schedule</strong> is the operational backbone of a consistently high-rated STR. It ensures nothing is missed between guests, empowers your cleaning team to work efficiently, and protects the condition of your property over the long term.</p>
<p>This guide walks you through building a complete cleaning system: from the room-by-room checklist to timing frameworks, deep clean schedules, and best practices for managing your cleaning team.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="why-a-cleaning-schedule-not-just-a-checklist-matters">Why a Cleaning Schedule (Not Just a Checklist) Matters</h2>
<p>Many hosts have a cleaning checklist. Fewer have a true cleaning <em>schedule</em> — a structured system that accounts for:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Timing:</strong> How long each task takes; how to fit cleaning into the window between checkout and check-in</li>
<li><strong>Sequence:</strong> The most efficient order of operations to avoid re-contaminating clean areas</li>
<li><strong>Frequency:</strong> Which tasks happen every turnover vs. weekly vs. monthly vs. seasonally</li>
<li><strong>Assignment:</strong> Who is responsible for each task</li>
<li><strong>Quality control:</strong> How you verify the clean was done to standard</li>
</ul>
<p>Without a schedule, cleaning becomes reactive and inconsistent. A schedule transforms it into a repeatable, professional process.</p>
<p>For broader operational context, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="step-1-know-your-cleaning-window">Step 1: Know Your Cleaning Window</h2>
<p>Your cleaning window is the time between a guest's checkout and the next guest's check-in. This is your most important scheduling constraint.</p>
<p><strong>Common scenarios:</strong></p>
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Checkout Time</th>
<th>Check-in Time</th>
<th>Cleaning Window</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>10:00 AM</td>
<td>3:00 PM</td>
<td>5 hours</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>11:00 AM</td>
<td>3:00 PM</td>
<td>4 hours</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>11:00 AM</td>
<td>4:00 PM</td>
<td>5 hours</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>10:00 AM</td>
<td>2:00 PM</td>
<td>4 hours</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>For most properties, a 4–5 hour cleaning window is workable for one cleaner. Larger properties (3BR+) may require two cleaners working simultaneously to meet the window.</p>
<p><strong>Pro tip:</strong> Build one buffer day per week with no same-day turnovers. This gives your cleaning team breathing room and allows for deeper cleaning and restocking.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="step-2-design-the-cleaning-sequence">Step 2: Design the Cleaning Sequence</h2>
<p>Efficient cleaning follows a consistent sequence that prevents re-contaminating cleaned areas. The general principle: top to bottom, dry to wet, common areas to bedrooms.</p>
<p><strong>Recommended sequence:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Open windows and air out the property</strong> (first thing on arrival)</li>
<li><strong>Strip all beds</strong> and start the laundry</li>
<li><strong>Empty all trash</strong> throughout the property</li>
<li><strong>Clean the kitchen</strong> (counters, appliances, dishes, sink)</li>
<li><strong>Clean bathrooms</strong> (toilet, sink, shower/tub, mirror, floor)</li>
<li><strong>Wipe down all surfaces</strong> in common areas</li>
<li><strong>Vacuum all carpeted areas</strong></li>
<li><strong>Mop all hard floors</strong></li>
<li><strong>Make beds</strong> with fresh linens from the dryer</li>
<li><strong>Restock all supplies</strong> (toiletries, paper goods, coffee)</li>
<li><strong>Final walk-through</strong> for quality check</li>
<li><strong>Lock up and report completion</strong></li>
</ol>
<hr>
<h2 id="room-by-room-cleaning-checklist">Room-by-Room Cleaning Checklist</h2>
<h3 id="kitchen">Kitchen</h3>
<p><strong>Every Turnover:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Remove all food items left by guests</li>
<li>Clean inside microwave (wipe down walls and turntable)</li>
<li>Wipe stovetop, burner grates, and hood</li>
<li>Clean oven interior if used (check for spills)</li>
<li>Wipe countertops with disinfectant</li>
<li>Clean and disinfect sink; shine faucet</li>
<li>Run dishwasher and put away all clean dishes</li>
<li>Wipe cabinet exteriors and handles</li>
<li>Clean refrigerator exterior and handles; check interior for leftovers</li>
<li>Empty and reline trash and recycling bins</li>
<li>Mop kitchen floor</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weekly or As-Needed:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Clean inside refrigerator</li>
<li>Descale coffee maker</li>
<li>Wipe inside kitchen cabinets</li>
<li>Clean inside oven (full clean)</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3 id="bathrooms-all">Bathrooms (All)</h3>
<p><strong>Every Turnover:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Scrub and disinfect toilet bowl, seat, lid, and exterior</li>
<li>Wipe toilet base and floor around toilet</li>
<li>Scrub shower/tub walls and floor; remove soap scum</li>
<li>Clean shower door or curtain</li>
<li>Wipe sink, faucet, and countertop</li>
<li>Clean mirror (streak-free)</li>
<li>Restock toilet paper (at least 2–3 rolls per bathroom)</li>
<li>Replace used soap, shampoo, conditioner if below half</li>
<li>Hang fresh towels in a hotel-style fold</li>
<li>Empty trash and replace liner</li>
<li>Sweep and mop bathroom floor</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Monthly:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Descale showerhead</li>
<li>Clean grout lines in tile</li>
<li>Wipe exhaust fan vents</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3 id="bedrooms">Bedrooms</h3>
<p><strong>Every Turnover:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Strip all bed linens and pillowcases</li>
<li>Inspect mattress protector; replace if soiled</li>
<li>Make bed with fresh, wrinkle-free linens</li>
<li>Dust all surfaces (nightstands, dresser, lamps, headboard)</li>
<li>Clean mirrors</li>
<li>Check under beds for forgotten items</li>
<li>Wipe light switches and door handles</li>
<li>Inspect closet and hanging area; remove any guest items</li>
<li>Vacuum carpet or sweep/mop hardwood floors</li>
<li>Replace with fresh towels (if staged in bedrooms)</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3 id="living-and-common-areas">Living and Common Areas</h3>
<p><strong>Every Turnover:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Fluff and arrange couch cushions; check for crumbs</li>
<li>Wipe coffee table, side tables, and entertainment center</li>
<li>Dust shelves, décor items, and picture frames</li>
<li>Clean TV remote (high-touch surface — disinfect)</li>
<li>Wipe light switches and door handles throughout</li>
<li>Check for any guest belongings left behind</li>
<li>Vacuum all upholstered furniture</li>
<li>Vacuum carpets or sweep/mop floors</li>
<li>Clean sliding glass doors and windows (interior) if marked</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>As Needed:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Spot-clean upholstery stains</li>
<li>Steam clean carpets</li>
<li>Clean interior windows</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h3 id="entry-hallways-and-exterior">Entry, Hallways, and Exterior</h3>
<p><strong>Every Turnover:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Wipe down front door exterior and handle</li>
<li>Sweep porch or entry</li>
<li>Clean door mat (shake or vacuum)</li>
<li>Wipe mailbox if applicable</li>
<li>Check exterior lighting</li>
<li>Clear any trash or debris from yard, driveway, or patio</li>
<li>Clean outdoor furniture and table surfaces (if applicable)</li>
<li>Check and restock welcome basket if provided</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="step-3-deep-clean-schedule">Step 3: Deep Clean Schedule</h2>
<p>Your regular turnover cleaning maintains baseline standards. A <strong>deep clean schedule</strong> addresses the tasks that cannot be done at every turnover without extending the cleaning window.</p>
<p><strong>Monthly Deep Clean Tasks:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Clean inside the oven</li>
<li>Descale appliances (coffee maker, kettle, showerheads)</li>
<li>Vacuum under and behind furniture</li>
<li>Clean behind refrigerator</li>
<li>Wipe inside all kitchen and bathroom cabinets</li>
<li>Clean grout lines</li>
<li>Wash windows inside and out</li>
<li>Launder all blankets, duvet covers, and decorative pillows</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Quarterly Deep Clean Tasks:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Professionally steam clean carpets and upholstery</li>
<li>Clean all drains (kitchen, bathrooms)</li>
<li>Inspect and clean HVAC filters, vents, and returns</li>
<li>Clean ceiling fans (wipe blades)</li>
<li>Inspect caulk in bathrooms; recaulk if needed</li>
<li>Inventory and replace worn linens, towels, and supplies</li>
<li>Professional grout cleaning (tile bathrooms)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Annual or Seasonal Tasks:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Full exterior wash (power wash if applicable)</li>
<li>Window washing inside and out (professional if multi-story)</li>
<li>Gutter cleaning</li>
<li>Chimney inspection and cleaning (if fireplace)</li>
<li>Pool opening and closing procedures (seasonal)</li>
<li>Pest prevention treatment per <a href="https://www.epa.gov/safepestcontrol">EPA pest guidelines</a></li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="step-4-linen-management-system">Step 4: Linen Management System</h2>
<p>Linen management is one of the most time-consuming aspects of STR cleaning. An efficient system minimizes delays.</p>
<p><strong>The Two-Set System:</strong>
Keep two full sets of linens per bed. While Set A is on the bed for the current guests, Set B is freshly laundered and stored. At turnover, strip Set A, stage Set B immediately, and launder Set A for the next stay.</p>
<p><strong>The Three-Set System (Recommended for High Occupancy):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Set A: On the bed</li>
<li>Set B: Freshly washed and staged at the property or with the cleaner</li>
<li>Set C: Backup, stored off-site or in a linen closet</li>
</ul>
<p>With three sets, your cleaner never waits for laundry. They arrive with clean linens, make the beds immediately, and take the dirty linens to wash.</p>
<p><strong>Linen Care Best Practices:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Use white linens — they look crisp, are easy to bleach, and signal cleanliness to guests</li>
<li>Wash at 60C (140F) to sanitize per <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/hygiene/about/when-and-how-to-clean-surfaces.html">CDC cleaning guidelines</a></li>
<li>Use a commercial laundry service for high-volume hosts — the cost often justifies the time saved</li>
<li>Inspect linens for stains, tears, and pilling at every wash; retire worn items promptly</li>
</ul>
<hr>

<p>Keep a restocking supply kit at the property or in your cleaner's vehicle. When a supply drops below par, it gets restocked at the next turnover.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="managing-your-cleaning-team">Managing Your Cleaning Team</h2>
<h3 id="communicating-turnover-schedule">Communicating Turnover Schedule</h3>
<p>Your cleaning team needs accurate, timely information about when they are needed. Best practices:</p>
<ul>
<li>Use a shared Google Calendar or your property management software's task feature</li>
<li>Send automatic notifications when a booking is confirmed (or checkout is approaching)</li>
<li>Give cleaners at least 24 hours notice for turnovers; same-day turnovers should be the exception</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="providing-a-written-checklist">Providing a Written Checklist</h3>
<p>Never assume your cleaner knows exactly what you expect. Provide a printed or digital checklist for every property. Walk through it together on their first clean and solicit their feedback — experienced cleaners often have operational insight that improves your system.</p>
<p>Many hosts use apps like Properly, Breezeway, or TurnoverBnB to send digital checklists, track task completion with photo confirmation, and communicate with cleaning teams.</p>
<p>For more on how automation tools can manage your cleaning operations, see our <a href="/blog/airbnb-automation-tools">airbnb automation tools</a> guide.</p>
<h3 id="quality-control">Quality Control</h3>
<p>Trust but verify. Options for quality control:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Photo documentation:</strong> Ask cleaners to send photos of key areas (bed, bathroom, kitchen) after each clean</li>
<li><strong>Self-inspection:</strong> If you are local, do a walk-through after cleaning before the guest arrives for your most important stays</li>
<li><strong>Guest feedback loop:</strong> Monitor your cleanliness sub-rating religiously. A pattern of 4-star cleanliness ratings indicates a process issue</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="contingency-planning">Contingency Planning</h3>
<p>What happens when your cleaner calls in sick on a same-day turnover? Every host needs a contingency plan:</p>
<ul>
<li>A backup cleaner you have already trained and briefed</li>
<li>A professional cleaning service you can call on short notice</li>
<li>A network of fellow hosts who can recommend reliable cleaners</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>A professional cleaning schedule is what separates good STR hosts from great ones. Guests who arrive to a perfectly clean, carefully staged property are already primed to leave a 5-star review before they have unpacked their bags.</p>
<p>Build your schedule systematically — define your cleaning window, document the sequence, create room-by-room checklists, establish a deep clean calendar, and implement a supply management system. Then train your team to execute consistently, every time.</p>
<p>Cleanliness is not just about hygiene — it is a direct investment in your review profile, occupancy, and revenue. Treat it accordingly.</p>
<p>For more guidance on building a professional short-term rental operation, see our <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-property-management">vacation rental property management</a> and <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-turnover-checklist">vacation rental turnover checklist</a> guides.</p>
<p><strong>Cleaning Schedule Key Takeaways:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Define your cleaning window and plan cleaning around it</li>
<li>Follow a consistent sequence: top to bottom, dry to wet</li>
<li>Separate every-turnover tasks from monthly and quarterly deep cleans</li>
<li>Use a two or three-set linen system to eliminate wait time</li>
<li>Provide written checklists and photo confirmation to your cleaning team</li>
<li>Monitor your cleanliness sub-rating after every stay</li>
</ul>
