Your house rules are one of the most important documents in your short-term rental operation. They set guest expectations before arrival, protect your property during stays, and give you a documented reference point if something goes wrong.

Well-crafted house rules prevent misunderstandings, reduce damage, and help you maintain a 5-star review profile. Vague or missing rules, on the other hand, lead to noise complaints from neighbors, unexpected guests, property damage, and difficult review situations.

This guide provides a complete house rules template for short-term rentals that you can adapt for your property, along with guidance on how to communicate your rules effectively and enforce them professionally.

Why House Rules Matter for STR Hosts

Legal and Platform Protection

On Airbnb, your house rules are part of the booking agreement. The FTC advises consumers to review terms before purchasing. When a guest books, they agree to follow your rules. If a guest violates your rules and causes damage or creates problems, having documented rules strengthens any claim you make through Airbnb's resolution process or your insurance provider.

Guest Experience Management

Rules are not just about restriction — they are about setting the right expectations. A guest who knows exactly what to expect arrives in the right mindset. Clear rules about check-in time, parking, and WiFi usage eliminate the most common guest questions.

Neighbor and Community Relations

Many STR conflicts with neighbors and local governments stem from guest behavior — noise, parking, and parties. Strong house rules protect your relationships with the surrounding community.

For broader context on managing your STR professionally, see our short-term rental management guide.

How to Display Your House Rules

Airbnb's House Rules Section

Airbnb provides a dedicated house rules section in your listing settings. Use this to enter key rules that guests must accept before booking. Keep this list to your most essential rules — a wall of text discourages guests from reading anything.

Physical Guest Welcome Book

Place a printed or digital welcome book (using tools like Hostfully or Touch Stay) at the property. This can include your full rules plus property instructions, local recommendations, and emergency information.

Pre-Arrival Message

Include a brief rules summary in your automated pre-arrival message. Focus on the rules most relevant to arrival day: check-in time, parking, noise hours, and checkout procedures.

QR Code or Digital House Manual

Some hosts use a QR code displayed at the property that links to a digital version of their house rules and property manual. This keeps information accessible without printing.

Complete House Rules Template for Short-Term Rentals

Below is a comprehensive template. Customize sections for your property type, local context, and personal preferences. Items in brackets [ ] indicate where you should fill in your specific details.

Welcome and Introduction

Welcome to [Property Name]! We are thrilled to have you as our guest. To ensure a comfortable stay for you and a respectful experience for our neighbors, please review and follow these house rules. By booking, you agree to these terms.

For any questions or issues during your stay, contact us at [Phone/Email].

Check-In and Check-Out

Check-in time: [3:00 PM] or after Check-out time: By [11:00 AM]

Early check-in and late check-out are available when possible. Please message us at least 24 hours in advance to request — we will confirm based on the cleaning schedule.

Self check-in instructions will be sent to you [48 hours] before arrival. Please follow the instructions carefully to access the property.

Upon check-out, please:

  • Strip beds and leave linens in a pile (or in the location specified)
  • Load and start the dishwasher
  • Dispose of all trash in the [location of trash/recycling bins]
  • Return all keys to the lockbox (if applicable)
  • Close and lock all windows and doors

Maximum Occupancy

This property accommodates a maximum of [X] guests. The number of guests staying overnight must not exceed this limit. Day visitors are permitted up to [X] additional people with prior approval.

Exceeding the maximum occupancy without approval may result in immediate termination of the reservation without refund.

Noise and Quiet Hours

Quiet hours: [10:00 PM to 8:00 AM]

During quiet hours, please keep noise to a level that cannot be heard outside the property. This property is located in a [residential neighborhood / building with shared walls] and we ask all guests to be respectful of neighbors.

At all times, guests are expected to:

  • Keep outdoor noise at a moderate level
  • Avoid slamming doors
  • Use headphones for music and videos after quiet hours
  • Limit outdoor gatherings to [X] people

Noise violations reported by neighbors may result in immediate termination of the reservation.

No Parties or Events

Events, parties, and large gatherings are not permitted at this property. This includes:

  • Birthday or graduation parties
  • Wedding or rehearsal events
  • Corporate events
  • Any gathering with guests not registered in the booking

If you are looking for a space for an event, please inquire separately so we can assess whether we can accommodate your needs.

Pets

This property [is / is not] pet-friendly.

[If pet-friendly:]

  • Dogs are permitted (maximum [X] dogs, [weight limit if applicable])
  • Cats are [permitted / not permitted]
  • A pet fee of $[XX] per stay applies (charged separately)
  • Pets must not be left unattended in the property for more than [X] hours
  • Please clean up after pets in all outdoor areas
  • Pets are not permitted on furniture or beds unless specifically approved
  • Any pet damage will be charged to the guest

[If not pet-friendly:] No pets are permitted at this property, including emotional support animals that are not certified service animals. Review HUD's pet policy guidance carefully. Undisclosed pets may result in a fee and/or reservation cancellation.

Smoking

Smoking of any kind is strictly prohibited inside this property, including cigarettes, cigars, marijuana, and vaping.

Designated smoking areas (if applicable): [outdoor area description, e.g., "back patio only, dispose of butts in the ashtray provided"]

A cleaning fee of $[XXX] will be assessed if smoking inside the property is detected.

Parking

[Number] parking space(s) are available for guests:

  • [Description: driveway, dedicated lot, street parking]
  • [Any restrictions: no vehicles over certain height, no commercial vehicles]

Street parking rules: [note any permit requirements, street cleaning days, time restrictions]

Please do not block [neighbor's driveway / fire hydrant / other].

Trash and Recycling

Trash pickup is on [day(s) of the week]. Please put bins at the curb by [time] the night before.

Trash bins are located [location]. Recycling bins are located [location].

Please separate: [recycling categories — paper, glass, cans, plastics per local rules].

Do not leave trash bags outside the designated bins or in the yard.

Utilities and Appliances

To keep costs reasonable and the property well-maintained:

  • Turn off lights, fans, and TVs when leaving the property
  • Keep the thermostat set between [65°F and 75°F / 18°C and 24°C]
  • Do not open windows and run the AC/heat simultaneously
  • Report any appliance issues promptly rather than attempting repairs

Appliance instructions: [Specify any non-obvious appliances — how to use the TV inputs, fireplace safety, outdoor grill lighting procedure, etc.]

WiFi

WiFi Network: [Network Name] Password: [Password]

Please use the WiFi for personal use only. Do not use the network for illegal activity including piracy, torrenting, or downloading prohibited content.

Security Cameras

This property has [no interior cameras / exterior cameras only]. Exterior cameras are located at [front door, driveway, etc.] and are used for security purposes only.

[If using cameras, Airbnb requires disclosure in the listing. Be transparent with guests.]

Property Care

Guests are responsible for keeping the property in good condition during their stay. Please:

  • Report any damage or maintenance issues to us immediately
  • Do not rearrange or remove furniture from the property
  • Do not attach anything to walls with nails, tape, or adhesive without permission
  • Use [cutting boards, coasters, trivets] to protect surfaces
  • Keep the kitchen clean and wipe down surfaces after cooking
  • Do not leave food open that might attract insects or pests

Prohibited Activities

The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Illegal activity of any kind on the property
  • Weapons (unless locked in an approved storage device per local law)
  • Excessive use of fireworks
  • Open flames other than the designated fireplace or outdoor grill
  • Use of pools or hot tubs (if applicable) outside designated hours [X AM to X PM]
  • Commercial photography or filming without prior written approval

Pool and Hot Tub Rules (If Applicable)

  • Pool hours: [7:00 AM to 10:00 PM]
  • No diving — maximum depth is [X feet]. Review CPSC pool safety guidelines.
  • No glass in pool or hot tub areas
  • Children must be supervised by an adult at all times
  • Shower before entering the hot tub
  • Hot tub temperature is preset — do not adjust settings
  • Maximum [X] people in the hot tub at one time

Lost Keys and Lockouts

If you are locked out of the property, contact us at [phone number] and we will arrange access as quickly as possible. After-hours lockout service may incur a fee of $[XX].

Lost or unreturned keys will incur a replacement fee of $[XX].

Guest Responsibilities Summary

By booking this property, you agree to:

  1. Follow all house rules during your stay
  2. Respect neighbors and the surrounding community
  3. Pay for any damage caused during your stay beyond normal wear and tear
  4. Not exceed the maximum occupancy limit
  5. Leave the property in reasonable condition at checkout

Violations of these rules may result in immediate reservation termination without refund and/or additional charges through the booking platform.

Tips for Writing Effective House Rules

Keep Rules Firm but Friendly

Your tone matters. Rules written as commands ("Do not do X or you will be charged") feel adversarial. Rules framed with context ("We ask guests to keep noise down after 10pm to respect our neighbors") set the same expectation more warmly.

Explain the Why

Guests are more likely to follow rules when they understand the reason. "No smoking inside" is a rule. "No smoking inside — our bedding and furnishings absorb odors quickly and affect other guests' experiences" is a reason that builds understanding.

Do Not Overwhelm With Length

A 30-item list of rules discourages guests from reading any of them. Focus on the rules that actually matter for your property and market. Remove generic filler.

Revisit After Each Problem

Every time something goes wrong, ask whether a house rule could have prevented it. Add or refine rules based on real experience with your specific property and guest mix.

House Rules and Guest Screening

Your house rules work together with your guest screening process. Guests who do not fit your rules profile can be screened out before booking. See our guide on guest screening for short-term rentals for a full approach to identifying and accepting the right guests.

Enforcing Your House Rules

Having house rules is only half the equation. Enforcement — when necessary — requires:

Noise Monitoring Technology: Devices like Minut or NoiseAware detect decibel levels without recording audio. They alert you when noise exceeds a threshold so you can contact guests before a neighbor complaint is filed.

Security Deposits: While Airbnb has shifted away from traditional security deposits, you can use damage protection add-ons or require deposits through direct booking platforms.

Clear Communication: If a guest violates a rule, address it promptly but professionally. Message first, then call if necessary. Keep all communication within the Airbnb platform for documentation.

Airbnb's Resolution Center: For confirmed rule violations that result in damage or additional cleaning, submit a claim through Airbnb's resolution center promptly after checkout.

Conclusion

Your house rules are not bureaucratic fine print — they are a core tool for protecting your property, managing guest expectations, and maintaining your hosting reputation. A well-crafted set of rules reduces friction before it starts.

Use the template above as a starting point, customize it for your property, and integrate your rules into every touchpoint of the guest journey: the listing, the booking confirmation, the pre-arrival message, and the welcome book at the property.

For more on managing the full guest experience, explore our short-term rental management guide and airbnb property management tips.

House Rules Quick Reference:

  • Cover check-in/out, occupancy, noise, pets, smoking, parking, and utilities
  • Keep rules friendly in tone but firm in expectation
  • Display rules in the listing, pre-arrival message, and on-property welcome book
  • Revisit and update rules based on actual guest incidents
  • Pair rules with noise monitoring and screening for full protection
