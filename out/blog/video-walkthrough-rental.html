Creating Video Walkthroughs for Rental Properties

A video walkthrough gives prospective tenants something static photos simply cannot: the experience of moving through a space. When a renter watches a well-produced video tour of your property, they can assess room flow, ceiling heights, natural light at different times of day, and the overall feel of the home — all before scheduling a single showing.

For landlords and property managers, this translates into fewer wasted showings, more qualified applicants, and faster lease-up times. This guide walks you through everything you need to know to create effective rental property video walkthroughs, from equipment and planning to filming, editing, and distribution.

Why Video Walkthroughs Work

The data on video marketing in real estate is compelling. Listings with video receive dramatically more inquiries than those without, and tenants who view a video before scheduling a showing are far more likely to apply after the visit. Video reduces uncertainty — and uncertainty is what keeps prospective tenants from committing.

Key benefits of rental video walkthroughs:

  • Higher inquiry rates — Video listings stand out in crowded search results and generate more clicks
  • Pre-qualified leads — Tenants who've watched a full video tour are more serious and better informed
  • Fewer no-shows — Tenants who've seen the property via video have more realistic expectations and fewer reasons to cancel
  • Remote tenant access — Critical for corporate relocation, military, and out-of-state tenants who can't visit in person. HUD rental assistance programs connect relocating tenants with housing.
  • Reusable marketing asset — A high-quality video can be reused every time the property turns over

Video is one component of a strong rental property marketing strategies approach, best combined with professional photography and compelling written descriptions.

Equipment You Need

You don't need a Hollywood budget to produce an effective rental video walkthrough. Many successful landlords film entirely on a smartphone. What matters more than equipment is technique and planning.

Minimum Equipment Setup

  • Smartphone with a good camera — iPhone 14 or newer, Google Pixel, or Samsung Galaxy all produce excellent video
  • Gimbal or stabilizer — Essential for smooth movement; a handheld gimbal costs $80-150 and eliminates shaky footage
  • External microphone — If you're narrating, a clip-on lavalier mic dramatically improves audio quality
  • Tripod — For static shots of individual rooms
  • Portable LED light panel — One or two panels ($30-60 each) help in darker rooms and basements

Upgraded Equipment (for multiple properties)

  • Mirrorless camera or DSLR — Produces more cinematic footage with better low-light performance
  • Wide-angle lens — Essential for small rooms; makes spaces look larger and more inviting
  • Drone — For capturing the exterior, yard, neighborhood, and building (check FAA drone regulations before flying)

Planning Your Video Walkthrough

Poor planning is the number one reason rental videos fall flat. Before you press record, take 20-30 minutes to plan the shoot.

Step 1: Clean and Stage the Property

A video amplifies everything — including clutter, scuffed walls, and dated fixtures. Before filming:

  • Deep clean every room, including baseboards and windows
  • Remove all personal items if the unit is occupied
  • Stage each room with simple, neutral decor
  • Ensure all light bulbs are working and matching in color temperature
  • Open all blinds and curtains for maximum natural light

Review our tips on rental property staging for a complete pre-shoot checklist.

Step 2: Plan Your Route

Walk through the property once before filming and map out your camera path. A logical route mimics how a tenant would actually tour the home:

  1. Exterior — Building front, entrance, parking
  2. Entry/foyer
  3. Living room
  4. Dining area
  5. Kitchen
  6. Hallways and storage
  7. Bedrooms (largest first)
  8. Bathrooms
  9. Utility areas (laundry, mechanical room)
  10. Outdoor spaces (yard, balcony, patio)
  11. Building amenities (lobby, gym, pool)

Step 3: Decide on Narration vs. Music

Video walkthroughs work with either narration or background music. Each has advantages:

Format Best For
Narrated Explaining features tenants can't see visually (neighborhood, utility costs, lease terms)
Music only Letting the property speak for itself; avoids landlord nervousness on camera
On-screen text overlay Hybrid approach; highlights key features without narration

Filming Techniques for Professional Results

Shoot in Landscape Mode

Always film horizontally (landscape orientation). Vertical video works for Instagram Stories but looks unprofessional on YouTube, Zillow, and most listing platforms. Set your phone to landscape before you start.

Use Slow, Deliberate Movements

The biggest mistake first-time rental videographers make is moving the camera too fast. Walk slowly — about half your normal pace. Pan across rooms slowly. Give each space time to breathe on screen. Rushed footage feels chaotic and exhausting to watch.

Start Wide, Move Closer

For each room, begin with a wide establishing shot from the doorway, then slowly move into the room to highlight specific features. This mimics how a person naturally enters and experiences a space.

Use Static Shots for Key Features

Not every shot needs to be moving. Pause on:

  • Kitchen countertops and appliances
  • Bathroom vanities and fixtures
  • Built-in storage and closets
  • Unique architectural details (vaulted ceilings, exposed brick, built-ins)
  • Views from windows

Film at the Right Time of Day

Natural light dramatically affects how a property looks on video. For most units:

  • Morning light suits east-facing rooms
  • Afternoon light suits west-facing rooms
  • Overcast days actually produce the most even, flattering light with no harsh shadows

Avoid filming in direct harsh sunlight, which creates blown-out windows and deep shadows.

Editing Your Rental Video Walkthrough

You don't need to be a professional editor to produce a polished video. Several free and low-cost tools make editing accessible to anyone.

Recommended Editing Tools

  • iMovie (free, Mac/iOS) — Simple, intuitive, and produces professional results
  • CapCut (free, iOS/Android) — Excellent for adding text overlays and transitions
  • DaVinci Resolve (free, desktop) — Professional-grade tool with a learning curve but powerful output
  • Adobe Premiere Rush (subscription) — Cross-platform, excellent for multi-property landlords

Editing Best Practices

  • Keep it under 3-4 minutes — Attention spans are short; hit every room without lingering too long
  • Add a title card — Open with the property address or a key feature ("3BR/2BA in the Heart of Midtown")
  • Include text overlays — Highlight key specs (bed/bath count, square footage, price, available date)
  • Use consistent transitions — Simple cuts or smooth fades; avoid flashy transitions that distract
  • End with a clear call to action — "Schedule a Showing at [phone/website]" or "Apply Now at [URL]"
  • Color correct if needed — Boost brightness slightly and reduce yellow tones for a clean, fresh look

Where to Publish and Share Your Video

A video walkthrough is only effective if the right people see it. Distribute it across every channel in your marketing mix.

Rental Listing Platforms

  • Zillow — Accepts embedded video links (YouTube, Vimeo) and has its own 3D Home video tool
  • Apartments.com — Allows video uploads on enhanced listings
  • Facebook Marketplace — Video listings stand out significantly in the feed
  • Realtor.com — Supports video on premium listings

Video Hosting Platforms

Upload your video to YouTube and/or Vimeo first, then share the link across platforms. The FTC advertising guidelines apply to all rental marketing content. YouTube videos are indexed by Google, meaning your property video can actually appear in Google search results — a major bonus for rental listing SEO.

Social Media

Share clips or the full video on:

  • Instagram Reels (15-60 second highlights work best)
  • Facebook (full video or a 60-second teaser with a link to the full version)
  • TikTok (if your target market skews younger)
  • LinkedIn (for corporate or executive rentals)

See our guide on social media marketing for rental properties for platform-specific content strategies.

Your Property Management Website

Embed the video directly on your property listing page. Videos on landing pages increase the time visitors spend on the page, which signals quality to Google and improves your SEO performance.

Video Walkthrough vs. 3D Virtual Tour

Video walkthroughs and 3D virtual tours serve similar purposes but have distinct differences. Understanding when to use each helps you allocate your marketing budget effectively.

Feature Video Walkthrough 3D Virtual Tour
Production cost Low ($0 with smartphone) Moderate ($100-400 with Matterport)
Viewer experience Passive (watches a fixed path) Interactive (explores freely)
Time investment 30-90 min filming + editing 1-2 hrs scanning
Platform compatibility Universal Requires platform support
Best for All property types Larger, more complex units

For properties that warrant the investment, combining a video walkthrough with a 3D virtual tour creates the most complete remote viewing experience. See our dedicated guide on 3D virtual tours for rental properties for a full comparison of tools and pricing.

Quick Checklist: Before You Film

  • Property is clean, staged, and clutter-free
  • All light bulbs working, warm-white preferred
  • Blinds and curtains open for natural light
  • Camera charged and storage cleared
  • Gimbal charged and calibrated
  • Route planned and practiced once
  • Thermostat set (no HVAC noise during narration)
  • Pets and people removed from the unit

Conclusion

Video walkthroughs are no longer a luxury reserved for high-end or luxury rental listings. They've become a standard expectation in competitive rental markets, and landlords who adopt them early gain a significant advantage in attracting serious, qualified tenants.

The investment is modest — often just a few hours of your time and a smartphone. The return is fewer vacancy days, better-qualified applicants, and a reusable marketing asset that works for every future turn.

Pair your video walkthrough strategy with professional photography, compelling written descriptions, and a strong multi-platform distribution plan as part of your complete rental property marketing strategies approach.
