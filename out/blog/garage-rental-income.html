An unused garage or parking space is one of the most overlooked income sources in real estate. Whether you have excess parking at a multifamily property, a detached garage you're not using, or even a driveway in a high-demand urban area, there's likely money sitting idle.

Garage rental income can add hundreds to thousands of dollars annually to your rental property revenue with minimal ongoing management. Here's how to do it right.

What Can You Rent a Garage For?

The use case significantly affects both pricing and tenant dynamics:

1. Vehicle Parking

The most common garage rental use. Options include:

  • Single car parking (enclosed garage)
  • Open-air parking space or driveway spot
  • RV/boat/motorcycle parking
  • Overnight or monthly parking in urban areas

2. Storage Space

Many tenants or local residents need extra storage beyond what their units provide:

  • Furniture storage
  • Seasonal items (holiday decorations, winter gear)
  • Business inventory
  • General overflow storage

3. Workshop or Hobby Space

Some tenants or locals will pay a premium for a clean, functional workspace:

  • Woodworking or metalworking shop
  • Auto repair space
  • Artist studio
  • Home gym setup

4. Commercial/Business Use

In commercial or mixed-use areas, garage space can be rented to small businesses:

  • Small contractor/trades operations
  • E-commerce fulfillment
  • Equipment storage

Garage Rental Income: Pricing Guide by Market

Urban parking (cities with parking scarcity):

  • Covered, enclosed garage spot in NYC/SF/Chicago/Boston: $200–$600+/month
  • Uncovered parking space in same markets: $100–$350/month
  • Monthly parking near transit hub: $75–$250/month

Suburban parking:

  • Enclosed single-car garage: $80–$200/month
  • Outdoor parking/driveway space: $40–$100/month
  • RV/boat storage spot (if large enough): $75–$200/month depending on size

Storage use:

  • Standard one-car garage for storage: $75–$175/month
  • Two-car garage for storage: $125–$300/month
  • Climate-controlled storage garage: 20–30% premium over standard

Workshop/hobby space:

  • Basic garage workshop: $150–$400/month
  • Premium workshop with 240V outlet, good lighting, insulation: $250–$600/month

Compare your local rates by searching Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, and SpotHero in your area.

Calculating Your Annual Garage Rental Income

Example 1: Urban parking, Chicago

  • Enclosed parking spot: $225/month
  • Vacancy (parking spaces tend to have low vacancy once occupied): 5%
  • Annual income: $225 × 12 × 0.95 = $2,565/year

Example 2: Suburban two-car garage split into storage

  • Storage rental: $150/month
  • Vacancy: 8%
  • Annual income: $150 × 12 × 0.92 = $1,656/year

Example 3: Workshop space

  • Monthly rent: $350/month
  • Vacancy: 10%
  • Annual income: $350 × 12 × 0.90 = $3,780/year

These amounts seem modest individually, but add them to your property's NOI and the impact on value at a 6.5% cap rate is significant:

  • $2,565/year ÷ 0.065 = $39,461 in additional property value
  • $3,780/year ÷ 0.065 = $58,154 in additional property value

This is genuine value creation from space that was otherwise generating nothing. For more on this principle, see our guide on net operating income and how ancillary income affects property value.

Legal and Zoning Considerations

Before renting out garage space, check the following:

Zoning Laws

Most residential zones allow parking rentals to tenants in the building. Renting to the general public (non-tenants) may require a different zoning classification or permit. Check Census housing data for local market context. Commercial use (workshop, business storage) may also trigger zoning issues in residential zones.

HOA and Condo Rules

If your property is in an HOA, read the CC&Rs carefully. Many HOAs restrict rentals of common parking areas or garages to non-residents.

Insurance

Contact your insurance carrier before renting garage space for commercial use or workshops. Standard residential landlord policies may not cover injuries that occur in workshop spaces or commercial storage situations. A commercial rider or umbrella policy may be needed.

Building Code

For workshop use, ensure adequate electrical capacity, ventilation, and any required fire separation from the main structure. Garages rented as living spaces require full habitability improvements including egress windows, insulation, HVAC, and bathroom access—check local ADU regulations.

Business Use Considerations

If a tenant uses your garage for business operations, consider whether you need a commercial lease rather than a standard residential addendum.

How to Rent Out Your Garage

Step 1: Determine Use and Set Pricing

Decide what use you're permitting (parking, storage, workshop) and price based on local market research.

Step 2: Prepare the Space

For storage/parking: clean thoroughly, check for structural issues, ensure good lighting and secure lock. For workshop: add 240V outlet if needed, improve lighting, ensure ventilation.

Step 3: Market the Space

Platforms that work well for garage rentals:

  • Craigslist: Best for local general audience
  • Facebook Marketplace: Strong for local community
  • Neighbor.com: Specifically for storage space rentals
  • SpotHero / ParkWhiz: Specifically for parking
  • Your existing tenant pool: Always offer to existing tenants first—easiest transaction

Step 4: Create a Simple Lease Addendum

For tenants already renting your main unit, add a parking/storage addendum to their existing lease. For outside renters, use a simple month-to-month parking or storage agreement.

Key terms to include:

  • Permitted use (parking only / storage only / specific items allowed)
  • Monthly rental amount and payment due date
  • Access terms (key, code, or PIN)
  • Landlord access rights for maintenance/inspection
  • Prohibited items (flammable materials, hazardous chemicals, etc.)
  • Liability disclaimer (tenant stores at own risk)
  • Termination provisions (typically 30-day notice either party)

Step 5: Screen Tenants for Garage Use

For large storage or workshop tenants (especially commercial), basic screening is worthwhile:

  • Verify identity
  • Check references for commercial tenants
  • Review what items/equipment they plan to store

Maximizing Garage Rental Income

Upgrade to Command a Premium

Small improvements can significantly increase rental rates:

  • Good LED lighting: $200–$500 investment → $25–$50/month premium
  • Insulated garage door: $800–$1,500 investment → worth it for climate-controlled storage or workshop premium
  • Epoxy floor coating: $1,000–$2,500 investment → strong appeal for workshop and nice storage tenants
  • Security camera + keypad entry: $300–$600 investment → substantial appeal and justifies premium pricing

Offer Monthly vs. Daily Parking

If you're in an urban area near offices, restaurants, or entertainment, consider whether daily or evening parking might generate more than monthly rental. A $15/day parking space used 15 days/month generates $225—same as a $225/month monthly tenant, but with more flexibility and potentially higher peak-day revenue.

Rent to Multiple Tenants

A large 2-car garage can sometimes be partitioned for two separate tenants paying separate fees. $150/month × 2 = $300/month vs. $200/month for one tenant.

Bundle With Unit Rental at a Premium

Offering a parking space as a bundle with the main unit (at a premium for "parking included") can be more valuable than separating them. Tenants who need parking and can get it bundled often pay a higher total premium than if parking were offered separately.

Garage Rental Income vs. ADU Conversion

An important strategic decision: convert the garage to an ADU for maximum income, or rent it as-is for lower income with minimal investment?

Option Monthly Income Upfront Cost Cash-on-Cash Return
Garage parking rental $100–$200 $0–$500 Very high (low cost)
Garage storage rental $100–$200 $200–$1,000 High
Garage ADU conversion $800–$2,500 $60,000–$150,000 8–15%

If your market supports strong ADU rents and construction costs are reasonable, ADU conversion provides dramatically higher absolute income. If your market has modest rents, renting the garage as-is may provide a better return on your marginal dollar.

Learn more in our ADU rental income guide to run the full comparison for your specific market.

Taxes on Garage Rental Income

Garage rental income is taxable and must be reported as rental income per IRS rules. Keep records of:

  • Monthly rental income received
  • Maintenance and improvement costs
  • Proportion of property taxes and insurance attributable to the garage
  • Depreciation on the garage structure (if separately tracked)

If the garage is an appurtenant part of your rental property, expenses are typically handled as part of your overall rental property deductions. See our rental property tax deductions guide for the complete framework.

Summary

Garage rental income is one of the simplest, highest-ROI income strategies available to property owners. You're monetizing space that already exists—no construction, minimal capital, just a simple lease agreement and basic marketing.

Start by assessing your garage or parking assets, research comparable rates in your market, and implement a simple month-to-month agreement. Even $150/month in additional garage income adds $27,692 in property value at a 6.5% cap rate.
