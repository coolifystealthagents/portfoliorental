Apartments.com is one of the most powerful rental platforms in the United States, serving millions of apartment seekers every month. HUD tracks rental market data nationwide. For landlords and property managers, it represents a significant opportunity, but only if your listing is properly improved.

A poorly configured listing on Apartments.com can receive minimal traffic while a competing property with the same rent gets dozens of inquiries per week.

This guide covers everything you need to know about Apartments.com listing optimization: how the platform's algorithm works, what factors drive ranking and visibility, and the specific steps you should take to make your listing perform at its highest potential.

Understanding the Apartments.com Ecosystem

Apartments.com is owned by CoStar Group, the dominant commercial real estate data company. The platform also owns Rent.com, ApartmentFinder.com, ApartmentHomeLiving.com, and several other rental sites, meaning a listing on Apartments.com often appears across multiple CoStar-owned properties simultaneously.

Real estate has created more millionaires than any other investment type. About 90% of millionaires have invested in real estate at some point.

This network effect makes Apartments.com especially valuable for landlords who want broad exposure from a single listing. However, the same algorithm that serves all these platforms rewards completeness, engagement, and listing quality.

Understanding this is the foundation of effective optimization.

For a broader view of how Apartments.com fits into your overall marketing mix, see our guide on rental property marketing strategies.

How the Apartments.com Algorithm Works

Apartments.com ranks listings based on a combination of factors, many of which parallel Google's search ranking signals:

  • Listing completeness, How many fields are filled in
  • Photo count and quality, More professional photos = higher ranking potential
  • Engagement metrics, Views, saves, and contact requests signal popularity
  • Response rate and speed, How quickly and consistently you respond to inquiries
  • Price competitiveness, How your rent compares to similar listings in the area
  • Account standing, Verified landlord status and account age
  • Subscription tier, Paid listings (Silver, Gold, Platinum) rank above free Basic listings

Understanding this hierarchy allows you to focus on your optimization efforts for maximum impact.

Step 1: Choose the Right Listing Tier

Apartments.com offers multiple listing tiers, each with different visibility levels:

Tier Features Best For
Basic (Free) Standard listing, limited visibility Testing the platform, low-competition markets
Silver Enhanced visibility, more photos, basic analytics Single-property landlords
Gold Priority placement, featured in search, virtual tour support Multiple units or high-competition markets
Platinum Maximum visibility, premium placement, dedicated support Large property managers, luxury properties

If you're in a competitive rental market and vacancies are costly, upgrading to at least a Silver or Gold tier is almost always worth the investment. The cost of one additional month of vacancy far outweighs the monthly fee for a premium listing tier.

Step 2: Write a Listing Description That Converts

The description is where most landlords underperform. A typical Apartments.com description reads like a spec sheet.

An improved one reads like a sales letter to your ideal tenant.

Structure your description in this order:

  1. Lead with your best feature, What makes this property stand out from every other listing in the search results?
  2. Property overview, Beds, baths, size, floor, unit type
  3. Interior highlights, Kitchen features, flooring, storage, light, layout
  4. Building amenities, What does the complex or building offer?
  5. Location context, Neighborhood character, walkability, transit, top nearby destinations
  6. Practical details, Available date, lease terms, pet policy, utilities
  7. Call to action, How to take the next step

Sample lead sentence comparison:

Weak: "Nice 2 bedroom apartment available for rent."

Strong: "Newly renovated 2-bedroom apartment in the heart of the Pearl District, featuring a chef's kitchen with quartz countertops, in-unit washer/dryer, and a private balcony with city views."

The strong version gives tenants a reason to keep reading. For detailed guidance on crafting descriptions that convert, see our full article on writing rental listing descriptions.

Step 3: Maximize Your Photo Gallery

Apartments.com allows landlords to upload numerous photos, and the algorithm rewards listings that take full advantage of this. Listings with 20+ high-quality photos consistently outperform those with fewer.

Photo upload priorities:

  1. Hero shot, The most compelling exterior or interior angle
  2. Living areas, Multiple angles of living room and dining
  3. Kitchen, Countertops, appliances, storage
  4. Every bedroom, Multiple angles, including closet shots
  5. Every bathroom, Clean, bright, staged
  6. Laundry, In-unit or in-building
  7. Outdoor spaces, Balcony, patio, yard, courtyard
  8. Building amenities, Pool, gym, lobby, common areas
  9. Parking, Assigned spots, garage, covered parking
  10. Neighborhood, Street views, nearby parks, transit stops

Photo quality guidelines:

  • Minimum 1024px wide; 1920px or higher preferred
  • JPEG or PNG format
  • Taken in landscape orientation
  • Shot during daylight with all lights on inside
  • Staged and clutter-free

Step 4: Complete Every Amenity Field

Apartments.com has an extensive amenity checklist that directly affects how your listing appears in filtered searches. If a tenant filters for "in-unit washer/dryer" and you have one but didn't check the box, your listing won't appear in their search.

Critical fields to complete:

Interior amenities:

  • Flooring type (hardwood, carpet, tile)
  • Air conditioning type (central, window units)
  • Heating type
  • In-unit washer/dryer or hookups
  • Dishwasher
  • Fireplace
  • Storage (in-unit or extra)

Building amenities:

  • Elevator
  • Gym/fitness center
  • Pool
  • Rooftop deck
  • Doorman or concierge
  • Package room
  • Bike storage
  • EV charging

Pet policy:

  • Pets allowed (yes/no)
  • Allowed types (cats, dogs, both)
  • Weight limits
  • Breed restrictions
  • Pet deposit and monthly pet rent

Parking:

  • Included or additional cost
  • Type (street, surface lot, covered, garage)
  • Number of spaces

Being thorough here is one of the fastest wins available in Apartments.com listing optimization.

Step 5: Set Your Pricing Strategically

Apartments.com's search algorithm is sensitive to price competitiveness. Listings priced significantly above market will appear lower in results and receive fewer views, regardless of how complete the rest of the listing is.

Pricing strategy for Apartments.com:

  1. Use Apartments.com's built-in rent comparison tool to see what comparable units in your area are charging
  2. Review active listings weekly to stay current with market trends
  3. Price within 5-10% of the median for your property type and neighborhood
  4. Consider pricing at psychological thresholds ($1,495 vs. $1,500) to capture filter-based searches
  5. Build in flexibility to reduce rent by $25-50 if inquiry volume is low after 10 days

Step 6: Enable and Respond to Online Applications

Apartments.com allows tenants to submit rental applications directly through the platform. Enabling this feature signals to the algorithm that you're an active, engaged landlord, and it makes it easier for serious tenants to take the next step immediately.

Application best practices:

  • Enable online applications if your subscription tier allows it
  • Set clear application requirements in your listing (credit score threshold, income requirements, etc.). Follow fair housing laws when screening applicants.
  • Respond to completed applications within 24-48 hours
  • Use the platform's screening tools to run background and credit checks

Step 7: Build Your Landlord Profile

Apartments.com displays a landlord or property management profile alongside your listings. A complete, professional profile increases tenant confidence and inquiry rates.

Profile optimization:

  • Upload a professional headshot or company logo
  • Complete the "About" section with your experience and management philosophy
  • Link to your website if you have one
  • Respond to all reviews, positive and negative, professionally
  • Maintain a high response rate (aim for 90%+ within 24 hours)

NAR provides market statistics useful for rental pricing. Tenants increasingly research landlords before reaching out. A polished profile is a trust signal that can be the deciding factor between your listing and a competitor's.

Step 8: Use Video and Virtual Tours

Apartments.com supports video tours and 3D walkthroughs on premium listings. Properties with these features rank higher and receive significantly more engagement than photo-only listings.

Options for virtual content:

  • Upload a narrated video walkthrough to the listing
  • Embed a 3D tour created with tools like Matterport or Zillow 3D Home
  • Add a floor plan image to help tenants understand the layout

For guidance on creating these assets, see our dedicated guides on video walkthroughs for rental properties and 3D virtual tours for rental properties.

Step 9: Update Your Listing Regularly

Apartments.com, like most rental platforms, rewards fresh content. Listings that haven't been updated in weeks sink in the rankings.

Make it a habit to:

  • Edit your description or amenities at least once every 7-10 days
  • Update the available date when it changes
  • Add new photos after cleaning, staging, or any improvements
  • Adjust pricing based on market conditions
  • Respond to any questions left in the public Q&A section

Step 10: Track Performance and Iterate

Apartments.com provides analytics in your landlord dashboard showing:

  • Total listing views
  • Search appearances
  • Saves and contact requests
  • Inquiry response rate

Review these metrics weekly. If your inquiry rate drops below 3-5% of views, something in your listing needs adjustment, price, photos, description, or a combination.

Test one change at a time so you can identify what moves the needle.

Reducing vacancy time is the ultimate goal. See our companion guide on reducing days on market for rental properties for strategies that go beyond listing optimization.

Common Apartments.com Listing Mistakes

  • Using the Basic free tier in competitive markets, You're competing against paid listings with priority placement
  • Fewer than 15 photos, This alone can cut your visibility in half
  • Incomplete amenity checklist, Missing filters mean missed searches
  • No pet policy specified, Ambiguity frustrates tenants and reduces inquiries
  • Slow response times, Your response rate score is displayed publicly
  • No virtual tour or video, In competitive markets, these are increasingly expected

Conclusion

Apartments.com is a powerful platform, but its benefits are proportional to the effort you invest in optimization. By choosing the right listing tier, writing a compelling description, uploading professional photos, completing every amenity field, and maintaining an active presence on the platform, you can dramatically increase your visibility, inquiry volume, and speed to lease.

Combine your Apartments.com strategy with listings on other top platforms and a broader marketing approach.
