Cleanliness is the single most important factor in short-term rental reviews. Guests forgive small inconveniences — a noisy street, a compact layout, average furnishings — but they rarely forgive a dirty property. A single low score in cleanliness drags your overall rating down and costs you future bookings.

A well-designed short-term rental cleaning schedule is the operational backbone of a consistently high-rated STR. It ensures nothing is missed between guests, empowers your cleaning team to work efficiently, and protects the condition of your property over the long term.

This guide walks you through building a complete cleaning system: from the room-by-room checklist to timing frameworks, deep clean schedules, and best practices for managing your cleaning team.

Why a Cleaning Schedule (Not Just a Checklist) Matters

Many hosts have a cleaning checklist. Fewer have a true cleaning schedule — a structured system that accounts for:

  • Timing: How long each task takes; how to fit cleaning into the window between checkout and check-in
  • Sequence: The most efficient order of operations to avoid re-contaminating clean areas
  • Frequency: Which tasks happen every turnover vs. weekly vs. monthly vs. seasonally
  • Assignment: Who is responsible for each task
  • Quality control: How you verify the clean was done to standard

Without a schedule, cleaning becomes reactive and inconsistent. A schedule transforms it into a repeatable, professional process.

Step 1: Know Your Cleaning Window

Your cleaning window is the time between a guest's checkout and the next guest's check-in. This is your most important scheduling constraint.

Common scenarios:

Checkout Time Check-in Time Cleaning Window
10:00 AM 3:00 PM 5 hours
11:00 AM 3:00 PM 4 hours
11:00 AM 4:00 PM 5 hours
10:00 AM 2:00 PM 4 hours

For most properties, a 4–5 hour cleaning window is workable for one cleaner. Larger properties (3BR+) may require two cleaners working simultaneously to meet the window.

Pro tip: Build one buffer day per week with no same-day turnovers. This gives your cleaning team breathing room and allows for deeper cleaning and restocking.

Step 2: Design the Cleaning Sequence

Efficient cleaning follows a consistent sequence that prevents re-contaminating cleaned areas. The general principle: top to bottom, dry to wet, common areas to bedrooms.

Recommended sequence:

  1. Open windows and air out the property (first thing on arrival)
  2. Strip all beds and start the laundry
  3. Empty all trash throughout the property
  4. Clean the kitchen (counters, appliances, dishes, sink)
  5. Clean bathrooms (toilet, sink, shower/tub, mirror, floor)
  6. Wipe down all surfaces in common areas
  7. Vacuum all carpeted areas
  8. Mop all hard floors
  9. Make beds with fresh linens from the dryer
  10. Restock all supplies (toiletries, paper goods, coffee)
  11. Final walk-through for quality check
  12. Lock up and report completion

Room-by-Room Cleaning Checklist

Kitchen

Every Turnover:

  • Remove all food items left by guests
  • Clean inside microwave (wipe down walls and turntable)
  • Wipe stovetop, burner grates, and hood
  • Clean oven interior if used (check for spills)
  • Wipe countertops with disinfectant
  • Clean and disinfect sink; shine faucet
  • Run dishwasher and put away all clean dishes
  • Wipe cabinet exteriors and handles
  • Clean refrigerator exterior and handles; check interior for leftovers
  • Empty and reline trash and recycling bins
  • Mop kitchen floor

Weekly or As-Needed:

  • Clean inside refrigerator
  • Descale coffee maker
  • Wipe inside kitchen cabinets
  • Clean inside oven (full clean)

Bathrooms (All)

Every Turnover:

  • Scrub and disinfect toilet bowl, seat, lid, and exterior
  • Wipe toilet base and floor around toilet
  • Scrub shower/tub walls and floor; remove soap scum
  • Clean shower door or curtain
  • Wipe sink, faucet, and countertop
  • Clean mirror (streak-free)
  • Restock toilet paper (at least 2–3 rolls per bathroom)
  • Replace used soap, shampoo, conditioner if below half
  • Hang fresh towels in a hotel-style fold
  • Empty trash and replace liner
  • Sweep and mop bathroom floor

Monthly:

  • Descale showerhead
  • Clean grout lines in tile
  • Wipe exhaust fan vents

Bedrooms

Every Turnover:

  • Strip all bed linens and pillowcases
  • Inspect mattress protector; replace if soiled
  • Make bed with fresh, wrinkle-free linens
  • Dust all surfaces (nightstands, dresser, lamps, headboard)
  • Clean mirrors
  • Check under beds for forgotten items
  • Wipe light switches and door handles
  • Inspect closet and hanging area; remove any guest items
  • Vacuum carpet or sweep/mop hardwood floors
  • Replace with fresh towels (if staged in bedrooms)

Living and Common Areas

Every Turnover:

  • Fluff and arrange couch cushions; check for crumbs
  • Wipe coffee table, side tables, and entertainment center
  • Dust shelves, décor items, and picture frames
  • Clean TV remote (high-touch surface — disinfect)
  • Wipe light switches and door handles throughout
  • Check for any guest belongings left behind
  • Vacuum all upholstered furniture
  • Vacuum carpets or sweep/mop floors
  • Clean sliding glass doors and windows (interior) if marked

As Needed:

  • Spot-clean upholstery stains
  • Steam clean carpets
  • Clean interior windows

Entry, Hallways, and Exterior

Every Turnover:

  • Wipe down front door exterior and handle
  • Sweep porch or entry
  • Clean door mat (shake or vacuum)
  • Wipe mailbox if applicable
  • Check exterior lighting
  • Clear any trash or debris from yard, driveway, or patio
  • Clean outdoor furniture and table surfaces (if applicable)
  • Check and restock welcome basket if provided

Step 3: Deep Clean Schedule

Your regular turnover cleaning maintains baseline standards. A deep clean schedule addresses the tasks that cannot be done at every turnover without extending the cleaning window.

Monthly Deep Clean Tasks:

  • Clean inside the oven
  • Descale appliances (coffee maker, kettle, showerheads)
  • Vacuum under and behind furniture
  • Clean behind refrigerator
  • Wipe inside all kitchen and bathroom cabinets
  • Clean grout lines
  • Wash windows inside and out
  • Launder all blankets, duvet covers, and decorative pillows

Quarterly Deep Clean Tasks:

  • Professionally steam clean carpets and upholstery
  • Clean all drains (kitchen, bathrooms)
  • Inspect and clean HVAC filters, vents, and returns
  • Clean ceiling fans (wipe blades)
  • Inspect caulk in bathrooms; recaulk if needed
  • Inventory and replace worn linens, towels, and supplies
  • Professional grout cleaning (tile bathrooms)

Annual or Seasonal Tasks:

  • Full exterior wash (power wash if applicable)
  • Window washing inside and out (professional if multi-story)
  • Gutter cleaning
  • Chimney inspection and cleaning (if fireplace)
  • Pool opening and closing procedures (seasonal)
  • Pest prevention treatment per EPA pest guidelines

Step 4: Linen Management System

Linen management is one of the most time-consuming aspects of STR cleaning. An efficient system minimizes delays.

The Two-Set System: Keep two full sets of linens per bed. While Set A is on the bed for the current guests, Set B is freshly laundered and stored. At turnover, strip Set A, stage Set B immediately, and launder Set A for the next stay.

The Three-Set System (Recommended for High Occupancy):

  • Set A: On the bed
  • Set B: Freshly washed and staged at the property or with the cleaner
  • Set C: Backup, stored off-site or in a linen closet

With three sets, your cleaner never waits for laundry. They arrive with clean linens, make the beds immediately, and take the dirty linens to wash.

Linen Care Best Practices:

  • Use white linens — they look crisp, are easy to bleach, and signal cleanliness to guests
  • Wash at 60C (140F) to sanitize per CDC cleaning guidelines
  • Use a commercial laundry service for high-volume hosts — the cost often justifies the time saved
  • Inspect linens for stains, tears, and pilling at every wash; retire worn items promptly

Step 5: Restocking and Supply Management

Every turnover requires restocking supplies. Create a par level system: a minimum quantity of each supply that must be present when a guest arrives.

Sample Par Levels for a 2-Bedroom Property:

Supply Item Par Level Per Stay
Toilet paper rolls 6 (2 per bathroom + spares)
Paper towels 2 rolls
Dish soap 1 full bottle
Dishwasher pods 4
Hand soap (each bathroom) 1 full dispenser
Shampoo + conditioner 1 bottle each per bathroom
Body wash/soap 1 per bathroom
Trash bags 3–4
Coffee pods/packets 6–10
Laundry pods (guest use) 3

Keep a restocking supply kit at the property or in your cleaner's vehicle. When a supply drops below par, it gets restocked at the next turnover.

Managing Your Cleaning Team

Communicating Turnover Schedule

Your cleaning team needs accurate, timely information about when they are needed. Best practices:

  • Use a shared Google Calendar or your property management software's task feature
  • Send automatic notifications when a booking is confirmed (or checkout is approaching)
  • Give cleaners at least 24 hours notice for turnovers; same-day turnovers should be the exception

Providing a Written Checklist

Never assume your cleaner knows exactly what you expect. Provide a printed or digital checklist for every property. Walk through it together on their first clean and solicit their feedback — experienced cleaners often have operational insight that improves your system.

Many hosts use apps like Properly, Breezeway, or TurnoverBnB to send digital checklists, track task completion with photo confirmation, and communicate with cleaning teams.

Quality Control

Trust but verify. Options for quality control:

  • Photo documentation: Ask cleaners to send photos of key areas (bed, bathroom, kitchen) after each clean
  • Self-inspection: If you are local, do a walk-through after cleaning before the guest arrives for your most important stays
  • Guest feedback loop: Monitor your cleanliness sub-rating religiously. A pattern of 4-star cleanliness ratings indicates a process issue

Contingency Planning

What happens when your cleaner calls in sick on a same-day turnover? Every host needs a contingency plan:

  • A backup cleaner you have already trained and briefed
  • A professional cleaning service you can call on short notice
  • A network of fellow hosts who can recommend reliable cleaners

Turnover Checklist Summary

Use this as a quick-reference for each turnover:

Arrival Tasks:

  • Open windows to air out
  • Strip all beds; start laundry
  • Collect and dispose of all trash

Cleaning Tasks:

  • Kitchen — counters, appliances, sink, floor
  • All bathrooms — toilet, shower, sink, floor
  • Common areas — wipe surfaces, vacuum, mop
  • Bedrooms — dust, vacuum/sweep
  • Entry and exterior — sweep, wipe door

Staging Tasks:

  • Make all beds with fresh linens
  • Hang fresh towels
  • Restock all supplies to par level
  • Reset décor and staging elements
  • Test all electronics and locks

Final Checks:

  • Walk through every room
  • Confirm all lights off and thermostat set
  • Lock all doors and windows
  • Report completion to host or PMS

Conclusion

A professional cleaning schedule is what separates good STR hosts from great ones. Guests who arrive to a perfectly clean, carefully staged property are already primed to leave a 5-star review before they have unpacked their bags.

Build your schedule systematically — define your cleaning window, document the sequence, create room-by-room checklists, establish a deep clean calendar, and implement a supply management system. Then train your team to execute consistently, every time.

Cleanliness is not just about hygiene — it is a direct investment in your review profile, occupancy, and revenue. Treat it accordingly.

For more guidance on building a professional short-term rental operation, see our vacation rental property management and vacation rental turnover checklist guides.

Cleaning Schedule Key Takeaways:

  • Define your cleaning window and plan cleaning around it
  • Follow a consistent sequence: top to bottom, dry to wet
  • Separate every-turnover tasks from monthly and quarterly deep cleans
  • Use a two or three-set linen system to eliminate wait time
  • Provide written checklists and photo confirmation to your cleaning team
  • Monitor your cleanliness sub-rating after every stay
