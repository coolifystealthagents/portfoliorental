20:T3593,<p>Checkout is the last chapter of every guest's stay — and for hosts, it sets up the next one. Clear, friendly checkout instructions accomplish two things simultaneously: they give guests a positive final experience that contributes to a strong review, and they ensure the property is ready for an efficient turnover.</p>
<p>Too many hosts either skip checkout instructions entirely (leaving guests guessing), or write them in a tone that reads more like a legal warning than a farewell. This guide gives you copy-paste <strong>checkout instruction templates</strong> for every delivery format, along with best practices for keeping checkout smooth and stress-free.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="why-checkout-instructions-matter">Why Checkout Instructions Matter</h2>
<p>Well-designed checkout instructions directly impact:</p>
<p><strong>Guest experience:</strong> Guests who know exactly what to do at checkout feel calm and confident. Those who don't feel anxious about whether they've done enough — or worse, do nothing and feel guilty.</p>
<p><strong>Review scores:</strong> Checkout instructions contribute to the "communication" and "value" review categories. <a href="https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/mortgages/airbnb-rental-investment-property">NerdWallet explains rental hosting</a> costs and returns. Clear instructions signal that you're a thoughtful, professional host.</p>
<p><strong>Turnover efficiency:</strong> When guests follow consistent checkout procedures, your cleaning team can work faster and more efficiently between bookings.</p>
<p><strong>Damage documentation:</strong> A standard checkout procedure (photos of key areas before departure) creates a baseline for any damage claims.</p>
<p>For the full operational framework, see our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-checkout-instructions-philosophy">The Checkout Instructions Philosophy</h2>
<p>Before writing your checkout instructions, decide on your philosophy:</p>
<p><strong>Option A: Minimal checkout tasks</strong>
You charge a higher cleaning fee and ask guests to do very little — just remove trash and leave. This maximizes the guest experience and reviews but requires more cleaner time and cost.</p>
<p><strong>Option B: Moderate checkout tasks</strong>
Guests handle a handful of tasks (start dishwasher, strip beds, take out trash) that meaningfully reduce the cleaner's workload. Most common approach.</p>
<p><strong>Option C: Extensive checkout tasks</strong>
Guests handle significant cleaning tasks in exchange for a lower or no cleaning fee. Works with budget travelers but can feel onerous and affect reviews.</p>
<p>For most STR hosts, <strong>Option B</strong> strikes the right balance. The tasks are simple and reasonable, guests generally comply without complaint, and the cleaner can work faster.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="template-1-in-property-checkout-card-print-and-display">Template 1: In-Property Checkout Card (Print and Display)</h2>
<p>This is a laminated card or framed print to display visibly in the property — ideal on the kitchen counter, by the front door, or on the back of the door.</p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Checkout is at [TIME] on [Date]</strong></p>
<p>Thank you so much for staying with us! Before you head out:</p>
<p><strong>5 Easy Steps:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Dishes</strong> — Load and start the dishwasher (or wash and dry if no dishwasher)</li>
<li><strong>Trash</strong> — Empty all trash bins into the large can [location — e.g., "by the garage"] and replace liners</li>
<li><strong>Towels</strong> — Leave used towels in the bathtub or shower</li>
<li><strong>Beds</strong> — Strip the sheets and leave them on the mattress (or leave beds as-is — your preference)</li>
<li><strong>Lock up</strong> — Close and lock all windows and doors; leave the key [on the counter / in the lockbox]</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Please take all personal items — we can't be responsible for items left behind.</strong></p>
<p>Wi-Fi, streaming services, and all other access will deactivate at checkout time.</p>
<p>Safe travels! We'd love to host you again. 🏡</p>
<p>— [Your Name / Property Name]</p>
<hr>
<p><em>Print this card on cardstock, laminate it, and display it prominently. Update the checkout time and key return instructions for your property.</em></p>
<hr>
<h2 id="template-2-pre-checkout-message-night-before">Template 2: Pre-Checkout Message (Night Before)</h2>
<p>Send this via Airbnb messaging (or your PMS) the evening before checkout.</p>
<hr>
<blockquote>
<p>Hi [Guest Name],</p>
<p>Hope you've had a wonderful time at [Property Name]! Just a quick reminder that checkout is tomorrow by [TIME].</p>
<p>To make sure everything is set:
✓ Load and start the dishwasher
✓ Leave used towels in the shower/bathtub
✓ Take out any trash
✓ Lock the door and leave the key [location]</p>
<p>That's it — the rest is on us!</p>
<p>Safe travels to wherever you're headed next. It's been a pleasure hosting you.</p>
<p>[Your Name]</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<p><strong>Why the night before?</strong> Guests who read checkout instructions the morning of checkout often feel rushed. A night-before reminder gives them time to feel prepared and is less likely to feel like a demand.</p>
<p>For your complete guest messaging system, see our <a href="/blog/guest-communication-short-term-rental">guest communication templates guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="template-3-morning-of-checkout-reminder">Template 3: Morning-of Checkout Reminder</h2>
<p>For guests who haven't messaged back or for stays where you want an extra touchpoint:</p>
<hr>
<blockquote>
<p>Good morning, [Guest Name]!</p>
<p>Just a quick note — checkout is today by [TIME]. The instructions are on the kitchen counter if you need them.</p>
<p>I hope you had a fantastic stay. If you have a moment, a review means the world to us. I'll be leaving you a great one as well!</p>
<p>Have a safe trip home.</p>
<p>[Your Name]</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h2 id="template-4-welcome-book-checkout-section">Template 4: Welcome Book Checkout Section</h2>
<p>Include a dedicated checkout section in your <a href="/blog/welcome-book-vacation-rental">welcome book</a>:</p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Checkout Instructions</strong></p>
<p>Checkout is by <strong>[TIME]</strong> on your departure day.</p>
<p>We've tried to keep this simple — just a few steps:</p>
<p><strong>Before you leave:</strong></p>
<ul class="contains-task-list">
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Run the dishwasher (pods are under the sink)</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Leave used towels in the shower or bathtub</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Strip the beds and leave linens on the mattress</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Take trash to the outdoor bin [location description]</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Check all rooms for personal belongings</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Lock all windows and exterior doors</li>
<li class="task-list-item"><input type="checkbox" disabled> Return the key to [location]</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Optional but appreciated:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Start the washing machine if you've used beach/pool towels</li>
<li>Wipe down kitchen counters after your last use</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>If something was damaged or broken during your stay:</strong>
Please let us know before you leave — we always appreciate honesty and handle these situations fairly.</p>
<p><strong>Forgot something?</strong>
Email us at [address] — we hold found items for 30 days and can ship them back to you.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="template-5-digital-checkout-instructions-for-touch-stay-hostfully-lodgify">Template 5: Digital Checkout Instructions (for Touch Stay / Hostfully / Lodgify)</h2>
<p>Digital welcome books allow you to include rich formatting. Structure your digital checkout section this way:</p>
<p><strong>Header:</strong> Checkout Day — Everything You Need to Know</p>
<p><strong>Section 1: Checkout Time</strong>
Your checkout time is [TIME]. Late checkout is available upon request and subject to availability — just message us the day before and we'll do our best.</p>
<p><strong>Section 2: Your Checkout Checklist</strong>
[Interactive checklist with checkboxes]</p>
<ul>
<li>Dishwasher loaded and running</li>
<li>Towels left in bath/shower</li>
<li>Beds stripped (linens left on mattress)</li>
<li>Trash in outdoor bin</li>
<li>Personal items collected</li>
<li>Windows and doors locked</li>
<li>Key returned to [location]</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Section 3: What Happens Next</strong>
Our cleaning team arrives after you leave to get everything sparkling for the next guests. This is why your checkout time matters — thank you for helping us keep everything on schedule!</p>
<p><strong>Section 4: Before You Go</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Did we earn a 5-star review? Leave one at [link]</li>
<li>Book your next stay: [direct booking link or Airbnb listing]</li>
<li>Follow us for special offers: [social media or email signup]</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-not-to-include-in-checkout-instructions">What Not to Include in Checkout Instructions</h2>
<p>Just as important as what you include is what you don't.</p>
<p><strong>Avoid:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Long lists of rules that read like consequences ("failure to comply will result in...")</li>
<li>Excessive tasks (mopping floors, cleaning the oven, doing all the laundry)</li>
<li>Confusing or location-ambiguous instructions ("put the key where you found it" — tell them exactly where)</li>
<li>Multiple contradictory checkout times across different documents</li>
<li>Tiny font or hard-to-read formatting</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Keep it under 8-10 bullet points.</strong> Anything longer starts feeling onerous and affects how guests experience their departure.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="handling-late-checkouts">Handling Late Checkouts</h2>
<p>Late checkout requests are common and handling them graciously (when possible) generates goodwill.</p>
<p><strong>If same-day turnover permits a late checkout:</strong></p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Absolutely! As long as our next guests aren't checking in until [time], you're welcome to stay until [late time]. Happy to make that work for you."</p>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>If same-day turnover doesn't allow it:</strong></p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I wish I could — we actually have guests arriving today and the cleaning team needs the full window. I hope you understand! If you need to store luggage while you explore, [nearby hotel / luggage storage option] is a great option."</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h2 id="checkout-key-return-options">Checkout Key Return Options</h2>
<p>Choose the method that fits your property and security setup:</p>
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Option</th>
<th>Best For</th>
<th>Notes</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Smart lock (self-latching)</td>
<td>Most properties</td>
<td>Guest simply leaves; lock self-secures</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Key in lockbox</td>
<td>Properties with physical keys</td>
<td>Guest returns key, you can confirm remotely</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Key on kitchen counter</td>
<td>Low-security needs</td>
<td>Easy for guest, less secure</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>Key with neighbor or keyholder</td>
<td>Rural properties</td>
<td>Requires neighbor coordination</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p><strong>Recommendation:</strong> Smart locks are the gold standard — no key handoffs, automatic security, and you receive an alert when the guest has left. The <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/careful-connections-building-security-internet-things">FTC covers smart home</a> device security best practices. See <a href="/blog/damage-prevention-short-term-rental">damage prevention</a> for more on smart lock setup.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="post-checkout-workflow-for-hosts">Post-Checkout Workflow for Hosts</h2>
<p>Once your guests check out, your job isn't done:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Confirm checkout</strong> — check your smart lock log or send a quick message to confirm departure</li>
<li><strong>Notify your cleaner</strong> — trigger the turnover (many PMS tools do this automatically via <a href="/blog/airbnb-automation-tools">Airbnb automation tools</a>)</li>
<li><strong>Review cleaner photos</strong> — confirm property condition before releasing the security deposit</li>
<li><strong>Leave a guest review</strong> — do this within 24-48 hours while the stay is fresh</li>
<li><strong>Send a review request message</strong> — a warm, brief post-checkout message inviting them to review</li>
</ol>
<p>For the complete turnover checklist your cleaning team should use, see our <a href="/blog/vacation-rental-turnover-checklist">vacation rental turnover checklist</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Checkout instructions are a small but meaningful part of the guest experience. They signal that you're organized, thoughtful, and respectful of the guest's time — and they set your cleaning team up for success.</p>
<p>Use the templates in this guide to create checkout instructions that are warm, clear, and appropriately brief. Display them prominently in the property, send a reminder the night before, and follow up with a gracious review request the morning of checkout.</p>
<p>Together, these habits create a professional, memorable final impression that contributes directly to the five-star reviews that drive your STR's long-term success. For the complete guide to managing your short-term rental, visit our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
