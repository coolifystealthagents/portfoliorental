Mid-Term Rentals: The Sweet Spot for Income

Every real estate investor eventually confronts the classic trade-off: the high nightly rates of short-term rentals versus the predictable, hands-off income of long-term leases. For years those were the only two choices. But a third model has emerged as a powerful middle path — one that thousands of savvy property investors are now using to beat both strategies on a risk-adjusted basis.

Mid-term rentals — furnished properties leased for 30 to 90 days at a time — offer the income premium of short-term rentals without the exhausting turnover. They provide the stability of longer stays without locking you into a year-long lease at below-market rates. When executed correctly, they can be the most efficient income model available for furnished residential real estate.

What Is a Mid-Term Rental?

A mid-term rental (sometimes called a "medium-term rental" or "MTR") is a furnished residential lease of approximately 30 to 90 days, though stays can stretch to 6 months in some cases.

The key characteristics:

  • Fully furnished — Guests bring only their personal belongings. You supply furniture, cookware, linens, and all utilities.
  • Month-to-month or fixed term — Stays can be a fixed 30, 60, or 90 days, or rolling monthly.
  • Higher rent than long-term — You charge a monthly rate significantly above what a 12-month unfurnished lease would command.
  • Lower turnover than STR — Compared to 2–7 night short-term stays, monthly stays radically reduce cleaning, restocking, and messaging overhead.

Who Books Mid-Term Rentals?

The demand pool for mid-term rentals is wide and growing:

  • Travel nurses and healthcare workers — The largest single segment. Contract nurses typically work 13-week assignments in hospitals across the country and need furnished housing close to their facility.
  • Corporate relocators — Employees transitioning between cities often need temporary housing for 1–3 months while house-hunting or completing a lease.
  • Remote workers and digital nomads — Workers who move between cities seasonally, often staying 1–3 months per location.
  • Insurance displacement guests — Homeowners displaced by fire, flood, or other damage. FEMA disaster assistance covers temporary housing needs. These stays are often funded by insurance and command premium rates.
  • Construction and project workers — Engineers, project managers, and skilled tradespeople on fixed-term assignments.
  • Students and visiting researchers — Graduate students, medical residents, and academic researchers on semester-length stays.

Mid-Term vs. Short-Term vs. Long-Term: A Financial Comparison

Understanding where mid-term fits in the income spectrum requires comparing all three models.

Factor Short-Term (STR) Mid-Term (MTR) Long-Term (LTR)
Nightly/monthly rate Highest High Lower
Turnover frequency Very high (weekly) Low (monthly) Minimal (annual)
Cleaning costs High Moderate Minimal
Management intensity Very high Moderate Low
Vacancy risk High (seasonal) Moderate Low
Regulatory risk High Moderate Low
Revenue predictability Low Moderate High

Practical example: A 3-bedroom property in a mid-size city:

  • STR at $175/night × 65% occupancy = $3,412/month in room revenue, minus $800–1,200/month in cleaning, supplies, and platform fees = ~$2,400–2,600 net
  • MTR at $3,800/month — one turnover every 60–90 days, minimal cleaning cost, utility pass-through — ~$3,400–3,600 net
  • LTR at $2,200/month unfurnished — nearly zero management cost — ~$2,100–2,200 net

In this scenario, the mid-term rental delivers the highest net income with significantly less operational burden than the STR.

Where to List Mid-Term Rentals

Unlike STRs, which funnel through Airbnb and Vrbo, mid-term rentals have their own dedicated platforms:

Furnished Finder

Furnished Finder is the dominant platform for travel nurses and healthcare workers. Hosts pay an annual listing fee rather than a per-booking commission, making it highly cost-efficient. Traffic is enormous in healthcare markets — cities with major hospital systems, medical schools, and research centers perform especially well.

Airbnb Monthly Stays

Airbnb now supports monthly stays natively, with a dedicated filter for 28+ night bookings. You can adapt your existing Airbnb listing to attract mid-term guests by offering monthly discounts in your pricing settings. This is the lowest-friction way to test the mid-term market if you already have an Airbnb listing.

Vrbo

Vrbo allows monthly rentals as well. It performs best in vacation markets where families and remote workers want seasonal stays.

Corporate Housing Platforms

Platforms like Blueground, Landing, and Zeus Living operate as B2B marketplaces targeting corporate clients and offer premium rates — though they often take larger cuts and have stricter property requirements.

Setting the Right Mid-Term Rate

Pricing a mid-term rental requires a different mental model than nightly STR pricing. You are not competing against hotels or weekend getaway listings. You are competing against:

  • Long-term unfurnished apartments in your area
  • Extended-stay hotels
  • Other furnished monthly rentals on Furnished Finder and Airbnb

Pricing Formula

A common starting point is to calculate your monthly STR revenue at typical occupancy and then match or slightly undercut that figure to price competitively:

MTR Target Rate = (Average Daily Rate × Expected Occupancy %) × 30 days × 85–90%

The 85–90% factor reflects the discount guests expect for committing to a longer stay, offset by your dramatically lower operating costs. In practice, many hosts find their net monthly income equals or exceeds STR income once turnover costs are factored out.

Learn how Bankrate calculates rental return on investment. Also factor in utilities. Most mid-term hosts include all utilities in the monthly rate (electricity, internet, water) but set a usage cap and charge overages. This simplifies billing for guests and protects you from extreme utility spikes.

Furnishing for Mid-Term Guests

Mid-term guests have different needs than weekend vacationers. They are essentially moving in temporarily and need a functional home, not a resort. Prioritize:

Must-Haves

  • High-speed WiFi — Non-negotiable. Advertise actual speeds.
  • Full kitchen — Stocked with enough cookware, dishes, and utensils for daily meal prep.
  • Comfortable workspace — A desk, task chair, and good lighting for remote workers.
  • Washer and dryer in-unit — Guests staying 30+ days cannot visit a laundromat every week.
  • Quality mattress — A bad mattress will generate your first negative review.
  • Ample storage — Closet space, dresser drawers, and hangers for an actual wardrobe.

Nice-to-Haves

  • Smart TV with streaming apps
  • Blackout curtains in bedrooms
  • Extra sets of linens (guests will do their own laundry)
  • Coffee maker and basic pantry staples
  • Parking

What to Skip

Skip the vacation-focused extras: board games, beach toys, hot tub marketing. Mid-term guests care about livability, not leisure amenities.

Legal and Regulatory Considerations

Mid-term rentals sit in a unique regulatory position. Most cities define "short-term rental" as stays of fewer than 30 days, meaning 30+ day rentals often avoid the strictest STR regulations, licensing requirements, and hotel taxes that apply to nightly rentals.

However, rentals of 30+ days in most U.S. states trigger tenant protections. HUD tenant rights resources explain key legal protections. Depending on your state:

  • Tenants in month-to-month arrangements may require 30–60 days' notice to vacate
  • Eviction proceedings may apply if a guest refuses to leave
  • State landlord-tenant law may govern your lease terms

Best practice: Use a written lease for every mid-term stay. Include your payment terms, utility policies, early termination clause, and check-out requirements. Consult a local real estate attorney to ensure your lease complies with state landlord-tenant law.

Managing Mid-Term Rentals Efficiently

The operational efficiency of mid-term rentals is one of their greatest advantages. With one guest staying 60 days instead of ten 6-night stays, you dramatically reduce:

  • Cleaning appointments (typically one deep clean between guests, not ten)
  • Supply restocking (guests manage their own consumables)
  • Guest communication (check-in, one mid-stay check-in, check-out)
  • Keypad code changes and lock management

Self-Check-In is Essential

Smart locks and digital keypad systems are a must. Guests arriving from long travel days for a contract job do not want to coordinate a key handoff. Use a smart lock system that allows you to assign unique codes per guest and rotate them between stays.

Pair your smart lock with an automated message sequence:

  1. Booking confirmation with property details
  2. 48-hour pre-arrival message with directions and check-in code
  3. Day-of welcome message
  4. 2-week check-in message
  5. 1-week pre-checkout reminder

Mid-Term Rentals as a Seasonal Strategy

Even if you primarily operate as an STR host, mid-term rentals can be a powerful off-season revenue tool. Many STR markets have defined slow seasons where nightly occupancy drops below 50%. Rather than discounting heavily to fill nights, consider transitioning to mid-term for those months.

A beach property that commands $300/night in summer but struggles in January–February can pivot to a mid-term rental at $3,500/month for winter, locking in reliable income during slow months while maintaining STR flexibility for peak season.

This blended approach is increasingly popular among professional hosts. It requires smart calendar management and clear communication across your listing platforms, but it solves one of the most persistent problems in the STR business: slow-season cash flow.

Summary

Mid-term rentals represent a genuine sweet spot in the rental property income spectrum. They offer:

  • Higher net income than long-term leases after accounting for furnished premium
  • Lower operational burden than short-term rentals with less turnover, cleaning, and guest management
  • More regulatory flexibility than nightly STRs in most markets
  • Diverse and stable demand from travel nurses, corporate workers, and relocators

Whether you pursue mid-term rentals as your primary strategy or layer them into a blended seasonal approach, understanding this model is essential for any STR investor looking to maximize returns.
