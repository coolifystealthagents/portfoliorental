30:T3406,<p>One of the most significant challenges for rental property owners — particularly those managing properties across multiple locations — is maintaining awareness of what is happening at each property between visits. A pipe leaks for three days before anyone notices. A heating system fails during a cold snap. A vacant unit gets broken into. These scenarios share a common thread: if the landlord had known sooner, the outcome would have been far less costly.</p>
<p>Remote property monitoring systems address this gap by deploying sensors and smart devices that continuously monitor property conditions and send alerts when something needs attention — without requiring the landlord to be physically present.</p>
<p>This guide covers the components of a comprehensive remote monitoring system, recommended technology, and how to build a monitoring infrastructure that scales across a rental portfolio.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-remote-property-monitoring-can-detect">What Remote Property Monitoring Can Detect</h2>
<p>A well-designed monitoring system can alert you to:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Water leaks</strong> — At water heaters, supply lines, under sinks, from HVAC drain pans</li>
<li><strong>Freeze conditions</strong> — Temperature drops that put pipes at risk</li>
<li><strong>HVAC failure</strong> — Loss of heat or cooling, suggesting a system malfunction</li>
<li><strong>Power outages</strong> — Loss of power to the property</li>
<li><strong>Unauthorized access</strong> — Motion detection or access alerts in vacant properties</li>
<li><strong>Smoke or fire</strong> — Smoke detector alerts per <a href="https://www.cpsc.gov/Safety-Education/Safety-Education-Centers/Fire-Safety">CPSC fire safety guidelines</a></li>
<li><strong>Carbon monoxide</strong> — CO detector alerts with remote notification</li>
<li><strong>Basement flooding</strong> — Water level detection in basement areas</li>
<li><strong>High humidity</strong> — Conditions that could lead to <a href="https://www.epa.gov/mold">mold growth</a></li>
<li><strong>Energy usage anomalies</strong> — Unusually high consumption suggesting HVAC issues or unauthorized occupancy</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="building-blocks-of-a-remote-monitoring-system">Building Blocks of a Remote Monitoring System</h2>
<h3 id="1-smart-thermostats-temperature-monitoring">1. Smart Thermostats (Temperature Monitoring)</h3>
<p>A smart thermostat provides continuous temperature monitoring — alerting you when the unit temperature drops below a set threshold (important for freeze protection) or when the HVAC system is unable to maintain the set temperature (suggesting failure).</p>
<p><strong>Best options:</strong> Google Nest, ecobee, Honeywell Home T6</p>
<p>A smart thermostat is typically the first monitoring device to install at any rental property. See our detailed guide on <a href="/blog/smart-thermostat-rental-property">smart thermostats for rental units</a>.</p>
<h3 id="2-water-leak-sensors">2. Water Leak Sensors</h3>
<p>Water sensors placed near high-risk appliances detect leaks before they cause significant damage. They are inexpensive, easy to install, and provide alerts within seconds of detecting moisture.</p>
<p><strong>Best options:</strong> Moen Flo sensors, Govee WiFi sensors, SmartThings Water sensors</p>
<p>For detailed guidance on water sensor selection and placement, see our guide on <a href="/blog/water-leak-detection-rental">water leak detection for landlords</a>.</p>
<h3 id="3-smoke-and-co-detectors-with-remote-alerts">3. Smoke and CO Detectors with Remote Alerts</h3>
<p>Traditional smoke and CO detectors alarm audibly but do not notify the landlord. Smart connected detectors (or adapters for existing detectors) can send push notifications to your phone:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Nest Protect</strong> — Combination smoke/CO detector with mobile alerts; excellent for rental properties</li>
<li><strong>First Alert OneLink Safe &#x26; Sound</strong> — Combination smoke/CO with Alexa integration and mobile alerts</li>
<li><strong>Roost Smart Battery</strong> — A smart battery that fits many existing smoke detectors and adds Wi-Fi notification capability without replacing the detector</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="4-smart-electrical-monitoring">4. Smart Electrical Monitoring</h3>
<p>Smart electrical monitors track power usage at the circuit or whole-home level:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Sense Energy Monitor</strong> — Installs in the electrical panel; tracks whole-home energy usage, identifies individual appliance signatures, and alerts you to power anomalies</li>
<li><strong>Emporia Energy Monitor</strong> — Monitors individual circuits; useful for tracking HVAC usage, identifying failing appliances, and detecting unusual power draw</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Use cases for landlords:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Detect HVAC systems running continuously (can indicate thermostat failure or refrigerant issue)</li>
<li>Monitor vacant properties for unauthorized occupancy (power usage without known tenants)</li>
<li>Track utility expenses for utility-paid properties</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="5-motion-sensors-vacant-properties">5. Motion Sensors (Vacant Properties)</h3>
<p>For vacant units, motion sensors alert you to unauthorized entry or occupancy:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>SimpliSafe sensors</strong> — Wireless, easy to deploy temporarily during vacancy</li>
<li><strong>Wyze Motion Sensor</strong> — Inexpensive, Wi-Fi connected, good for basic intrusion detection</li>
<li><strong>Ring Alarm Motion Detector</strong> — Integrates with Ring ecosystem and Amazon monitoring</li>
</ul>
<p>Configure motion sensors to send alerts only during periods when the unit should be vacant. Remove or reconfigure before new tenant move-in.</p>
<h3 id="6-door-and-window-sensors">6. Door and Window Sensors</h3>
<p>Contact sensors on entry doors and accessible windows alert you when they open:</p>
<ul>
<li>Useful for vacant properties to detect unauthorized entry</li>
<li>Can be used to confirm contractor entry at a specific time</li>
<li>Work in conjunction with smart locks for complete entry monitoring</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="7-wi-fi-connected-cameras">7. Wi-Fi Connected Cameras</h3>
<p>For vacant properties or common areas, cameras provide visual verification when other sensors trigger:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Arlo Pro 4</strong> — Outdoor-rated, solar-powered option available</li>
<li><strong>Ring Stick Up Cam</strong> — Easy installation, battery or wired options</li>
<li><strong>Wyze Cam Outdoor</strong> — Budget-friendly</li>
</ul>
<p>Note: Camera placement must comply with tenant privacy requirements. See our guide on <a href="/blog/security-cameras-rental-property">security camera rules for rental properties</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="hub-based-vs-cloud-based-systems">Hub-Based vs Cloud-Based Systems</h2>
<h3 id="cloud-based-wi-fi-direct">Cloud-Based (Wi-Fi Direct)</h3>
<p>Many modern smart devices connect directly to Wi-Fi and communicate via the manufacturer's cloud service. Pros:</p>
<ul>
<li>Simple setup — no hub required</li>
<li>Each device has its own app</li>
<li>Widely available</li>
</ul>
<p>Cons:</p>
<ul>
<li>Requires a reliable Wi-Fi signal at each device location</li>
<li>Multiple apps for different devices</li>
<li>Cloud service dependency (if manufacturer discontinues service, device may become non-functional)</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="hub-based-z-wave-or-zigbee">Hub-Based (Z-Wave or Zigbee)</h3>
<p>A smart home hub (SmartThings, Hubitat) acts as a central controller for Z-Wave and Zigbee devices. Pros:</p>
<ul>
<li>Works without internet (local processing)</li>
<li>Single platform for all devices regardless of brand</li>
<li>More reliable for critical applications like leak detection</li>
</ul>
<p>Cons:</p>
<ul>
<li>Requires hub installation at each property</li>
<li>More complex initial setup</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>For landlords with multiple properties:</strong> A hub-based system offers more reliable and consistent monitoring. Hubitat is particularly popular with landlords because it processes automation locally without cloud dependency.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="monitoring-platforms-for-multi-property-landlords">Monitoring Platforms for Multi-Property Landlords</h2>
<p>Several platforms are designed specifically for landlords managing monitoring across multiple properties:</p>
<h3 id="smartrent">SmartRent</h3>
<p>SmartRent is a proptech platform designed for institutional and professional landlords:</p>
<ul>
<li>Smart thermostat, smart lock, water sensor, and leak detection integration</li>
<li>Single dashboard for all properties</li>
<li>Designed for multi-family buildings (20+ units)</li>
<li>Integration with major property management software (Yardi, RealPage, AppFolio)</li>
<li>White-label resident app</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="dwelo">Dwelo</h3>
<p>Similar to SmartRent; comprehensive smart home platform designed for multi-family rentals with centralized monitoring and management.</p>
<h3 id="alertify-formerly-party-squasher">Alertify (formerly Party Squasher)</h3>
<p>Alertify is designed for short-term rental operators:</p>
<ul>
<li>Noise monitoring</li>
<li>Occupancy detection</li>
<li>Smoking detection (cigarette smoke sensors)</li>
<li>Integration with Airbnb and VRBO platforms</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="self-managed-dashboard">Self-Managed Dashboard</h3>
<p>For smaller portfolios (2-20 properties), a self-managed approach works well:</p>
<ul>
<li>Choose a smart home platform (SmartThings, Apple Home, or Google Home)</li>
<li>Add compatible sensors from major manufacturers</li>
<li>Configure automated alerts for all critical conditions</li>
<li>Monitor from a single app</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="telecommunications-infrastructure">Telecommunications Infrastructure</h2>
<p>For monitoring systems to work, each property needs:</p>
<h3 id="reliable-wi-fi">Reliable Wi-Fi</h3>
<p>At minimum, a reliable broadband connection and router at each monitored property. Consider:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Eero</strong> or <strong>Google Nest WiFi</strong> — Mesh Wi-Fi systems that provide whole-property coverage reliably</li>
<li>Dedicated router (separate from tenant's service) for landlord monitoring devices</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="cellular-backup">Cellular Backup</h3>
<p>For critical monitoring (vacant properties, high-risk units), cellular backup ensures monitoring continues during internet outages. Devices like the SimpliSafe cellular communicator or LTE backup modems provide this capability.</p>
<h3 id="power-backup">Power Backup</h3>
<p>Smart home hubs and routers lose function during power outages unless backed up. A small UPS (uninterruptible power supply) for your router and hub ensures monitoring continues during short power outages.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="privacy-and-tenant-communication">Privacy and Tenant Communication</h2>
<h3 id="disclosure">Disclosure</h3>
<p>Disclose all monitoring devices in your lease. Specifically describe:</p>
<ul>
<li>What devices are installed (thermostats, smoke/CO detectors, water sensors, motion sensors)</li>
<li>What each monitors</li>
<li>How alerts are handled</li>
<li>That cameras (if any) are located only in exterior/common areas</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="tenant-consent">Tenant Consent</h3>
<p>Include a technology addendum per <a href="https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/privacy-security">FTC privacy guidelines</a> that tenants sign. This protects you legally and ensures tenants are not surprised.</p>
<h3 id="during-tenancy">During Tenancy</h3>
<p>Remove or disable motion sensors and cameras in occupied units (they are only appropriate for vacant properties). Thermostats, water sensors, and smoke/CO detectors can operate throughout the tenancy as they monitor property conditions, not tenant behavior.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="building-a-monitoring-rollout-plan">Building a Monitoring Rollout Plan</h2>
<p>For landlords with existing portfolios who want to add monitoring:</p>
<p><strong>Phase 1 (Immediate protection — $100-$200 per unit):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Smart thermostat at each unit</li>
<li>Water sensor at water heater and washing machine location</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Phase 2 (Expanded coverage — $100-$200 per unit additional):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Water sensors at all remaining high-risk locations</li>
<li>Connected smoke/CO detector</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Phase 3 (Full monitoring — $200-$400 per unit additional):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Smart electrical monitoring</li>
<li>Hub-based system for reliability</li>
<li>Motion sensors for vacant unit periods</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>Remote property monitoring systems are no longer futuristic technology — they are practical, affordable, and increasingly essential for landlords who want to protect their assets between visits. Starting with the highest-value sensors (smart thermostat and water leak sensors) provides immediate protection at modest cost. Building out to a comprehensive monitoring platform over time creates a robust early-warning system that dramatically reduces emergency repairs, tenant disruption, and insurance claims.</p>
