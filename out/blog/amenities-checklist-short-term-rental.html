Walk into any top-rated vacation rental and you'll notice something: it feels complete. Nothing is missing.

Guests aren't hunting through cabinets for a can opener or calling the host because there's no toilet paper. That feeling of completeness, of thoughtfulness, is what drives five-star reviews and repeat bookings.

This full amenities checklist for short-term rentals covers everything from baseline necessities to premium upgrades, organized room by room so you can audit your property systematically.

Why Amenities Directly Impact Your Revenue

Amenities aren't just about comfort, they're about competitiveness. On platforms like Airbnb and VRBO, guests filter searches by amenity.

Did You Know?

Real estate has created more millionaires than any other investment type. About 90% of millionaires have invested in real estate at some point.

A property without Wi-Fi, air conditioning, or a washer/dryer is immediately excluded from a significant portion of search results.

Beyond search filters, amenities drive:

  • Review scores, especially the "accuracy" and "value" categories
  • Booking conversion, guests comparing listings choose the one that feels more complete
  • Nightly rate potential, premium amenities justify higher pricing (see our short-term rental pricing strategy)
  • Repeat bookings, guests remember properties that anticipated their needs

Tier 1: Absolute Essentials (Non-Negotiable)

These are the baseline amenities guests expect at any price point. Missing any of these will almost certainly generate negative reviews.

Technology & Connectivity

  • High-speed Wi-Fi (minimum 50 Mbps; 100+ Mbps preferred)
  • Wi-Fi password clearly displayed (in multiple locations)
  • Working TV with streaming services (Netflix, Hulu, or similar)
  • Reliable cell signal or documented dead zone disclosure

Climate Control

  • Air conditioning (in warm climates or summer destinations)
  • Heating (in cool climates or winter destinations)
  • Ceiling fans or portable fans as backup

Safety

  • Smoke detectors (in every bedroom and common area)
  • Carbon monoxide detector (CPSC safety guidelines)
  • Fire extinguisher (accessible and inspected, per FEMA fire safety tips)
  • First aid kit
  • Emergency contact list posted visibly

Entry & Access

  • Keypad/smart lock or clearly explained key system
  • Working exterior lighting
  • Secure parking (if applicable)

Tier 2: Room-by-Room Essentials Checklist

Kitchen Essentials

Appliances:

  • Coffee maker (drip or pod-based)
  • Microwave
  • Toaster or toaster oven
  • Blender
  • Electric kettle (especially for international guests)

Cookware & Tools:

  • Pots and pans (minimum: small, medium, large)
  • Baking sheet
  • Cutting board (at least two)
  • Sharp knives (chef's knife, paring knife, serrated)
  • Colander
  • Mixing bowls
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Wooden spoon, spatula, ladle, tongs

Dishes & Glassware (plan for maximum occupancy + 2):

  • Dinner plates
  • Salad/side plates
  • Bowls (cereal and soup)
  • Mugs
  • Wine glasses
  • Drinking glasses
  • Cutlery set

Consumable Supplies:

  • Dish soap
  • Sponge and scrub brush
  • Dishwasher pods (if applicable)
  • Dish towels (3+ per turnover)
  • Paper towels
  • Trash bags (sized to fit all bins)
  • Aluminum foil and plastic wrap
  • Salt, pepper, cooking oil (starter supplies)
  • Coffee and tea (starter quantities)

Bedroom Essentials

Bedding (per bed):

  • Mattress protector
  • Two fitted sheets (one on, one spare)
  • Two flat sheets or duvet covers
  • Comforter or duvet (appropriate weight for season/climate)
  • Four pillows per queen/king bed (two per twin)
  • Extra blanket in closet or visible storage

Bedroom Comfort:

  • Bedside table with lamp and outlet/USB charging port
  • Blackout curtains or blinds
  • Mirror (full-length or large dresser mirror)
  • Adequate closet or storage space (hangers, shelves, or drawers)
  • Luggage rack or bench
  • Alarm clock or accessible phone charging (guests wake up at various times)

Bathroom Essentials

Per Bathroom:

  • Toilet paper (minimum 4 rolls per bathroom at start of stay)
  • Shampoo and conditioner
  • Body wash or bar soap
  • Hand soap at sink
  • Bath towels (2 per guest minimum)
  • Hand towels (1-2 per bathroom)
  • Washcloths (1-2 per guest)
  • Bath mat (non-slip)
  • Hair dryer
  • Makeup/shaving mirror
  • Toilet brush and holder
  • Trash can with liner

Nice to Have:

  • Facial tissues
  • Cotton balls and swabs
  • Disposable razors
  • Toothbrush and toothpaste (travel size)
  • Shower cap
  • Loofah or bath sponge

Living Room / Common Area

  • Comfortable seating for maximum occupancy
  • Coffee table with adequate surface space
  • Adequate lighting (overhead + table/floor lamps)
  • Throw blankets (2-3)
  • Board games or card games
  • Books or local magazines
  • Charging stations or power strips
  • Bluetooth speaker (increasingly expected)

Laundry Area

  • Washer and dryer (or coin laundry instructions)
  • Laundry detergent (starter supply)
  • Dryer sheets or dryer balls
  • Laundry basket or bag
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Drying rack

Outdoor Spaces (If Applicable)

Patio / Deck:

  • Outdoor seating (table + chairs)
  • Grill (propane or charcoal with fuel/charcoal)
  • Grill brush and tongs
  • String lights or outdoor lighting

Yard / Grounds:

  • Hose or watering access
  • Outdoor games (cornhole, bocce, ladder toss)
  • Fire pit with firewood (if safe and legal)

Tier 3: Premium Amenities That Justify Higher Rates

Once the essentials are covered, these upgrades differentiate your property and support premium pricing.

Technology Upgrades

  • Smart TV (65"+) with universal remote
  • Smart home devices (Alexa, Google Home for lighting/temperature control)
  • EV charging outlet in garage or driveway
  • Noise monitoring device (Minut, NoiseAware) for peace of mind
  • Ring or Nest doorbell camera (exterior only, disclosed in listing)

Kitchen Upgrades

  • Espresso machine or high-end coffee maker
  • Instant Pot or air fryer
  • Full spice rack
  • Pour-over or French press for coffee enthusiasts
  • Wine opener and bottle stopper set
  • Cocktail kit (shaker, strainer, jigger)

Bedroom Upgrades

  • Hotel-quality pillows (memory foam or down alternatives)
  • Premium mattress (guests notice)
  • White noise machine in each bedroom
  • Reading lights with USB ports on each bedside table

Guest Experience Extras

  • Welcome basket (local snacks, wine, sparkling water)
  • Local guidebook with personal recommendations
  • Umbrella (often forgotten by guests)
  • Baby gear (crib, high chair, baby bathtub), great niche differentiator
  • Pack of playing cards and a few novels

Consumable Restocking: How Much Is Enough?

A common mistake is stocking just enough for one guest, leaving them to scramble if they use more than average. A useful rule of thumb:

Item Minimum Stock
Toilet paper 4 rolls per bathroom
Paper towels 2 full rolls
Dish soap One full bottle
Trash bags 2 per bin size
Laundry detergent 3-4 loads worth
Coffee pods / grounds Enough for 2 mornings
Shampoo/conditioner Travel-size bottles, full

Listing Your Amenities Correctly

Having an amenity is only half the battle, you need to list it accurately to attract the right guests and avoid expectation mismatches.

Best practices for listing amenities:

  • Mark only what's actually available and functional
  • Add descriptions for unique or premium items ("Nespresso machine with capsule starter pack")
  • Upload photos of key amenities, a photo of the well-stocked kitchen is more compelling than a checkbox
  • Update your listing when you add or remove items

Guests booking on Airbnb and VRBO filter heavily by amenities, accurate listings mean you appear in the right searches. According to VRBO's host resources, properties with 10+ amenities listed consistently outperform those with fewer in search ranking. EPA indoor air quality standards also apply to rental properties.

Seasonal Amenity Adjustments

Don't set your amenity supply once and forget it. Adjust for the season:

Summer additions:

  • Beach towels (if near water)
  • Sunscreen (travel size)
  • Insect repellent
  • Cooler for day trips
  • Fan or extra cooling capacity

Winter additions:

  • Extra blankets and flannel sheets
  • Hot cocoa or cider packets
  • Boot tray near entrance
  • Space heater (with safety disclosure)
  • Umbrella and rain gear

Holiday seasons:

  • Seasonal decorations
  • Board games for longer stays
  • Extra pantry items for cooking

Conclusion

A fully stocked, thoughtfully equipped short-term rental is one of the most reliable paths to consistent five-star reviews. Guests rarely comment on amenities when they're all there, but they always notice when something is missing.

Use this checklist to audit your property room by room, focus on the Tier 1 essentials first, then layer in the premium upgrades that fit your property type and target guest. Pair your amenities strategy with strong guest communication and you've built a guest experience that stands out in any market.
