Stessa Review: Best Free Landlord Accounting Tool?

Stessa has built a strong reputation among real estate investors by doing one thing exceptionally well: tracking the financial performance of rental properties. Unlike platforms that try to handle every aspect of property management, Stessa focuses on accounting, income and expense tracking, portfolio analytics, and tax preparation—and does it better than most competitors at any price point.

This review covers what Stessa actually delivers, how the free and paid plans compare, and whether it is the right tool for your portfolio.

What Stessa Is (and Is Not)

Before going further, it is worth being direct about what Stessa is designed to do.

Stessa is a financial tracking and accounting tool for rental property investors. It is not a full property management platform. It does not handle maintenance requests, tenant screening, lease management, or vacancy listings. If you need a single platform that covers operations alongside accounting, Stessa is not the right primary tool—though it works well as a financial layer alongside an operational platform.

What Stessa does exceptionally well:

  • Tracking income and expenses across a portfolio of rental properties
  • Automatic transaction import and categorization from bank accounts
  • Portfolio-level and property-level financial dashboards
  • Tax preparation support, particularly for Schedule E
  • Cash flow, NOI, and return metric calculation

For landlords whose primary pain point is financial visibility and tax preparation, Stessa is among the best options in the market—and the free plan covers most of what you need.

Stessa Pricing

Stessa operates on a freemium model:

Free Plan

  • Unlimited properties
  • Automatic transaction import and categorization
  • Income and expense tracking
  • Real-time financial dashboards
  • Schedule E tax preparation support
  • Net operating income and cash flow reporting
  • Rent roll

Stessa Pro (~$20/month or ~$200/year)

  • All free features plus:
  • Unlimited document storage
  • Receipts scanning and OCR
  • Advanced reporting exports
  • Priority customer support
  • Custom categories and notes

Stessa Tax ($125/year per return)

  • Preparation and filing of Schedule E through CPA partners
  • Directly integrates with your Stessa financial data

For most landlords, the free plan covers the core use case adequately. Stessa Pro is worth the upgrade if document management and OCR receipt scanning save meaningful time, or if you want to export detailed reports in customized formats.

The Stessa Tax service is priced competitively compared to CPA fees for rental property returns and offers the advantage of working directly from your Stessa data rather than exporting and re-importing.

Setting Up Stessa

Setup is straightforward. The process:

  1. Create an account and add properties (address, acquisition cost, current value estimate)
  2. Connect bank accounts and credit cards via Plaid integration
  3. Review automatically imported transactions and confirm categorizations
  4. Add any transactions that weren't captured automatically

Stessa pulls bank transaction data and uses algorithms to categorize expenses—mortgage payments, property taxes, insurance, repairs, management fees, and so on. The categorization is not perfect out of the box, but after reviewing and correcting a few weeks of transactions, the system learns your patterns and improves.

Initial setup for a five-property portfolio typically takes two to three hours, including bank connection and initial transaction review.

Income and Expense Tracking

This is Stessa's core strength. The platform tracks every financial event tied to your rental properties:

  • Rent collected (manual entry or via connected bank account)
  • Mortgage payments (principal and interest split)
  • Property taxes
  • Insurance premiums
  • Maintenance and repair costs
  • Capital improvements
  • Property management fees
  • HOA dues
  • Utilities (landlord-paid)
  • Professional fees (accounting, legal)
  • Mileage and travel

Categories align directly with IRS Schedule E requirements, which makes tax preparation significantly easier. When it's time to file, your expenses are already organized in the format your CPA needs—or the format required to file directly through Stessa Tax.

Financial Dashboards and Reporting

Stessa's dashboards provide real-time financial visibility across your portfolio. Key metrics available:

Portfolio Level:

  • Total portfolio value (manual or estimated)
  • Total equity
  • Net operating income (portfolio aggregate)
  • Cash flow after debt service
  • Year-to-date income and expenses

Property Level:

  • Gross rental income
  • Operating expenses (broken down by category)
  • Net operating income
  • Debt service
  • Cash flow
  • Cash-on-cash return

The property-level breakdowns make it easy to identify underperforming assets or unusual expense spikes. If one property's maintenance costs are running 40% above average, Stessa makes that visible in a way that a manual spreadsheet often obscures.

For investors tracking portfolio-level performance and making acquisition decisions based on returns, Stessa's reporting is genuinely useful. See our article on rental property cash flow analysis for context on how to use these metrics in portfolio decisions.

Rent Collection in Stessa

Stessa launched rent collection capabilities, allowing tenants to pay online through the Stessa platform. When rent is collected through Stessa, it is automatically recorded and categorized in the financial system—eliminating the need to manually reconcile bank deposits.

Rent collection via Stessa:

  • ACH transfers (free for standard deposits, faster settlement available for a fee)
  • Tenants access a payment portal
  • Automated payment reminders
  • Payment history tracked in the tenant ledger

This feature makes Stessa more useful as a light operational tool, not just a financial tracker. However, it does not include lease management, maintenance requests, or tenant screening—landlords needing those functions still need a supplementary operational tool.

Tax Preparation Support

Tax preparation is one of Stessa's strongest value propositions. The platform is built around Schedule E alignment, meaning expense categories map directly to IRS reporting requirements.

At year-end, Stessa generates:

  • Income and expense summaries by property
  • Schedule E ready reports
  • Document archives for receipts and statements

For landlords who hand this over to a CPA, providing Stessa exports saves hours of manual compilation. For landlords who file independently, Stessa's categorized data makes the process significantly faster.

The Stessa Tax service extends this by connecting you with CPAs who work directly with the Stessa data format. This is a premium service priced at $125 per return—competitive for straightforward Schedule E filings with a few properties.

For more on rental property tax optimization, see our article on rental property tax deductions.

What Stessa Does Not Do

Being direct about Stessa's gaps saves frustration:

  • No maintenance management: Tenants cannot submit maintenance requests through Stessa. Work orders and vendor tracking are not part of the platform.
  • No lease management: Leases are not stored or managed in Stessa beyond document uploads in Pro.
  • No tenant screening: Credit checks, background reports, and eviction searches are not available.
  • No vacancy listings: Stessa does not publish listings to Zillow or other platforms.
  • No comprehensive property management accounting: Stessa is investor-facing, not property-manager-facing. Trust accounting, CAM reconciliation, and multi-client accounting are not supported.

For landlords who need operational tools alongside financial tracking, Stessa works best as one component of a two-tool stack. An operational platform like Avail, TurboTenant, or TenantCloud handles leasing and maintenance; Stessa handles the financial layer.

For a full view of how tools can work together, see best property management software for portfolios.

Stessa vs. Competitors

Stessa vs. Landlord Studio: Both focus on financial tracking. Landlord Studio has a stronger mobile-first expense capture experience with receipt scanning on the free plan. Stessa has better portfolio analytics and dashboards. For mobile-first expense capture, Landlord Studio edges ahead; for portfolio-level financial visibility, Stessa is stronger.

Stessa vs. QuickBooks: QuickBooks is a full-featured accounting platform that handles far more complexity than Stessa. It also requires significantly more setup and does not understand rental property-specific categories natively. For a landlord with five properties, Stessa is faster to set up and produces better property-specific reporting. For a landlord with a complex multi-entity portfolio or business income beyond rentals, QuickBooks offers more flexibility.

Stessa vs. Buildium/AppFolio accounting: Enterprise platforms include accounting comparable to or exceeding Stessa's capabilities, but they charge accordingly. For pure financial tracking at no cost, Stessa is hard to match.

Who Stessa Works Best For

Stessa is ideal for:

  • Real estate investors managing 1–30+ rental properties who want clear financial visibility
  • Landlords filing Schedule E who want tax preparation support
  • Investors using multiple platforms who need a dedicated financial tracking layer
  • Landlords who have outgrown spreadsheets but don't need full operational software
  • New landlords who want to understand their property's financial performance before investing in paid tools

Stessa is not ideal for:

  • Landlords who need one platform to handle operations, maintenance, and accounting in one place
  • Operators managing commercial or mixed-use assets with complex lease accounting
  • Property managers handling accounting for investor-owner clients who need trust accounting

Summary

Stessa is the best free financial tracking tool available for rental property investors. Its automatic transaction import, Schedule E alignment, portfolio dashboards, and cash flow reporting are genuinely useful for any landlord who wants to understand their properties' financial performance.

The free plan is sufficient for most investors. Stessa Pro adds document management and OCR that justify the upgrade for active investors who process high transaction volumes.

The clear limitation is what Stessa does not do—operations, maintenance, leasing, and screening all require a separate tool. For investors who already have an operational platform and need a financial layer on top, Stessa fills that role better than any competitor at its price point.
