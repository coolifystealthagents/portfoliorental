Managing access to multi-family rental properties presents unique challenges that single-family landlords do not face. Building entry, package rooms, amenity spaces, parking garages, and individual unit doors all require controlled access for tenants, guests, vendors, and delivery personnel. A comprehensive keyless entry system simplifies this complexity, improves security, reduces key management headaches, and delivers a premium experience that attracts quality tenants.

This guide covers the full spectrum of keyless entry technology for multi-family properties — from building entry and intercoms to unit door locks and package delivery access.

The Multi-Family Access Control Challenge

Traditional key-based access for multi-family properties creates a cascade of management problems:

  • Key proliferation — Tenants lose keys, share keys, and make unauthorized copies
  • Turnover costs — Rekeying all affected locks at every turnover is expensive and time-consuming
  • Guest and vendor access — Managing building entry for guests, contractors, cleaners, and delivery services requires human coordination
  • Security gaps — Traditional intercoms can be easily bypassed; physical keys can be copied

Keyless entry systems address all of these issues while providing an access audit trail that traditional keys cannot.

Components of a Multi-Family Keyless Entry System

A comprehensive system addresses multiple access points:

1. Building Entry / Lobby Access

The front entry is the most critical control point. Options range from basic keypad entry to sophisticated video intercom systems with remote access.

Keypad Entry

  • Simple numeric keypad at the main entry
  • Tenant enters a code to access the building
  • Pros: Simple, low cost; Cons: Codes can be shared widely, no guest management

Key Fob / Proximity Card Systems

  • Tenants carry a key fob or card that is read by a wireless reader at entry points
  • Easily deactivated when a fob is lost or a tenant moves out
  • Can control multiple access points (garage, pool, package room, lobby) with a single credential
  • Industry standard for mid-size to large multi-family properties

Smart Intercom / Video Intercom Systems

  • Visitors ring the unit intercom; tenant can see and speak with the visitor
  • Tenant remotely unlocks the door via intercom panel or smartphone app
  • Modern systems like Latch, Butterfly MX, Salto, and 2N offer full smartphone-based management
  • Package delivery integration: provide delivery carriers (Amazon, FedEx, USPS) with time-limited PIN access

2. Package Room Access

Package theft is one of the top concerns for apartment renters. Smart package room solutions:

  • Smart locker systems (Amazon Hub, Luxer One, Parcel Pending) — Automatically assign packages to lockers; tenants receive unique retrieval codes
  • Controlled-access package rooms — Smart lock on the package room door; only tenants can enter; carriers are given time-limited access

3. Amenity Space Access

Pool, gym, rooftop, and other amenity spaces benefit from controlled access to ensure only current tenants use these spaces:

  • Key fob readers on amenity space doors (same credential as building entry)
  • Time-limited access schedules (pool closed after 10 PM)
  • Access logging for liability documentation

4. Parking Garage Access

  • Vehicle gate controllers with key fob or license plate recognition
  • Prevents non-tenant use of parking spaces
  • Remote gate management for landlord and vendors

5. Individual Unit Doors

For individual unit doors in multi-family properties, see our detailed guide on smart locks for rental properties.

Top Keyless Entry Products for Multi-Family

Latch (Building Access Platform)

Latch is a purpose-built multi-family access platform offering:

  • L series hardware for unit doors
  • R series for common area doors (lobbies, amenity spaces, package rooms)
  • Full property management platform with resident app
  • Multiple access credentials: app, NFC card, pin code
  • Works with property management software including Yardi and RealPage
  • Built-in guest access management and delivery access
  • Best for: Mid-size to large apartment buildings (20+ units)

ButterflyMX Smart Video Intercom

ButterflyMX focuses specifically on the video intercom use case:

  • Full video intercom with resident smartphone app
  • Residents can unlock from anywhere via phone
  • Virtual keys for guests (time-limited)
  • Package delivery integration
  • Works with existing building wiring in many installations
  • Best for: Buildings that need a best-in-class entry intercom

Salto KS (Keys as a Service)

Salto KS is a cloud-based access control platform:

  • Works with Salto's electronic door hardware
  • Mobile credentials on resident smartphones
  • Online access management without physical key distribution
  • Good for multi-family buildings of any size
  • Best for: Landlords who want a scalable, enterprise-grade platform

KISI

KISI is a cloud-based access control platform suitable for multi-family:

  • Works with standard electronic locks and readers
  • Mobile app access with backup PIN codes
  • Detailed access logs
  • Integrates with many property management systems
  • Best for: Tech-forward landlords who want API integration flexibility

Aiphone GT Series (Traditional Video Intercom)

For landlords who prefer proven technology over cutting-edge platforms:

  • Hardwired video intercom system
  • Unit intercom panels allow residents to see and speak with visitors
  • Door release from intercom panel
  • Highly reliable; minimal technology dependence
  • Best for: Properties where smartphone-dependent systems raise usability concerns for older residents

Implementation Considerations

Building Infrastructure

Before selecting a system, assess your building's infrastructure:

  • Existing wiring — Can existing intercom wiring be repurposed, or is new wiring needed?
  • Internet/Wi-Fi — Cloud-based systems require reliable internet at entry points; consider a dedicated connection for access control
  • Power — Electronic access control requires constant power; battery backup is essential for entry systems
  • Door hardware compatibility — Assess whether existing door hardware can be retrofitted or must be replaced

Tenant Onboarding

When transitioning from physical keys to keyless entry:

  • Conduct a building meeting or individual orientations to demonstrate the system
  • Provide written instructions and tech support resources
  • Offer a transition period where both physical keys and the new system work simultaneously
  • Ensure all tenants are successfully onboarded before retiring physical keys

Vendor and Maintenance Access

A key advantage of smart access systems is simplifying vendor access:

  • Issue time-limited PINs for contractors. See find reliable contractors for sourcing tips
  • Issue scheduled PINs for recurring vendors (cleaners, landscapers)
  • All vendor access is logged with timestamp
  • No need to physically hand off keys or be present for access

Emergency Services Access

Work with your local fire department on emergency access:

  • Many buildings install a Knox Box at the main entry containing emergency access keys or master fobs
  • Ensure your electronic access system has manual override capability (physical key backup)
  • Confirm your fire suppression and alarm systems are compatible with any door control systems (doors must unlock automatically when fire alarm activates in most codes). Check FEMA fire safety resources for guidance.

Cost Overview

System Component Estimated Cost
Video intercom (per building entry) $1,500-$5,000
Key fob reader per door $400-$800
Key fob per resident $10-$25
Smart locker system (12 lockers) $8,000-$15,000
Parking gate controller $2,000-$6,000
Monthly software/platform fee $50-$300/month depending on units
Professional installation $500-$2,000+ per project

For a 20-unit building, a complete keyless entry system with building intercom, common area access control, and smart unit door locks typically costs $15,000-$40,000 installed — a significant capital investment that should be evaluated against tenant retention, reduced administrative costs, and potential rent premium.

ROI and Tenant Impact

Reduced Administrative Costs

  • Eliminate physical key management — no cutting, tracking, or replacing lost keys
  • No rekeying costs at turnover — deactivate the prior tenant's credential in seconds
  • Reduced need for staff presence during vendor access

Rent Premium and Competitive Advantage

In competitive markets, keyless entry is a top rental property amenity upgrade. NAR's technology survey confirms rising tenant demand for smart features. Buildings with modern access systems attract better tenants and can charge a modest premium ($25-$75/month) for the convenience and security.

Reduced Liability

Comprehensive access logs provide documentation of who accessed the building and when. This is valuable for:

  • Investigating incidents (theft, vandalism)
  • Demonstrating proper access control to insurers. Forbes covers access control trends.
  • Responding to tenant disputes about unauthorized access

Conclusion

Keyless entry systems for multi-family rental properties represent a significant but worthwhile investment in operational efficiency, tenant experience, and building security. The most effective systems take a comprehensive approach — building entry, amenity access, package delivery, and individual unit locks — creating a seamless experience from the street to the apartment door.
