What Is the BRRRR Strategy?

BRRRR stands for Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat. It is a real estate investment strategy that allows portfolio owners to recycle their capital across multiple properties instead of having cash tied up in a single deal. For investors looking to build a rental property portfolio, BRRRR is one of the most capital-efficient paths to scale.

The core idea is simple: buy a distressed property below market value, renovate it to force appreciation, rent it out, refinance to pull your initial investment back out, and then repeat the process with the recovered capital. The strategy has been widely popularized by the BiggerPockets investor community.

Phase 1: Buy

The foundation of a successful BRRRR deal is buying right. You need to acquire properties at a significant discount to their after-repair value (ARV).

Finding BRRRR-Worthy Deals

  • Distressed properties — Look for homes that need cosmetic or moderate rehab but have solid structural bones
  • Off-market deals — Direct mail, driving for dollars, and wholesaler relationships often yield the best discounts
  • Auction and bank-owned properties — REOs and foreclosures can be acquired well below ARV

The 70% Rule

A common guideline: pay no more than 70% of ARV minus repair costs.

Example: If a property has an ARV of $200,000 and needs $30,000 in repairs, your maximum purchase price should be ($200,000 x 0.70) - $30,000 = $110,000.

Before committing, run each potential deal through a structured rental property analysis to confirm the numbers work.

Phase 2: Rehab

The rehab phase is where you force appreciation. Focus on renovations that increase both rental value and appraised value.

Choosing renovations with strong ROI is critical during the rehab phase.

High-Impact Renovations

  1. Kitchen updates — New countertops, cabinets, and appliances
  2. Bathroom refresh — New vanity, tile, and fixtures
  3. Flooring — Replace carpet with LVP or hardwood
  4. Paint — Fresh interior and exterior paint
  5. Curb appeal — Landscaping, front door, lighting

Rehab Budget Management

  • Get multiple contractor bids before starting
  • Add a 15-20% contingency buffer
  • Track expenses weekly against budget
  • Prioritize speed — every extra month costs you holding costs
  • Use a maintenance cost strategy to keep rehab expenses predictable

Phase 3: Rent

Once rehab is complete, place a qualified tenant quickly. Your rental income needs to support the refinanced mortgage payment with cash flow to spare.

Setting the Right Rent

  • Pull comparable rentals within a 1-mile radius
  • Price competitively to minimize vacancy
  • Account for the upgraded condition of your property — renovated units command premium rents

Tenant Screening

Strong tenant screening protects your investment:

  • Credit check (minimum 620 score recommended)
  • Income verification (3x monthly rent)
  • Landlord references
  • Background and eviction check

Phase 4: Refinance

The refinance is where the BRRRR strategy pays off. You are converting the equity you created through rehab into usable capital.

Seasoning Requirements

Most lenders require a 6-12 month seasoning period before they will refinance based on the new appraised value. Some portfolio lenders and DSCR lenders have shorter or no seasoning requirements. Explore your rental property financing options to find the best refinance terms for your situation.

Maximizing Your Appraisal

  • Provide the appraiser with a list of comparable sales
  • Document all improvements with before/after photos
  • Ensure the property is clean and well-presented
  • Have the lease in place to demonstrate rental income

Cash-Out Refinance Math

Example:

  • Purchase price: $110,000
  • Rehab costs: $30,000
  • Total invested: $140,000
  • After-repair appraised value: $200,000
  • Cash-out refinance at 75% LTV: $150,000
  • Capital recovered: $150,000 - $140,000 = $10,000 profit + all original capital returned

Use a rental property ROI calculator to model different refinance scenarios before committing.

Phase 5: Repeat

With your capital returned, reinvest into the next BRRRR deal. Each successful cycle adds a cash-flowing property to your portfolio while keeping your capital working.

Scaling Tips

  • Build a team: contractor, property manager, lender, agent
  • Systematize your deal analysis process
  • Develop multiple deal sources to maintain pipeline
  • Consider hiring a project manager once you are running 3+ rehabs
  • Learn more about scaling your rental business as your portfolio grows

Common BRRRR Mistakes

  1. Overestimating ARV — Be conservative with your after-repair value estimates
  2. Underestimating rehab costs — Always include contingency and holding costs
  3. Skipping tenant screening — A bad tenant can erase months of profit
  4. Ignoring seasoning periods — Plan your timeline around lender requirements
  5. Over-improving — Renovate to rental grade, not luxury grade

Is BRRRR Right for You?

The BRRRR strategy works best for investors who:

  • Have access to $50,000+ in initial capital (or hard money lending)
  • Are willing to manage or oversee renovation projects
  • Want to scale a rental portfolio without continuously injecting new capital
  • Can handle the complexity of coordinating multiple phases

If you are just starting out, consider doing your first deal as a straightforward buy-and-hold to build experience before attempting a full BRRRR cycle.
