24:T3628,<p>A well-crafted welcome book is one of the most valuable tools in a vacation rental host's arsenal — and one of the most overlooked. While some hosts leave nothing at all, and others leave a single laminated sheet, the best hosts create a thoughtful, complete guest guide that makes every stay feel effortless and personal.</p>
<p>This guide walks you through everything you need to know to create a <strong>welcome book for your vacation rental</strong>: what to include, how to organize it, how to make it look great, and how to keep it updated over time.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="what-is-a-vacation-rental-welcome-book">What Is a Vacation Rental Welcome Book?</h2>
<p>A welcome book (also called a house manual, guest guide, or welcome binder) is a physical or digital document left at your property that gives guests everything they need to enjoy their stay. It covers:</p>
<ul>
<li>How the property works (appliances, systems, quirks)</li>
<li>House rules and important policies</li>
<li>Check-in and checkout procedures</li>
<li>Local recommendations (restaurants, activities, transportation)</li>
<li>Emergency contacts and nearby services</li>
</ul>
<p>A great welcome book reduces guest questions by 50-70% — meaning fewer messages during their stay, fewer negative reviews about confusion, and more time for guests to actually enjoy themselves. It's a cornerstone of excellent <a href="/blog/guest-communication-short-term-rental">guest communication for short-term rentals</a>.</p>
<p>For the full STR operations picture, visit our <a href="/blog/short-term-rental-management-guide">short-term rental management guide</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="physical-vs-digital-welcome-books-which-is-better">Physical vs. Digital Welcome Books: Which Is Better?</h2>
<p>Both formats have their place, and many hosts use both.</p>
<h3 id="physical-welcome-book">Physical Welcome Book</h3>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Available immediately when guests arrive (no phone needed)</li>
<li>Works during internet outages</li>
<li>Feels warm and personal</li>
<li>Guests actually read it</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Can get damaged, lost, or defaced</li>
<li>Requires manual updates when info changes</li>
<li>Can look low-quality if not done well</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Primary living spaces, older guest demographics, properties in areas with unreliable cell service</p>
<h3 id="digital-welcome-book">Digital Welcome Book</h3>
<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Easy to update instantly</li>
<li>Can include clickable links, maps, and videos</li>
<li>Can be shared before arrival via message</li>
<li>No physical maintenance required</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Requires guest to have their phone handy</li>
<li>Less tactile/personal feeling</li>
<li>Tech issues can make it inaccessible</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Tech-forward guests, urban properties, portfolio hosts managing many properties</p>
<p><strong>Tools for digital welcome books:</strong> Hostfully, Touch Stay, Lodgify, and Notion (for budget-conscious hosts)</p>
<p><strong>Recommendation:</strong> Create a digital version first, then print a condensed physical version for in-property use.</p>
<hr>
<h2 id="the-8-essential-sections-of-a-vacation-rental-welcome-book">The 8 Essential Sections of a Vacation Rental Welcome Book</h2>
<h3 id="section-1-welcome-message">Section 1: Welcome Message</h3>
<p>Start with a warm, personal greeting. This sets the tone for the entire guest experience.</p>
<p><strong>Template:</strong></p>
<blockquote>
<p>Welcome to [Property Name]!</p>
<p>We're so glad you're here. This home has been a labor of love for us, and we genuinely hope it becomes your favorite place to stay in [City/Area].</p>
<p>This guide has everything you need — from how to work the coffee maker to where to find the best tacos in town. Read through it when you get a moment, but don't stress about memorizing anything. We're just a text away if you need us.</p>
<p>Enjoy every minute.</p>
<p>— [Your Name(s)]</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Include your contact information here and reiterate that guests should reach out if anything is unclear.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="section-2-property-essentials">Section 2: Property Essentials</h3>
<p>This is the "how things work" section — the practical information guests need first.</p>
<p><strong>Must include:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Wi-Fi:</strong> Network name and password (make this large and easy to find)</li>
<li><strong>Parking:</strong> Exact instructions with a photo or diagram if needed</li>
<li><strong>Check-in/Checkout times</strong> and procedure</li>
<li><strong>Heating and cooling:</strong> How to use the thermostat, acceptable temperature range</li>
<li><strong>Washer/dryer:</strong> Controls, detergent location, any special instructions</li>
<li><strong>Trash and recycling:</strong> Bin locations, pickup schedule, sorting rules</li>
<li><strong>TV and streaming:</strong> How to access streaming apps, smart TV instructions</li>
<li><strong>Smart home devices:</strong> How to use any Alexa/Google devices, smart locks, etc.</li>
</ul>
<p>For each item, add a one-sentence description of any quirks: "The back right burner on the stove runs a bit hot — adjust accordingly."</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="section-3-kitchen-guide">Section 3: Kitchen Guide</h3>
<p>Guests often struggle with unfamiliar appliances. A brief kitchen guide prevents both frustration and appliance misuse.</p>
<p><strong>Cover:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Coffee maker (type, where pods/grounds are, how to clean)</li>
<li>Oven and stove (any quirks or digital controls)</li>
<li>Dishwasher (settings, detergent pods location)</li>
<li>Garbage disposal (if applicable — note if there isn't one)</li>
<li>Location of: utensils, pots, cutting boards, spices, cleaning supplies</li>
<li>What's available to cook with vs. what should not be used</li>
</ul>
<p>Cross-reference this with your <a href="/blog/amenities-checklist-short-term-rental">amenities checklist</a> to make sure everything listed is actually stocked.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="section-4-house-rules">Section 4: House Rules</h3>
<p>Be clear and specific, but not punitive in tone. State rules as information, not warnings.</p>
<p><strong>Common rules to include:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>No-smoking policy</strong> (inside and potentially on property) -- see <a href="https://www.epa.gov/indoor-air-quality-iaq">EPA indoor air quality</a> guidance</li>
<li><strong>Pet policy</strong> (allowed/not allowed, any fees)</li>
<li><strong>Guest limits</strong> (maximum number of overnight guests)</li>
<li><strong>Noise/quiet hours</strong> (especially important for multi-unit buildings or neighborhoods)</li>
<li><strong>Parking rules</strong> (number of vehicles, where to park)</li>
<li><strong>Pool/hot tub rules</strong> (if applicable)</li>
<li><strong>Checkout expectations</strong> (dishes, trash, towels)</li>
<li><strong>Event policy</strong> (parties allowed or not)</li>
</ul>
<p>For your full checkout expectations, see our <a href="/blog/checkout-instructions-template">checkout instructions template</a> — you can summarize the key points in the welcome book and reference the full instructions on a separate card.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="section-5-local-area-guide">Section 5: Local Area Guide</h3>
<p>This is where your welcome book goes from useful to unforgettable. Guests want the insider knowledge that only a local can provide — not Google results, but real recommendations.</p>
<p><strong>Organize by category:</strong></p>
<p><strong>Restaurants &#x26; Cafes</strong>
List 5-10 favorites with a sentence about each:</p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li><strong>[Restaurant Name]</strong> — Best breakfast burritos in the city; get there before 9am or expect a wait. 0.3 miles away.</li>
<li><strong>[Restaurant Name]</strong> — A hidden gem for date night; make a reservation. Great wine list.</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>Activities &#x26; Attractions</strong></p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li><strong>[Park/Trail]</strong> — Easy 3-mile loop with a waterfall at the end; parking fills up by 9am on weekends</li>
<li><strong>[Museum/Gallery]</strong> — Free on Thursday evenings; skip the main exhibits and go straight to [specific wing]</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>Practical Information</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Nearest grocery store (with hours)</li>
<li>Nearest pharmacy</li>
<li>Gas stations</li>
<li>Urgent care clinic</li>
<li>Hospital (with address)</li>
<li>ATM locations</li>
<li>Public transit options</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Getting Around</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Ride-share availability in the area</li>
<li>Bike rental locations</li>
<li>Parking tips for downtown/popular areas</li>
</ul>
<p>This section is worth the investment of time. Guests who follow your local recommendations consistently mention them in reviews. For a more detailed approach, see our <a href="/blog/local-area-guide-vacation-rental">local area guide for vacation rental guests</a>.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="section-6-emergency-information">Section 6: Emergency Information</h3>
<p>Place this section clearly — guests should be able to find it fast in a stressful moment.</p>
<p><strong>Include:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Your phone number (and backup contact if you're unavailable)</li>
<li>Local emergency number (911 in the US)</li>
<li>Non-emergency police line</li>
<li>Nearest urgent care clinic and hospital</li>
<li><a href="https://www.cdc.gov/poison-control/">Poison control center</a> (1-800-222-1222)</li>
<li>Gas company (for suspected leaks)</li>
<li>Electrician emergency contact</li>
<li>Property management contact (if different from you)</li>
<li>Location of: fire extinguisher, first aid kit, circuit breaker, water shutoff valve. The <a href="https://www.cpsc.gov/Safety-Education/Safety-Guides/General-Information">CPSC home safety checklist</a> covers essential safety items.</li>
</ul>
<p>Consider placing the emergency section as a laminated card on the fridge in addition to the welcome book.</p>
<hr>
<h3 id="section-7-checkout-instructions">Section 7: Checkout Instructions</h3>
<p>Keep this section brief in the welcome book — a friendly reminder of what's expected.</p>
<p><strong>Template:</strong></p>
<blockquote>
<p><strong>Checkout by [Time]</strong></p>
<p>To help us prepare for the next guests:</p>
<ul>
<li>Leave used towels in the bathtub or shower</li>
<li>Strip the beds and leave linens on the mattress</li>
<li>Load and start the dishwasher</li>
<li>Take out any trash and recyclables</li>
<li>Return furniture to its original position</li>
<li>Lock all doors and windows before leaving</li>
<li>Leave the key [in lockbox / on kitchen counter / other]</li>
</ul>
<p>That's it! Thank you for being a wonderful guest.</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h3 id="section-8-faq-page">Section 8: FAQ Page</h3>
<p>Anticipate the top 10 questions you get from guests and answer them here.</p>
<p><strong>Common FAQs:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>"Where is the [nearest] grocery store?" — [Answer]</li>
<li>"Can I check in early / check out late?" — [Answer]</li>
<li>"The [appliance] isn't working — what do I do?" — [Contact info + basic troubleshooting]</li>
<li>"Where can I find extra [towels/blankets/pillows]?" — [Location]</li>
<li>"Is the neighborhood safe to walk at night?" — [Honest answer]</li>
<li>"Where should I watch the sunset?" — [Local insider tip]</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="designing-your-physical-welcome-book">Designing Your Physical Welcome Book</h2>
<p>Presentation matters. A professional-looking welcome book signals that you take hosting seriously.</p>
<p><strong>Options by budget:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Budget:</strong> Print at home in color, put in a clear binder with tabbed dividers</li>
<li><strong>Mid-range:</strong> Design in Canva, print at a local print shop, bind at Staples</li>
<li><strong>Premium:</strong> Hire a designer on Fiverr or use a service like Blurb to create a professionally printed booklet</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Design tips:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Use your property's color palette</li>
<li>Include photos of the property, nearby attractions, and recommended restaurants</li>
<li>Use icons to make sections scannable</li>
<li>Keep fonts readable (minimum 12pt for body text)</li>
<li>Include a table of contents</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="keeping-your-welcome-book-updated">Keeping Your Welcome Book Updated</h2>
<p>An outdated welcome book is worse than none — it sends guests to closed restaurants and gives them wrong codes. Set a calendar reminder to review and update your welcome book:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>After every third booking:</strong> Check that all codes, passwords, and appliance instructions are current</li>
<li><strong>Seasonally:</strong> Update local recommendations (hours change, restaurants close)</li>
<li><strong>After any property changes:</strong> New appliances, updated checkout procedures, new rules</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>A great welcome book is an investment that pays dividends in reduced guest questions, higher review scores, and a reputation for exceptional hospitality. The hosts who take the time to create a thoughtful, personalized guest guide consistently outperform those who don't — on every metric that matters.</p>
